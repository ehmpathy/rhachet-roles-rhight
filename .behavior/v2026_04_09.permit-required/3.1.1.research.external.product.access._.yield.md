## 3. Legal Considerations

### 3.1 Copyright of Enacted Codes

**Source 22** [academic/legal]
- **Title**: Veeck v. Southern Building Code Congress Int'l, Inc.
- **URL**: https://law.justia.com/cases/federal/appellate-courts/F3/293/791/521953/
- **Date**: 2002
- **Quote**: "A three-fifths majority of the court's fifteen judges held that copyright protection no longer applied to model codes once they were enacted into law. As law, the model codes enter the public domain and are not subject to the copyright holder's exclusive prerogatives."
- **Credibility**: [official] - 5th Circuit Court of Appeals (en banc)
- **Finding**: In 5th Circuit, enacted codes lose copyright protection

**Source 23** [official]
- **Title**: Georgia v. Public.Resource.Org, Inc. - Supreme Court
- **URL**: https://www.law.cornell.edu/supremecourt/text/18-1150
- **Date**: 2020
- **Quote**: "Under the government edicts doctrine, officials empowered to speak with the force of law cannot be the authors of the works they create in the course of their official duties."
- **Credibility**: [official] - U.S. Supreme Court
- **Finding**: Government edicts (including annotations) not copyrightable; extends to state/local

**Source 24** [practitioner]
- **Title**: Public.Resource.Org - EFF
- **URL**: https://www.eff.org/cases/publicresource-freeingthelaw
- **Date**: 2023
- **Quote**: "In a democracy, the people must have the right to read, know, and speak about the laws by which we choose to govern ourselves. Our laws belong to all of us."
- **Credibility**: [practitioner] - Electronic Frontier Foundation
- **Finding**: Active litigation supporting free access to incorporated standards

### 3.2 Web Scraping Legality

**Source 25** [official]
- **Title**: hiQ Labs v. LinkedIn - 9th Circuit (2022)
- **URL**: https://law.justia.com/cases/federal/appellate-courts/ca9/17-16783/17-16783-2022-04-18.html
- **Date**: 2022-04-18
- **Quote**: "The Ninth Circuit reaffirmed following a remand from the U.S. Supreme Court that the law did not apply to the automatic collection of publicly accessible data."
- **Credibility**: [official] - 9th Circuit Court of Appeals
- **Finding**: CFAA does not prohibit scraping publicly accessible data

**Source 26** [practitioner]
- **Title**: EFF - Victory in hiQ v. LinkedIn
- **URL**: https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2019/09/victory-ruling-hiq-v-linkedin-protects-scraping-public-data
- **Date**: 2019-09
- **Quote**: "Automated scraping of publicly accessible data likely does not violate the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA)... authorization commonly means that something is not generally available, and that access requires permission of some sort, whereas here, 'the default is free access.'"
- **Credibility**: [practitioner] - EFF analysis
- **Finding**: Public data scraping likely legal under CFAA, but other claims (trespass, copyright) may apply

**Source 27** [practitioner]
- **Title**: Is Web Scraping Legal? - ScraperAPI 2026
- **URL**: https://www.scraperapi.com/web-scraping/is-web-scraping-legal/
- **Date**: 2026
- **Quote**: "In the United States, there is no single legal or regulatory framework that governs scraping. Instead, the legality of scraping depends fundamentally on what data you collect, how you collect it, what you do with it, and which legal frameworks apply."
- **Credibility**: [practitioner] - industry analysis
- **Finding**: Context-dependent; multiple legal frameworks may apply

## 7. Recommendations for Permit-Required System

### 7.1 Building Code Access Strategy

1. **Primary**: Indiana Administrative Code 675 IAC (free, official)
2. **Secondary**: UpCodes (verify current free tier status)
3. **Fallback**: Direct ICC link with paywall warning to user
4. **Cache**: Immutable caching for versioned code text (RFC 8246)

### 7.2 Permit Database Access Strategy

1. **Preferred**: Apply for Accela API partnership via DBNS (permitquestions@indy.gov)
2. **Interim**: Web portal scraping with rate limiting and robots.txt compliance
3. **Fallback**: Manual lookup with structured output
4. **Monitor**: Open Indy Data Portal (data.indy.gov) for future permit data exposure

### 7.3 Legal Risk Mitigation

1. Cite enacted law, not model codes where possible
2. Cache code text with edition/year versioning
3. Respect robots.txt and implement exponential backoff
4. Document data sources and access methods
5. Include disclaimers: "not legal advice"
