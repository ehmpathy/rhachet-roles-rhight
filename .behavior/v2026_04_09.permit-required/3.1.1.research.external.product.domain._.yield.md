- **Active**: "Construction is underway and inspections are being performed."
- **Expired**: "The permit has been inactive for too long—typically 180 days without an inspection request."

- **Void/Cancelled**: "The permit has been permanently closed."

### 1.4 Jurisdiction / Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ)

**[FACT] [official]**
> "An organization, office, or individual responsible for enforcing the requirements of a code or standard, or for approving equipment, materials, an installation, or a procedure."

— NFPA 101 Life Safety Code (2018 edition)
- source: [Shovels - AHJ in Construction](https://www.shovels.ai/blog/ahj-in-construction/)

**[FACT] [official]**
> "Where public safety is primary, the AHJ may be a federal, state, local, or other regional department or individual such as a fire chief; fire marshal; chief of a fire prevention bureau, labor department, or health department; building official; electrical inspector; or others having statutory authority."

— NFPA Annex
- source: [LotusWorks - What is an AHJ](https://lotusworks.com/what-is-ahj/)

**[FACT] [official]**
> "In the United States authorities having jurisdictions (AHJ) over the development and enforcement of building codes are: states, counties, and municipalities."

— OneClick Code
- source: [OneClick Code - Building Code Adoption](https://www.oneclickcode.com/blog/building-code-adoption-states-local)

**[FACT] [official]**
Jurisdiction hierarchy models:
- **Statewide Adoption with No Local Amendments**: "No matter if the project is in the hills or in the plains, if a project is in a State Code State the project will have the same building code requirements"
- **Statewide Adoption with Local Flexibility**: "base code is adopted statewide, but local authorities are permitted to make amendments"
- **Local Code Adoption**: "codes are adopted by local governments ('home rule'), generally through an action of the city or county"

— OneClick Code
- source: [OneClick Code - Building Code Adoption](https://www.oneclickcode.com/blog/building-code-adoption-states-local)

**[FACT] [official]**
> "States tend to follow one of two types of rules regarding empowerment of local jurisdictions: Home Rule or Dillon's Rule. Ten states are exclusively Home Rule: AK, IA, MA, MT, NJ, NV, OH, OR, UT and WV. Nine states are exclusively Dillon Rule states: AL, DE, MS, NE, NH, OK, VA, VT, and WY."

— ICC Code Adoption Document
- source: [ICC - How States Adopt I-Codes](https://www.iccsafe.org/wp-content/uploads/adoption_ordinances/HowStatesAdopt_I-Codes.pdf)

### 1.5 Inspection

**[FACT] [official]**
> "The system uses the inspections object to manage inspection records during their complete lifecycle."

— Accela Developer Documentation
- source: [Accela Root Objects](https://developer.accela.com/docs/construct-rootObjects.html)

**[FACT] [official]**
> "The system defines some users as inspectors and you can only assign inspections to these users."

— Accela Developer Documentation
- source: [Accela Root Objects](https://developer.accela.com/docs/construct-rootObjects.html)

**[FACT] [official]**
Inspection sequence (typical order):
1. **Post Address** - Job site address visible from street
2. **Temporary Construction Power** - Meter socket, disconnect, ground fault protection
3. **Footing Inspection** - "done after the excavation is complete...but prior to the placement of concrete"
4. **Foundation Inspection** - After foundation walls formed, before framing
5. **Rough-In Underground Plumbing** - "done after the underground or under slab plumbing is installed but prior to its being covered"
6. **Sewer Inspection** - Building sewer installed, before covering
7. **Water Service** - Water service piping in place, prior to covering
8. **Gas Line Air Test** - After gas piping installation, before concealment
9. **Plumbing Rough-In** - Above-ground water and drainage piping
10. **HVAC Rough-In** - Heating/cooling system and ductwork
11. **Electrical Rough-In** - Premises wiring system
12. **Frame and Roof Inspection** - "done after the structures walls, floors, and other framing members are in place"
13. **Insulation Inspection** - Verifies R values and air sealing before wallboard
14. **Final Inspection** - After all work completion and sub-trade approvals

— City of Gallatin, TN
- source: [Gallatin TN - Typical Required Inspections](https://www.gallatintn.gov/185/Typical-Required-Inspections)

**[FACT] [practitioner]**
Inspection result categories:
- **APPROVED**: "Work is approved and OK to proceed"
- **APPROVED WITH CORRECTIONS**: "Inspection is approved and the builder may proceed, but corrections will need to be checked again at the next required inspection"
- **NOT APPROVED/CORRECTIONS ISSUED**: "Inspection was not approved, corrections have been issued, and work must be checked again prior to additional construction"

— Snohomish County WA
- source: [Snohomish County FAQ](https://www.snohomishcountywa.gov/Faq.aspx?QID=1212)

- source: [Accela Root Objects](https://developer.accela.com/docs/construct-rootObjects.html)

— Verified Contractor Authority
- source: [Verified Contractor Authority](https://verifiedcontractorauthority.com/contractor-license-verification)

**[FACT] [official]**
Standard verification process:
1. **Identify the Trade and Jurisdiction**: "Confirm what type of work is being performed (GC, electrical, plumbing, HVAC, etc.) and in which state and municipality"
2. **Determine the Correct Licensing Authority**: "State board, county office, or municipal department"
3. **Obtain License Number**: "Request this directly from the contractor in writing"
4. **Query Official Database**: "Use only the government-operated lookup tool"
5. **Check Disciplinary History**: "Most state licensing boards display formal complaints, citations, and disciplinary history"
6. **Document Results**: "Screenshot or print the database result with timestamp"

— Verified Contractor Authority
- source: [Verified Contractor Authority](https://verifiedcontractorauthority.com/contractor-license-verification)
