# 3.1.5.research.reflection.product.rootcause._.v1

root cause analysis for the patenter role.

---

## what is the symptom?

**observable problem**: inventors struggle to file quality patent applications.

**how it manifests**:
- inventors don't know what prior art exists before they invest in applications
- inventors spend weeks on manual research with uncertain coverage
- inventors pay $15-30k to attorneys who spend hours on research and draft
- inventors file weak claims that get rejected or narrowed
- inventors miss patentable opportunities because the process feels too opaque

**when it occurs**:
- at the "I have an idea, now what?" moment
- when inventors try to search patents themselves and drown in noise
- when attorneys return work that feels disconnected from the invention
- when office actions surprise inventors with prior art they didn't know about

---

## 5 whys tree

### branch 1: knowledge gap

1. **why do inventors struggle?** → they don't know what prior art exists
2. **why don't they know?** → prior art search is tedious and requires specialized knowledge
3. **why is search tedious?** → patent databases return noisy results; claim language is arcane
4. **why are tools arcane?** → tools are designed for patent attorneys, not inventors
5. **why attorney-focused?** → **the patent industry has been attorney-centric; inventors were never the direct user**

### branch 2: cost barrier

1. **why do inventors struggle?** → attorney fees are $15-30k per application
2. **why so expensive?** → attorneys spend 20-40 hours on research and draft
3. **why so many hours?** → search is manual, claim draft is manual, objection prep is manual
4. **why manual?** → no automation tools exist for the inventor-to-attorney workflow
5. **why no automation?** → **patent process was paper-based until recently; digital tools are nascent**

### branch 3: confidence gap

1. **why do inventors struggle?** → they're not sure if their idea is patentable
2. **why uncertain?** → they can't assess novelty vs prior art
3. **why can't assess?** → they don't understand what makes claims strong vs weak
4. **why don't understand?** → patent language is specialized; no accessible education
5. **why no education?** → **patent literacy was never prioritized for inventors; it was delegated to attorneys**

## root cause summary

| layer | issue | is root cause? |
|-------|-------|----------------|
| surface | inventors don't file or file weak applications | no — symptom |
| mid | prior art search is tedious and expensive | no — consequence |
| mid | attorney fees are $15-30k | no — consequence |
| mid | tools are designed for attorneys, not inventors | no — consequence |
| deep | **patent industry is attorney-centric; inventors were never the direct user** | **yes** |
| deep | **patent process was paper-based; digital tools are nascent** | **yes** |
| deep | **patent literacy was delegated to attorneys, not taught to inventors** | **yes** |

the root causes converge: **the patent ecosystem was built for attorneys, not inventors**. inventors were expected to hire attorneys, not do their own research.

---

## recommendation

### address root cause

build inventor-focused tools that:
1. **democratize prior art research** — search + fetch skills make patent databases accessible
2. **structure the workflow** — route guides inventors through research → distillation → draft
3. **educate while guide** — briefs explain patent language, claims structure, office action patterns
4. **prepare for attorney handoff** — output is structured for attorney review, not replacement

### why not just address symptoms?

| symptom fix | problem |
|-------------|---------|
| "improve search results" | doesn't help if inventors don't know what to search |
| "lower attorney fees" | not in our control |
| "simplify patent language" | USPTO sets the rules |

the leverage point is the **tools layer**: we can't change USPTO or attorneys, but we can build tools that make inventors capable of quality research themselves.

### what we're NOT in scope for

| root cause | why out of scope | future follow-up |
|------------|------------------|------------------|
| patent literacy education | too broad for a CLI tool | briefs + route provide some education |
| attorney-centric industry | systemic change, not our fight | our tool shifts some power to inventors |
| USPTO process complexity | regulatory, not in our control | we work within the system |

---

## summary

**root cause**: the patent ecosystem was built for attorneys, not inventors. digital tools are nascent.

**address**: build inventor-focused tools (patenter role) that democratize search, structure workflow, and educate via briefs.

**out of scope**: systemic industry change, USPTO reform, attorney replacement.

**this is a leverage point**: we can't change the industry, but we can give inventors tools to participate more effectively.
