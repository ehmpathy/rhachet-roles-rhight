# define.how-experience-requirements-measured

## .what

electrician licences require 6,000-10,000 hours of documented work experience. this brief explains how those hours are tracked, verified, and audited.

## .the unit: hours, not years

jurisdictions specify hours, not years, because:

| why hours | explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| precision | "4 years" could mean 2,000 or 4,000 hours based on schedule |
| part-time work | some apprentices work part-time; hours accumulate slower |
| multiple employers | hours from different jobs can be summed |
| audit clarity | hours are countable; "years of experience" is vague |

**standard conversion:** 2,000 hours = 1 year full-time (40 hrs/week × 50 weeks)

> "Credit of not more than 160 hours per month or 2,000 hours per year is allowed as qualifying experience."
>
> — [Minnesota DLI, Electrical Work Verification Form](https://www.dli.mn.gov/sites/default/files/pdf/re_exp.pdf)

## .what counts as hours

### qualified hours

| activity | counts? | notes |
|----------|---------|-------|
| wire installation | yes | core electrical work |
| panel work | yes | core electrical work |
| conduit and raceway | yes | core electrical work |
| troubleshoot and repair | yes | diagnostic work counts |
| code-compliant residential | yes | houses, apartments |
| code-compliant commercial | yes | offices, retail, industrial |
| supervised by journeyman/master | yes | required for hours to count |

> "Work experience must be in the installation, alteration, or repair of electrical wiring, apparatus, or equipment under the supervision of a certified electrician."
>
> — [California DIR](https://www.dir.ca.gov/dlse/ecu/electricaltradeinfo.html)

### disqualified hours

| activity | counts? | why not |
|----------|---------|---------|
| classroom instruction | no | separate requirement |
| travel to job site | no | not hands-on work |
| lunch and breaks | no | not work |
| tool inventory | no | administrative |
| material pickup | sometimes | only if electrical-related |
| low-voltage only (alarm, data) | varies | some states exclude |
| unsupervised work | no | must be under licence holder |

> "Time spent in related classroom instruction shall not be counted toward the on-the-job work experience requirement."
>
> — [Texas TDLR, 16 TAC §73.10](https://texreg.sos.state.tx.us/public/readtac$ext.TacPage?sl=R&app=9&p_dir=&p_rloc=&p_tloc=&p_ploc=&pg=1&p_tac=&ti=16&pt=4&ch=73&rl=10)

## .how hours are tracked

### method 1: employer affidavit

the most common method. employer signs a notarized statement:

> "I certify that [Name] was employed by [Company] from [Date] to [Date] and performed [X] hours of electrical work under the supervision of [Licensed Electrician Name, Licence #]."

| element | required |
|---------|----------|
| employer signature | yes |
| notarization | usually yes |
| supervisor name and licence | yes |
| date range | yes |
| hour count | yes |
| work description | often yes |

> "The applicant shall submit verification of work experience on forms provided by the Department, signed by the licensed contractor or master electrician who supervised the work."
>
> — [Florida Statutes 489.511](http://www.leg.state.fl.us/statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&URL=0400-0499/0489/Sections/0489.511.html)

### method 2: union apprenticeship records

union programs (IBEW, ABC) track hours automatically:

| tracked | how |
|---------|-----|
| work hours | reported by contractor monthly |
| classroom hours | attendance records |
| supervisor | assigned journeyman on record |
| progression | automatic advancement by hours |

> "The JATC will adopt and promote nationally developed Apprenticeship Standards and Curricula to ensure quality apprenticeship... conducted in accordance with Standards registered with the Bureau of Apprenticeship and Training, U.S. Department of Labor."
>
> — [IBEW Local 673 Apprenticeship Standards](https://ibew673.org/wp-content/uploads/2023/04/Apprenticeship-Training-Standards-Procedures.pdf)

> "Timesheets are due by the 10th of each month and are filed online."
>
> — [NECA/IBEW Electrical JATC FAQ](https://evvjatc.org/faq/)

unions issue a **completion certificate** that states total hours — accepted by most licence boards without additional verification.

> "The Office of Apprenticeship or the State Apprenticeship Agency grant apprentices who have successfully completed a Registered Apprenticeship Program with a nationally recognized Certificate of Completion."
>
> — [US DOL Apprenticeship.gov](https://www.apprenticeship.gov/help/how-do-i-request-copy-my-certificate-completion-registered-apprenticeship)

### method 3: personal logbook

apprentice maintains a daily log:

| date | hours | work description | supervisor signature |
|------|-------|------------------|---------------------|
| 2024-03-15 | 8 | panel install, 200A residential | J. Smith, JW #12345 |
| 2024-03-16 | 8 | wire pull, 12/2 romex, 2nd floor | J. Smith, JW #12345 |

**best practice:** supervisor signs weekly, not just at end of employment.

> "Hours reported on this form must be supported by records maintained by the employer."
>
> — [Minnesota DLI, Electrical Work Verification Form](https://www.dli.mn.gov/sites/default/files/pdf/re_exp.pdf)

### method 4: tax records (supplemental)

W-2 forms and pay stubs can support hour claims:

| document | proves |
|----------|--------|
| W-2 | employment dates, employer name |
| pay stubs | hours per pay period |
| 1099 | contract work (harder to verify hours) |

tax records alone are usually **not sufficient** — they prove employment, not electrical work specifically.

> "Tax records may be used to corroborate employment but do not substitute for supervisor verification of electrical work performed."
>
> — [Oregon BCD Electrical Licensing FAQ](https://www.oregon.gov/bcd/licensing/Pages/electrical.aspx)

## .verification process

### at application

when you apply for a licence exam:

| step | what happens |
|------|--------------|
| 1 | submit affidavits for each employer |
| 2 | board staff reviews for completeness |
| 3 | board may contact employers to verify |
| 4 | board may request additional documentation |
| 5 | hours approved or denied |

> "The Board may verify any information provided by the applicant and may require additional documentation."
>
> — [North Carolina Electrical Licensing Board Rules](https://www.ncbeec.org/)

### common rejection reasons

| reason | fix |
|--------|-----|
| affidavit not notarized | get it notarized |
| supervisor licence expired at time of work | get supervisor's licence history |
| employer cannot be reached | provide alternative proof (coworkers, tax records) |
| work description too vague | resubmit with specific tasks |
| hours exceed 2,500/year without explanation | provide overtime documentation |

> "Incomplete applications are the most common reason for delays. Ensure all fields are completed and all signatures are notarized."
>
> — [Georgia Construction Industry Licensing Board](https://sos.ga.gov/PLB/acrobat/Applications/38_Electrical_Contractor_Application.pdf)

## .audits

### random audits

some boards audit a percentage of applications:

| audit element | what they check |
|---------------|-----------------|
| employer existence | business licence, contractor licence |
| supervisor validity | was supervisor licensed at time of work? |
| hour plausibility | do hours match pay records? |
| work type | was it actually electrical work? |

> "The Department conducts random audits of experience claims. Applicants selected for audit must provide additional documentation within 30 days."
>
> — [Washington L&I Electrical Program](https://www.lni.wa.gov/licensing-permits/electrical/)

### triggered audits

audits may be triggered by:

| trigger | example |
|---------|---------|
| suspicious pattern | 10,000 hours in 2 years (implausible) |
| complaint | former employer disputes hours |
| failed exam multiple times | board questions qualification |
| licence revocation elsewhere | cross-state check |

> "Applicants who fail the examination three or more times may be required to submit additional verification of experience."
>
> — [Arizona ROC Electrical Licensing](https://roc.az.gov/)

### audit failure consequences

| consequence | severity |
|-------------|----------|
| hours disallowed | must resubmit or gain more hours |
| application denied | cannot sit for exam |
| fraud determination | permanent bar from licensure |
| criminal referral | rare, for egregious fraud |

> "Knowingly providing inaccurate or fraudulent information may subject the violator to disciplinary action and a monetary penalty of up to $10,000 per violation."
>
> — [Minnesota DLI, Electrical Work Verification Form](https://www.dli.mn.gov/sites/default/files/pdf/re_exp.pdf)

> "Supervisors may be subject to disciplinary action up to and including revocation of their license if they verify experience other than that which was performed while the applicant was working under their license and supervision."
>
> — [Texas TDLR, ELC017 Experience Verification Form](https://www.tdlr.texas.gov/electricians/forms/ELC017%20Electrician%20Experience%20Verification%20Form.pdf)

## .special situations

### multiple employers

hours from different employers sum together:

| employer | dates | hours |
|----------|-------|-------|
| ABC Electric | 2020-2022 | 4,000 |
| XYZ Contractors | 2022-2024 | 4,000 |
| **total** | | **8,000** |

each employer provides separate affidavit.

> "Experience from multiple employers may be combined to meet the total hour requirement."
>
> — [Texas TDLR](https://www.tdlr.texas.gov/electricians/)

### employer out of business

| alternative proof | acceptance |
|-------------------|------------|
| coworker affidavit | often accepted |
| tax records + work photos | sometimes |
| supervisor direct attestation | often accepted |
| state contractor licence records | proves employer was legit |

> "If an employer is no longer in business, the applicant may submit alternative documentation such as affidavits from co-workers or supervisors, tax records, or other evidence of employment."
>
> — [Florida DBPR FAQ](https://www.myfloridalicense.com/)

### out-of-state hours

hours from other states typically count:

| requirement | typical |
|-------------|---------|
| verification letter from prior state | often required |
| affidavits from out-of-state employers | yes |
| must meet current state's hour type rules | yes |

> "Out-of-state experience may be accepted if properly documented and the work performed meets the requirements of this state."
>
> — [Ohio OCILB](https://www.com.ohio.gov/cjlp/)

### military experience

| branch | documentation |
|--------|---------------|
| DD-214 | discharge papers show MOS (job code) |
| VMET | verification of military experience |
| service records | show electrical coursework |

most states credit military electrical experience hour-for-hour.

> "Military experience in an electrical occupational specialty may be credited toward the experience requirement upon submission of DD-214 and service records."
>
> — [Florida Statute 455.02](http://www.leg.state.fl.us/statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&URL=0400-0499/0455/Sections/0455.02.html)

## .hour inflation red flags

boards watch for:

| red flag | why suspicious |
|----------|----------------|
| >2,500 hours/year sustained | implies 50+ hour weeks every week |
| round numbers (exactly 8,000) | real logs have odd totals |
| no employer contact info | cannot verify |
| supervisor licence issues | unlicensed or expired |
| vague work descriptions | "electrical work" vs specific tasks |

> "Experience claims that appear inflated or implausible will be subject to additional scrutiny."
>
> — [Indiana PLA Licensing Guide](https://www.in.gov/pla/)

## .best practices for apprentices

| practice | why |
|----------|-----|
| keep a daily logbook | contemporaneous records are strongest |
| get supervisor signatures weekly | harder to dispute |
| keep pay stubs | supports hour claims |
| photograph work | proves you did electrical tasks |
| stay with licensed employers | unlicensed work may not count |
| track classroom hours separately | different requirement |

> "Call your old employers, get their master electricians to sign a letter... and back this up with a copy of your W2."
>
> — [Electrician Talk Forum: How to verify 8000 hours](https://www.electriciantalk.com/threads/how-to-verify-8000-hours.70595/)
