# define.what-is-a-license

## .what

a professional license is government-issued permission to practice an occupation.

## .the key metaphor

a license is a **key** — it unlocks the legal right to do something that would otherwise be prohibited.

| domain | the key unlocks |
|--------|-----------------|
| driver | operate a vehicle on public roads |
| medical | diagnose and treat patients |
| legal | represent clients in court |
| plumber | install water systems |
| electrician | wire structures |
| contractor | build structures |

without the key, the door stays locked — these activities are illegal.

## .license vs certification vs registration

| term | issued by | legal weight | example |
|------|-----------|--------------|---------|
| **license** | government | required by law | medical license, driver's license |
| **certification** | private org | voluntary credential | AWS certified, PMP |
| **registration** | government | roster requirement | registered nurse, registered agent |

**license** = you cannot legally practice without it
**certification** = you can practice, but this credential shows competence
**registration** = government knows you exist, may have minimal requirements

## .the grant

a license is a grant from the sovereign (the state) to the individual. the state says:

> "we have determined you meet minimum standards. you may now practice."

this grant can be:
- **renewed** — if you maintain requirements
- **suspended** — temporarily revoked for violations
- **revoked** — permanently terminated for serious violations
- **expired** — lapsed due to non-renewal

## .sources

> "Professional certifications and occupational licenses are distinct credentials. A certification is a voluntary credential issued by a non-governmental body, while a license is a mandatory credential issued by a government agency."
>
