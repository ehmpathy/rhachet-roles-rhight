# define.what-licenses-mean

## .what

a license signals **minimum competency** and **accountability** — not excellence.

## .what a license guarantees

| guarantee | description |
|-----------|---------|
| **passed exam** | met minimum knowledge threshold at one point in time |
| **met requirements** | education, experience, background check |
| **registered** | government knows who they are |
| **accountable** | can be investigated, disciplined, revoked |

## .what a license does NOT guarantee

| not guaranteed | reality |
|----------------|---------|
| **quality** | minimum bar, not excellence |
| **current knowledge** | exam was years ago; CE is minimal |
| **no complaints** | may have complaints not yet adjudicated |
| **no malpractice** | past issues may not appear on record |
| **best choice** | only that they're legal to practice |

## .the minimum bar

a license says:

> "this person cleared the lowest acceptable threshold to legally practice"

it does **not** say:

> "this person is good at their job"

## .the accountability value

the real value of a license for consumers:

| if it goes wrong | you can |
|--------------------|---------|
| file complaint | with the board |
| trigger investigation | board must respond |
| seek discipline | suspension, revocation possible |
| verify history | public record of past actions |

without a license, you have no regulatory recourse — only civil courts.

## .how to read license status

| status | what it means | trust level |
|--------|---------------|-------------|
| **active, clean** | valid, no disciplinary history | baseline trust |
| **active, disciplined** | valid, but past violations | investigate further |
| **expired** | lapsed, not legal to practice | do not hire |
| **suspended** | temporarily barred | do not hire |
| **revoked** | permanently barred | do not hire |

## .complementary signals

licenses are one signal among many:

| signal | what it shows |
|--------|---------------|
| license | legal to practice |
| insurance | can cover damages |
| bond | financial guarantee |
| reviews | customer experience |
| references | past work quality |
| portfolio | demonstrated skill |

a licensed contractor with bad reviews is legal but risky.
an unlicensed contractor with great reviews is illegal.

## .the bottom line

a license is **necessary but not sufficient**:
- necessary: you need it to legally practice
- not sufficient: it doesn't prove quality

verify the license, then do your other due diligence.
