# define.who-controls-licenses

## .what

licenses are controlled primarily by **state governments**, with federal involvement in specific cross-border domains.

## .the 10th amendment basis

> "powers not delegated to the United States... are reserved to the States"

**police power** — states have constitutional authority to regulate for:
- health
- safety
- welfare

this is why most professional licenses are state-issued.

## .state control

states control licenses via **regulatory boards**:

| component | role |
|-----------|------|
| state legislature | passes laws that create license requirements |
| license board | sets standards, administers exams, enforces rules |
| board members | often practitioners from the profession itself |

### the board structure

typical board composition:
- licensed professionals (majority)
- public members (minority)
- appointed by governor or legislature

this creates a **self-regulation** model — the profession polices itself.

## .federal control

federal government licenses **cross-border** occupations:

| agency | licenses |
|--------|----------|
| FAA | pilots, mechanics, air traffic controllers |
| FCC | radio operators, broadcast engineers |
| NRC | nuclear facility operators |
| Coast Guard | merchant mariners |
| SEC | securities brokers (via FINRA) |

rationale: these roles inherently cross state lines.

## .the fragmentation problem

because states control licenses:
- **no portability** — a license in Indiana doesn't work in Ohio
- **50 different systems** — each state has own requirements, exams, fees
- **reciprocity varies** — some states recognize others, some don't

this creates friction for workers who move or work near state borders.

### reciprocity efforts

some progress via:
- **interstate compacts** — agreements between states to recognize each other
- **model laws** — uniform standards states can adopt
- **military spouse exceptions** — expedited process for relocated families

## .local control

some licenses are **municipal**, not state:

| trade | controlled by |
|-------|---------------|
| general contractors | often city/county |
| electricians | often city/county |
| plumbers | sometimes state, sometimes local |

Indiana example:
- plumbers: **state** (PLA)
- electricians: **local** (varies by city)
- contractors: **local** (varies by county)

## .the verification implication

to verify a license, you must know:
1. **which jurisdiction** controls that license type
2. **which agency** within that jurisdiction
3. **which portal/API** that agency provides

there is no single national database for most professions.
