# define.licensure-history

## .what

professional licensure evolved from medieval guilds to modern state regulatory boards.

## .timeline

### medieval period (1200s-1500s)

**guilds** controlled entry into trades:
- gained "charters" from monarchs granting exclusive practice rights
- master-apprentice system enforced quality
- limited competition via restricted membership

early medical licensure emerged:
- 1200s-1300s: Germany, Naples, Sicily, Spain required physician licenses
- 1518: King Henry VIII established College of Physicians (England)

### colonial america (1600s-1700s)

light regulation, limited to:
- bakers
- ferry operators
- innkeepers
- lawyers
- leather merchants
- peddlers

### pre-civil war america

only **three occupations** required modern licensure:
1. doctors
2. lawyers
3. ship pilots

### late 1800s (post-civil war)

expansion began:
- barbers, embalmers, midwives, pharmacists, veterinarians
- by 1890s: majority of states licensed physicians, dentists, pharmacists

### progressive era (1890-1920)

major expansion:
- 1907: Wyoming first to license engineers and surveyors
- rationale: industrialization created new risks requiring professional standards

### modern era (1950s-present)

explosive growth:
- 1950s: ~5% of occupations required licenses
- 2020s: ~25% of occupations require licenses

| decade | licensed workforce |
|--------|-------------------|
| 1950s | 4-5% |
| 1970s | 10% |
| 2000s | 20% |
| 2020s | 25% |

## .the pattern

licensure expands via:
1. **incident** — harm occurs (structure collapse, patient death)
2. **outrage** — public demands action
3. **legislation** — state creates license requirement
4. **board** — practitioners control entry

rarely do licenses get *removed* once established — they accumulate.

### medieval guilds

>
> — [History of Professional Licensure, DAVRON](https://www.davron.net/the-history-of-professional-licensure/)

### early medical licenses

> "The 13th and 14th centuries saw elementary forms of medical licensing in Germany, Naples, Sicily, and Spain."
>
> — [A Brief History of Occupational Licensing, Heritage Foundation](https://www.heritage.org/government-regulation/report/brief-history-occupational-licensing)

### pre-civil war

> "Prior to the Civil War, the US and state governments required the modern licensing of only three occupations: doctors, lawyers, and ship pilots."
>
> — [The Origins and Evolution of Occupational Licensing, NBER](https://www.nber.org/papers/w33580)

### progressive era

> "In 1907, Wyoming became the first state to require engineers and surveyors to register under the state."
>
> — [Occupational Licensing, Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Occupational_licensing)

### modern growth

> "Sixty years ago, America's economy rested on manufactured goods, and only 4 percent–5 percent of occupations were subject to a licensing requirement."
>
> — [A Brief History of Occupational Licensing, Heritage Foundation](https://www.heritage.org/government-regulation/report/brief-history-occupational-licensing)
