# define.owner-builder-experience-path

## .what

owner-builder permits let you do electrical work on your own property without a license. can those hours count toward licensure? in some states, explicitly yes — california has a dedicated form for it.

## .california: the explicit path

california's contractors state license board (CSLB) explicitly recognizes owner-builder experience for the C-10 electrical contractor license.

> "Credit for experience is given only for experience at a journey level or as a foreman, supervising employee, contractor, or owner-builder."
>
> — [CSLB Step 3: Qualifying Experience](https://www.cslb.ca.gov/contractors/applicants/contractors_license/exam_application/experience_for_exam.aspx)

### the owner-builder form

CSLB provides a dedicated "Project List Owner Builder" form for documenting this experience:

> "An owner-builder is 'an owner who builds or improves a structure on his or her property' (Business and Professions Code section 7044)."
>
> — [CSLB Project List Owner Builder](https://www.cslb.ca.gov/OnlineServices/WebApplication/InteractivePDFs/ProjectListOwnerBuilder.aspx)

the form requires:
- square footage of project
- copy of building permit and final inspection record
- description of trade duties you personally performed
- explanation of how past experience/training prepared you

> "All experience claims must be verified by a qualified and responsible person, such as an employer, contractor, foreman/supervisor, fellow employee, other journeyman, union representative, building inspector, architect, engineer, or homeowner."
>
> — [CSLB Certificate of Work Experience](https://www.cslb.ca.gov/Resources/FormsAndApplications/CertificationOfWorkExperience.pdf)

note: for owner-builder work, a building inspector who passed your work can potentially verify hours.

## .the house flipper path

house flippers accumulate projects naturally. with proper documentation, each flip builds toward licensure.

### hours per project (realistic estimates)

| project type | square footage | estimated hours |
|--------------|----------------|-----------------|
| bathroom remodel | 50-100 sq ft | 8-20 hours |
| kitchen remodel | 150-300 sq ft | 20-40 hours |
| full house rewire | 1,500 sq ft | 150-200 hours |
| full house rewire | 2,500 sq ft | 250-350 hours |
| addition | 500 sq ft | 50-100 hours |

> "Most contractors estimate at least one hour per 100 square feet for wiring installation."
>
> — [HomeGuide: Cost to Rewire a House](https://homeguide.com/costs/cost-to-rewire-a-house)

### flipper math to 8,000 hours

| pace | projects/year | hours/project | years to 8,000 |
|------|---------------|---------------|----------------|
| casual flipper | 1-2 | 150-300 | 15-25 years |
| active flipper | 4-6 | 150-300 | 5-10 years |
| full-time flipper | 8-12 | 150-300 | 3-5 years |

a full-time house flipper who does their own electrical could realistically accumulate 1,500-3,000 hours/year.

### the supervisor question for flippers

house flippers face the same supervision requirement, but have options:

| verifier type | how it works |
|---------------|--------------|
| licensed supervisor | hire electrician to check work, sign log |
| building inspector | CSLB accepts inspectors as verifiers |
| fellow journeyman | if you have licensed friends in the trade |

> "The person verifying your claim must have firsthand knowledge of your experience — that is, they must have observed the work that you have done."
>
> — [CSLB Acceptable Documents](https://www.cslb.ca.gov/AcceptableDocuments)

## .states that require supervision regardless

some states require supervision for hours to count, period:

> "Experience gained by violating electrical laws does not count... experience must be performed under the supervision of a properly certified electrician."
>
> — [Washington L&I Electrical Trainee Requirements](https://www.lni.wa.gov/licensing-permits/electrical/electrical-licensing-exams-education/electrical-trainee)

in these states, the path still works — but you must hire supervision.

## .the supervision workaround

for states that require supervised experience, hire a licensed electrician to supervise your owner-builder work:

| element | how it works |
|---------|--------------|
| you | pull owner-builder permit, do the work |
| licensed electrician | checks your work periodically, available for questions |
| logbook | you maintain daily log, supervisor signs weekly |
| affidavit | supervisor signs experience verification at end |

### cost structure

| expense | typical range |
|---------|---------------|
| owner-builder permit | $50-200 |
| materials | varies |
| supervisor hourly rate | $50-100/hour |
| supervisor visits | 1-2 hours/week while active |

compare to: $5,000-15,000 per project to hire a contractor.

## .the dayjob + home renovation path

someone could accumulate license hours while employed elsewhere:

| timeline | activity | hours |
|----------|----------|-------|
| year 1-2 | rewire your house evenings/weekends | 200-400 |
| year 2-3 | addition or detached structure | 100-200 |
| year 3-5 | help friends/family (under same supervisor) | 200-400 |
| cumulative | keep dayjob, build experience gradually | total |

at 10-20 hours/week evenings/weekends, this path takes 8-15 years. but you keep your income.

## .documentation requirements

| document | purpose |
|----------|---------|
| owner-builder permit | proves work was legal |
| final inspection record | proves work was code-compliant |
| daily logbook | contemporaneous hour record |
| photos of work | supports claims if audited |
| supervisor/inspector signatures | verification of hours |

### logbook format

| date | hours | work description | verifier signature |
|------|-------|------------------|-------------------|
| 2024-03-15 | 4 | rough-in bedroom circuit, 14/2 romex | J. Smith, JW #12345 |
| 2024-03-16 | 6 | panel install, 200A main | Inspector B. Jones |

## .risks and limitations

| risk | mitigation |
|------|------------|
| state doesn't recognize owner-builder | check BEFORE you invest years |
| inspector won't sign verification | use licensed supervisor instead |
| limited experience variety | supplement with commercial/industrial somehow |
| hour estimates inflated | keep contemporaneous detailed logs |

### verify before you start

before this path, contact your license board:

> "I plan to do owner-builder work on my residence. Can those hours count toward my journeyman/contractor application if properly documented? Who can verify my experience?"

get the answer in written form.

## .comparison to traditional apprenticeship

| aspect | traditional apprenticeship | owner-builder path |
|--------|---------------------------|-------------------|
| income | apprentice wages ($15-25/hr) | keep dayjob income |
| hours/week | 40+ | 10-40 (depends on flip pace) |
| time to qualify | 4 years | 3-15 years |
| classroom requirement | included | separate (trade school) |
| experience variety | residential + commercial | residential only (usually) |
| cost | paid to learn | pay supervisor + materials |
| end result | journeyman license | contractor license (in CA) |

## .who this works for

**ideal candidates:**
- house flippers who do their own renovations anyway
- real estate investors who build a portfolio
- someone with a stable career who wants a second income stream
- someone who renovates their own property over years

**poor fit:**
- someone in a hurry to get licensed
- someone without property or capital to flip
- someone who needs commercial/industrial experience
