# define.insurance-agent-license-process

## .what

the insurance agent licensing process involves completing pre-licensing education, passing state examinations, submitting fingerprints for background checks, applying through state departments or NIPR, obtaining carrier appointments, and maintaining compliance through continuing education and biennial license renewals.

## .why

> "an insurance producer license allows individuals to sell, solicit or negotiate insurance products."
>
> — [NIPR Understanding the Insurance Licensing Process](https://nipr.com/learn/learn-articles/understanding-the-insurance-licensing-process)

the licensing process ensures only qualified individuals sell insurance:

> "by setting minimum standards for education, examination, and ethical conduct, the PLMA seeks to protect consumers by ensuring that only qualified and knowledgeable producers are licensed to sell insurance."
>
> — [NAIC Producer Licensing Model Act Analysis](https://3hcg.com/blog/the-naic-producer-licensing-model-act-shaping-the-insurance-industrys-regulatory-landscape)

## lines of authority

the NAIC Producer Licensing Model Act defines six major lines of authority that insurance producers can be licensed to sell:

> "a line of authority is a subject area or flavor of insurance that an insurance producer can sell."
>
> — [AgentSync Lines of Authority](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/all-about-lines-of-authority)

### the six major lines

> "the six major lines defined in the Producer Licensing Model Act (PLMA) are: (1) Life, (2) Accident and Health or Sickness, (3) Property, (4) Casualty, (5) Variable Life and Variable Annuity Products and (6) Personal Lines."
>
> — [NAIC Uniform Licensing Standards](https://content.naic.org/sites/default/files/call_materials/Uniform%20Licensing%20Standards%20Final%20-%201.6.17.pdf)

each line covers specific insurance types:

> "life: insurance coverage on human lives, including benefits of endowment and annuities. accident and health/sickness: coverage for sickness, bodily injury or accidental death. property: insurance coverage for the direct or consequential loss or damage to property. casualty: coverage against legal liability including injury, disability, or property damage."
>
> — [AgentSync Lines of Authority Definitions](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/all-about-lines-of-authority)

### limited lines

> "states may offer up to nine limited line licenses, which cover specific insurance types. four core limited lines are mandatory: car rental, credit, crop, and travel insurance."
>
> — [AgentSync Lines of Authority](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/all-about-lines-of-authority)

## pre-licensing education requirements

most states require completion of approved education courses before taking the licensing examination:

> "many states require you to complete a pre-licensing insurance course to qualify for the state insurance exam."
>
> — [National Online Insurance School](https://www.nationalonlineinsuranceschool.com/license-requirements/insurance-license-requirements.php)

### hour requirements by state

> "states that require prelicensing education shall require 20 credit hours of prelicensing education per major line of authority."
>
> — [NAIC Uniform Licensing Standards](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/what-naic-uniform-licensing-standards-mean-for-insurance-producers)

state requirements vary significantly:

> "new york insurance law requires an individual who is seeking an agent, broker or public adjuster license to complete a pre-licensing course approved by the department."
>
> — [New York Department of Financial Services](https://www.dfs.ny.gov/apps_and_licensing/agents_and_brokers/prelicensing_education)

new york requires:
- life, accident and health agent/broker: 40 hours
- property/casualty agent/broker: 90 hours
- personal lines agent/broker: 40 hours

> "you must take a total of 40 hours of pre-license education for a P&C license (a 20-hour course on property insurance plus a 20-hour course on casualty insurance)."
>
> — [North Carolina Department of Insurance](https://staterequirement.com/how-to-get-property-casualty-license/north-carolina-property-casualty-license/)

### recent changes

> "governor gavin newsom has signed AB 943 into law, making california the latest state to eliminate its insurance producer pre-licensing education requirement."
>
> — [NAIFA Advocacy](https://advocacy.naifa.org/news/california-eliminates-pre-licensing-education-requirement-for-insurance-producers)

> "effective june 24, 2025, georgia has updated the prelicensing course hours from 20 hours to 8 hours per individual line."
>
> — [AgentSync Georgia Compliance](https://agentsync.io/compliance-library/jurisdictions/georgia)

## state examinations

all states require passing a licensing examination administered by approved testing vendors:

> "psi develops valid and legally defensible test content for insurance licensing across national and state levels."
>
> — [PSI Insurance Licensure](https://www.psiexams.com/licensure/insurance/)

### testing vendors and delivery

> "most states rely on pearson vue, prometric, or psi for insurance license exams, with only alabama and kentucky using other providers."
>
> — [StateRequirement Insurance License Exam](https://staterequirement.com/insurance-license-exam/)

> "test takers can take computer-based tests at one of 400 psi test centers across all 50 us states and territories, or test securely at home with online proctoring solutions."
>
> — [PSI Insurance Licensure](https://www.psiexams.com/licensure/insurance/)

### exam format and passing scores

> "the exam is multiple-choice, timed based on the number of questions in the exam, and typically has a minimum passing score of between 60% and 70%."
>
> — [National Online Insurance School](https://www.nationalonlineinsuranceschool.com/license-requirements/insurance-license-requirements.php)

> "the number of questions ranges from 40 to 150 across the different categories of examinations."
>
> — [California Department of Insurance](https://www.insurance.ca.gov/0200-industry/0010-producer-online-services/0200-exam-info/examtimesandquestion.cfm)

## background checks and fingerprinting

most states require criminal background checks as part of the licensing process:

> "the majority of states mandate fingerprinting for resident producer and adjuster license applicants."
>
> — [AgentSync Fingerprinting and Background Checks](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/getting-to-know-you-how-states-handle-fingerprinting-and-background-checks-for-insurance-licensees)

### types of background checks

> "states employ three depth levels for background checks: general background checks, criminal background checks using fingerprints, and fbi background checks which are most comprehensive and search federal records."
>
> — [AgentSync Fingerprinting and Background Checks](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/getting-to-know-you-how-states-handle-fingerprinting-and-background-checks-for-insurance-licensees)

> "nearly all states require applicants to submit electronic fingerprints to run a criminal background check."
>
> — [National Online Insurance School](https://www.nationalonlineinsuranceschool.com/license-requirements/insurance-license-requirements.php)

### state-specific requirements

> "florida and california require fingerprints from all applicants, including those with reciprocal licenses."
>
> — [AgentSync Fingerprinting and Background Checks](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/getting-to-know-you-how-states-handle-fingerprinting-and-background-checks-for-insurance-licensees)

> "three states—arkansas, iowa, and kentucky—do not require fingerprints but still conduct background checks."
>
> — [AgentSync Fingerprinting and Background Checks](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/getting-to-know-you-how-states-handle-fingerprinting-and-background-checks-for-insurance-licensees)

## application process

applications are submitted through state departments of insurance or NIPR:

> "nipr is a centralized platform for managing producer licensing data, allowing insurance producers to apply for, renew and update their licenses through nipr."
>
> — [NIPR Licensing Center](https://nipr.com/licensing-center)

### national producer number

> "a national producer number (npn) is a unique naic identifier assigned during the licensing application process."
>
> — [NIPR NPN Lookup](https://nipr.com/licensing-center/look-up-a-national-producer-number)

> "currently, the pdb provides information from all 50 states, the district of columbia, the u.s. virgin islands, guam and puerto rico."
>
> — [NIPR Producer Database](https://nipr.com/products-and-services/producer-database)

### application requirements

> "applicants must be 18 years of age or older."
>
> — [AgentSync NAIC Uniform Licensing Standards](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/what-naic-uniform-licensing-standards-mean-for-insurance-producers)

> "states must use the most current version of the naic application."
>
> — [AgentSync NAIC Uniform Licensing Standards](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/what-naic-uniform-licensing-standards-mean-for-insurance-producers)

### fees and costs

> "the total cost to get an insurance license typically ranges from $150 to $400, depending on the state and the type of license."
>
> — [National Online Insurance School](https://www.nationalonlineinsuranceschool.com/insurance-license-blog/2026/03/how-much-does-it-cost-to-get-an-insurance-license/)

> "most states use the national insurance producer registry (nipr), which charges a separate $5.60 transaction fee."
>
> — [AgentSync Producer Licensing Fees](https://agentsync.io/blog/compliance/expectations-for-state-by-state-insurance-producer-licensing-fees)

## appointment by insurers

after obtaining a license, producers must be appointed by insurance carriers to sell their products:

> "an appointment is how insurance carriers tell the state which producers or agencies can sell their products."
>
> — [AgentSync Insurance Carrier Appointments](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/insurance-101-all-about-insurance-carrier-appointments)

> "without one, a producer can't legally sell a carrier's insurance products."
>
> — [AgentSync Insurance Carrier Appointments](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/insurance-101-all-about-insurance-carrier-appointments)

### appointment types

> "most states mandate that carriers report producer appointments, but some—including alaska, illinois, oregon—don't require reporting."
>
> — [AgentSync Insurance Carrier Appointments](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/insurance-101-all-about-insurance-carrier-appointments)

> "some states treat appointments as perpetual (as long as the producer has a valid license), while others require annual appointment renewal."
>
> — [AgentSync Insurance Carrier Appointments](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/insurance-101-all-about-insurance-carrier-appointments)

### just-in-time appointments

> "just-in-time appointments legally give insurance carriers the ability to delay appointing a producer (and paying the associated fees) until the producer actually begins writing business for that carrier."
>
> — [AgentSync Just-In-Time Appointments](https://agentsync.io/blog/producer-management/taking-advantage-of-just-in-time-carrier-appointments)

> "states that allow jit appointments generally require the appointments to be made within 14 to 45 days of the agent submitting their first piece of business."
>
> — [AgentSync Carrier Appointments by State](https://agentsync.io/blog/producer-management/insurance-carrier-appointment-faqs-by-state)

## continuing education

licensed producers must complete ongoing education to maintain their licenses:

> "continuing education is one tool used by insurance regulators to ensure the ongoing technical competency of licensed insurance professionals."
>
> — [NAIC State Licensing Handbook](https://content.naic.org/state_licensing_handbook.htm)

### hour requirements

> "licensed producers must complete 24 hours of CE for all major LOAs with three of those hours dedicated to ethics."
>
> — [AgentSync NAIC Uniform Licensing Standards](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/what-naic-uniform-licensing-standards-mean-for-insurance-producers)

> "most states require between 20 and 30 hours of CE every two-year period."
>
> — [FirstConnect Insurance CE Requirements](https://www.firstconnectinsurance.com/blog/how-many-hours-of-continuing-education-are-required-for-insurance-license-renewal/)

### texas requirements

> "all agent and adjuster licenses require 24 hours of continuing education per two-year renewal period."
>
> — [Texas Department of Insurance](https://www.tdi.texas.gov/agent/agcehome.html)

> "at least half the hours must be classroom or 'classroom equivalent.'"
>
> — [Texas Department of Insurance](https://www.tdi.texas.gov/agent/agcehome.html)

### penalties

> "failure to meet requirements can result in state fines (up to $500 in some states), delayed renewals, license suspension or revocation."
>
> — [FirstConnect Insurance CE Requirements](https://www.firstconnectinsurance.com/blog/how-many-hours-of-continuing-education-are-required-for-insurance-license-renewal/)

## license renewal

licenses must be renewed periodically to remain valid:

> "naic encourages states to eliminate perpetual licenses and adopt a biennial renewal process for insurance licenses."
>
> — [NAIC State Licensing Handbook](https://content.naic.org/sites/default/files/inline-files/Chapters%2016-20.pdf)

### renewal process

> "resident business entities are required to renew their license on or before their biennial due date and must submit a renewal application and pay a biennial renewal fee through nipr."
>
> — [NIPR License Renewal](https://nipr.com/licensing-center/apply-for-a-license/renew-your-license)

> "license renewal fees are $20 - $100 per cycle, paid to the state every renewal cycle (typically every 2 years)."
>
> — [National Online Insurance School](https://www.nationalonlineinsuranceschool.com/insurance-license-blog/2026/03/how-much-does-it-cost-to-get-an-insurance-license/)

### reinstatement

> "a license is eligible for reinstatement for up to one year after the expiration date of the license. after one year, a new application will have to be filed."
>
> — [California Department of Insurance](https://www.insurance.ca.gov/0200-industry/0020-apply-license/0200-indiv-non-resident/late-renewals.cfm)

> "if a license is reinstated after the expiration date, all insurance company appointments are cancelled."
>
> — [Texas Insurance Training Academy](https://texasinsurancetrainingacademy.com/blog/what-if-a-producer-lets-their-insurance-license-lapse)

## resident vs non-resident licensing

producers must obtain licenses in each state where they conduct business:

> "a resident license is for those whose primary residence is in the state where they are doing business, and to obtain one, you must complete state-specific pre-licensing education and pass the licensing exam."
>
> — [NIPR Understanding the Insurance Licensing Process](https://nipr.com/learn/learn-articles/understanding-the-insurance-licensing-process)

> "a non-resident license allows you to sell insurance in states where you are not a resident, and to apply, you need to provide proof of your resident license and submit a non-resident application through nipr."
>
> — [NIPR Understanding the Insurance Licensing Process](https://nipr.com/learn/learn-articles/understanding-the-insurance-licensing-process)

### reciprocity

> "the foundation of reciprocity is your resident license. most states require you to hold a license in your home state before you can apply for non-resident licenses elsewhere."
>
> — [America's Professor Non-Resident Licenses](https://americasprofessor.com/general/live-in-one-state-and-want-to-sell-insurance-in-another-what-you-need-to-know-about-non-resident-insurance-licenses/)

> "reciprocity is one of the most significant impacts of the PLMA, allowing producers licensed in one state to more easily become licensed in another."
>
> — [NAIC Producer Licensing Model Act Analysis](https://3hcg.com/blog/the-naic-producer-licensing-model-act-shaping-the-insurance-industrys-regulatory-landscape)

### non-reciprocal states

> "california, new york, and hawaii are known as non-reciprocal states. they generally require you to take their specific state exam regardless of how many other licenses you hold."
>
> — [Insurance Schools Adjuster License Reciprocity](https://www.insurance-schools.com/Adjuster-License-Reciprocity.aspx)

### continuing education for non-residents

> "in most cases, as long as you remain in good standing and complete the CE requirements for your home state, most reciprocal states will accept that as fulfillment of their own CE requirements."
>
> — [America's Professor Non-Resident Licenses](https://americasprofessor.com/general/live-in-one-state-and-want-to-sell-insurance-in-another-what-you-need-to-know-about-non-resident-insurance-licenses/)

## summary

| step | requirement |
|------|-------------|
| 1. determine line of authority | choose from six major lines: life, health, property, casualty, variable products, personal lines |
| 2. complete pre-licensing education | 20-90 hours depending on state and line of authority (some states exempt) |
| 3. pass state examination | 60-70% passing score, 40-150 questions, administered by PSI or Pearson VUE |
| 4. submit fingerprints | criminal background check required in most states; FBI check in some |
| 5. submit application | through state DOI or NIPR; receive National Producer Number (NPN) |
| 6. obtain carrier appointments | required to sell specific carriers' products; perpetual or annual depending on state |
| 7. complete continuing education | 24 hours biennial including 3 hours ethics in most states |
| 8. renew license | biennial renewal through NIPR; $20-100 fee per cycle |
| 9. non-resident licensing | apply through NIPR with proof of resident license; reciprocity available in most states |
