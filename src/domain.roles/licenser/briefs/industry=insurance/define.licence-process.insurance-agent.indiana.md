# define.licence-process.insurance-agent.indiana

## .what

indiana insurance agent licensing requires pre-licensing education, passing a state examination administered by pearson vue, submitting an application through NIPR or sircon, and maintaining compliance through biennial continuing education renewal.

## .why

> "the mission of the IDOI Agency Services Division is to assure that insurance professionals are properly educated and licensed to conduct the business of insurance in the State of Indiana."
>
> — [Indiana Department of Insurance](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/)

---

## indiana department of insurance role

the indiana department of insurance (IDOI) serves as the regulatory authority for insurance agent licensing:

> "the mission of the Indiana Department of Insurance is to protect Indiana's insurance consumers by monitoring and regulating the financial and market conduct activities of insurance companies and agents."
>
> — [Indiana Department of Insurance](https://www.in.gov/idoi/)

the commissioner holds centralized authority:

> "the powers, duties, management and control of the department of insurance are vested in the insurance commissioner."
>
> — [Indiana Code 27-1-1-2](https://law.justia.com/codes/indiana/title-27/article-1/chapter-1/section-27-1-1-2/)

the commissioner is appointed based on expertise:

> "the insurance commissioner is appointed by the governor and shall be familiar with and known to possess knowledge of the subject of insurance and be skilled in matters pertaining thereto."
>
> — [Indiana Code 27-1-1-2](https://law.justia.com/codes/indiana/title-27/article-1/chapter-1/section-27-1-1-2/)

---

## indiana code title 27 statutory framework

insurance producer licensing in indiana is governed by IC 27-1-15.6:

> "an 'insurance producer' means a person required to be licensed under the laws of Indiana to sell, solicit, or negotiate insurance."
>
> — [Indiana Code 27-1-15.6](https://law.justia.com/codes/indiana/title-27/article-1/chapter-15-6/)

license qualifications are codified:

> "unless denied licensure under section 12 of this chapter, a person who has met the requirements of sections 5 and 6 of this chapter shall be issued an insurance producer license."
>
> — [Indiana Code 27-1-15.6-7](https://law.justia.com/codes/indiana/title-27/article-1/chapter-15-6/section-27-1-15-6-7/)

continuing education requirements are established in chapter 15.7:

> "to renew a license, a resident insurance producer must complete at least twenty-four (24) hours of credit in continuing education courses."
>
> — [Indiana Code 27-1-15.7](https://law.justia.com/codes/indiana/title-27/article-1/chapter-15-7/)

---

## lines of authority

indiana offers multiple lines of authority for insurance producers:

> "an insurance producer may receive qualification for a license in one or more of the following lines of authority: (1) Life — insurance coverage on human lives, including benefits of endowment and annuities; (2) Accident and health or sickness — insurance coverage for sickness, bodily injury, or accidental death; (3) Property — insurance coverage for the direct or consequential loss of or damage to property; and (4) Casualty — insurance coverage against legal liability."
>
> — [Indiana Code 27-1-15.6](https://law.justia.com/codes/indiana/title-27/article-1/chapter-15-6/)

each line requires separate qualification:

> "as a resident producer in Indiana, you may sell Life, Health, Property or Casualty insurance, but you must be licensed in each line of authority to sell. For example, a property agent cannot sell a health policy."
>
> — [IDOI Resident Licensing Guidelines](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/resident-licensing/resident-licensing-guidelinesrequirements/)

limited lines licenses are also available:

> "the commissioner may issue a limited lines producer's license without examination to a limited lines travel insurance producer and a limited line credit insurance producer."
>
> — [Indiana Code 27-1-15.6-18](https://law.justia.com/codes/indiana/title-27/article-1/chapter-15-6/section-27-1-15-6-18/)

## pre-licensing education

indiana requires pre-licensing education based on line of authority:

> "applicants must be at least 18 years of age and maintain principal place of residence or principal place of business in Indiana."
>
> — [IDOI Resident Licensing Guidelines](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/resident-licensing/resident-licensing-guidelinesrequirements/)

education hours vary by license type:

| license type | hours required |
|--------------|----------------|
| life | 20 |
| health | 20 |
| life & health | 40 |
| personal lines property and casualty | 20 |
| personal & commercial property and casualty | 40 |
| title | 10 |

> "education must be completed through state-approved providers before exam registration. certificates remain valid for six months."
>
> — [IDOI Resident Licensing Guidelines](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/resident-licensing/resident-licensing-guidelinesrequirements/)

application timing is critical:

> "you must apply for licensure within 6 months of completing your prelicensing education requirement and passing the exam, or you will be required to retake it."
>
> — [AD Banker Indiana Requirements](https://www.adbanker.com/licensing-requirements/insurance/indiana/pre-licensing/)

## state examination

indiana uses pearson vue for exam administration:

> "the State of Indiana uses Pearson VUE to administer its insurance agent licensing examinations at multiple locations throughout Indiana and nationwide."
>
> — [IDOI Examination Procedure Checklist](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/resident-licensing/examination-procedure-checklist/)

the exam tests specific knowledge areas:

> "before becoming licensed by the department, each applicant for licensing as an insurance producer must pass an examination for the line(s) of authority for which application is made. The examination tests the candidate's knowledge concerning the line(s) of authority, duties and responsibilities of a licensee and the Indiana insurance laws and administrative rules."
>
> — [IDOI Licensing](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/)

passing score requirement:

> "the passing percentage needed on all IDOI insurance licensing exams is 70%."
>
> — [StateRequirement Indiana Exam](https://staterequirement.com/insurance-license-exam/indiana-insurance-license-exam/)

exam structure varies by type:

| exam type | questions | time (min) | fee |
|-----------|-----------|------------|-----|
| life producer | 95 | 120 | $50-69 |
| health producer | 95 | 120 | $50-69 |
| life & health producer | 150 | 180 | $50-69 |
| property & casualty producer | 150 | 180 | $69 |
| personal lines producer | 115 | 120 | $69 |
| surplus lines producer | 55 | 60 | $50-69 |

> "candidates must make an online reservation at least twenty-four (24) hours before the desired examination date at www.pearsonvue.com/in/insurance."
>
> — [Pearson VUE Indiana Insurance](https://www.pearsonvue.com/us/en/in/insurance.html)

## background checks

indiana has notably lenient background check requirements:

> "Indiana is one of eight states that don't require fingerprinting, a background check, or even two forms of identification for insurance producers."
>
> — [AgentSync Background Checks](https://agentsync.io/blog/producer-management/state-and-carrier-requirements-vary-on-insurance-background-checks)

however, criminal history can still affect licensing:

> "the Indiana insurance commissioner may suspend, revoke, or refuse to issue or renew a producer's license if the producer has been convicted of a felony."
>
> — [Indiana Insurance License Defense](https://www.indyjustice.com/other-practice-areas/professional-licensing/indiana-insurance-license-defense/)

federal law also applies:

> "the Federal Violent Crime Control and Law Enforcement Act of 1994 (18 U.S.C. 1033, 1034) contains a provision prohibiting any person who has been convicted of a felony involving dishonesty or breach of trust from participating in the business of insurance without receiving specific written consent from the appropriate state insurance regulatory official."
>
> — [AgentSync Compliance](https://agentsync.io/blog/compliance/how-to-renew-your-insurance-license-with-a-criminal-background-part-2-hawaii-through-maryland)

## application process

applications are submitted electronically:

> "license application and reporting services are provided by the National Insurance Producer Registry (NIPR) and Sircon—powered by Vertafore."
>
> — [IDOI Licensing](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/)

citizenship or work authorization is required:

> "all applicants must be a United States citizen or legal alien who possesses work authorization and must submit supporting documentation."
>
> — [IDOI Resident Licensing Guidelines](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/resident-licensing/resident-licensing-guidelinesrequirements/)

application fees:

| license type | fee |
|--------------|-----|
| resident producer | $40 |
| non-resident producer | $90 |
| surplus lines | $80 |
| electronic processing | $5.15 |

> "all license application fees are nonrefundable and nontransferable."
>
> — [IDOI Licensing](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/)

processing timeline:

> "the IDOI reviews filings in the order it receives them, and this process usually takes five to seven business days from the date you submit your application."
>
> — [StateRequirement Indiana License](https://staterequirement.com/indiana-insurance-license/)

## appointment

indiana has simplified appointment requirements:

> "Indiana doesn't require insurance producer appointments. The carrier must maintain a list of all authorized licensees and submit the list to the state upon request."
>
> — [AgentSync Carrier Appointments](https://agentsync.io/blog/producer-management/insurance-carrier-appointment-faqs-by-state)

termination notification requirements still apply:

> "an insurer or authorized representative of an insurer that terminates the appointment, employment, contract, or other insurance business relationship with a producer shall notify the commissioner not more than thirty (30) days after the effective date of the termination."
>
> — [Indiana Code 27-1-15.6-15](https://law.justia.com/codes/indiana/title-27/article-1/chapter-15-6/section-27-1-15-6-15/)

producer notification is also required:

> "not more than fifteen (15) days after making the notification to the commissioner, the insurer shall mail a copy of the notification to the producer at the producer's last known address."
>
> — [Indiana Code 27-1-15.6-15](https://law.justia.com/codes/indiana/title-27/article-1/chapter-15-6/section-27-1-15-6-15/)

## continuing education

standard continuing education requirements:

> "producers in Accident & Health, Life, Property & Casualty, and Personal Lines must complete 24 CE hours, including 3 hours of ethics, every 2-year license term."
>
> — [Kaplan CE Requirements](https://www.kaplanfinancial.com/insurance-continuing-education/indiana/state-requirements)

ethics requirement specifics:

> "if the insurance producer has a qualification for life, health, or annuity insurance, at least three (3) of the hours of credit must be related to ethical practices in the marketing and sale of those products."
>
> — [Indiana Code 27-1-15.7](https://law.justia.com/codes/indiana/title-27/article-1/chapter-15-7/)

carry-over limitations:

> "not more than 12 carry-over hours may be applied to the next license renewal period, and CE hours completed in the Ethics or Long Term Care course categories will not be applied as carry-over credits."
>
> — [Success CE Indiana](https://successce.com/insurance-ce-requirements-in/)

exceptions exist for certain license types:

> "limited Lines licenses (Credit, Travel, Funeral Director, Crop Hail) require no CE."
>
> — [IDOI License Renewal Guidelines](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/resident-license-renewal/individual-license-renewal-guidelinesrequirements/)

## renewal

license renewal follows a biennial schedule:

> "licenses issued after July 1, 2011 last two years, with expiration on the last day of the producer's birth month."
>
> — [IDOI License Renewal Guidelines](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/resident-license-renewal/individual-license-renewal-guidelinesrequirements/)

renewal notice timing:

> "the Indiana Department of Insurance emails renewal invoices approximately 90 days before expiration. If for some reason you do not receive a renewal invoice, it is still your responsibility to renew your license."
>
> — [IDOI License Renewal Guidelines](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/resident-license-renewal/individual-license-renewal-guidelinesrequirements/)

renewal fees:

| renewal type | fee |
|--------------|-----|
| resident producer renewal | $40 |
| reinstatement (within 12 months) | $120 + $40 renewal |
| public adjuster | $50 |

reinstatement options:

> "resident producers who allow their licenses to expire and completed required continuing education before the license expired, may reinstate the license within twelve (12) months of the license expiration date."
>
> — [IDOI Reinstatement Guidelines](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/resident-license-renewal/individual-license-renewal-guidelinesrequirements/reinstating-a-resident-license-that-has-been-expired-for-less-than-twelve-12-months/)

penalty waiver provisions:

> "the department of insurance may waive the penalty if the renewal fee is received not more than thirty (30) days after the expiration date of the license."
>
> — [BetterCE Indiana Renewal](https://www.betterce.com/license-management/indiana-insurance-agent-renewal-requirements/)

## summary

| step | requirement |
|------|-------------|
| eligibility | 18+ years old, indiana resident, US citizen or legal alien with work authorization |
| pre-licensing education | 20-40 hours depending on line of authority, from approved provider |
| state examination | pearson vue administered, 70% passing score, $50-69 fee |
| background check | not required by state, but federal law applies for felonies involving dishonesty |
| application | NIPR or sircon, $40 resident fee, 5-7 business days processing |
| appointment | not required by state, carrier maintains list |
| continuing education | 24 hours every 2 years including 3 ethics hours |
| renewal | biennial on birth month, $40 fee, 90 days advance notice |
