# define.carrier-access.aggregators.insurance-agent

## .what

insurance aggregators, clusters, and networks are organizations that pool independent agents' premiums and resources to access carriers and products they could not obtain individually, providing an alternative path to carrier appointments for agents who cannot meet production minimums on their own.

## .why

> "an insurance aggregator (also known as a cluster or network) is a collection of independent agents that work together for the sake of having larger group bonuses, meeting enormous production requirements by being part of the same group, and leveraging group familiarity to gain more appointments."
>
> — [AgentSync Aggregators Networks Clusters](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/insurance-101-what-are-aggregators-networks-and-clusters)

carriers prefer larger agencies because they generate faster market share gains and revenue, creating barriers for smaller agencies:

> "carriers often set premium volume thresholds to maintain an appointment, which for a brand-new independent agent can be difficult to meet on your own."
>
> — [Smart Choice Getting Appointed](https://www.smartchoiceagents.com/tips/how-get-appointed-insurance-companies)

## definitions: aggregators, clusters, and networks

### aggregator

aggregators band together multiple independent agencies under a single contractual framework:

> "members pool premiums and resources to access carriers and products they couldn't obtain individually."
>
> — [AgentSync Aggregators Networks Clusters](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/insurance-101-what-are-aggregators-networks-and-clusters)

aggregators are also called National Marketing Organizations (NMOs) and provide shared administrative resources, technology platforms, and marketing support.

### network

networks are similar to aggregators but typically feature more formalized structures:

> "networks often feature specialized training programs, enhanced compliance support, and captive agreements with specific carriers, offering less flexibility than aggregators regarding carrier selection."
>
> — [AgentSync Aggregators Networks Clusters](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/insurance-101-what-are-aggregators-networks-and-clusters)

### cluster

clusters tend to be smaller and more regional:

> "regional or locally-based alliances offering benefits comparable to aggregators and networks but with smaller membership and less rigorous requirements. members are geographically concentrated rather than nationally distributed."
>
> — [AgentSync Aggregators Networks Clusters](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/insurance-101-what-are-aggregators-networks-and-clusters)

## why agents use aggregators

smaller agencies face significant obstacles obtaining direct carrier appointments:

> "most carriers require minimum production commitments ranging from $50,000 to $500,000 in annual premium depending on the carrier and product line."
>
> — [Renegade Insurance Carrier Appointments](https://renegadeinsurance.com/insurance-carrier-appointments-101-a-guide-to-growing-your-agency/)

> "missing your production commitment results in contract termination."
>
> — [Smart Choice Getting Appointed](https://www.smartchoiceagents.com/tips/how-get-appointed-insurance-companies)

aggregators solve the production minimum problem:

> "aggregators allow agents to benefit from the collective premium volume of our network, making it easier to meet production requirements."
>
> — [Agents Alliance Services](https://agentsalliance.insure/2025/10/03/how-to-get-appointed-with-carriers-as-an-independent-insurance-agent/)

> "we negotiate contracts with carriers on behalf of our members, giving you instant access to markets."
>
> — [Agents Alliance Services](https://agentsalliance.insure/2025/10/03/how-to-get-appointed-with-carriers-as-an-independent-insurance-agent/)

>
> — [SIAA The SIAA Model](https://www.siaa.com/about/the-siaa-model)

>
> — [SIAA History](https://www.siaa.com/about/history)

SIAA provides access to over 40 national and regional carriers:

> "with access to over 40 of the top national and regional insurance companies providing top-level commissions, national and local incentives, and profit-sharing without minimums."
>
> — [SIAA Access to Companies](https://www.siaa.com/siaa-advantage/access-to-companies)

## how to join an aggregator

### application process

aggregators evaluate prospective members based on credentials and fit:

> "wholesale appointment applications typically require an active P&C license certificate, E&O insurance certificate, agency information including years in operation, and a description of current book of business."
>
> — [Agency Height Wholesale Carrier](https://agencyheight.com/how-to-get-appointed-wholesale-carrier/)

SIAA connects prospective members with regional master agencies:

> "master agencies employ local staff called agency growth coaches who work directly with member agencies, providing start-up guidance, commercial lines premium growth assistance, and ongoing support."
>
> — [SIAA The SIAA Model](https://www.siaa.com/about/the-siaa-model)

### requirements vary by aggregator

some aggregators serve new agencies while others require established production:

> "SIAA serves two primary segments: startup and new agencies, and established agencies seeking growth and profitability enhancement."
>
> — [SIAA What We Do](https://www.siaa.com/about/what-we-do)

## aggregator fee structures

### membership fees

aggregators charge various fee combinations:

> "membership costs include initiation fees ranging from hundreds to thousands of dollars, plus monthly fees as fixed rates, sales percentages, or hybrid models."
>
> — [AgentSync Aggregators Networks Clusters](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/insurance-101-what-are-aggregators-networks-and-clusters)

### commission structures

commission structures vary by appointment type:

> "a typical industry average is 10% for wholesale/brokered arrangements. producer appointments typically earn a base commission of 10-15%, with certain carriers offering more, some upwards of 17%. direct appointments provide access to base commission plus bonus opportunities, profit sharing, marketing resources, and spiffs."
>
> — [ASNOA Three Carrier Appointment Types](https://asnoa.com/2021/are-you-compromising-on-money-ownership/)

### profit sharing access

SIAA operates a five-income-stream model:

> "members achieve 17.6% average annual member premium growth. member agencies collectively earned $500+ million in additional income."
>
> — [SIAA Agency Income Growth](https://www.siaa.com/siaa-advantage/agency-income-growth)

producer-level appointments may exclude profit sharing:

> "producer appointments explicitly exclude profit-sharing opportunities since agents lack location-specific codes."
>
> — [ASNOA Three Carrier Appointment Types](https://asnoa.com/2021/are-you-compromising-on-money-ownership/)

## direct appointments vs producer sub-codes

### direct appointments

direct appointments provide maximum independence:

> "direct appointments give you the most freedom; you use your own process to service clients, you have your own credentials to sign into carrier portals, and you are on the DEC pages."
>
> — [Dark Horse Insurance Direct vs Sub-Codes](https://darkhorseinsurance.com/direct-carrier-appointments-vs-producer-sub-codes/)

direct appointments have higher production requirements:

> "direct appointments require four to six weeks with vetting and typical production requirements of $50,000-$250,000 average."
>
> — [ASNOA Three Carrier Appointment Types](https://asnoa.com/2021/are-you-compromising-on-money-ownership/)

### producer sub-codes

sub-codes provide access without full appointment requirements:

> "producer sub-codes are designations granted by larger agencies or managing general agents (MGAs) that allow agents to access carriers and markets without meeting stringent volume requirements."
>
> — [Dark Horse Insurance Direct vs Sub-Codes](https://darkhorseinsurance.com/direct-carrier-appointments-vs-producer-sub-codes/)

sub-codes limit operational autonomy:

> "you operate within a third party's workflow with no authority to service the client on your own."
>
> — [ASNOA Three Carrier Appointment Types](https://asnoa.com/2021/are-you-compromising-on-money-ownership/)

> "producer appointments grant the authority and credentials to make endorsement changes but remain bound by agency workflows and lack carrier portal access."
>
> — [ASNOA Three Carrier Appointment Types](https://asnoa.com/2021/are-you-compromising-on-money-ownership/)

## book of business ownership

ownership rights are a critical consideration when joining an aggregator:

> "book ownership refers to the rights and control an insurance agent possesses over their book of business, which consists of policies they have sold and clients they serve."
>
> — [Chicagoland SIA Book Ownership](https://benefits.chicagolandsia.com/chicagoland-sia-blog/understanding-book-ownership-a-comparative-analysis-for-insurance-agents)

### network-based ownership varies

> "book ownership varies by network structure. some grant full ownership; others impose limitations or controls on one or more aspects of ownership, such as commission deposits or client database access."
>
> — [Chicagoland SIA Book Ownership](https://benefits.chicagolandsia.com/chicagoland-sia-blog/understanding-book-ownership-a-comparative-analysis-for-insurance-agents)

some aggregators explicitly grant full ownership:

> "Chicagoland SIA explicitly states members maintain full autonomy and ownership of their book of business."
>
> — [Chicagoland SIA Book Ownership](https://benefits.chicagolandsia.com/chicagoland-sia-blog/understanding-book-ownership-a-comparative-analysis-for-insurance-agents)

> "agents keep ownership of your book of business through their partnership model."
>
> — [Agents Alliance Services](https://agentsalliance.insure/2025/10/03/how-to-get-appointed-with-carriers-as-an-independent-insurance-agent/)

### ownership by appointment type

> "wholesale/brokered: you are likely to own the business or will be, at minimum, listed as the Agent of Record. however, transfers may prove to be difficult."
>
> — [ASNOA Three Carrier Appointment Types](https://asnoa.com/2021/are-you-compromising-on-money-ownership/)

> "producer: it is unlikely that you own the business, though some networks may grant contractual ownership."
>
> — [ASNOA Three Carrier Appointment Types](https://asnoa.com/2021/are-you-compromising-on-money-ownership/)

> "direct: typically, you own the business, though agents should scrutinize exit clauses and transfer restrictions in contracts."
>
> — [ASNOA Three Carrier Appointment Types](https://asnoa.com/2021/are-you-compromising-on-money-ownership/)

## aggregator contract terms

### exit provisions

departure terms require careful review:

> "exit fees discourage departures. portfolio buyback options or forced surrender affecting client retention must be understood. do you have the option to buy back your portfolio or do you have to surrender it and lose your clients?"
>
> — [AgentSync Aggregators Networks Clusters](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/insurance-101-what-are-aggregators-networks-and-clusters)

### non-compete provisions

> "noncompete agreements typically range from 1-2 years."
>
> — [Chicagoland SIA Book Ownership](https://benefits.chicagolandsia.com/chicagoland-sia-blog/understanding-book-ownership-a-comparative-analysis-for-insurance-agents)

> "noncompete restrictions limiting outside carrier relationships must be understood before joining."
>
> — [AgentSync Aggregators Networks Clusters](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/insurance-101-what-are-aggregators-networks-and-clusters)

### key questions before joining

> "before joining a network, agents should confirm: exclusive subcodes granting sole writing authority, direct access to commission and production reports, privacy controls over client information, freedom to continue with network carriers post-departure, and written subcode release upon contract completion."
>
> — [Chicagoland SIA Book Ownership](https://benefits.chicagolandsia.com/chicagoland-sia-blog/understanding-book-ownership-a-comparative-analysis-for-insurance-agents)

## aggregator vs wholesaler distinction

aggregators and wholesalers serve different functions in the distribution chain:

> "a wholesale broker is a type of insurance broker who acts as an intermediary between a retail broker and an insurer but has no contact with the insured."
>
> — [IRMI Wholesale Broker Definition](https://www.irmi.com/term/insurance-definitions/wholesale-broker)

key differences:

| aspect | aggregator | wholesaler |
|--------|------------|------------|
| primary function | pool agent premiums for carrier access | place hard-to-place individual risks |
| membership | ongoing relationship with fees | transactional, per-submission |
| carrier access | multiple standard market carriers | E&S and specialty markets |
| production requirements | minimal through pooling | none, activity-based |
| book ownership | varies by contract | typically agent retains |

> "retail agents don't need a special license to provide their clients with access to the wholesale insurance marketplace."
>
> — [3HCG Wholesale Insurance Explained](https://3hcg.com/blog/wholesale-insurance-explained-understanding-the-role-of-insurance-wholesalers)

## pros and cons of the aggregator model

### advantages

**market access without production minimums:**

> "members gain greater market access, higher commissions, and additional administrative support by leveraging pooled premiums and combined bargaining power with major carriers."
>
> — [AgentSync Aggregators Networks Clusters](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/insurance-101-what-are-aggregators-networks-and-clusters)

**higher commissions:**

> "members achieve significantly higher commission rates than they could on their own plus negotiate benefits typically reserved for large agencies."
>
> — [AgentSync Aggregators Networks Clusters](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/insurance-101-what-are-aggregators-networks-and-clusters)

**shared resources:**

> "access to centralized technology platforms, agency management systems, compliance tools, marketing support, and administrative assistance."
>
> — [AgentSync Aggregators Networks Clusters](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/insurance-101-what-are-aggregators-networks-and-clusters)

> "members can save up to 55% vs. standalone solutions through SIAA's agency tech stack options."
>
> — [SIAA Technology](https://www.siaa.com/siaa-advantage/technology)

**proven results:**

> "I have far surpassed my estimate—my agency is now 4.5 times larger than my captive book at 6 years!"
>
> — [SIAA Success Stories](https://www.siaa.com/success-stories)

### disadvantages

**ongoing fees:**

membership costs reduce net income through initiation fees and monthly charges.

**reduced autonomy:**

producer sub-code arrangements limit operational independence and carrier portal access.

**exit complications:**

> "exit fees discourage departures. portfolio buyback options or forced surrender affecting client retention."
>
> — [AgentSync Aggregators Networks Clusters](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/insurance-101-what-are-aggregators-networks-and-clusters)

**potential non-competes:**

agents may be restricted from carrier relationships outside the network.

**profit sharing limitations:**

> "producer-level appointments typically exclude profit-sharing access due to lacking location-specific codes."
>
> — [ASNOA Three Carrier Appointment Types](https://asnoa.com/2021/are-you-compromising-on-money-ownership/)

## summary

| aspect | typical standard |
|--------|------------------|
| aggregator function | pool premiums, negotiate carrier access |
| major aggregators | SIAA, Smart Choice, ASNOA, Goosehead, Brightway |
| SIAA premium volume | $18.1 billion total |
| SIAA carrier access | 40+ national and regional carriers |
| membership fees | hundreds to thousands initiation, monthly fees |
| commission (wholesale) | ~10% industry average |
| commission (producer) | 10-17% base, no profit sharing |
| commission (direct) | base plus bonuses, profit sharing |
| book ownership | varies by contract and appointment type |
| non-competes | typically 1-2 years |
| production requirement | none/minimal through aggregator pooling |
| launch timeline | days to one week (vs 4-6 weeks direct) |
