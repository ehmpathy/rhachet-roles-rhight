# define.carrier-access.wholesalers.insurance-agent

## .what

insurance agents access wholesalers, MGAs, and surplus lines brokers to place hard-to-place risks, with access requirements varying by wholesaler type, state licensing mandates, E&O coverage, and production history rather than direct carrier appointments.

## .why

> "wholesale brokers are the primary gateway between retail independent agents and E&S carriers."
>
> — [Agency Height: How Independent Agents Access E&S Carriers](https://agencyheight.com/how-to-access-es-carriers-independent-agent/)

agents seeking to serve clients with unusual, high-risk, or specialty coverage needs must understand how to access the wholesale distribution channel:

> "agents who can say yes when the standard market says no keep more clients and write more premium."
>
> — [Smart Choice Agents: Excess & Surplus Insurance Placement](https://www.smartchoiceagents.com/tips/excess-surplus-insurance-how-place-es-insurance-risks-effectively-and-right-market)

## definitions: wholesalers, MGAs, and surplus lines brokers

### wholesale broker

> "a wholesale broker is a type of insurance broker who acts as an intermediary between a retail broker and an insurer but has no contact with the insured."
>
> — [IRMI: Wholesale Broker Definition](https://www.irmi.com/term/insurance-definitions/wholesale-broker)

wholesale brokers serve two primary functions:

> "wholesale brokers often possess expertise in particular coverage lines, handle unusual or difficult-to-place risks, or have greater market access or influence with certain insurers."
>
> — [IRMI: Wholesale Broker Definition](https://www.irmi.com/term/insurance-definitions/wholesale-broker)

### managing general agent (MGA)

> "an MGA is a specialized type of insurance agent/broker that, unlike traditional agents/brokers, is vested with underwriting authority from an insurer."
>
> — [IRMI: Managing General Agent Definition](https://www.irmi.com/term/insurance-definitions/managing-general-agent-(mga))

the key distinction between MGAs and other wholesalers is binding authority:

> "an MGA has binding authority, or 'the pen' for one or more carriers, while a wholesaler or broker has no binding authority with the carrier."
>
> — [Insurance Journal Forums: MGA vs. Wholesaler](https://www.insurancejournal.com/forums/viewtopic.php?t=1828)

MGAs perform functions typically handled by insurers:

> "depending on the appointment, a managing general agent may perform one of many tasks normally performed by an insurer, including sub-contracting with independent agents, negotiating commissions, handling claims, issuing policies, and processing endorsements."
>
> — [IRMI: Managing General Agent Definition](https://www.irmi.com/term/insurance-definitions/managing-general-agent-(mga))

### surplus lines broker

> "a surplus lines broker is a broker who is licensed to place coverage with nonadmitted insurers (insurers not licensed to do business in a given state)."
>
> — [IRMI: Surplus Lines Broker Definition](https://www.irmi.com/term/insurance-definitions/surplus-lines-broker)

surplus lines brokers access non-admitted markets:

> "most states require an agent to have a separate license to write surplus lines coverage."
>
> — [IRMI: Surplus Lines Broker Definition](https://www.irmi.com/term/insurance-definitions/surplus-lines-broker)

## how agents access wholesalers vs direct carriers

### direct carrier appointments

direct appointments require formal agreements with production requirements:

> "direct appointments give you the most freedom; you use your own process to service clients, you have your own credentials to sign into carrier portals, and you are on the DEC pages."
>
> — [Dark Horse Insurance: Direct Carrier Appointments vs Producer Sub-Codes](https://darkhorseinsurance.com/direct-carrier-appointments-vs-producer-sub-codes/)

production thresholds for direct appointments are substantial:

> "most appointments include minimum annual written premium requirements — often $50,000–$250,000 per year with the carrier within the first 12–24 months."
>
> — [Agency Height: How to Get Appointed with a Wholesale Carrier](https://agencyheight.com/how-to-get-appointed-wholesale-carrier/)

### wholesaler access

retail agents do not need special licenses to access wholesalers:

> "retail agents don't need a special license to provide their clients with access to the wholesale insurance marketplace."
>
> — [3HCG: Wholesale Insurance Explained](https://3hcg.com/blog/wholesale-insurance-explained-understanding-the-role-of-insurance-wholesalers)

wholesaler relationships have lower barriers to entry:

> "a wholesale appointment is a formal working agreement between a retail independent agent and a wholesale broker or MGA that authorizes you to submit business to them, establishes how commissions are split, and outlines the terms of the relationship."
>
> — [Agency Height: How to Get Appointed with a Wholesale Carrier](https://agencyheight.com/how-to-get-appointed-wholesale-carrier/)

production requirements differ significantly:

> "typically, no production requirements, just activity."
>
> — [ASNOA: Three Carrier Appointment Types](https://asnoa.com/2021/are-you-compromising-on-money-ownership/)

## surplus lines license requirements

### state license mandates

surplus lines brokers must hold specialized licenses:

> "every state, as a part of its surplus lines law, requires the issuance of a surplus lines broker license."
>
> — [WSIA: What is Surplus Lines](https://www.wsia.org/wcm/wcm/About/What_is_Surplus_Lines.aspx)

the licensed surplus lines broker carries regulatory responsibilities:

> "the licensed surplus lines broker is responsible for: (1) selecting an eligible surplus lines insurer; (2) reporting the surplus lines transaction to insurance regulators; (3) remitting the premium tax due; and (4) assuring compliance with all the requirements of the surplus lines codes."
>
> — [WSIA: What is Surplus Lines](https://www.wsia.org/wcm/wcm/About/What_is_Surplus_Lines.aspx)

### prerequisite licenses

surplus lines authority requires base licenses:

> "unlicensed surplus line broker applicants must complete prelicensing education requirements and pass both the Property and Casualty Broker-Agent license examinations."
>
> — [California Department of Insurance: Surplus Line Broker FAQ](https://www.insurance.ca.gov/0200-industry/0200-prod-licensing/0400-other-info/0800-questions-answers/surplus-line-broker-faq.cfm)

### nonresident license under NRRA

the Nonadmitted and Reinsurance Reform Act simplified multi-state operations:

> "the NRRA generally provides that the placement of nonadmitted insurance will be regulated by an insured's home state, and that no other state may require a surplus lines broker to be licensed to sell, solicit, or negotiate nonadmitted insurance with respect to the insured."
>
> — [Wikipedia: Nonadmitted and Reinsurance Reform Act of 2010](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nonadmitted_and_Reinsurance_Reform_Act_of_2010)

## diligent search requirements

### the diligent search obligation

before surplus lines coverage placement, brokers must demonstrate market unavailability:

> "one of the most significant regulatory requirements imposed by state surplus lines laws is that a surplus lines broker must complete a diligent search of the admitted markets."
>
> — [WSIA: What is Surplus Lines](https://www.wsia.org/wcm/wcm/About/What_is_Surplus_Lines.aspx)

the search represents good-faith efforts:

> "a diligent search represents the attempt to find the coverage from admitted insurers before a policy is placed in the surplus lines market."
>
> — [WSIA: What is Surplus Lines](https://www.wsia.org/wcm/wcm/About/What_is_Surplus_Lines.aspx)

### declination requirements

most states require multiple declinations:

> "generally, three companies licensed to write the kind and type of insurance must decline a risk before it can be placed in the surplus lines market."
>
> — [WSIA: What is Surplus Lines](https://www.wsia.org/wcm/wcm/About/What_is_Surplus_Lines.aspx)

requirements apply per-risk:

> "some states expressly indicate that the diligent search requirement be satisfied on a 'per risk' basis, including each and every time an insurance policy is renewed."
>
> — [Troutman Pepper: A Deep Dive Into the Diligent Effort Requirement](https://www.troutman.com/insights/a-deep-dive-into-the-diligent-effort-search-requirement.html)

### recent changes

some states have repealed diligent search requirements:

> "Virginia, Mississippi, Louisiana, and Wisconsin have already repealed their diligent search requirements for surplus lines policies."
>
> — [McDermott Will & Emery: Florida Set to Repeal Diligent Search](https://www.mwe.com/insights/florida-set-to-repeal-diligent-search-for-surplus-lines-policies/)

## qualification requirements for wholesaler access

### retail agent requirements

retail agents need appropriate line authority:

> "as a retail agent, you need a standard property and casualty license in your state."
>
> — [Agency Height: How Independent Agents Access E&S Carriers](https://agencyheight.com/how-to-access-es-carriers-independent-agent/)

wholesalers assess agent credentials:

> "wholesale appointment applications typically require an active P&C license certificate, E&O insurance certificate, agency information including years in operation, and a description of current book of business."
>
> — [Agency Height: How to Get Appointed with a Wholesale Carrier](https://agencyheight.com/how-to-get-appointed-wholesale-carrier/)

### MGA requirements

MGAs must meet NAIC Model Act criteria:

> "individuals qualify as MGAs when they: manage all or part of an insurer's business, and as an agent produce/underwrite gross direct written premium equal to or exceeding 5% of policyholder surplus, while also adjusting claims exceeding $10,000 or negotiating reinsurance."
>
> — [AgentSync: The MGA Act](https://agentsync.io/blog/producer-management/understanding-the-mga-act)

MGAs require written contracts with insurers:

> "no person acting in the capacity of an MGA shall place business with an insurer unless there is in force a written contract."
>
> — [NAIC Model MGA Act #225](https://content.naic.org/sites/default/files/model-law-225.pdf)

## E&O insurance requirements

### carrier and wholesaler mandates

E&O coverage is universally required:

> "agents are usually required by their carriers, managing general agents, aggregators, wholesalers, and sometimes state insurance department to provide proof of E&O insurance."
>
> — [NAPA Benefits: E&O Insurance](https://www.napa-benefits.org/insurance/errors-and-omissions-eando-insurance)

wholesalers require minimum coverage:

> "most wholesalers require active E&O coverage, typically $1 million per occurrence minimum, before they'll process an appointment."
>
> — [Agency Height: How to Get Appointed with a Wholesale Carrier](https://agencyheight.com/how-to-get-appointed-wholesale-carrier/)

### state requirements

few states mandate E&O by law:

> "only Rhode Island legally requires insurance professionals to carry E&O coverage, with agents, brokers and producers needing minimum limits of $250,000 per occurrence and $500,000 aggregate."
>
> — [TechInsurance: E&O Insurance Requirements by State](https://www.techinsurance.com/errors-omissions-insurance/state-laws)

### recommended coverage levels

industry standards suggest higher limits:

> "most insurance agencies should maintain at least $1,000,000 per claim with a $1,000,000 aggregate limit as a baseline."
>
> — [BR Risk Group: How Much E&O Insurance Do I Need](https://www.brriskgroupins.com/how-much-eo-insurance-do-i-need/)

## experience requirements

### wholesaler expectations

wholesalers evaluate experience as part of appointment:

> "wholesalers typically assess key details such as the agency's years of experience and book of business mix when evaluating appointments."
>
> — [Agency Height: How to Get Appointed with a Wholesale Carrier](https://agencyheight.com/how-to-get-appointed-wholesale-carrier/)

> "getting a few years of experience as an agent or broker is often a requirement for hire."

professional designations enhance qualifications:

> "some useful certifications include Certified Insurance Counselor (CIC), Certified Risk Manager (CRM), Chartered Property Casualty Underwriter (CPCU), Associate in General Insurance (AINS), and Certified Managing General Agent (CMGA)."

> — [ABTrainingCenter: ASLI Certification](https://www.abtrainingcenter.com/asli.asp)

## bond requirements

### surplus lines broker bonds

many states require surety bonds:

> "the states that require surplus lines broker bonds include California, Virginia, Alabama, Florida, Kentucky, Ohio, Rhode Island, Maryland, Arkansas and Georgia, among others."
>
> — [Surety Bonds: Surplus Lines Broker Bonds Complete Guide](https://www.suretybonds.com/license-permit/surplus-lines-broker-bonds)

bond amounts vary by state:

> "surplus lines brokers in California must post $50,000 surety bonds, while Ohio requires surplus brokers to post $25,000 bonds."
>
> — [Surety Bonds: Surplus Lines Broker Bonds Complete Guide](https://www.suretybonds.com/license-permit/surplus-lines-broker-bonds)

some states use tiered requirements:

> "in Washington, a resident surplus line broker must maintain a bond in the amount of $2,500, or five percent of the premiums from placement of coverage with surplus line insurers in the previous calendar year, whichever is greater, but not to exceed $100,000."
>
> — [Washington RCW 48.15.070](https://app.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=48.15.070)

### MGA financial requirements

MGAs face higher bond thresholds:

> "an MGA is required to obtain a surety bond. the amount of this bond may range from $100,000–$500,000."
>
> — [AD Banker: Become a Managing General Agent](https://www.adbanker.com/blog/everything-you-need-to-know-about-becoming-a-managing-general-agent-in-insurance/)

## wholesaler relationships vs direct appointments

### key structural differences

wholesaler relationships differ fundamentally from carrier appointments:

> "you operate within a third party's workflow with no authority to service the client on your own."
>
> — [ASNOA: Three Carrier Appointment Types](https://asnoa.com/2021/are-you-compromising-on-money-ownership/)

direct appointments offer greater control:

> "direct relationships with carriers often lead to higher commission rates and access to exclusive products and programs."
>
> — [Dark Horse Insurance: Direct Carrier Appointments vs Producer Sub-Codes](https://darkhorseinsurance.com/direct-carrier-appointments-vs-producer-sub-codes/)

### commission structures

wholesale relationships involve commission splits:

> "the more commission the wholesale broker retains, the less the retail agent gets, with every unfair commission split equating to a payment the retail agent makes to their wholesaler broker."
>
> — [Ethos Insurance: 5 Ways Retailers Pay For Their Wholesale Insurance Broker](https://www.ethosinsurance.com/blog/5-ways-retailers-pay-for-their-wholesale-insurance-broker)

### producer sub-codes as alternative

sub-codes provide access without full appointment:

> "producer sub-codes are designations granted by larger agencies or managing general agents (MGAs) that allow agents to access carriers and markets without meeting stringent volume requirements."
>
> — [Dark Horse Insurance: Direct Carrier Appointments vs Producer Sub-Codes](https://darkhorseinsurance.com/direct-carrier-appointments-vs-producer-sub-codes/)

## role of surplus lines in market access

### the surplus lines market

surplus lines serves as a market safety valve:

> "often called the 'safety valve' of the insurance industry, surplus lines insurers fill the need for coverage in the marketplace by insuring those risks that are declined by the standard underwriting and pricing processes of admitted insurance carriers."
>
> — [Nebraska DOI: Surplus Lines](https://doi.nebraska.gov/sites/default/files/doc/SurplusLinesRevised.pdf)

the market has grown substantially:

> "the U.S. surplus lines market surpassed $100 billion in direct premiums written in 2023 and grew an additional 12.2% in 2024 to $131 billion."
>
> — [NAIC: Surplus Lines](https://content.naic.org/insurance-topics/surplus-lines)

### consumer notice requirements

brokers must disclose surplus lines status:

> "every state requires a notification to the insured party in a surplus lines transaction that discloses: (1) the surplus lines policy is not covered by the state guaranty fund; and (2) the insurance is placed with a surplus lines company."
>
> — [WSIA: What is Surplus Lines](https://www.wsia.org/wcm/wcm/About/What_is_Surplus_Lines.aspx)

## how MGAs provide market access

### delegated authority model

MGAs extend carrier reach through delegated authority:

> "binding authority is a contractual agreement under which an insurance capacity provider — an insurer, Lloyd's syndicate, or group of syndicates — delegates the authority to underwrite and bind insurance contracts to a third party."
>
> — [Regure: What is Binding Authority](https://www.getregure.com/glossary/binding-authority/)

carriers benefit from MGA distribution:

> "carriers gain market access and premium production without building internal underwriting teams or distribution infrastructure."
>
> — [Novatae: What is an MGA in Insurance](https://novatae.com/news/what-is-an-mga-in-insurance)

### Lloyd's coverholder access

MGAs serve as coverholders for Lloyd's:

> "in the USA, coverholders are commonly known as managing general agents (MGAs)."
>
> — [Lloyd's: Coverholder Guide](https://www.surpluslines.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/Lloyds-of-London-Coverholder-Guide.pdf)

coverholders provide efficient access:

> "coverholders can bind coverage from a syndicate without going to London and waiting to see an underwriter at the syndicate's box at Lloyd's."
>
> — [Vertafore: What is Lloyd's of London](https://www.vertafore.com/resources/blog/understanding-insurance-what-lloyds-london)

## the submission process

### work with wholesalers

retail agents follow a structured submission process:

> "the wholesale process follows these steps: risk submission, underwriting review, quote & negotiation, policy placement, ongoing support."
>
> — [3HCG: Wholesale Insurance Explained](https://3hcg.com/blog/wholesale-insurance-explained-understanding-the-role-of-insurance-wholesalers)

quality submissions matter:

> "a complete submission/application with all relevant supporting documentation—such as loss runs, etc., mean that fewer questions will need to be asked."
>
> — [Founders Professional: Components of a Great Insurance Submission](https://www.founderspro.com/2022/07/19/the-components-of-a-great-insurance-submission-to-a-wholesale-brokerage/)

## summary

| aspect | requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| retail agent license | active P&C license in state of operation |
| surplus lines license | required for brokers that place with non-admitted insurers |
| E&O insurance | typically $1M/$1M minimum for wholesaler appointments |
| surety bond (surplus lines) | $10,000–$100,000 dependent on state |
| surety bond (MGA) | $100,000–$500,000 |
| production requirement | none/minimal for wholesalers; $50K–$250K for direct carriers |
| diligent search | 3 declinations in most states (some states have repealed) |
| written contract | required between MGA and insurer per NAIC Model Act |
| experience | evaluated by wholesaler; years in operation and book mix |
| professional designations | CIC, CPCU, ASLI, CMGA enhance qualifications |
