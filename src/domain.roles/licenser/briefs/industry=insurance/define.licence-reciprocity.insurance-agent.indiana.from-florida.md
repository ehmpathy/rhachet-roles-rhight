# define.licence-reciprocity.insurance-agent.indiana.from-florida

## .what

Florida-licensed insurance producers can obtain an Indiana non-resident producer license through NIPR reciprocity without examination or pre-licensing courses, receiving the same lines of authority held in Florida.

## .why

> "A nonresident person shall receive a nonresident producer license if: (1) the person is currently licensed as a resident and in good standing in the person's home state; (2) the person has submitted the proper request for licensure and has paid the required fees; (3) the person has submitted or transmitted the application for licensure that was submitted to the person's home state or a completed uniform application; and (4) the person's home state awards non-resident producer licenses to residents of Indiana on the same basis."
>
> — [Indiana Code 27-1-15.6-8, Nonresident Producer License](https://law.justia.com/codes/indiana/title-27/article-1/chapter-15-6/section-27-1-15-6-8/)

## florida resident license as home state

Florida issues several producer license types that map to Indiana lines of authority.

| florida license | florida code | indiana equivalent |
|-----------------|--------------|-------------------|
| Life, Health (including Annuities and Variable) | 2-15 | Life, Accident & Health |
| Property & Casualty (General Lines) | 2-20 | Property & Casualty |
| Life Insurance (Annuities and Variable Contracts) | 2-14 | Life |
| Health Insurance | 2-40 | Accident & Health |

> "The Florida 2-20 Property and Casualty Agent License, or 'General Lines Agent License,' allows an individual, after being appointed by the Insurance Company, to transact property, casualty, surety, health, marine, and miscellaneous lines."
>
> — [Central Insurance School, Florida 2-20 License](https://centralinsuranceschool.com/prelicensing/2-20PropertycasualtyGeneralLinesAgentLicense.aspx)

> "Reciprocity only works for the same lines of authority. If you'd like to have a different line of authority in a different state you'll need to take a course and exam."
>
> — [All-Lines Training, Insurance Agent License Reciprocity](https://alllinestraining.com/insurance-agent-license-reciprocity/)

**critical:** your Florida license must be active and in good standing before Indiana will issue the non-resident license.

## indiana non-resident license requirements

Indiana grants non-resident licenses on a reciprocal basis without requiring examination or pre-licensing education for producers from states like Florida that offer reciprocity.

> "Non-residents qualify for licensing if they are from a state that grants non-resident licenses on a reciprocal basis. The license scope matches what the applicant's home state permits."
>
> — [Indiana Department of Insurance, Non-Resident Agent Licensing](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/non-resident-agent-licensing/)

> "No pre-licensing course required. No examination required."
>
> — [Indiana Department of Insurance, Non-Resident Agent Licensing](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/non-resident-agent-licensing/)

### eligibility criteria

| requirement | detail |
|-------------|--------|
| minimum age | 18 years old |
| resident license | active and in good standing in Florida |
| reciprocity | Florida offers reciprocity to Indiana residents |
| background | no fingerprinting required by Indiana |

> "Indiana is one of eight states that don't require fingerprinting, a background check, or even two forms of identification for insurance licenses."
>
> — [AgentSync, How States Handle Fingerprinting](https://agentsync.io/blog/insurance-101/getting-to-know-you-how-states-handle-fingerprinting-and-background-checks-for-insurance-licensees)

## NIPR process

The National Insurance Producer Registry (NIPR) provides electronic submission of non-resident license applications.

### step-by-step application

| step | action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | obtain your National Producer Number (NPN) if you don't have one |
| 2 | verify your Florida license is active in the NAIC Producer Database |
| 3 | access NIPR Gateway at www.nipr.com |
| 4 | select Indiana as your target state |
| 5 | choose lines of authority matching your Florida license |
| 6 | provide valid business email address |
| 7 | submit application and pay fees |

> "From the NIPR portal, you select which states you want to apply to, choose your lines of authority, upload any required documents, and pay the fees. NIPR routes your application to each state's insurance department."
>
> — [LicenseLateral, Non-Resident Insurance Guide](https://www.licenselateral.com/blog/non-resident-insurance-guide)

> "Applicant must provide a valid business email address."
>
> — [NIPR, Indiana Non-Resident Licensing Individual](https://nipr.com/licensing-center/state-requirements/indiana-non-resident-licensing-individual)

### alternative submission methods

| method | platform |
|--------|----------|
| electronic | www.nipr.com |
| electronic | www.sircon.com/Indiana |
| paper | NAIC Uniform Application mailed to IDOI |

> "Applicants can submit applications through: Sircon (electronic), NIPR (electronic), Uniform NAIC paper applications."
>
> — [Indiana Department of Insurance, Non-Resident Agent Licensing](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/non-resident-agent-licensing/)

### special requirements for variable lines

> "Applicants for the variable line of authority must supply a FINRA Central Registry Depository (CRD) number on the application."
>
> — [NIPR, Indiana Non-Resident Licensing Individual](https://nipr.com/licensing-center/state-requirements/indiana-non-resident-licensing-individual)

### special requirements for surplus lines

> "Applicant must hold equivalent license in resident state to apply for surplus line of authority."
>
> — [NIPR, Indiana Non-Resident Licensing Individual](https://nipr.com/licensing-center/state-requirements/indiana-non-resident-licensing-individual)

## fees

### initial license fees

| fee type | amount |
|----------|--------|
| Indiana state license fee | $90.00 |
| NIPR transaction fee | $5.60 |
| total (standard) | $95.60 |

> "Pay the state license fee of $90 for non-residents."
>
> — [Agenzee, Indiana Licensing and Renewal Guide](https://agenzee.com/guides/indiana-licensing-and-renewal-guide/)

### retaliatory fees

Indiana applies retaliatory fees based on what Florida charges Indiana residents.

> "Indiana Code 27-1-20-12(a) requires the Indiana Department of Insurance to impose fees on another state's insurance professionals equal to what the other state charges Indiana insurance professionals. These fees are referred to as 'retaliatory fees.'"
>
> — [Indiana Department of Insurance, Non-Resident Retaliatory Fees](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/resident-licensing-related-links/non-resident-retaliatory-fees/)

**note:** Florida-specific retaliatory fees are published in the IDOI fee schedule PDF at www.in.gov/idoi/2600.htm

> "ALL LICENSE APPLICATION FEES ARE NONREFUNDABLE AND NONTRANSFERABLE."
>
> — [Indiana Department of Insurance, License Renewal Procedures for Non-Residents](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/non-resident-agent-license-renewal/license-renewal-procedures-for-non-residents/)

## CE reciprocity

Indiana accepts Florida CE compliance for non-resident license holders.

> "Nonresident license holders are currently required to adhere to their home state continuing education requirements."
>
> — [NIPR, Indiana Non-Resident Licensing Individual](https://nipr.com/licensing-center/state-requirements/indiana-non-resident-licensing-individual)

> "Non-resident producers who have satisfied their home state's continuing education requirement are exempt."
>
> — [AD Banker, Indiana Insurance CE Requirements](https://www.adbanker.com/licensing-requirements/insurance/indiana/continuing-education/)

### florida CE requirements (your compliance obligation)

| requirement | hours |
|-------------|-------|
| total CE every 2 years | 24 hours (less than 6 years licensed) |
| total CE every 2 years | 20 hours (6+ years licensed) |
| law and ethics update | 4 hours |
| NFIP flood training (if selling flood) | 3 hours |

> "Insurance agents and adjusters who have been licensed less than 6 years are required to complete 24 total hours every 2 years. After being licensed for 6 years, the CE requirement reduces to 20 total hours every 2 years."
>
> — [eInsuranceTraining, Florida Insurance Continuing Education Requirements](https://www.einsurancetraining.com/career-central/florida-insurance-continuing-education-requirements-based-on-experience-level)

### indiana long-term care partnership exception

> "The basic 8-hour and 5-hour renewal requirements are waived for agents holding an Indiana non-resident health license if Indiana has licensing reciprocity with that state, but the 7-hour Indiana Partnership continuing education requirement does apply to non-resident agents and is not waived."
>
> — [Indiana Department of Insurance, LTC Agent Training Requirements](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/resident-licensing-related-links/ltc-agent-training-requirements/)

**critical:** if selling Indiana Long Term Care Partnership (ILTCP) products, you must complete the 7-hour ILTCP course in a classroom setting. This is not waived for non-residents.

> "The 7-hour Partnership course is one-time only and classroom-only (no self-study option)."
>
> — [Indiana Department of Insurance, LTC Agent Training Requirements](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/resident-licensing-related-links/ltc-agent-training-requirements/)

## appointment requirements in IN

Indiana is a "registry state" meaning formal appointments are not required.

> "Indiana doesn't require insurance producer appointments. Registry states must maintain an internal registry of authorized licensees to submit to the state upon request."
>
> — [AgentSync, Indiana Insurance Licensing](https://agentsync.io/compliance-library/jurisdictions/indiana)

> "Appointments cannot be processed through NIPR, and there are no appointment renewal requirements."
>
> — [AgentSync, Indiana Insurance Licensing](https://agentsync.io/compliance-library/jurisdictions/indiana)

### when appointments apply

> "An insurance producer shall not act as an agent of an insurer unless the insurance producer becomes appointed. An insurance producer who is not acting as an agent of an insurer is not required to become appointed."
>
> — [Indiana Code 27-1-15.6-12](https://codes.findlaw.com/in/title-27-insurance/in-code-sect-27-1-15-6-12/)

### termination notification

> "An insurer or authorized representative of an insurer that terminates the appointment, employment, contract, or other insurance business relationship with a producer shall notify the commissioner not more than thirty (30) days after the effective date of the termination."
>
> — [AgentSync, Insurance Carrier Appointment FAQs](https://agentsync.io/blog/producer-management/insurance-carrier-appointment-faqs-by-state)

## renewal

### license duration and expiration

> "Licenses issued or renewed after July 1, 2012 are valid for 2 years, expiring on the last day of the producer's birth month."
>
> — [Indiana Department of Insurance, License Renewal Procedures for Non-Residents](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/non-resident-agent-license-renewal/license-renewal-procedures-for-non-residents/)

### renewal fees

| fee type | amount |
|----------|--------|
| non-resident producer renewal | $90.00 |
| NIPR transaction fee | $5.60 |

> "The non-resident producer renewal fee is $90.00 for all lines including limited lines."
>
> — [Indiana Department of Insurance, License Renewal Procedures for Non-Residents](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/non-resident-agent-license-renewal/license-renewal-procedures-for-non-residents/)

### renewal timeline

| period | timeframe |
|--------|-----------|
| early renewal window | 90 days before expiration |
| grace period | 30 days after expiration |
| late renewal period | 31 days to 1 year after expiration |

> "Renewal can begin 90 days prior to the expiration date of the license. The renewal grace period ends 30 days past the expiration date."
>
> — [NIPR, Indiana Non-Resident Renewal Individual](https://nipr.com/licensing-center/state-requirements/indiana-non-resident-renewal-individual)

### reinstatement (expired less than 1 year)

| fee type | amount |
|----------|--------|
| renewal fee | $90.00 |
| reinstatement penalty | $270.00 (3x renewal) |
| total | $360.00 |

> "Your license can be reinstated if it has been expired for less than one year by paying the renewal fee plus a penalty of three times the renewal fee."
>
> — [Indiana Department of Insurance, Reinstating A Non-Resident Expired License](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/non-resident-agent-license-renewal/reinstating-a-non-resident-expired-license/)

### expired more than 1 year

> "Your license may not be reinstated if it has been expired for more than one year; instead, you must apply for a new license."
>
> — [Indiana Department of Insurance, Reinstating A Non-Resident Expired License](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/non-resident-agent-license-renewal/reinstating-a-non-resident-expired-license/)

### electronic notifications

> "The Department no longer mails renewal notices; all notifications are sent electronically to registered email addresses."
>
> — [Indiana Department of Insurance, License Renewal Procedures for Non-Residents](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/non-resident-agent-license-renewal/license-renewal-procedures-for-non-residents/)

### address change requirement

> "The statute requires producers to report address changes within 30 days, with a $100 penalty for non-compliance."
>
> — [Indiana Department of Insurance, License Renewal Procedures for Non-Residents](https://www.in.gov/idoi/licensing/non-resident-agent-license-renewal/license-renewal-procedures-for-non-residents/)

## summary

| step | requirement |
|------|-------------|
| 1 | maintain active Florida resident producer license |
| 2 | obtain National Producer Number (NPN) via NIPR |
| 3 | verify Florida license appears in NAIC Producer Database |
| 4 | submit application via NIPR or Sircon |
| 5 | pay $90 state fee + $5.60 NIPR fee (retaliatory fees may apply) |
| 6 | receive license within 3-7 business days |
| 7 | no appointments required (Indiana is registry state) |
| 8 | maintain Florida CE compliance (24 hours/2 years) |
| 9 | complete 7-hour ILTCP classroom course if selling partnership LTC |
| 10 | renew biennially on last day of birth month ($90) |
