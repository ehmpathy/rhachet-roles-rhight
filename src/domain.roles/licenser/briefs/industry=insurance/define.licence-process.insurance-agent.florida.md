# define.licence-process.insurance-agent.florida

## .what

florida insurance agent licensing requires pre-licensing education, state examination, fingerprint-based background check, application through MyProfile, and insurer appointment, all regulated under florida statute chapter 626 by the department of financial services.

## .why

> "the Department of Financial Services' Division of Insurance Agent and Agency Services protects the public by licensing individuals and entities and investigating alleged violations of the Florida Insurance Code."
>
> — [Florida Department of Financial Services](https://myfloridacfo.com/division/agents)

---

## florida department of financial services role

the florida department of financial services (DFS) oversees insurance agent licensing through its division of insurance agent and agency services:

> "the Division of Insurance Agent and Agency Services focuses on providing accurate information regarding insurance agents, adjusters, limited surety (bail bond) agents, navigators, insurance-related entities, including education providers and instructors."
>
> — [Florida DFS Division of Insurance Agent and Agency Services](https://myfloridacfo.com/division/agents)

the division administers four primary functional areas:

> "the division oversees licensing, compliance, education, and industry awareness to keep agents, adjusters, and agencies informed about regulatory matters."
>
> — [Florida DFS Division of Insurance Agent and Agency Services](https://myfloridacfo.com/division/agents)

florida's insurance regulatory structure is unique:

> "florida is the only US state where insurance oversight is split between two separate agencies."
>
> — [Insurance Journal](https://www.insurancejournal.com/news/southeast/2025/04/07/818622.htm)

---

## florida statute chapter 626

chapter 626 of the florida statutes, titled "insurance field representatives and operations," governs all insurance agent licensing:

> "chapter 626 establishes insurance agent and agency licensing requirements, examination procedures, appointment processes, and disciplinary actions."
>
> — [Florida Statutes Title XXXVII Chapter 626](https://www.flsenate.gov/Laws/Statutes/2025/Chapter626/All)

### key statutory sections

> "626.112 - no person may be, act as, or advertise or hold himself or herself out to be an insurance agent unless currently licensed by the department and appointed by an appropriate appointing entity or person."
>
> — [Florida Statute 626.112](https://law.justia.com/codes/florida/title-xxxvii/chapter-626/part-i/section-626-112/)

> "626.171 - applicants must file written applications with the department including personal information, prelicensing education method, license history, and fingerprints. applications must be under oath and signed by the applicant."
>
> — [Florida Statute 626.171](https://www.flsenate.gov/Laws/Statutes/2025/Chapter626/All)

> "626.221 - the department shall not issue any license as agent, solicitor, customer representative, or adjuster to any individual who has not qualified for, taken, and passed to the satisfaction of the department a written examination."
>
> — [Florida Statute 626.221](https://www.flsenate.gov/laws/statutes/2000/626.221)

## lines of authority

florida recognizes multiple lines of authority for insurance agents:

> "florida recognizes four major lines of authority: life, health, property, and casualty."
>
> — [Kaplan Financial Education](https://www.kaplanfinancial.com/insurance/florida/state-requirements)

### license types

> "the florida 2-15 license covers life and health insurance, including annuities and variable contracts."
>
> — [Florida DFS License Qualifications](https://myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/agents-and-adjusters/qualifications)

> "the florida 2-20 property and casualty agent license, or general lines agent license, allows an individual to transact property, casualty, surety, health, marine, and miscellaneous lines of insurance."
>
> — [Central Insurance School](https://centralinsuranceschool.com/prelicensing/2-20PropertycasualtyGeneralLinesAgentLicense.aspx)

## pre-licensing education hours

florida requires completion of state-approved pre-licensing education before taking the state examination:

> "florida requires potential agents to complete an approved prelicensing education course for each line of authority before attempting the state exam."
>
> — [AD Banker](https://www.adbanker.com/licensing-requirements/insurance/florida/pre-licensing/)

### hours by license type

> "pre-licensing education hour requirements: life (including annuities & variable contracts): 30 hours; health: 40 hours; health & life (including annuities & variable contracts): 60 hours; general lines (property & casualty): 200 hours; personal lines: 60 hours; all-lines adjuster: 40 hours."
>
> — [Florida DFS Education Information](https://www.myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/education/licensees)

> "the hours stated above include three (3) hours on the subject of Ethics."
>
> — [American Online Insurance School](https://aoischool.com/pre-licensing-state-requirements/florida-state-pre-licensing-requirements/)

### course validity

> "upon passing your course's certification exam with 70% or higher, you receive a certificate of completion that remains valid for four years."
>
> — [Aceable Insurance](https://insurance.aceable.com/resources/pre-license/how-to-get-your-insurance-license-in-florida/)

## state examination (pearson VUE)

the florida department of financial services contracts with pearson VUE to administer insurance licensing examinations:

> "pearsonVUE serves as florida's official testing vendor for insurance licensure examinations."
>
> — [Florida DFS Examinations](https://myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/agents-and-adjusters/exams)

### examination format

> "626.241 - exams test ability and competence and knowledge of the kinds of insurance and transactions to be handled. separate exams required for general lines, life, health, and adjuster licenses."
>
> — [Florida Statute 626.241](https://www.flsenate.gov/Laws/Statutes/2025/Chapter626/All)

> "the passing score for all florida insurance exams is 70%."
>
> — [StateRequirement](https://staterequirement.com/insurance-license-exam/florida-insurance-license-exam/)

### scheduling and fees

> "appointments may be made up to one calendar day prior to the day you wish to test, subject to availability."
>
> — [Pearson VUE Florida Insurance](https://www.pearsonvue.com/us/en/fl/insurance.html)

> "state exam fee: $44.00 (paid to vendor)."
>
> — [Florida DFS Fees](https://www.myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/agents-and-adjusters/fees)

### testing format

> "effective immediately, florida insurance candidates seeking to schedule an exam may only reserve their appointment in a physical test center. OnVUE online proctoring is no longer available for new reservations."
>
> — [Florida DFS Examinations](https://myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/agents-and-adjusters/exams)

### attempt limits

> "the florida department of financial services limits individuals to 5 exam attempts for the same exam type during a 12 month period."
>
> — [Pearson VUE Florida Insurance](https://www.pearsonvue.com/us/en/fl/insurance.html)

### score validity

> "a passing score is valid for one (1) year from the date that the score was earned."
>
> — [Florida DFS Examinations](https://myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/agents-and-adjusters/exams)

## background check (IdentoGO)

florida requires fingerprint-based criminal background checks for all insurance license applicants:

> "fingerprinting is a mandatory requirement for almost all of the licenses, registrations, and certifications issued by florida's department of financial services."
>
> — [Florida DFS Fingerprinting Information](https://myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/agents-and-adjusters/fingerprinting-information)

### provider and process

> "you must be fingerprinted through IdentoGO by Idemia, formerly MorphoTrust USA. we do not accept fingerprint results from other vendors or other states."
>
> — [Florida DFS Fingerprinting Information](https://myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/agents-and-adjusters/fingerprinting-information)

> "once you have submitted your fingerprints to IdentoGO by Idemia, your fingerprints are submitted electronically to the florida department of law enforcement. the results of your fingerprint-based criminal history check are then submitted electronically to florida department of financial services."
>
> — [Florida DFS Fingerprinting Information](https://myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/agents-and-adjusters/fingerprinting-information)

### submission methods

> "florida resident applicants should register for livescan and go to the nearest IdentoGO location to have their fingerprints submitted electronically."
>
> — [Florida DFS Fingerprinting Information](https://myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/agents-and-adjusters/fingerprinting-information)

> "out of state applicants will enter their zip code on the registration site to view the nearest livescan locations. if there isn't a location close enough, the option to submit fingerprint cards by mail is available."
>
> — [Florida DFS Fingerprinting Information](https://myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/agents-and-adjusters/fingerprinting-information)

### cost

> "livescan submissions cost $49.50 plus local sales tax, while fingerprint card submissions cost $50.75 plus tax."
>
> — [Florida DFS Fingerprinting Information](https://myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/agents-and-adjusters/fingerprinting-information)

### validity period

> "effective july 1, 2018, fingerprint/criminal history results are valid for only one (1) year for applicants who do not currently hold a valid florida insurance agent license."
>
> — [Florida DFS Fingerprinting Information](https://myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/agents-and-adjusters/fingerprinting-information)

> "for an individual applicant who is currently licensed and is seeking an additional license, and the individual has been fingerprinted through IdentoGO within the last 48 months or four (4) years, that applicant will not be required to submit fingerprints again."
>
> — [Florida DFS Fingerprinting Information](https://myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/agents-and-adjusters/fingerprinting-information)

## application via MyProfile

all license applications are submitted through the florida DFS MyProfile online portal:

> "MyProfile is your interface with the florida department of financial services' division of insurance agent and agency services. your account shows your licensing and compliance information at your convenience."
>
> — [Florida DFS MyProfile Info](https://myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/myprofile-info-and-tutorials)

### application functions

> "MyProfile allows insurance agents to submit applications for agent or adjuster licenses, print a license and certification letters, monitor continuing education status and transcripts, update personal information, view pending applications and departmental messages, and terminate licenses or appointments."
>
> — [Florida DFS MyProfile Info](https://myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/myprofile-info-and-tutorials)

### application fees

> "license application: $50.00 (exception: title agents pay $10.00). license ID: $5.00."
>
> — [Florida DFS Fees](https://www.myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/agents-and-adjusters/fees)

> "military members and veterans are exempt from application fees."
>
> — [Florida DFS Fees](https://www.myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/agents-and-adjusters/fees)

### application timeline

> "applicants have one year from passing their state exam to submit a license application. after that period expires, the examination results become void and retesting is required."
>
> — [Florida DFS FAQ](https://www.myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/faq)

## appointment process

a florida insurance license alone does not authorize an individual to sell insurance; an appointment from an insurer is also required:

> "no person may be, act as, or advertise or hold himself or herself out to be an insurance agent unless currently licensed by the department and appointed by an appropriate appointing entity or person. just being a licensed agent does not allow a person to sell insurance."
>
> — [Florida Statute 626.112](https://law.justia.com/codes/florida/title-xxxvii/chapter-626/part-i/section-626-112/)

### appointment submission

> "all appointments must be submitted electronically through eAppoint; the department will not accept paper appointments."
>
> — [Florida DFS FAQ](https://www.myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/faq)

> "carriers must report appointments within 45 days of the effective date via NIPR."
>
> — [AgentSync Florida Compliance](https://agentsync.io/compliance-library/jurisdictions/florida)

### appointment fees

> "appointment fees: residents: $60.00; nonresidents: $60.00 + $6.00 per additional county."
>
> — [Florida DFS Fees](https://www.myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/agents-and-adjusters/fees)

> "late fees: $250 (initial) or $25 (renewal)."
>
> — [Florida DFS Fees](https://www.myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/agents-and-adjusters/fees)

### appointment duration

> "initial appointments are good for no less than 24 months and no longer than 36 months, depending on when the appointment was made in relation to your birth month, and the appointment always expires on the last day of your birth month."
>
> — [Florida School of Insurance](https://floridaschool.com/eschool/servlet/CourseZipServlet/154/?section_uid=20538)

## continuing education

florida requires ongoing continuing education to maintain licensure:

> "626.2815 - licensees must complete 4-hour update courses every 2 years plus 6-20 elective hours depending on license type and experience level."
>
> — [Florida Statute 626.2815](https://www.leg.state.fl.us/statutes/index.cfm?App_mode=Display_Statute&URL=0600-0699/0626/Sections/0626.2815.html)

### hours by experience level

> "insurance agents and adjusters who have been licensed less than 6 years are required to complete 24 total hours every 2 years."
>
> — [Florida DFS FAQ](https://www.myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/faq)

> "after being licensed for 6 years, the CE requirement reduces to 20 total hours every 2 years."
>
> — [Florida DFS FAQ](https://www.myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/faq)

> "agents licensed six or more years can reduce requirements to 16 elective hours per cycle. those with 25+ years of experience plus a CPCU/CLU designation or risk management degree qualify for just 6 elective hours."
>
> — [Florida DFS FAQ](https://www.myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/faq)

### ethics requirement

> "florida requires all licensed insurance agents to complete a 4-hour law and ethics course during each renewal period."
>
> — [Florida DFS Continuing Education](https://www.myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/agents-and-adjusters/continuing-education)

> "when two or more licenses are held, the licensee must complete a 4-hour course specific to at least one license type held."
>
> — [Florida DFS Continuing Education](https://www.myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/agents-and-adjusters/continuing-education)

### compliance deadline

> "new agents begin their 24-month CE requirement immediately upon licensure, with completion due by the last day of their birth month, then every two years thereafter."
>
> — [Florida DFS FAQ](https://www.myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/faq)

## renewal

florida insurance licenses are perpetual, provided continuing education requirements are met:

> "insurance agent and adjuster licenses are perpetual (no renewal required), provided that the licensee is properly appointed."
>
> — [AgentSync Florida Compliance](https://agentsync.io/compliance-library/jurisdictions/florida)

> "there is no fee or paperwork required to renew your license after you complete your required CE hours."
>
> — [BetterCE Florida Requirements](https://www.betterce.com/license-management/florida-insurance-agent-renewal-requirements/)

### compliance enforcement

> "there is no grace period allowed for florida insurance agents who fail to complete their CE requirements by their CE due date. agents who do not complete their CE on time will have up to approximately 90 days to complete any outstanding CEs, sign a stipulation agreement with the florida department of financial services, and pay a fine of $250."
>
> — [AD Banker Florida CE](https://www.adbanker.com/licensing-requirements/insurance/florida/continuing-education/)

### appointment renewal

> "appointment renewals occur every 24 months during the licensee's birth month, assessed at $60 per appointment. a $25 late fee applies if renewals aren't completed within 45 days of expiration."
>
> — [Florida DFS FAQ](https://www.myfloridacfo.com/division/agents/licensing/faq)

## summary

| step | requirement |
|------|-------------|
| 1. pre-licensing education | complete state-approved course: 30 hrs (life), 40 hrs (health), 60 hrs (life & health), 200 hrs (general lines), includes 3 hrs ethics |
| 2. fingerprinting | submit fingerprints via IdentoGO ($49.50-$50.75); valid 1 year for new applicants |
| 3. state examination | pass pearson VUE exam with 70% score ($44); valid 1 year; max 5 attempts per 12 months |
| 4. license application | apply via MyProfile portal ($50 + $5 license ID); submit within 1 year of passed exam |
| 5. background check | await FDLE/FBI criminal history results processed through DFS |
| 6. license issuance | receive license upon approval; perpetual validity |
| 7. appointment | obtain insurer appointment via eAppoint ($60); required to sell insurance |
| 8. continuing education | complete 24 hrs biennial CE (includes 4 hrs ethics); due by birth month |
| 9. appointment renewal | renew every 24 months ($60); due by birth month |
