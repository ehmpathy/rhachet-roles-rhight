# define.carrier-access.public-lookup.insurance-agent

## .what

public access to insurance agent carrier appointment data allows consumers, insurers, and regulators to verify whether an agent is authorized to sell insurance products on behalf of a specific carrier, with transparency varying significantly by state jurisdiction.

## .why

> "state insurance departments oversee producer activities as part of a comprehensive regulatory framework designed to protect insurance consumer interests in insurance transactions."
>
> — [NAIC Producer Licensing](https://content.naic.org/insurance-topics/producer-licensing)

consumers need the ability to verify that agents representing carriers are legitimately authorized:

> "the Department of Financial Services' Division of Insurance Agent and Agency Services protects the public by licensing individuals and entities and investigating alleged violations of the Florida Insurance Code."
>
> — [Florida Department of Financial Services](https://myfloridacfo.com/division/agents)

> "the California Department of Insurance protects insurance consumers and maintains the integrity of the insurance industry by assisting with the implementation and enforcement of laws relating to the qualifications and licensing requirements for persons and organizations conducting insurance business in the state."
>
> — [California Department of Insurance](https://www.insurance.ca.gov/0200-industry/)

---

## what is an appointment

an appointment is the contractual authorization from a carrier to an agent:

> "the authority given by an insurer or employer to a licensee to transact insurance or adjust claims on behalf of an insurer or employer."
>
> — [Florida Statutes 626.015(4)](https://www.flsenate.gov/Laws/Statutes/2023/626.015)

licensing and appointment are distinct requirements:

> "No person may be, act as, or advertise...an insurance agent...unless he or she is currently licensed by the department and appointed by an appropriate appointing entity."
>
> — [Florida Statutes 626.112](https://www.flsenate.gov/Laws/Statutes/2023/626.112)

agents must maintain separate appointments for each carrier they represent:

> "An agent shall be required to have a separate appointment as to each insurer by whom he or she is appointed as an agent."
>
> — [Florida Statutes 626.331](https://www.flsenate.gov/Laws/Statutes/2023/626.331)

## state-level public lookup tools

### florida

florida provides one of the most comprehensive public appointment lookup systems:

> "Users can search by 'Appointing Entity' to identify carrier appointments. The tool displays whether appointments are currently active or inactive with corresponding status dates."
>
> — [Florida Licensee Search](https://licenseesearch.fldfs.com/)

the florida system displays:
- individual name and national producer number (NPN)
- license status and category
- appointing entity (carrier name)
- appointment category (auto, health, life & annuity, etc.)
- appointment status (active/inactive)
- appointment status date range

> "The most accurate information possible is used, but information found through this service is not guaranteed to be accurate or timely; nor does the Department of Financial Services (DFS) guarantee its suitability for any purpose."
>
> — [Florida Licensee Search Disclaimer](https://licenseesearch.fldfs.com/)

### california

california provides license verification with limited appointment visibility:

> "Consumers can 'Check a License' by searching 'for license status information based on license number or name.'"
>
> — [California Department of Insurance](https://www.insurance.ca.gov/01-consumers/101-help/)

consumer hotline available for additional verification:

> "1-800-927-4357 provides insurance information and assistance."
>
> — [California Department of Insurance Consumer Help](https://www.insurance.ca.gov/01-consumers/101-help/)

### texas

texas offers agent lookup with basic appointment information:

> "You can 'Find an agent by name or license number' through their online lookup tool."
>
> — [Texas Department of Insurance](https://www.tdi.texas.gov/agent/)

for businesses managing appointments:

> "TDI offers a process to 'Add or remove a licensed agent from an insurer (appointment transactions).'"
>
> — [Texas Department of Insurance](https://www.tdi.texas.gov/agent/)

### new york

new york provides basic license verification:

> "The Department of Financial Services provides a 'Producer/Licensee Search' application accessible through the DFS Portal. This tool allows the public to 'determine if a license has been issued and to view the license number.'"
>
> — [New York Department of Financial Services](https://www.dfs.ny.gov/apps_and_licensing/agents_and_brokers/home)

### indiana

indiana provides license verification through the Sircon Consumer Inquiry portal:

> "The Consumer Inquiry tool allows users to search for insurance producer license information by name, license number, or National Producer Number (NPN)."
>
> — [Sircon Consumer Inquiry](https://www.sircon.com/ComplianceExpress/Inquiry/consumerInquiry.do)

notably, indiana does not require formal carrier appointment filings to the state:

> "Indiana doesn't require insurance producer appointments. The carrier must maintain a list of all authorized licensees and submit the list to the state upon request."
>
> — [AgentSync Carrier Appointments](https://agentsync.io/blog/producer-management/insurance-carrier-appointment-faqs-by-state)

this means consumers in indiana can verify license status but may have limited visibility into specific carrier appointments compared to states like florida.

## NIPR producer database

the national insurance producer registry (NIPR) maintains the primary national database:

> "NIPR (National Insurance Producer Registry) is 'the centralized licensing and compliance resource for insurance professionals.'"
>
> — [NIPR](https://nipr.com/)

### public access scope

the producer database (PDB) provides industry-wide access:

> "The Producer Database (PDB) tracks licensed professionals with tools including detail reports, company appointment reports, and alerts."
>
> — [NAIC Producer Licensing](https://content.naic.org/insurance-topics/producer-licensing)

> "The Producer Database is publicly accessible and allows industry and consumers to search producer licensing and appointment details. It covers '50 states, the District of Columbia, Puerto Rico, Guam and the U.S. Virgin Islands.'"
>
> — [NAIC Producer Licensing](https://content.naic.org/insurance-topics/producer-licensing)

### regulator-only data

separate systems exist for regulatory oversight:

> "NIPR operates two distinct database systems: Producer Database (PDB) - Public/Industry Access...and State Producer Licensing Database (SPLD) - Regulator-Only, restricted to state insurance regulators for oversight purposes."
>
> — [NAIC Producer Licensing](https://content.naic.org/insurance-topics/producer-licensing)

### limitation: undisclosed states

some states do not require carriers to file appointment records with the state. in these states, carriers maintain their own lists and submit only upon request:

> "Indiana doesn't require insurance producer appointments. The carrier must maintain a list of all authorized licensees and submit the list to the state upon request."
>
> — [AgentSync Carrier Appointments](https://agentsync.io/blog/producer-management/insurance-carrier-appointment-faqs-by-state)

for agents licensed in undisclosed states, NIPR appointment data depends entirely on whether carriers voluntarily report it. since there is no state mandate to file appointments, carriers may not report to NIPR either — so appointment data for these states may be incomplete or absent even in the national database.

states with undisclosed appointment requirements include Indiana and others that rely on carrier-maintained lists rather than state filings. consumers who want to verify agents in these states should contact carriers directly to confirm appointment status.

## NAIC state based systems (SBS)

NAIC provides infrastructure for state regulatory data exchange:

> "NIPR manages appointment and termination information through its Gateway platform, facilitating 'the exchange of producer information between state insurance regulators and the entities they oversee.'"
>
> — [NAIC Producer Licensing](https://content.naic.org/insurance-topics/producer-licensing)

the system tracks licenses across all jurisdictions:

> "The U.S. currently has 'more than 2 million individuals and more than 236,000 business entities licensed to provide insurance services.'"
>
> — [NAIC Producer Licensing](https://content.naic.org/insurance-topics/producer-licensing)

## federal databases

there is no comprehensive federal insurance agent database due to state-based regulation:

> "Insurance is regulated by the states under a system stemming from the McCarran-Ferguson Act of 1945, which describes state regulation as being in 'the public interest' and gives it preeminence over federal law."
>
> — [Insurance Information Institute](https://www.iii.org/publications/commercial-insurance/how-it-functions/regulation)

> "Congress has become increasingly involved in both regulating insurance and overseeing states' regulation of insurance."
>
> — [Congressional Research Service](https://www.everycrsreport.com/reports/RL31982.html)

while the NMLS Consumer Access database exists for mortgage professionals, it does not cover insurance agents:

> "NMLSR ID means a number assigned by the Nationwide Mortgage Licensing System and Registry to facilitate electronic tracking and uniform identification of loan originators."
>
> — [12 CFR 1026.36](https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/12/1026.36)

## what consumers CAN see

consumers can typically access through state lookup tools:

- **license status**: active, inactive, expired, suspended, revoked
- **license number**: state-issued and national producer number (NPN)
- **appointment status**: whether appointed by a specific carrier
- **appointing entity**: name of the insurance company
- **appointment category**: lines of authority (life, health, property, casualty)
- **license expiration date**: when renewal is required
- **contact information**: business address (in some states)

> "An individual may hold concurrent appointments across different insurance categories, provided they have obtained the required licenses and qualifications for each category."
>
> — [Florida Statutes 626.331](https://www.flsenate.gov/Laws/Statutes/2023/626.331)

---

## what consumers CANNOT see

certain business information remains confidential:

- **production volumes**: premium amounts written
- **commission rates**: percentage compensation
- **contract terms**: carrier agreement details
- **internal performance metrics**: quality scores, persistency rates
- **termination reasons**: specific grounds for appointment termination

termination details are explicitly protected:

> "Any information, document, record, or statement furnished to the department or office [regarding terminations] is classified as confidential and exempt from public disclosure under Florida's public records law."
>
> — [Florida Statutes 626.511](https://www.flsenate.gov/Laws/Statutes/2023/626.511)

## how to verify an agent's appointments

### step 1: obtain identification

collect from the agent:
- full legal name
- national producer number (NPN)
- state license number
- name of carrier they claim to represent

### step 2: use state lookup tools

> "Illinois Department of Insurance works toward 'protecting consumers by providing assistance and information.'"
>
> — [Illinois Department of Insurance](https://idoi.illinois.gov/)

### step 3: verify with NIPR

access the Producer Database at www.nipr.com:

> "Access available at: www.nipr.com"
>
> — [NAIC Producer Licensing](https://content.naic.org/insurance-topics/producer-licensing)

### step 4: contact the carrier directly

carriers maintain their own appointment records and can confirm active status.

### step 5: contact your state insurance department

> "For licensing inquiries, applicants can call 512-676-6500 or email license@tdi.texas.gov."
>
> — [Texas Department of Insurance](https://www.tdi.texas.gov/agent/)

## state differences in appointment disclosure

### states with robust appointment visibility

florida provides detailed appointment lookups:
- appointer names
- appointment categories
- active/inactive status
- date ranges

### states with limited appointment visibility

some states show only:
- license status
- license number
- basic contact information

> "The publicly searchable information appears limited to basic license status and numbers."
>
> — [New York DFS Portal Analysis](https://www.dfs.ny.gov/apps_and_licensing/agents_and_brokers/home)

### variation factors

differences arise from:
- state public records laws
- regulatory infrastructure investments
- interpretation of consumer protection mandates

## appointment report requirements

insurers must report appointments to state regulators:

> "All initial and renewal appointments shall be submitted to the department on a monthly basis no later than 45 days after the date of appointment."
>
> — [Florida Statutes 626.371](https://www.flsenate.gov/Laws/Statutes/2023/626.371)

penalties exist for late reports:

> "If an appointing entity fails to notify the department within the required timeframe, they face a delinquent fee of $250 per appointee."
>
> — [Florida Statutes 626.371](https://www.flsenate.gov/Laws/Statutes/2023/626.371)

terminations must also be reported:

> "Parties terminating appointments must file a statement with the department detailing 'the reasons, if any, for and the facts relative to such termination.'"
>
> — [Florida Statutes 626.511](https://www.flsenate.gov/Laws/Statutes/2023/626.511)

## consumer protection rationale

transparency exists to protect consumers from unauthorized representation:

> "The fundamental reason for government regulation of insurance is to protect american consumers."
>
> — [NAIC White Paper on State Insurance Regulation](https://content.naic.org/sites/default/files/inline-files/topics_white_paper_hist_ins_reg.pdf)

appointment verification prevents fraud:

> "Insurance is more heavily regulated than other types of business because of the complexity of the insurance contracts, the lack of sufficient information for insurance consumers to adequately shop for prices and adequacy of coverage and because insurance contracts are generally contracts of adhesion."
>
> — [NAIC State Insurance Regulation White Paper](https://content.naic.org/sites/default/files/inline-files/topics_white_paper_hist_ins_reg.pdf)

market conduct depends on verifiable appointments:

> "Market conduct regulation is essential to guaranteeing that consumers are charged reasonable rates, have access to compliant products, and are safeguarded from unfair or unlawful practices by insurers."
>
> — [NAIC Market Conduct Regulation](https://content.naic.org/insurance-topics/market-conduct-regulation)

## summary

| aspect | public access |
|--------|--------------|
| license status | yes - all states |
| license number (state & NPN) | yes - all states |
| appointment status (active/inactive) | varies by state |
| appointer carrier name | varies by state |
| appointment category/line | varies by state |
| appointment dates | varies by state |
| termination reasons | no - confidential |
| commission rates | no - proprietary |
| production volumes | no - proprietary |
| contract terms | no - proprietary |

> "The public wants solvent insurers who are financially able to make good on the promises they have made and they want insurers to treat policyholders and claimants fairly."
>
> — [NAIC State Insurance Regulation White Paper](https://content.naic.org/sites/default/files/inline-files/topics_white_paper_hist_ins_reg.pdf)
