# define.contract-vs-quote

## .what

the difference between quotes and contracts, and what contract elements ensure insurance coverage applies to contractor work.

## .why

- a quote alone provides no legal protection
- insurance claims require proof the work was done under the insured business
- the right contract language creates the paper trail insurance companies need

---

## quote vs contract

| document | purpose | legally bound? | insurance relevance |
|----------|---------|----------------|---------------------|
| **quote** | estimate of cost | no | none — just an offer |
| **estimate** | same as quote | no | none |
| **proposal** | quote + scope details | no | none until signed |
| **contract** | agreement with terms | yes | establishes liability chain |
| **invoice** | request for payment | no | evidence of completed work |

a quote is an offer. a contract is a promise. only contracts create enforceable obligations.

---

## contract elements for insurance protection

### required elements

| element | what to include | why it matters |
|---------|-----------------|----------------|
| **parties** | your name + business legal name (not individual) | proves work is under insured entity |
| **license reference** | "License #12345" | ties work to licensed contractor |
| **insurance reference** | "Policy #ABC123 with [Insurer]" | documents coverage at contract time |
| **scope of work** | detailed description of work | defines what's covered if dispute |
| **worker identification** | names of workers, confirmed as employees | proves workers are covered |
| **indemnification** | contractor responsible for damages | establishes liability |
| **dates** | start date, completion date | proves when work occurred |
| **payment terms** | amount, schedule, payee (the business) | creates payment paper trail |

### recommended elements

| element | what to include | why it helps |
|---------|-----------------|--------------|
| **permits** | who pulls permits, permit numbers | proves work was permitted |
| **warranty** | workmanship guarantee period | extends protection |
| **dispute resolution** | how disputes are handled | avoids litigation |
| **change orders** | how scope changes are documented | prevents "that wasn't in the contract" |

## contract language examples

### party identification (critical)

```
bad:  "John Smith agrees to perform electrical work..."
good: "ABC Electric LLC, a [State] limited liability company,
       License #E-12345, agrees to perform electrical work..."
```

### worker identification (critical)

```
bad:  (no mention of who does the work)
good: "Work will be performed by John Smith, an employee of
       ABC Electric LLC, under the supervision of Robert Smith,
       License #E-12345."
```

### insurance reference (critical)

```
bad:  "Contractor maintains insurance."
good: "Contractor maintains general liability insurance with
       [Insurance Company], Policy #GL-789012, with minimum
       coverage of $1,000,000 per occurrence. Contractor has
       provided Certificate of Insurance that names Homeowner
       as Additional Insured for this project."
```

### indemnification clause

```
"Contractor shall indemnify and hold harmless Homeowner from
any claims, damages, or expenses that arise from Contractor's
work, and from claims by Contractor's employees or
subcontractors."
```

## minimum viable contract

for small jobs, at minimum include:

```
CONTRACTOR: [Business Legal Name]
LICENSE: [State] License #[Number]
INSURANCE: [Insurance Company] Policy #[Number]

SCOPE: [Detailed description of work]

WORKERS: Work performed by [Name(s)], employee(s) of [Business Name]

PRICE: $[Amount], payable to [Business Name]

DATE: Work to begin [date], complete by [date]

[Business Name] maintains liability insurance and agrees to be
responsible for any damages caused by this work.

_______________________     _______________________
Homeowner Signature         Contractor Signature
Date: ___________           Date: ___________
                            Title: ___________
```

## red flags in contracts

| red flag | risk |
|----------|------|
| individual name as contractor (not business) | work may not be under insured entity |
| no license number | may be unlicensed work |
| no insurance reference | no proof of coverage |
| "contractor or assigns" language | unknown party may do work |
| payment to individual, not business | breaks business connection |
| no scope detail | disputes about what was covered |
| verbal agreement, no written contract | no evidence for insurance claim |

---

## the paper trail for insurance claims

if a problem occurs, insurance requires:

1. **proof of contract** with insured business
2. **proof the worker** was employee/agent of that business
3. **proof the work** was within scope of contract
4. **proof of damages** caused by the work

your contract should make items 1-3 undeniable.

---

## summary

| document | creates legal protection? | sufficient for insurance? |
|----------|---------------------------|---------------------------|
| verbal agreement | weak | no |
| quote only | no | no |
| quote + handshake | no | no |
| signed contract (individual) | yes, but... | risky — may not be covered |
| signed contract (business + insurance ref) | yes | yes |

always contract with the **business entity**, reference the **license and insurance**, and name the **workers**.
