# define.three-layer-defense

## .what

three independent layers of protection that ensure insurance will pay out when an owner or employee performs contractor work at your property.

## .why

any single layer can be challenged. three layers together make denial nearly impossible:
- layer 1 fails → layer 2 and 3 still prove coverage
- layers reinforce each other
- creates redundant paper trail from independent sources

---

## the three layers

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  LAYER 1: INSURANCE VERIFICATION                       │
│  you verified directly with the insurance company      │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  LAYER 2: PERMIT                                       │
│  city record ties work to licensed business            │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  LAYER 3: WRITTEN CONFIRMATION                         │
│  contractor confirmed worker is owner/employee         │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

---

## layer 1: insurance verification

**what you did:**
- obtained COI with you as additional insured
- called insurance company directly (number you looked up, not from COI)
- insurance company confirmed policy is active and you are covered

**what it proves:**
- policy exists and is active
- your property is covered under this policy
- you can file a claim directly

**evidence:**
- COI document
- notes from phone verification (date, rep name, confirmation)

---

## layer 2: permit

**what you did:**
- contractor pulled permit from the city for this work

**what it proves:**
- work is officially filed under the licensed business name
- this is business work, not a side job
- city inspector will verify code compliance
- public record exists independent of contractor

**evidence:**
- permit number
- city records (searchable online in most jurisdictions)
- inspection sign-off

**why this layer is strong:**
- you didn't create this record — the city did
- contractor cannot deny they did this work
- insurance cannot claim "this wasn't business work"

---

## layer 3: written confirmation

**what you did:**
- obtained written statement (text/email) that worker is owner or employee

**what it proves:**
- worker is authorized to perform work under the business
- worker is covered under business insurance policy
- establishes employment/ownership relationship

**evidence:**
- saved text message or email
- screenshot with timestamp

**legal basis:**
- text messages are admissible under Federal Rules of Evidence Rule 901
- authentication requires: known sender, timestamp, context
- see: `ref.text-messages-as-evidence.[ref].md`

---

## how the layers reinforce each other

| insurance tries to deny because... | your defense |
|-----------------------------------|--------------|
| "policy wasn't active" | layer 1: insurance company confirmed it by phone |
| "this wasn't business work" | layer 2: city permit filed under business name |
| "worker wasn't covered" | layer 3: written confirmation of ownership/employment |
| "you're not covered" | layer 1: you're listed as additional insured |
| "we have no record of this job" | layer 2: permit is public record |

## assumption: owner or employee does the work

this defense assumes:
- the person who performs the work is the owner, co-owner, or employee
- NOT a subcontractor

if a subcontractor performs the work, additional verification is required:
- subcontractor's own insurance
- or confirmation that general contractor's policy covers subcontractor work

---

## summary

| layer | source | what it proves |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| 1 | insurance company | you can claim against the policy |
| 2 | city/government | business officially did this work |
| 3 | contractor | worker is authorized under the business |

three independent sources. three types of evidence. one solid defense.
