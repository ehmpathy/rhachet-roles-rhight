# howto.check-cg-22-94

## .what

how to verify whether a contractor's insurance policy contains endorsement CG 22 94, which removes coverage for subcontractor work.

## .why

- CG 22 94 strips out the subcontractor exception from CGL policies
- if present, damage caused by subcontractors is NOT covered
- insurers have been attaching this endorsement to reduce costs
- contractors often don't know their policy has it
- verification takes 2 minutes and prevents major coverage gaps

---

## what CG 22 94 does

| policy status | subcontractor damage |
|---------------|---------------------|
| standard CGL (no CG 22 94) | **covered** — subcontractor exception applies |
| CGL with CG 22 94 | **NOT covered** — exception removed |

the endorsement is titled: "Exclusion — Damage to Work Performed by Subcontractors on Your Behalf"

---

## how to check

### method 1: ask the insurance company directly

when you call to verify the COI, add this question:

> "does this policy contain endorsement CG 22 94, or any endorsement that excludes coverage for work performed by subcontractors?"

**what to listen for:**

| response | means |
|----------|-------|
| "no, that endorsement is not on this policy" | subcontractor work is covered |
| "yes, that endorsement is attached" | subcontractor work is NOT covered |
| "i need to check" / "i'm not sure" | ask them to verify and call back |
| "what's CG 22 94?" | explain: "it's an endorsement that excludes damage from subcontractor work" |

**document the answer:** note the rep's name, date, and their response.

---

### method 2: request the full policy

ask the contractor for a complete copy of their insurance policy, not just the COI.

> "can you provide a copy of the full policy, with all endorsements? i need to verify coverage for subcontractor work."

**what to look for:**

1. search for "CG 22 94" in the document
2. search for "subcontractor" in endorsements section
3. look for language: "This insurance does not apply to... damage to work performed by subcontractors"

**red flag:** if they refuse to provide the full policy, they may not know what's in it — or they do know and it's unfavorable.

---

### method 3: ask the contractor directly

> "does your insurance policy cover work performed by subcontractors? has your insurer added any endorsements that exclude subcontractor work?"

**what their answer tells you:**

| response | interpretation |
|----------|----------------|
| "yes, subcontractors are covered" | good — but verify with insurer |
| "i don't know" | they haven't checked — ask them to verify |
| "i do all the work myself" | good if true — get it in text |
| "my insurance covers all my work" | vague — may not know about CG 22 94 |

---

## if CG 22 94 is present

you have three options:

### option 1: require owner/employee only

get written confirmation that no subcontractors will be used:

> "since your policy excludes subcontractor work, can you confirm in text that you or your employees will perform all work on this job?"

### option 2: verify subcontractor's own insurance

if subcontractors will be used, require:
- subcontractor's own COI
- you added as additional insured on subcontractor's policy
- verify subcontractor's policy does NOT have CG 22 94

### option 3: decline the contractor

if you can't get adequate coverage confirmation, find a different contractor whose policy covers subcontractor work.

---

## the 30-second phone addition

add this to your standard COI verification call:

> "one more question: does this policy contain endorsement CG 22 94, which excludes coverage for subcontractor work?"

that's it. 30 seconds. major protection.

---

## red flags

| red flag | risk |
|----------|------|
| contractor doesn't know what CG 22 94 is | may not know their own coverage |
| insurer can't answer quickly | policy may be complex or nonstandard |
| contractor refuses to provide full policy | may conceal coverage gaps |
| "my insurance covers all my work" | vague — doesn't address subcontractors |
| recently renewed policy | insurers often attach CG 22 94 at renewal |
