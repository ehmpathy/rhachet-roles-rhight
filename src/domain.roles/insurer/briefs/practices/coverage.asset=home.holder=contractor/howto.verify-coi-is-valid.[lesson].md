# howto.verify-coi-is-valid

## .what

how to verify a Certificate of Insurance (COI) is legitimate and that you are actually listed as additional insured.

## .why

- COIs can be forged or altered
- contractors may send generic COIs without actually added you
- the phone number on a fake COI leads to a fake verification line
- verification takes 5 minutes and prevents major losses

---

## verification methods

### method 1: phone verification (most reliable)

**do not use the phone number on the COI** — look it up yourself.

1. **find the insurer's real phone number**
   - google "[insurance company name] claims" or "verify certificate"
   - or go to the insurer's official website and find their contact number
   - compare to the number on the COI — if different, be suspicious

2. **call and say:**
   > "i received a certificate of insurance and want to verify it. the policy number is [number], the insured is [business name], and i should be listed as additional insured for work at [your address]."

3. **ask them to confirm:**
   - is this policy currently active?
   - is [business name] the named insured?
   - am i listed as additional insured for [your address]?
   - what are the policy effective dates?

4. **document the call:**
   - date and time
   - representative name or ID
   - confirmation number if provided
   - answers to your questions

---

### method 2: online verification (limited availability)

not all insurers offer this, but check for:

#### QR code on the COI
- many electronic COIs have a QR code
- scan it — should link to insurer's verification portal
- verify the URL domain matches the insurance company

#### verification URL on the COI
- look for text like "verify at [url]" or "certificate verification"
- go to that URL directly (type it, don't click links in emailed PDFs)
- enter the certificate or policy number

#### ACORD eCOI portal
- some insurers use electronic ACORD certificates
- look for "eCOI" branding or ACORD logo
- these often have built-in verification links

#### insurer-specific portals

| insurer | verification method |
|---------|---------------------|
| state farm | call required |
| liberty mutual | libertymutual.com/claims |
| travelers | travelers.com |
| hartford | call required |
| nationwide | nationwide.com |
| progressive commercial | call required |

most require a phone call for additional insured verification.

---

### method 3: verify the license online (always do this)

even if you can't verify insurance online, you can verify the license:

1. google: `[your state] contractor license lookup`
2. enter the license number from the COI or contract
3. verify:
   - license holder name matches
   - license is active (not expired, suspended, revoked)
   - license type covers the work (electrical, general, etc.)
   - some states show insurance info on license lookup

#### state license lookup examples

| state | url |
|-------|-----|
| california | cslb.ca.gov/onlineservices/checklicenseII |
| texas | tdlr.texas.gov/verify |
| florida | myfloridalicense.com/wl11.asp |
| new york | labor.ny.gov/verify |

google `[your state] contractor license verification` for others.

## what to check on the COI

### required fields

| field | what to verify |
|-------|----------------|
| **certificate holder** | should be YOUR name and address |
| **insured** | should be the business name (not individual) |
| **policy number** | note this for phone verification |
| **effective date** | must be before today |
| **expiration date** | must be after the job completes |
| **additional insured** | should show YES or your name/address |
| **description of operations** | should reference your address or "all operations" |

### COI form check

- legitimate COIs are usually **ACORD 25** forms
- look for "ACORD 25" printed in the corner
- ACORD is the industry standard — other formats are suspicious

---

## red flags

| red flag | what it means |
|----------|---------------|
| phone number on COI doesn't match insurer's real number | likely fake |
| insurer has no record of the policy | fake COI |
| policy exists but you're not listed | generic COI, you weren't added |
| effective date is after today | not yet active |
| expiration date is before job completion | will lapse before work done |
| certificate holder is blank or wrong | may not be issued for your job |
| no ACORD form number | non-standard, possibly fabricated |
| COI is a low-quality image or scan | may be altered |
| insurance company doesn't exist | completely fake |

## quick verification checklist

before work begins:

- [ ] looked up insurer's phone number independently (not from COI)
- [ ] called and confirmed policy is active
- [ ] called and confirmed i am listed as additional insured
- [ ] asked if policy contains endorsement CG 22 94 (subcontractor exclusion)
- [ ] verified contractor license online
- [ ] COI shows my name and address as certificate holder
- [ ] effective dates cover the job period
- [ ] saved a copy of the COI with date received

**caution:** if policy has CG 22 94 and subcontractors perform the work, you may have no coverage. see `ref.subcontractor-coverage-case-law.[ref].md`

---

## if verification fails

| result | action |
|--------|--------|
| policy doesn't exist | do not proceed — fake COI |
| policy exists but you're not additional insured | ask contractor to actually add you, get new COI, re-verify |
| insurer can't find your address | endorsement may not be processed yet — wait and re-verify |
| contractor can't explain discrepancies | do not proceed |

---

## the 5-minute call template

> "hi, i'm verifying a certificate of insurance before contractor work at my home.
>
> policy number: [number]
> named insured: [business name]
> my address: [your address]
>
> can you confirm:
> 1. this policy is currently active?
> 2. i'm listed as additional insured for work at my address?
> 3. what are the policy dates?
> 4. does this policy contain endorsement CG 22 94?
>
> thank you."

save the rep's name and any confirmation number they give you.
