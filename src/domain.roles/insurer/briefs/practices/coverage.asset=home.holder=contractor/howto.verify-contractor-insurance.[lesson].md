# howto.verify-contractor-insurance

## .what

step-by-step process to verify contractor insurance coverage and create a paper trail that ensures claims will pay out.

## .why

- insurance companies deny claims when the worker was not legitimately covered
- family businesses often have employees work under the owner's license
- if the paper trail does not connect, you have no protection

---

## the paper trail

insurance pays out when you can prove:

```
worker → employed by → business → insured by → policy → covers → your property
```

each link must be documented. if any link is absent, insurance can deny.

---

## step-by-step process

### step 1: verify the license (before you schedule work)

**action:** look up the license number on your state's contractor license board website.

**verify:**
- [ ] license is active (not expired, suspended, or revoked)
- [ ] no unresolved complaints or disciplinary actions
- [ ] license type covers the work you need (electrical, plumber, general, etc.)

**ask the contractor:**
- "what is your license number?"
- "will you [or your father, if applicable] supervise this job?"

**document:** screenshot the license lookup result with date.

---

### step 2: get certificate of insurance (before you sign contract)

**action:** request a Certificate of Insurance (COI) from the contractor.

**request this exact language:**
> "please provide a certificate of insurance that lists my property address [your address] as the job location, and names me as Additional Insured for this project."

**verify the COI shows:**
- [ ] business name matches who you will contract with
- [ ] policy is current (check effective and expiration dates)
- [ ] your address is listed as job location
- [ ] you are listed as "Additional Insured"
- [ ] coverage amount is adequate ($1M+ general liability typical)

**if they refuse or cannot provide this:** do not proceed.

---

### step 3: call the insurance company (before you sign contract)

**action:** call the insurance company directly via the phone number on the COI (not a number the contractor gives you).

**say:**
> "i received a certificate of insurance for policy #[number]. i want to verify that work performed by [worker name] at [your address] is covered under this policy."

**ask:**
- "is this policy currently active?"
- "is [worker name] covered as an employee or agent?"
- "does the policy cover residential work at my address?"

**document:** note the date, time, representative name, and their answers.

---

### step 4: sign a proper contract (before work begins)

**action:** sign a written contract that establishes the business relationship.

**the contract must include:**
- [ ] business legal name as the contractor (not individual name)
- [ ] license number
- [ ] insurance policy number
- [ ] names of workers who will perform the work
- [ ] statement that workers are employees of the business
- [ ] your address as job site
- [ ] scope of work
- [ ] price and payment terms (payable to business, not individual)

**see example contract below.**

---

### step 5: get written confirmation (before work begins)

**action:** send an email to the contractor and save their reply.

**send:**
> "before we begin, please confirm in reply:
> 1. [worker name] is an employee of [business name]
> 2. work at [your address] is covered under [insurance company] policy #[number]
> 3. [license holder name] will supervise or be responsible for this work"

**save:** their written "yes" reply. this is evidence if you need to file a claim.

---

### step 6: pay the business (before, at completion, and after work)

**action:** make all payments to the business entity, not the individual worker.

**do:**
- [ ] write checks to "[Business Name LLC]"
- [ ] or pay via invoice that shows business name
- [ ] keep copies of all payments

**do not:**
- pay cash with no receipt
- pay to individual's personal account
- pay via Venmo/Zelle to personal account

## example contract

```
HOME IMPROVEMENT CONTRACT

DATE: [date]

HOMEOWNER:
[Your Name]
[Your Address]

CONTRACTOR:
[Business Legal Name, e.g., "ABC Electric LLC"]
[Business Address]
License #: [State License Number]
Insurance: [Insurance Company], Policy #[Policy Number]

WORKERS:
Work will be performed by [Worker Name(s)], employee(s) of
[Business Legal Name], under the supervision of [License Holder Name],
License #[Number].

SCOPE OF WORK:
[Detailed description of work to be performed]

PRICE:
Total: $[Amount]
Payment Schedule:
  - $[Amount] deposit upon contract execution
  - $[Amount] upon completion

All payments shall be made payable to [Business Legal Name].

INSURANCE:
Contractor maintains general liability insurance with [Insurance Company],
Policy #[Number], with coverage of $[Amount] per occurrence. Contractor
has provided Certificate of Insurance with Homeowner named as Additional
Insured for this project.

CONTRACTOR RESPONSIBILITY:
Contractor agrees to be responsible for all work performed under this
contract and for any damages caused by Contractor's work or employees.

SIGNATURES:

_______________________          _______________________
Homeowner                        Contractor
Date: ___________                Date: ___________
                                 Title: ___________
                                 (must be signed by business owner
                                  or authorized representative)
```

## checklist summary

before work begins, you should have:

| document | what it proves |
|----------|----------------|
| [ ] license lookup screenshot | license is valid |
| [ ] COI with you as additional insured | insurance covers your property |
| [ ] phone verification notes | insurance company confirmed coverage |
| [ ] signed contract with business | work is under insured entity |
| [ ] email confirmation | worker is employee, work is covered |
| [ ] payment to business | paper trail connects payment to business |

---

## red flags — do not proceed if:

| red flag | risk |
|----------|------|
| cannot provide COI with your address | no proof of coverage for your job |
| refuses additional insured request | conceals problems or lacks coverage |
| wants contract in individual name | work may not be under insured entity |
| wants payment to individual | breaks business connection |
| license holder "won't be involved at all" | may violate supervision requirements |
| verbal agreement only, resists written contract | no evidence for insurance claim |

---

## if a problem occurs

with the paper trail above, you can prove:
1. **contract** — work was done under [Business Name]
2. **COI** — [Business Name] was insured for work at your property
3. **email** — [Worker] was confirmed as employee of [Business Name]
4. **payment** — you paid [Business Name], not an individual

insurance cannot deny based on "worker wasn't covered" if you have this documentation.
