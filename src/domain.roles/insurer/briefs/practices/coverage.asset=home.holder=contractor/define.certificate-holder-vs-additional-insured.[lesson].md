# define.certificate-holder-vs-additional-insured

## .what

the critical distinction between two statuses on a Certificate of Insurance (COI) — one gives you a document, the other gives you rights.

## .why

- most homeowners receive COIs as certificate holders only
- contractors rarely explain they haven't actually added you to the policy
- without additional insured status, you cannot file a claim
- this is the single most important thing to verify before work begins

---

## the two statuses

| status | what you receive | can you file a claim? | legal relationship |
|--------|------------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| **certificate holder** | a copy of the certificate | **NO** | none — you're just informed the policy exists |
| **additional insured** | coverage under the policy | **YES** | you have contractual rights under the policy |

---

## certificate holder

**what it means:** you receive a copy of the COI as proof the contractor has insurance.

**what it gives you:** documentation only. no rights.

**analogy:** someone shows you their driver's license. you now know they have one. but if they crash into your car, you can't use their license to file a claim — you have no rights under it.

**on the COI:** your name appears in the "CERTIFICATE HOLDER" box at the bottom.

---

## additional insured

**what it means:** the contractor's insurance company has endorsed the policy to extend coverage to you for work performed at your property.

**what it gives you:**
- direct claim rights against the policy
- defense coverage if sued
- protection for liability related to the contractor's work

**analogy:** you're added to someone's car insurance as a covered driver. if there's an accident, you can file a claim directly because you have rights under the policy.

**on the COI:**
- the "ADDL INSD" column is checked (X) for the relevant coverage
- the "DESCRIPTION OF OPERATIONS" section names you + your address + "additional insured"
- may reference endorsement CG 20 10

---

## how to tell which you are

### step 1: check the ADDL INSD column

look for the column labeled "ADDL INSD" next to Commercial General Liability.

| box status | your status |
|------------|-------------|
| checked (X) | may be additional insured — verify in description |
| blank | you are NOT additional insured |

### step 2: read the description of operations

this section MUST contain language like:

> "Certificate holder is named as additional insured for [work type] at [your address]"

**if this section is blank or generic:** you are NOT additional insured, regardless of what the contractor claims.

### step 3: verify with the insurer

call the insurance company directly (look up their number, don't use the one on the COI):

> "I received a COI for policy [number]. can you confirm I am listed as additional insured for work at [your address]?"

---

## why contractors provide certificate-holder-only COIs

| reason | what they say | what it means |
|--------|---------------|---------------|
| default behavior | "here's my insurance" | generic COI, you weren't specifically added |
| cost avoidance | "this covers you" | endorsement costs extra, they didn't pay |
| ignorance | "you're on there" | they don't understand the distinction |
| deception | "you're protected" | they know the difference and hope you don't |

---

## how to request additional insured status

send this message to your contractor:

> "The COI you sent shows me as certificate holder only. I need to be listed as additional insured for work at [your address]. Can you ask your insurance agent to add me and send an updated certificate?"

**this is a standard, routine request.** legitimate contractors with proper insurance do this regularly. it typically takes 1-2 business days.

### if they resist

| their response | what it means | your response |
|----------------|---------------|---------------|
| "it's the same thing" | they don't understand or are misleading you | explain the difference; if they still refuse, reconsider |
| "my insurance doesn't do that" | unlikely — most policies allow it | ask them to check with their agent |
| "it costs extra" | true, but it's their cost to bear | their choice; find another contractor if they refuse |
| "i've never had to do that" | they've worked with uninformed homeowners | you're now informed; insist or find another |

---

## the risk of certificate-holder-only status

| scenario | certificate holder | additional insured |
|----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| contractor damages your property | you cannot claim | you can claim directly |
| contractor's employee is injured | you cannot claim defense | you're covered for defense |
| you're sued for incident | no protection | policy defends you |
| contractor denies responsibility | no recourse via insurance | direct claim rights |

---

## verification checklist

before work begins:

- [ ] ADDL INSD column is checked for CGL coverage
- [ ] description section names me + my address + "additional insured"
- [ ] called insurer directly to confirm my status
- [ ] policy dates cover the work period
- [ ] saved dated copy of COI
