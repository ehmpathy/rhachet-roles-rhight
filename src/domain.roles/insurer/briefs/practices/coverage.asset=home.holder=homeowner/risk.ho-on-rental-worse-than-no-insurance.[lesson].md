# risk.ho-on-rental-worse-than-no-insurance

## .what

keeping an HO-3 policy on a property you rent out is worse than having no insurance at all.

## .why

- you pay premiums for coverage that will not pay claims
- you carry false confidence that delays getting real coverage
- filing a claim exposes you to insurance fraud allegations
- denied claims damage your CLUE report for 7 years
- you are financially worse off than if you had saved the premiums

---

## the comparison

| factor | no insurance | HO-3 on rental | DP-3 (correct) |
|--------|--------------|----------------|----------------|
| **annual premium** | $0 | ~$1,500 wasted | ~$1,800 |
| **claim payout** | $0 | $0 (denied) | full coverage |
| **false confidence** | none — you know you're exposed | high — you think you're covered | none — you are covered |
| **fraud risk** | none | yes — filing claim = potential fraud | none |
| **CLUE damage** | none | 7-year record of denied claim | none |
| **after 5 years** | $0 spent, $0 recovered | $7,500 spent, $0 recovered | $9,000 spent, claims paid |

---

## why HO-3 on rental pays $0

### owner-occupancy requirement

HO-3 policies require you to live in the property as your primary residence. when you rent it out:

- you no longer occupy the property
- the policy's fundamental condition is violated
- all coverage becomes void — not just rental-related claims

### material misrepresentation

if you told the insurer you live there but you don't:

- this is material misrepresentation
- insurers can rescind the entire policy
- they can deny claims retroactively
- they can pursue fraud charges

### business activity exclusion

renting property is a business activity. HO policies exclude:

- injuries to tenants (business invitees)
- tenant-caused damage
- loss of rental income
- any claim arising from the rental relationship

---

## the hidden costs

### wasted premiums

you pay ~$1,500/year for a policy that will not pay claims. over 5 years, that's $7,500 with $0 return.

### false confidence

the worst cost is psychological. you believe you're protected, so you:

- delay getting proper DP-3 coverage
- take risks you wouldn't take if you knew you were uninsured
- may not set aside reserves for losses

### fraud exposure

when you file a claim on a violated policy:

- you sign a sworn proof of loss
- stating facts the insurer can verify
- if they discover rental activity, you face fraud investigation
- even if unintentional, you've made false statements under oath

### CLUE report damage

a denied claim stays on your CLUE report for 7 years:

- future insurers see the denial
- you pay higher premiums across all policies
- some insurers refuse to cover you
- the denial follows you, not the property

## case law confirmation

courts consistently uphold HO-3 claim denials on rental properties:

| case | outcome |
|------|---------|
| Terrell v. State Farm (2019) | 8 years undisclosed rental = denied |
| Jones v. Nationwide (2021) | fire claim denied, rental voided coverage |
| Greene v. Farmers (2018) | vacancy clause strictly enforced |
| Richer v. Travelers (2020) | Airbnb voided coverage for unrelated losses |

see `ref.ho3-dp3-claim-scenarios-matrix.[ref].md` for 18 additional cases.

---

## the core insight

> **no insurance = you know you're exposed**
>
> **HO-3 on rental = you're exposed but believe you're covered**

the second state is worse because:

1. you spend money on premiums
2. you take risks you wouldn't otherwise take
3. you face fraud exposure if you file a claim
4. you damage your insurance history when denied

---

## what to do instead

1. **if you're about to rent out your home**: get DP-3 before the first tenant moves in
2. **if you're already renting with HO-3**: get DP-3 immediately, cancel HO-3
3. **if you filed a claim on HO-3 while renting**: consult an attorney before the insurer investigates

see `howto.find-landlord-insurance.[lesson].md` for DP-3 shopping guidance.
