# define.homeowner-vs-landlord-insurance

## .what

when you need homeowner's insurance (HO), landlord insurance (DP), or both — based on how you use the property.

## .why

- you only insure a property once — not both HO and DP on the same unit
- wrong policy type = claim denial
- mixed-use scenarios (duplex, house hack, ADU) require careful coverage
- common confusion leads to coverage gaps

---

## the core rule

| how you use property | policy type |
|---------------------|-------------|
| you live there (primary residence) | HO (homeowners) |
| tenants live there (rental) | DP (landlord) |
| mixed (you + tenants in same structure) | depends on configuration |

**you never need both HO and DP on the same unit.** you need one or the other, based on occupancy.

---

## single property scenarios

### scenario 1: primary residence only

you live in the property full-time. no rental income.

| need | policy |
|------|--------|
| HO-3 | yes |
| DP-3 | no |

### scenario 2: rental property only

tenants live in the property. you live elsewhere.

| need | policy |
|------|--------|
| HO-3 | no |
| DP-3 | yes |

### scenario 3: vacation home (personal use)

you use it occasionally. no rental income.

| need | policy |
|------|--------|
| HO-3 | yes (secondary residence) |
| DP-3 | no |

### scenario 4: vacation rental (Airbnb/VRBO)

you rent it short-term when not in use.

| need | policy |
|------|--------|
| HO-3 | no |
| DP-3 | no |
| short-term rental policy | yes |

standard HO and DP policies typically exclude short-term rental. you need specialized coverage.

---

## multi-unit scenarios

### scenario 5: duplex — live in one, rent one

you occupy one unit. tenant occupies the other.

| unit | policy |
|------|--------|
| your unit | HO-3 (primary residence) |
| rental unit | DP-3 (landlord) |

**you need both policies** — one for each unit, because occupancy differs.

some insurers offer a single policy for owner-occupied multi-family (2-4 units). ask your agent.

### scenario 6: triplex/fourplex — live in one, rent others

same principle as duplex:

| units | policy |
|-------|--------|
| your unit | HO-3 |
| rental units | DP-3 (can be one policy for all rental units) |

### scenario 7: rent a room in your home

you live in the house. you rent one bedroom to a tenant.

| need | policy |
|------|--------|
| HO-3 with rental endorsement | yes |
| DP-3 | no |

add a "rental endorsement" or "roommate endorsement" to your HO policy. this extends coverage to include a tenant in your home.

### scenario 8: ADU (accessory dwelling unit)

you live in the main house. you built an ADU and rent it.

| structure | policy |
|-----------|--------|
| main house | HO-3 |
| ADU | DP-3 or HO endorsement |

options:
- add ADU to your HO policy via endorsement (if insurer allows)
- separate DP-3 for the ADU
- single multi-unit policy if insurer offers

---

## multiple property scenarios

### scenario 9: primary home + rental property

you live in house A. you rent out house B.

| property | policy |
|----------|--------|
| house A (primary) | HO-3 |
| house B (rental) | DP-3 |

two separate policies. may get discount for multiple policies with same carrier.

### scenario 10: primary home + vacation rental

you live in house A. you rent house B on Airbnb.

| property | policy |
|----------|--------|
| house A (primary) | HO-3 |
| house B (STR) | short-term rental policy |

---

## house hack insurance

"house hack" = live in one unit, rent others to offset mortgage.

| property type | your coverage |
|---------------|---------------|
| single-family + rented room | HO-3 with rental endorsement |
| duplex (live in one) | HO-3 + DP-3, or multi-family HO |
| triplex/fourplex (live in one) | HO-3 + DP-3 for rental units |

**key advantage:** because you live on-site, lenders treat it as primary residence. you may qualify for HO-3 rates on your unit (cheaper than DP-3).

---

## common mistakes

| mistake | consequence |
|---------|-------------|
| HO-3 on full rental property | claim denied |
| DP-3 on owner-occupied home | overpay, may have coverage gaps |
| no policy on ADU | no coverage for that structure |
| skip rental endorsement for roommate | liability gap |
| HO-3 on short-term rental | claim denied (business activity) |

---

## coverage comparison

| feature | HO-3 | DP-3 |
|---------|------|------|
| designed for | owner-occupied | rental/non-owner |
| personal property | your belongings covered | your belongings in unit (optional) |
| loss of use | pays your temporary lodging | pays lost rent |
| liability | you and family | landlord liability |
| tenant belongings | N/A | not covered (tenant needs HO-4) |

---

## umbrella insurance

if you own multiple properties or have tenants, consider umbrella insurance:

- provides additional liability beyond HO/DP limits
- typically $1M+ coverage
- costs ~$200-400/year
- covers all your properties under one policy

umbrella sits on top of your HO and DP policies. it does not replace them.

---

## decision tree

```
do you live in the property?
├── yes, full-time
│   └── do you have tenants in same structure?
│       ├── no → HO-3 only
│       ├── yes, roommate → HO-3 + rental endorsement
│       └── yes, separate unit (duplex/ADU) → HO-3 + DP-3
│
├── no, tenants live there
│   └── is it short-term rental?
│       ├── no (long-term) → DP-3 only
│       └── yes (Airbnb/VRBO) → STR policy
│
└── no, vacant/second home
    └── do you rent it ever?
        ├── no → HO-3 (secondary residence)
        └── yes → DP-3 or STR policy
```
