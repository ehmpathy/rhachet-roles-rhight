# evidence.anecdotal.rental-purchase-wrong-insurance

## .what

real-world cases and forum discussions documenting claim denials when rental properties were insured with homeowners policies (HO-3) instead of landlord policies (DP-3).

these cases illustrate the systemic silence described in `hazard.rental-purchase.systemic-silence.[lesson].md` — victims exist, but their stories appear in scattered forums rather than mainstream warnings.

## case 1: chicago six-unit fire ($1.6M denied)

**source:** [On-Site Adjusting](https://onsiteadjusting.com/the-hidden-risk-of-insurance-denials-primary-residence-vs-landlord-policies/)

| detail | value |
|--------|-------|
| property | 6-unit residential building, Chicago |
| situation | owner moved out, kept homeowners policy |
| loss | fire caused $1.6M+ in damages |
| outcome | claim denied — policy didn't match property status |
| reason | property was rental, policy required owner-occupancy |

**key quote:**
> "The building was no longer a primary residence, however, the type of insurance was not changed. A fire occurred, causing damages exceeding $1.6 million. The owner filed a fire insurance claim, but the insurance company denied compensation."

## pattern analysis

### why these stories are scattered

| factor | effect |
|--------|--------|
| embarrassment | victims don't want to admit they signed false application |
| fraud exposure | public speech risks investigation |
| settlement NDAs | partial settlements often require silence |
| no aggregation | individual stories don't reach critical mass |
| forum fragmentation | BiggerPockets, Reddit, Quora, Property118 — no single source |

### common thread

every case follows the same pattern:
1. owner has wrong policy type (HO-3 instead of DP-3)
2. owner doesn't know until claim time
3. insurer investigates, discovers misrepresentation
4. claim denied in full
5. owner bears entire loss
6. story appears in scattered forum, not mainstream news

### "found out the hard way"

this phrase appears repeatedly across sources:
- BiggerPockets author: "I found out the hard way"
- Property118: "turned a manageable incident into a financial setback"
- Quora: victims who ask "what chances do I have?"

**no one warns them beforehand. everyone learns at claim time.**

## why this evidence matters

these scattered stories confirm:

1. **the hazard is real** — not theoretical, people lose real money
2. **the silence is systemic** — stories appear in forums, not warnings
3. **the pattern is consistent** — HO-3 on rental → claim denied → owner loses
4. **no party warns beforehand** — victims all "found out the hard way"
