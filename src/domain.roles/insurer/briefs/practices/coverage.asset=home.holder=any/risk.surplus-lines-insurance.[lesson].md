# risk.surplus-lines-insurance

## .scope

**this risk applies to:**
- landlords who obtain coverage through surplus lines (non-admitted) carriers
- property owners dropped by standard insurers due to electrical, location, or claims history
- anyone who purchases coverage through Lloyd's of London syndicates or other E&S markets

**this risk does NOT apply to:**
- policies from admitted (licensed) carriers
- state FAIR plan policies (these have guaranty fund protection)

---

## .what

surplus lines insurance fills gaps when standard carriers refuse coverage — but these policies come with reduced consumer protections, higher denial rates, and no state guaranty fund if the carrier fails.

## .why

> **surplus lines carriers denied 67% of California residential claims in 2024. the national average is 37%. if your surplus lines carrier fails, you have no guaranty fund protection.**

---

## the protection gap

### what you lose with surplus lines

| protection | admitted carrier | surplus lines |
|------------|-----------------|---------------|
| state rate approval | ✅ rates reviewed | ❌ carrier sets any rate |
| policy form approval | ✅ standardized | ❌ carrier writes own terms |
| state guaranty fund | ✅ claims paid if carrier fails | ❌ no backup |
| regulatory oversight | ✅ full state supervision | ⚠️ minimal oversight |
| claims rules | ✅ strict timelines | ⚠️ looser requirements |
| bad faith remedies | ✅ full remedies | ⚠️ varies by state |

> "if a surplus lines company fails, or becomes insolvent, your claims could go unpaid. unlike licensed companies, surplus lines companies aren't members of a guaranty association."
>
> — [Texas Department of Insurance](https://www.tdi.texas.gov/pubs/consumer/cb015.html)

## claim denial rates

### surplus lines vs admitted carriers

| carrier type | denial rate | source |
|--------------|-------------|--------|
| **Sutton Specialty (surplus)** | **67%+** | NAIC 2024 |
| **7 of 11 CA surplus lines carriers** | **40%+** | NAIC California 2024 |
| national average (all carriers) | 37% | NAIC 2024 |
| national average (20 years ago) | 25% | NAIC historical |
| **Chubb (admitted)** | **5.8%** | KFF 2023 |
| Auto-Owners (admitted) | 15.9% | industry data |
| Nationwide (admitted) | 16.6% | industry data |
| Amica (admitted) | ~10% | NAIC complaint index 0.37 |

**key finding**: surplus lines denial rates (40-67%) are **2.5x to 4x higher** than admitted carriers (5.8-16.6%).

> "over 40% is cause for alarm."
>
> — Martin Weiss, founder of Weiss Ratings, via [SF Standard](https://sfstandard.com/2025/04/30/california-unregulated-insurance-boom-claims-denied/)

### why denials are higher

| factor | explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **no rate review** | carriers can underprice to win business, then deny claims to stay solvent |
| **loose policy language** | custom forms include broader exclusions |
| **"wear and tear" excuse** | common denial reason that's hard to challenge |
| **less regulatory pressure** | state insurance commissioners have limited authority |

## the insolvency risk

### no guaranty fund protection

if your surplus lines carrier fails, you are on your own.

| scenario | admitted carrier | surplus lines |
|----------|-----------------|---------------|
| carrier becomes insolvent | state guaranty fund pays claims (up to limits) | you file with receiver, may get pennies on dollar |
| carrier disappears | another carrier assumes policies | you scramble for new coverage |
| claim awaited at failure | guaranty fund processes | claim may never be paid |

> "policyholders who buy policies from insurance companies that do business on a 'surplus lines' basis might not have any guaranty fund protection whatsoever."
>
> — [Anderson Kill P.C.](https://andersonkill.com/newsletter/the-basics-of-insurance-company-insolvency-and-financial-distress/)

### why surplus lines carriers aren't covered

| factor | explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| no state license | don't contribute to guaranty fund |
| no assessments | other insurers don't subsidize their failures |
| trade-off | freedom from regulation = freedom from protection |

### what happens when they fail

| step | outcome |
|------|---------|
| 1. carrier fails | state insurance commissioner petitions for receivership |
| 2. receiver appointed | liquidator takes over assets |
| 3. claims filed | you submit claim to receiver |
| 4. distribution | assets distributed pro-rata after secured creditors |
| 5. shortfall | you absorb unrecovered losses |

### historical insolvencies

| carrier | year | impact |
|---------|------|--------|
| Executive Life Insurance Co. | 1991 | $4 billion withdrawn, 900,000+ policies affected |
| Mutual Benefit Life | 1991 | one of largest liquidations in US history |
| Real Legacy Co. (Puerto Rico) | 2017-2018 | Hurricane Irma/Maria losses, entered liquidation |
| Access Home Insurance (Louisiana) | 2021 | Hurricane Ida, placed in receivership |

## regulatory gaps

### what states can't do

| area | admitted carriers | surplus lines |
|------|-------------------|---------------|
| rate review | ✅ must approve rates | ❌ carrier sets rates freely |
| policy review | ✅ must approve forms | ❌ carrier writes own terms |
| market conduct exams | ✅ regular audits | ⚠️ limited |
| enforcement actions | ✅ full authority | ⚠️ limited to broker actions |

> "non-admitted carriers aren't licensed in the states where they operate. they can charge rates, modify terms, and employ unfair claim practices with minimal regulation."
>
> — [Public Citizen](https://www.citizen.org/article/the-rise-of-surplus-lines-insurance/)

### the broker is your only protection

surplus lines are regulated through brokers, not carriers:

| broker requirement | purpose |
|--------------------|---------|
| diligent search | must document that admitted carriers refused |
| financial check | must verify carrier meets state minimums |
| tax collection | must remit surplus lines tax to state |
| disclosure | must tell you policy is not state-guaranteed |

but brokers have conflicts:

| conflict | explanation |
|----------|-------------|
| commission incentive | paid to place policies, not protect you |
| carrier relationships | depend on carriers for future business |
| limited liability | you sue broker, not carrier, if problems arise |

## the "wear and tear" trap

### how it works

| step | what happens |
|------|-------------|
| 1 | you file legitimate claim (water damage, fire, etc.) |
| 2 | adjuster reviews (often remotely) |
| 3 | carrier cites "wear and tear" exclusion |
| 4 | claim denied |
| 5 | you appeal |
| 6 | appeal denied |
| 7 | your options: accept loss or sue |

### why it's effective for carriers

| factor | explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| subjective | reasonable people can disagree |
| document burden | you must prove damage was sudden, not gradual |
| cost to challenge | litigation costs $10,000-$50,000+ |
| policy language | surplus lines policies have broader "wear and tear" definitions |

### what counts as "wear and tear" (carrier view)

| claim type | carrier argument |
|------------|------------------|
| pipe burst | "pipe was old, gradual corrosion" |
| roof leak | "deferred maintenance" |
| electrical fire | "outdated system deteriorated" |
| appliance flood | "hose showed age-related wear" |

## how to protect yourself

### before you buy

| action | purpose |
|--------|---------|
| verify AM Best grade | A- or better indicates financial strength |
| research claim history | search "[carrier name] claim denied lawsuit" |
| read policy exclusions | understand what's NOT covered |
| document all property condition | photos, maintenance records, receipts |
| get multiple quotes | compare surplus lines carriers |

### carrier financial grades

| AM Best grade | interpretation | recommendation |
|---------------|----------------|----------------|
| A++, A+ | superior | acceptable |
| A, A- | excellent | acceptable |
| B++, B+ | good | proceed with caution |
| B or below | fair/marginal | avoid |
| NR | not rated | avoid |

### after purchase

| action | purpose |
|--------|---------|
| document property condition | timestamped photos annually |
| keep maintenance records | proves you didn't defer maintenance |
| respond to all carrier requests | don't give grounds for rescission |
| read renewal terms | carriers change exclusions |

### if you need to file a claim

| action | purpose |
|--------|---------|
| file immediately | don't miss deadlines |
| document all damage | photos, videos, receipts |
| get independent estimates | don't rely only on carrier's adjuster |
| keep all communications | emails, letters, call logs |
| know your deadlines | surplus lines have shorter windows in some states |

### if claim is denied

| option | cost | outcome |
|--------|------|---------|
| appeal internally | free | rarely succeeds |
| file with state | free | limited authority over surplus lines |
| hire public adjuster | 10-15% of recovery | may help negotiate |
| hire attorney | contingency or hourly | lawsuit for bad faith |

## when surplus lines make sense

despite the risks, surplus lines are appropriate when:

| scenario | why surplus lines |
|----------|-------------------|
| no admitted carrier will insure | it's surplus lines or no coverage |
| unique risk | standard policies don't fit |
| high-value property | need higher limits than admitted market offers |
| you can self-insure partially | use surplus lines for catastrophic coverage only |
| carrier has strong financials | AM Best A or better, decades of history |

### when to avoid

| scenario | why avoid |
|----------|-----------|
| carrier rated below A- | insolvency risk too high |
| carrier has litigation history | pattern of bad faith denials |
| you can't afford total loss | no guaranty fund backup |
| admitted carrier is available | always prefer admitted if possible |

## do surplus lines satisfy mortgage requirements?

### yes — but with caveats

most mortgage lenders accept surplus lines policies if they meet requirements:

| requirement | what lender checks |
|-------------|-------------------|
| coverage amount | replacement cost or loan balance |
| named insured | borrower and lender listed |
| mortgagee clause | lender named as loss payee |
| policy term | continuous coverage, no gaps |
| carrier financials | AM Best A- or better (some lenders) |

### when lenders may reject surplus lines

| situation | lender response |
|-----------|-----------------|
| carrier rated below A- | may require different carrier |
| policy excludes key perils | may require endorsement |
| coverage below loan balance | may require additional coverage |
| no mortgagee clause | will reject until added |

### Fannie Mae / Freddie Mac requirements

GSE-backed loans have specific requirements:

| requirement | standard |
|-------------|----------|
| carrier type | admits surplus lines if AM Best A- or better |
| coverage | replacement cost or unpaid principal |
| deductible | max 5% of face amount (wind/hail may be higher) |

> "surplus lines insurers are acceptable if they have an A.M. Best rating of A- or better"
>
> — Fannie Mae Servicing Guide

### bottom line

**surplus lines will satisfy your mortgage** — lender won't force-place if you have valid coverage. the risk is yours: if your surplus lines carrier denies a claim or goes insolvent, you still owe the mortgage.
