# hazard.underinsurance

## .what

coverage limits set below actual replacement cost, discovered only after total loss.

## .why

> **60-74% of US homes are underinsured. average gap: $139,000-$200,000. this is not edge case — it is baseline.**

---

## prevalence

| metric | value | source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| homes underinsured | 60-74% | CoreLogic, CU Boulder |
| severely underinsured (<75% coverage) | 36% | Philadelphia Fed |

## case studies

### marshall fire, colorado (2021)

| metric | value |
|--------|-------|
| claimants underinsured | 74% |
| severely underinsured | 36% |
| average shortfall | $139,000 |
| total gap (1,084 losses) | $179 million |

**Judi Kern** (Louisville City Council): had 50% of coverage needed to rebuild.

### camp fire, paradise (2018)

- 10,564 homes destroyed
- two-thirds faced finance shortfalls
- construction costs doubled post-fire
- insurer Merced Property & Casualty went bankrupt
- survivors who rebuilt saw premiums jump 1,039%

### tubbs fire, santa rosa (2017)

- 5,600+ structures burned
- two-thirds of victims underinsured (United Policyholders survey)
- some Fountaingrove shortfalls exceeded $1 million
- county population dropped 15,000 post-fire

**Ravi Nanjangud**: settlement fell short, couldn't get SBA loan, sold lot for $150K, moved to Boston.

### LA palisades/eaton fires (2025)

- 75% of total-loss claimants underinsured
- potentially costliest US natural disaster ever
- FAIR Plan $3M cap creates gaps
- one family: $1M rebuild cost, $750K policy limit

### hurricane helene, north carolina (2024)

- <1% of Buncombe County households had flood insurance
- 98% of flooded households won't receive insurance payouts
- entire town disappeared; most residents uninsured for flood

## case law

### Free v. Republic Insurance (1992)
**8 Cal. App. 4th 1726**

agent told insured coverage was adequate. after total loss, $141K limit insufficient (values had risen over 10 years). **court held agent liable** for misrepresentation.

### Volk v. State Farm

established: when insured specifically requests particular coverage, **insurer has duty to notify of deficient limits**.

### State Farm depreciation class actions

**Grout v. State Farm** (California): class of ~200,000 homeowners certified. alleged systematic depreciation of sales tax when ACV calculated.

**State Farm Missouri**: settled class action for non-material depreciation deductions.

## root causes

### who bears responsibility?

**default rule**: insured responsible for policy limits. agents have no affirmative duty to recommend increases.

**exceptions** (agent liable when):
1. agent misrepresents coverage scope
2. insured specifically requests particular coverage
3. agent holds themselves out as expert

### systemic causes

| cause | impact |
|-------|--------|
| elimination of guaranteed replacement cost | pre-1994: most policies included GRC. post-Northridge: shifted to capped ERC |
| faulty valuation tools | 360Value (Verisk) covers 2/3 of market; SF Chronicle proved it underestimates costs |
| construction cost inflation | 5.2% increase April 2024-2025; post-disaster surges double costs |
| inadequate inflation guard | 2-4% annual increases lag actual cost increases |
| consumer confusion | homeowners confuse market value with replacement cost |

### 360Value algorithm problems

internal 2019 Verisk report: prefill data for foundation shape **wrong 50% of time**.

California regulators found State Farm, CSAA, USAA used 360Value in ways that **directly caused steep underinsurance**.

## recovery impact

Marshall Fire study findings:

| outcome | underinsured | if fully insured (est.) |
|---------|--------------|------------------------|
| filed rebuild permit within 1 year | 18.8% | 25.4% |
| sold destroyed property | 9.7% | 5.4% |

underinsured homeowners **25% less likely** to rebuild.

---

## legislative response

### California SB 876 (2025-2026)

1. require minimum 50% extended replacement cost
2. require insurers to offer guaranteed replacement cost option
3. automatic 50% coverage increase if home lost in declared emergency
4. double penalties for fair claims violations in emergencies

---

## mitigation

| action | cost | benefit |
|--------|------|---------|
| extended replacement cost endorsement | $25-50/year | 25% buffer above limit |
| guaranteed replacement cost | varies (limited availability) | unlimited rebuild coverage |
| annual coverage review | free | catch gaps before loss |
| independent appraisal | $300-500 | verify replacement cost estimate |
