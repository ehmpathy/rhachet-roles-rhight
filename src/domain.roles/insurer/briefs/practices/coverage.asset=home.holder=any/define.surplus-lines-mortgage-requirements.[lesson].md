# define.surplus-lines-mortgage-requirements

## .what

whether surplus lines insurance satisfies mortgage lender requirements.

## .why

> **surplus lines CAN satisfy your mortgage — if the carrier meets rating thresholds. but the risk shifts to you: if the carrier denies your claim or fails, you still owe the mortgage.**

---

## the short answer

| question | answer |
|----------|--------|
| will surplus lines satisfy my mortgage? | **yes**, if carrier meets rating requirements |
| will lender force-place if I have surplus lines? | **no**, if policy meets requirements |
| am I protected if carrier denies claim? | **no** — you still owe the mortgage |
| am I protected if carrier goes insolvent? | **no** — no guaranty fund |

---

## GSE requirements (Fannie Mae / Freddie Mac)

### Fannie Mae

| guide section | requirement |
|---------------|-------------|
| B-2-01 | insurers must meet rating thresholds |
| B-6-01 | lender-placed insurance requirements |
| SVC-2012-04 | surplus lines allowed for lender-placed (180 days max) |

**rating thresholds** (carrier must meet ONE):

| agency | minimum grade |
|--------|---------------|
| AM Best | B or better |
| Demotech | A or better |
| KBRA | BBB or better |
| S&P Global | BBB or better |

> "an insurer only needs to meet one rating agency's requirement."
>
> — [Fannie Mae Multifamily Guide B-2-01](https://mfguide.fanniemae.com/node/4276)

### Freddie Mac

| guide section | requirement |
|---------------|-------------|
| Chapter 4703 | coverage must meet ISO equivalency |
| 4703.1 | annual confirmation of coverage |

Freddie Mac accepted Demotech Financial Stability Ratings in 1990, which opened a pathway for non-traditional carriers.

---

## FHA/HUD requirements

| source | requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| HUD Handbook 4000.1 | non-admitted carriers must have AM Best "A" or better |

**alternative ratings accepted:**

| agency | minimum grade |
|--------|---------------|
| AM Best | A or better |
| Demotech | A or better |
| S&P | BBB or better |

> "FHA explicitly allows private flood insurance from surplus lines carriers as an alternative to NFIP when it provides equivalent coverage."
>
> — [Federal Register 2022-25258](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2022/11/21/2022-25258/acceptance-of-private-flood-insurance-for-fha-insured-mortgages)

## what lenders check

| requirement | what lender verifies |
|-------------|---------------------|
| coverage amount | replacement cost or unpaid principal balance |
| mortgagee clause | lender named as loss payee with ISAOA language |
| named insured | borrower listed |
| perils covered | fire, wind, hail, vandalism (minimum) |
| deductible | max 5% of face amount (wind/hail may be higher) |
| carrier rating | meets GSE thresholds |
| continuous coverage | no gaps |

### mortgagee clause language

must include "its successors and/or assigns" (ISAOA):

```
[Lender Name], its successors and/or assigns
ISAOA/ATIMA
[Lender Address]
Loan #: [number]
```

> — [Fannie Mae B7-3-08](https://selling-guide.fanniemae.com/sel/b7-3-08/mortgagee-clause-named-insured-and-notice-cancellation-requirements)

---

## when lenders reject surplus lines

| reason | explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| carrier rating below threshold | doesn't meet AM Best B / Demotech A |
| no guaranty fund protection | lender concerned about insolvency risk |
| non-standard policy language | exclusions broader than ISO forms |
| mortgagee clause absent | lender not named as loss payee |

### practical solutions when rejected

| problem | solution |
|---------|----------|
| carrier rating too low | find surplus lines carrier with AM Best A- or better |
| lender wants admitted only | FAIR Plan + DIC wraparound |
| coverage gaps | add endorsement for required perils |

---

## the risk remains yours

the mortgage is protected. **you are not.**

| scenario | lender outcome | your outcome |
|----------|---------------|--------------|
| claim denied | mortgagee clause pays lender first | you may receive zero |
| carrier insolvent | lender files with receiver | you absorb shortfall |
| property destroyed | lender recovers loan balance | you lose equity |

> "if a surplus lines company fails, or becomes insolvent, your claims could go unpaid. unlike licensed companies, surplus lines companies aren't members of a guaranty association."
>
> — [Texas Department of Insurance](https://www.tdi.texas.gov/pubs/consumer/cb015.html)
