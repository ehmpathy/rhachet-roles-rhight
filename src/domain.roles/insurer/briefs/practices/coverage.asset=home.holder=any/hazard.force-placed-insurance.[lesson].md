# hazard.force-placed-insurance

## .what

force-placed insurance (also called "lender-placed insurance") is when your mortgage lender buys insurance on your behalf — without your consent — and bills you for it.

## .why

> **force-placed insurance costs 5-10x normal premiums and covers only the lender's interest, not yours. avoid it at all costs.**

---

## how it happens

when your hazard insurance:
- lapses or is cancelled
- is non-renewed (e.g., for K&T or obsolete electrical)
- is deemed insufficient by the lender

...your mortgage requires continuous coverage. the lender protects their collateral by force-placing a policy and billing you.

---

## the cost

| coverage type | cost multiple | notes |
|---------------|---------------|-------|
| standard admitted | 1x (baseline) | best case |
| surplus lines | 2-4x | E&S market |
| FAIR plan | 2-5x | state last resort |
| **force-placed** | **5-10x** | worst case |

> "Force-placed insurance typically costs five to ten times as much as a policy you could buy yourself."
>
> — [CFPB](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/ask-cfpb/what-is-force-placed-insurance-en-779/)

### why so expensive

| reason | explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| reverse competition | lender chooses insurer; borrower pays; no incentive for cheap |
| high-risk classification | coverage lapse signals risk |
| no discounts | no claims-free, bundling, or loyalty credits |
| limited market | few insurers offer force-placed coverage |

---

## what it covers vs. does NOT cover

### force-placed covers

- structure damage from fire, wind, hail, vandalism
- lender's loan balance (single-interest) OR full replacement (dual-interest)

### force-placed does NOT cover

| exclusion | impact |
|-----------|--------|
| personal belongings | no furniture, electronics, clothes |
| liability | no coverage if someone injured on property |
| medical payments | no guest injury coverage |
| loss of use | no temporary housing if displaced |
| your equity | single-interest policies only cover lender's balance |

**you pay 5-10x for coverage that protects the lender, not you.**

---

## the legal process

federal law (RESPA/Regulation X) requires lenders to follow this timeline:

| step | timing | requirement |
|------|--------|-------------|
| first notice | 45+ days before charge | reminder of insurance requirement, warning of force-placement |
| second notice | 30+ days after first, 15+ days before charge | same info plus estimated cost |
| force-placement | after both notices | lender places policy, bills borrower |
| cancellation on proof | within 15 days | lender must cancel and refund overlap |

---

## how to avoid force-placed insurance

### if you receive non-renewal notice

1. **act immediately** — you have 30-90 days typically
2. **contact independent broker** — access to multiple carriers
3. **apply to surplus lines** — 2-4x is better than 5-10x
4. **apply to FAIR plan** — 2-5x is better than 5-10x
5. **get any coverage** — anything is cheaper than force-placed

### the math

| scenario | annual premium (example) |
|----------|-------------------------|
| standard policy | $1,200 |
| surplus lines (3x) | $3,600 |
| FAIR plan (4x) | $4,800 |
| **force-placed (8x)** | **$9,600** |

surplus lines at 3x saves $6,000/year vs force-placed at 8x.

---

## how to remove force-placed insurance

### step-by-step

1. **obtain your own policy** — any coverage that meets lender requirements
2. **gather documentation**
   - declarations page
   - proof of premium payment
   - mortgagee clause with loan number
3. **submit to lender** — via designated insurance email/fax
4. **follow up** — call 48 hours after to confirm receipt
5. **verify cancellation** — lender must cancel within 15 days and refund

### lender must refund

if your new policy overlaps with force-placed, lender must refund the overlap period within 15 days.

---

## regulatory protection

### federal (CFPB/Regulation X)

| requirement | details |
|-------------|---------|
| two notices required | before initial placement |
| 15-day cancellation | upon proof of coverage |
| refund overlap | mandatory |
| bona fide charges | all charges must be "reasonable" |

### file a complaint

if your servicer violates force-placed rules:
- [consumerfinance.gov/complaint](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/complaint/)

### recent enforcement (July 2024)

CFPB issued $5 million penalty against a bank for force-placed insurance violations. they are actively monitoring servicers.

---

## strategic implications

### force-placed is the worst outcome

| outcome | cost | coverage |
|---------|------|----------|
| standard | 1x | full (structure + liability + belongings) |
| surplus lines | 2-4x | full |
| FAIR plan | 2-5x | basic (structure, limited perils) |
| **force-placed** | **5-10x** | **lender only** |

### avoid at all costs

even if you cannot find standard coverage:
1. surplus lines protects YOU and costs less
2. FAIR plan protects YOU and costs less
3. force-placed protects the LENDER and costs more

**any coverage you buy yourself is better than force-placed.**
