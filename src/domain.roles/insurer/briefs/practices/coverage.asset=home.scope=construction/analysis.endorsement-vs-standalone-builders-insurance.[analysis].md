# analysis.endorsement-vs-standalone-builders-insurance

## .what

compares economics of landlord policy endorsement vs standalone builder's insurance for renovation projects.

## .why

upfront savings from endorsements can be dwarfed by premium surcharges if a claim occurs. this analysis quantifies the tradeoff.

---

## tl;dr

| factor | endorsement | standalone |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| upfront cost | lower (~$200-500) | higher (~$700-1,000) |
| claim impact on landlord premium | YES — 10-40% surcharge for 3-7 years | NO — isolated CLUE report |
| 5-year cost if claim occurs | $2,000-4,500+ | $700-1,000 |
| recommendation | avoid for claim-prone work | **preferred** |

**verdict: standalone builder's insurance is better economics for any renovation with material claim risk.**

---

## the economics

### upfront cost comparison

| option | typical cost for $20k project |
|--------|-------------------------------|
| landlord policy endorsement | $200-500 |
| standalone builder's insurance | $700-1,000 (minimum premium) |
| **difference** | **$400-700 upfront savings** |

### claim impact analysis

#### endorsement claim scenario

when a claim occurs under an endorsement:

| impact | magnitude | duration |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| premium surcharge | 10-40% | 3-7 years |
| CLUE report entry | on landlord policy | 5-7 years |
| renewal risk | non-renewal possible | immediate |

**example calculation:**
- landlord premium: $1,500/year
- claim surcharge: 25%
- surcharge duration: 5 years
- **total surcharge: $1,875** ($375/year × 5 years)

net cost: $200 endorsement + $1,875 surcharge = **$2,075**

#### standalone claim scenario

when a claim occurs under standalone builder's insurance:

| impact | magnitude | duration |
|--------|-----------|----------|
| premium surcharge on landlord policy | NONE | N/A |
| CLUE report entry | separate from landlord policy | does not affect landlord insurance |
| renewal risk | NONE on landlord policy | N/A |

net cost: **$700-1,000** (policy premium only)

### break-even analysis

| scenario | endorsement cost | standalone cost | winner |
|----------|------------------|-----------------|--------|
| no claim | $200-500 | $700-1,000 | endorsement by $400-700 |
| claim occurs | $2,000-4,500+ | $700-1,000 | **standalone by $1,300-3,500** |

the upfront savings from endorsements are eliminated by a single claim with premium surcharges.

---

## why claims are isolated

### CLUE report mechanics

CLUE (Comprehensive Loss Underwriting Exchange) tracks claims by policy:

| policy type | CLUE entry | affects landlord premium? |
|-------------|------------|---------------------------|
| landlord policy endorsement | on landlord policy | YES |
| standalone builder's insurance | separate policy | NO |

standalone policies create separate claim histories. the landlord policy remains claim-free.

### carrier underwriting perspective

insurers view endorsement claims as landlord policy claims because:
- same policy number
- same loss history
- same renewal evaluation

standalone policies are evaluated independently — the landlord policy underwriter never sees the builder's insurance claim.

---

## additional endorsement risks

### non-renewal risk

| trigger | consequence |
|---------|-------------|
| large claim on endorsement | carrier may non-renew entire landlord policy |
| multiple small claims | pattern flags underwriting review |
| catastrophic loss | carrier exits relationship |

non-renewal forces shopping for new coverage, often at higher rates or with surplus lines carriers.

### coverage gaps

endorsements often have limitations standalone policies avoid:

| gap | endorsement | standalone |
|-----|-------------|------------|
| vacancy period | limited (30-60 days) | full project duration |
| materials in transit | often excluded | typically included |
| soft costs | rarely included | available as add-on |
| theft | limited or excluded | standard coverage |

---

## when endorsement might be acceptable

| scenario | endorsement ok? | reason |
|----------|-----------------|--------|
| cosmetic work (<$5k) | maybe | low claim probability |
| no fire/water exposure | maybe | limited loss scenarios |
| premium budget critical | if risk accepted | documented tradeoff |
| **electrical, plumb, HVAC, roof** | **NO** | high claim probability |

for any work with fire, water, or structural exposure, standalone is the correct choice.

---

## recommendation

**use standalone builder's insurance for all renovation projects with material claim risk.**

the $400-700 upfront savings from endorsements creates:
- $1,300-3,500 additional cost if claim occurs
- non-renewal risk on landlord policy
- coverage gaps for common construction losses

standalone builder's insurance isolates construction risk from landlord policy economics.
