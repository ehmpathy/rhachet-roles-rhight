# ref.builders-insurance.claim-denial-gotchas

## .what

common reasons builder's risk claims are denied and how to avoid them.

## .why

- builder's risk policies are NOT standardized (unlike CGL)
- each carrier writes their own form with different exclusions
- conditions (not just exclusions) cause most denials
- know gotchas to review policy before purchase

---

## tl;dr

| category | gotcha | outcome |
|----------|--------|---------|
| **admin error** | wrong address | claim denied |
| **admin error** | wrong % complete | claim denied or reduced |
| **admin error** | late/absent reports | partial payout only |
| **condition** | occupancy before completion | coverage ends |
| **condition** | vacancy >30-60 days | vandalism/water excluded |
| **condition** | >30% complete at inception | excluded without approval |
| **exclusion** | flood, earthquake | not covered (endorsement needed) |
| **exclusion** | faulty workmanship | not covered (GL covers) |
| **exclusion** | test damage | often excluded |

## are gotchas enumerated in the policy?

| type | in policy document? | notes |
|------|---------------------|-------|
| **exclusions** | **YES** | explicitly listed in "Exclusions" section |
| **conditions** | **YES** | listed in "Conditions" section — easy to miss |
| **subjectivities** | **SOMETIMES** | may be in binder letter, not policy |
| **report requirements** | **YES** | buried in conditions |
| **coverage triggers** | **YES** | require careful read |

**critical:** builder's risk policies are NOT standardized forms. each carrier writes their own. you MUST read each policy.

From [The Hartford](https://www.thehartford.com/insights/construction/builders-risk-insurance-coverages-exclusions):
> "Builder's risk insurance policies aren't written on a standard form like commercial general liability insurance, which is why it's important to look at the exclusions in your policy."

## category 1: administrative errors (your fault)

### wrong address

From [US Assure](https://usassure.com/resources/articles/7-tips-to-avoid-common-residential-builders-risk-claim-problems):
> "If the address cannot be identified, the claims department cannot pay your client."

**fix:** verify address exactly matches permit and deed before bind.

### wrong percentage complete

From US Assure:
> "incorrectly reported on their policy, so the claim was denied"

**fix:** report accurate % complete; update carrier as work proceeds.

### late or absent reports

From [US Assure lessons](https://usassure.com/resources/articles/builders-risk-insurance-lessons-learned-from-claim-setbacks):
> "Because just 66.7% of the inventory reports were submitted (four out of six), that percentage of the loss could be paid and then the deductible applied"

**result:** $4,990 paid instead of $11,230 claim.

**fix:** submit all required reports on schedule.

### pre-inception work

From US Assure:
> "Because the policy has an exclusion for structures that are over 30% complete (unless underwriting approval is granted), any work performed prior to the policy's inception date will not be covered"

**result:** only $15,000 covered instead of expected amounts.

**fix:** confirm construction status before policy inception; disclose to underwriter.

## category 2: coverage triggers (policy conditions)

### occupancy ends coverage

From US Assure:
> "coverage ends 60 days after the property is occupied in whole or in part, or put to its intended use"

**result:** claim completely denied.

**fix:** do NOT occupy or use property until project complete and coverage transitioned.

### vacancy clause

most policies suspend coverage for vandalism, glass breakage, and water damage if property vacant >30-60 consecutive days.

**fix:** verify vacancy clause duration; maintain activity at site.

### structure raised off foundation

From US Assure:
> "Raising entire structure off its foundation — an automatic disqualifier"

also: structure raised >15 feet = policy nonrenewed due to hazardous conditions.

**fix:** disclose foundation work to underwriter before bind.

### over 30% complete at inception

most policies exclude structures >30% complete when policy starts unless underwriter approves.

**fix:** get underwriter approval in writing if project already underway.

## category 3: standard exclusions (in every policy)

| exclusion | notes |
|-----------|-------|
| **flood** | requires separate endorsement |
| **earthquake** | requires separate endorsement |
| **wind (coastal)** | may require endorsement in beach zones |
| **faulty workmanship** | contractor GL covers this |
| **design defects** | excluded |
| **mechanical breakdown** | excluded |
| **employee theft** | outside theft covered; employee theft excluded |
| **wear and tear** | excluded |
| **settling/cracking/shrinkage** | normal settling excluded |
| **mold/pollution** | usually excluded unless endorsement |
| **test damage** | HVAC/electrical test often excluded |
| **ordinance/law upgrades** | code upgrade costs may require endorsement |

## category 4: soft costs (often misunderstood)

From [IA Magazine](https://www.iamagazine.com/2025/11/10/builders-risk-insurance-5-coverage-gaps-and-policy-misconceptions/):
> "One of the more challenging things to really understand is what's called soft costs...delay-related expenses like construction, loan interest, marketing and architectural engineering fees"

soft costs require **separate coverage** — not included by default.

| soft cost | covered by default? |
|-----------|---------------------|
| loan interest during delay | NO — endorsement needed |
| rental income loss | NO — endorsement needed |
| architectural fees | NO — endorsement needed |
| permit fees | NO — endorsement needed |
| real estate taxes | SOMETIMES — verify |

---

## category 5: test and commission

From IA Magazine:
> "Losses during testing of equipment, such as HVAC systems, are often excluded under a builders risk policy"

for electrical projects: test damage (arc flash, commission failures) may be excluded.

**fix:** ask for test/commission endorsement before bind.

## what to review before purchase

### documents to request

| document | what to review |
|----------|----------------|
| **sample policy form** | read exclusions section verbatim |
| **declarations page** | verify coverage amounts, deductibles |
| **endorsements list** | see what's added (flood, test, soft costs) |
| **conditions section** | report requirements, occupancy rules |
| **subjectivities** | conditions you must meet before coverage binds |

### questions to ask

| question | why it matters |
|----------|----------------|
| "can I see a sample policy?" | read exclusions before you buy |
| "what are the subjectivities?" | unmet conditions = denied claims |
| "is test coverage included?" | electrical test damage often excluded |
| "what's the occupancy clause?" | renovation may trigger early termination |
| "what reports are required?" | missed reports = reduced payouts |
| "is the extant structure covered?" | renovation requires this — often overlooked |
| "what's the vacancy clause duration?" | 30 vs 60 days matters |

## real claim denial examples (WebFetch verified)

### example 1: fire during renovation

**issue:** wrong policy type selected — extant structure not covered

**outcome:** "coverage was only triggered for 80% of the $200,000 renovation work and the $400,000 extant structure coverage was denied"

**lesson:** confirm extant structure coverage selected; note values separately.

### example 2: vandalism during renovation

**issue:** structure lifted >15 feet on pallets

**outcome:** policy nonrenewed; claim denied due to hazardous conditions

**lesson:** disclose all structural work to underwriter.

### example 3: hurricane during new construction

**issue:** construction 75% complete before policy effective date

**outcome:** only $15,000 covered due to >30% complete exclusion

**lesson:** bind policy BEFORE construction starts.

### example 4: theft during construction

**issue:** 4 of 6 required monthly reports submitted

**outcome:** only 66.7% of $11,230 claim paid = $4,990

**lesson:** submit ALL required reports on time.

### example 5: fire after tenant moved in

**issue:** tenant occupied before construction complete

**outcome:** claim completely denied — occupancy ended coverage

**lesson:** do NOT allow occupancy until coverage transitioned.
