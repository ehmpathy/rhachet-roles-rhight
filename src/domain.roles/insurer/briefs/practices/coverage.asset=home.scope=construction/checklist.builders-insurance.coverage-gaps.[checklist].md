# checklist.builders-insurance.coverage-gaps

## .what

hazards checklist to verify builder's risk coverage gaps before purchase.

## .why

- coverage gaps cause claim denials
- gaps are knowable before purchase via policy review
- checklist prevents oversight of common denial triggers

---

## pre-purchase checklist

### 1. extant structure coverage

| check | question | if NO |
|-------|----------|-------|
| [ ] | does policy include extant structure coverage? | renovation-only = structure fire uncovered |
| [ ] | is extant structure value listed on declarations? | must be explicit — not implied |
| [ ] | does renovation meet 20% threshold? | some carriers require reno ≥20% of structure value |
| [ ] | **is extant structure at RCV or ACV?** | ACV = 30-50% shortfall vs rebuild |

**critical valuation question:**

From [IA Magazine](https://www.iamagazine.com/2025/02/12/builders-risk-vs-homeowners-endorsements-for-luxury-home-renovation-2/):
> "Some reduce coverage for the extant structure to actual cash value and named perils, while some offer broad perils with replacement cost coverage for extant and new course of construction."

| carrier type | extant structure valuation |
|--------------|---------------------------|
| commodity (US Assure/Zurich, Steadily) | ACV (depreciated) |
| specialty (Chubb, AIG, some others) | RCV may be available — must ask |

**ACV formula:** replacement cost minus depreciation. older property = lower ACV = bigger gap.

**key question to ask:** "will replacement cost apply to the extant structure, or does coverage change to ACV?"

**Zurich/US Assure:** extant structure coverage available at ACV; requires renovation ≥20% of structure value.

From [US Assure](https://usassure.com/products/builders-risk-insurance/remodeling):
> "If extant structure coverage is needed, renovation limit must be at least 20% of the extant structure value"

---

### 2. faulty workmanship gap (GL COI)

| check | question | if NO |
|-------|----------|-------|
| [ ] | does contractor have GL insurance? | you have no recovery for faulty work |
| [ ] | is COI on file before work starts? | verbal promises unenforceable |
| [ ] | are you listed as additional insured? | COI alone = no direct claim right |
| [ ] | does GL cover subcontractors? | sub damage may be excluded |

**critical:** builder's risk excludes faulty workmanship — only contractor GL covers it.

From [The Hartford](https://www.thehartford.com/insights/construction/builders-risk-insurance-coverages-exclusions):
> "Damage from faulty work is covered by your contractor's general liability coverage, not your builder's risk policy."

---

### 3. flood and earthquake

| check | question | if NO |
|-------|----------|-------|
| [ ] | is flood endorsement included? | flood excluded by default |
| [ ] | is earthquake endorsement included? | earthquake excluded by default |
| [ ] | is property in flood zone? | FEMA zone A/V = high risk |

---

### 4. test and commission

| check | question | if NO |
|-------|----------|-------|
| [ ] | is test coverage endorsement included? | HVAC/electrical test damage excluded |
| [ ] | are commission failures covered? | arc flash, startup failures may be excluded |

**for electrical projects:** test endorsement critical.

---

### 5. soft costs

| check | question | if NO |
|-------|----------|-------|
| [ ] | is soft costs endorsement included? | delay costs not covered |
| [ ] | loan interest covered? | extra interest in delay = your cost |
| [ ] | rental income loss covered? | lost rent in delay = your cost |

---

### 6. vacancy clause

| check | question | if NO |
|-------|----------|-------|
| [ ] | what is vacancy threshold? (30/60 days) | shorter = more risk |
| [ ] | what perils are excluded in vacancy? | vandalism, water, glass typical |
| [ ] | can you maintain site activity? | activity resets clock |

---

### 7. occupancy clause

| check | question | if NO |
|-------|----------|-------|
| [ ] | when does occupancy end coverage? | typical: 60-90 days after occupancy |
| [ ] | is partial occupancy allowed? | "in whole or in part" = any occupancy |
| [ ] | is transition policy lined up? | must have HO/DP ready before move-in |

---

### 8. report requirements

| check | question | if NO |
|-------|----------|-------|
| [ ] | are progress reports required? | missed reports reduce payout proportionally |
| [ ] | what is report frequency? | monthly typical |
| [ ] | what format is required? | photo, written, percentage |

---

### 9. completion percentage

| check | question | if NO |
|-------|----------|-------|
| [ ] | is project <30% complete at inception? | >30% requires UW approval |
| [ ] | is UW approval documented? | verbal approval = no proof |

---

### 10. address and permit

| check | question | if NO |
|-------|----------|-------|
| [ ] | does policy address match permit? | mismatch = denial |
| [ ] | does policy address match deed? | mismatch = denial |

## documents to request before purchase

| document | what to review |
|----------|----------------|
| **sample policy form** | read exclusions section verbatim |
| **declarations page** | verify coverage amounts, deductibles, addresses |
| **endorsements list** | flood, test, soft costs included? |
| **conditions section** | report requirements, occupancy rules |
| **subjectivities** | conditions you must meet before coverage binds |

---

## questions to ask carrier

| question | why it matters |
|----------|----------------|
| "can I see a sample policy?" | read exclusions before you buy |
| "is extant structure covered?" | renovation-only = structure excluded |
| **"is extant structure at RCV or ACV?"** | **ACV = 30-50% shortfall vs rebuild** |
| **"can I endorse for RCV on extant structure?"** | **not all carriers offer this** |
| "what are the subjectivities?" | unmet conditions = denied claims |
| "is test coverage included?" | electrical test damage often excluded |
| "what's the occupancy clause?" | renovation may trigger early termination |
| "what reports are required?" | missed reports = reduced payouts |
| "what's the vacancy clause duration?" | 30 vs 60 days matters |

## coverage coordination strategies

### option A: builder's risk with RCV on extant structure

best if available — single policy covers all.

| check | question |
|-------|----------|
| [ ] | carrier offers RCV (not ACV) on extant structure? |
| [ ] | extant structure limit = full rebuild cost? |
| [ ] | renovation limit = full project cost? |

**carriers to ask:** Chubb, AIG, specialty/luxury markets.

### option B: DP endorsement + builder's risk (reno only)

use when builder's risk only offers ACV on extant structure.

| coverage need | solution | valuation |
|---------------|----------|-----------|
| extant structure | DP policy + renovation endorsement | RCV |
| renovation materials | builder's risk (reno only) | RCV |
| faulty workmanship | contractor GL | liability |

**DP endorsement requirements** (from [IRMI](https://www.irmi.com/articles/expert-commentary/renovations-can-cause-homeowners-insurance-coverage-gaps)):
- notify carrier BEFORE work starts
- contractor COI with you as additional insured
- $1M GL minimum per occurrence
- fire extinguishers, temp alarms, no smoking signs

**DP endorsement costs:**
- vacancy surcharge: ~25% of annual premium
- renovation surcharge: ~35% of annual premium
- removal of standard discounts

**warning:** without DP endorsement, many HO/DP policies exclude damage if renovation was in progress:
> "remodeling clients who opt to rely on their homeowners policies typically do not get coverage for the extant structure, as many property policies have exclusions for damage to the extant structure if renovations were in progress at the time of loss"

### option C: builder's risk with ACV on extant structure (gap accepted)

accept the 30-50% shortfall if:
- property is newer (less depreciation)
- you can self-insure the gap
- no RCV option available

**gap calculation:** RCV - ACV = your exposure.

## $20k electrical / $80k damage scenario

for this specific scenario, verify:

| check | specific question |
|-------|-------------------|
| [ ] | extant structure coverage included for full property value |
| [ ] | **extant structure at RCV (not ACV)?** — or use option B |
| [ ] | electrical test/commission endorsement included |
| [ ] | contractor GL COI on file with you as additional insured |
| [ ] | vacancy clause understood (renovation may pause) |
| [ ] | occupancy clause understood (don't move in early) |
| [ ] | address matches permit exactly |

**note on 20% threshold:** $20k reno on ~$200k property = 10%, below US Assure threshold. options:
- bundle additional scope to reach 20%
- use option B (DP endorsement + builder's risk reno-only)
- find carrier without 20% threshold
