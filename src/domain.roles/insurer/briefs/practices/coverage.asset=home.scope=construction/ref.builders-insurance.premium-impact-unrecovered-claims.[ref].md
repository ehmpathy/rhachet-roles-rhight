# ref.premium-impact-unrecovered-claims

## .what

when insurers pay claims but cannot recover via subrogation (owner-builder scenario), what happens to premiums and renewability?

## .why

- identifies the hidden cost of owner-builder work
- quantifies premium impact after fire claims
- explains non-renewal risk factors
- compares owner-builder vs contractor outcomes

---

## tl;dr

| factor | owner-builder | contractor |
|--------|---------------|------------|
| claim paid | YES | YES |
| subrogation | NO target | YES (contractor's insurance) |
| premium impact | 10-40% increase likely | often mitigated by recovery |
| non-renewal risk | elevated | lower |
| claim on record | 3-5 years | 3-5 years |

**bottom line**: owner-builders absorb premium consequences. contractors' insurance absorbs via subrogation.

## premium increases after fire claims

### general statistics

| metric | value | source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| single claim impact | 10-40% premium increase | Bankrate |
| fire claim specific | higher end of range | GEICO |
| claim on record duration | 3-5 years | industry standard |
| average 2024-2025 increase | 10.4% (baseline, no claim) | Insurance.com |

**source**: [Bankrate - Does Insurance Go Up After Claim](https://www.bankrate.com/insurance/homeowners-insurance/does-homeowners-insurance-go-up-after-a-claim/)

### fire claims vs other claims

| claim type | typical increase | why |
|------------|------------------|-----|
| fire | 20-40% | high severity |
| water | 15-25% | common, moderate severity |
| theft | 10-20% | lower severity |
| liability | varies | depends on payout |
| weather | 5-15% | often "no-fault" |

fire claims are among the most impactful because:
- high dollar amounts
- indicates property risk
- suggests potential for recurrence

## subrogation's effect on premiums

### how subrogation helps

| step | effect |
|------|--------|
| 1. claim paid | insurer out $X |
| 2. subrogation pursued | insurer recovers from responsible party |
| 3. recovery received | insurer loss reduced or eliminated |
| 4. premium calculation | loss ratio improves |

**key insight**: successful subrogation removes the claim from the insurer's loss calculation — or reduces its impact.

### when subrogation fails (owner-builder)

| step | effect |
|------|--------|
| 1. claim paid | insurer out $X |
| 2. no subrogation target | owner did the work |
| 3. no recovery possible | insurer absorbs full loss |
| 4. premium calculation | full loss counts |

**the difference**: unrecovered claims hurt loss ratios. loss ratios drive premium calculations.

**source**: [Openly - What is Subrogation](https://openly.com/the-open-door/articles/what-is-subrogation-openly)

## non-renewal risk factors

### why insurers non-renew

| factor | weight |
|--------|--------|
| multiple claims | HIGH |
| single large claim | MEDIUM-HIGH |
| claim type (fire) | HIGH |
| property condition | MEDIUM |
| geographic risk | MEDIUM |
| claims history (5-7 years) | HIGH |

**source**: [NerdWallet - Non-Renewal](https://www.nerdwallet.com/insurance/homeowners/learn/home-insurance-nonrenewal)

### owner-builder additional risk

| factor | effect |
|--------|--------|
| no subrogation recovery | insurer absorbed full loss |
| work done by non-professional | perceived higher future risk |
| permit record shows owner-builder | underwriter red flag |

insurers may view owner-builder electrical work as:
- higher recurrence risk
- indication of DIY property management
- signal for enhanced scrutiny

## the Maida family example

### what happened

| event | detail |
|-------|--------|
| cause | electrician's improper 220 line install |
| claim paid | YES — full (plus 16-month rental) |
| subrogation | likely pursued (contractor's insurance) |
| premium impact | NONE — "premiums didn't go up" |
| insurer | retained the policy |

**source**: [Policygenius - Maida Family Fire Claim](https://www.policygenius.com/homeowners-insurance/claims-diary-house-fire/)

### why premiums stayed flat

| factor | effect |
|--------|--------|
| contractor work | subrogation target existed |
| recovery likely | insurer loss reduced |
| no-fault claim | homeowner not responsible |
| good claims history | first claim in tenure |

**contrast**: if Maida family had done the electrical work themselves:
- no subrogation target
- insurer absorbs full loss
- premium increase likely
- possible non-renewal risk

## quantified impact scenarios

### scenario 1: owner-builder fire claim ($200K)

| year | premium (estimated) | notes |
|------|---------------------|-------|
| before claim | $2,000/yr | baseline |
| year 1 after | $2,600-2,800/yr | +30-40% |
| year 2-3 | $2,400-2,600/yr | gradual decrease |
| year 4-5 | $2,200-2,400/yr | claim older |
| year 6+ | $2,000-2,200/yr | claim drops off |

**5-year excess cost**: $2,000-4,000

### scenario 2: contractor fire claim ($200K) with subrogation

| year | premium (estimated) | notes |
|------|---------------------|-------|
| before claim | $2,000/yr | baseline |
| year 1 after | $2,000-2,200/yr | minimal or no increase |
| year 2+ | $2,000/yr | back to baseline |

**5-year excess cost**: $0-1,000

### the spread

| scenario | 5-year premium cost | difference |
|----------|---------------------|------------|
| owner-builder (no recovery) | $12,000-14,000 | +$2,000-4,000 |
| contractor (with recovery) | $10,000-11,000 | baseline |

## what owner-builders can do

### before claim

| action | benefit |
|--------|---------|
| document permit and inspection | evidence of compliance |
| maintain good claims history | buffer for one claim |
| consider umbrella policy | additional protection |
| shop rates proactively | know your options |

### after claim

| action | benefit |
|--------|---------|
| emphasize permit/inspection | work was code-compliant |
| get independent inspection | prove no future defects |
| request claims review | some insurers adjust |
| shop if non-renewed | FAIR plans as last resort |

---

## key takeaways

| question | answer |
|----------|--------|
| do premiums increase after fire claim? | YES — 10-40% typical |
| does subrogation help? | YES — recovery reduces or eliminates increase |
| owner-builder worse off? | YES — no subrogation target |
| how long does claim affect? | 3-5 years on record |
| can you be non-renewed? | YES — especially large claims |
| state protections? | vary widely |

**the real owner-builder cost**: not denial (ensuing loss covers fire), but premium increases without subrogation offset.
