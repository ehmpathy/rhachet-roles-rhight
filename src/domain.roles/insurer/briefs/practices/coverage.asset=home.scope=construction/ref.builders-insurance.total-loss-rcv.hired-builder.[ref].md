# ref.insurance-home-total-loss-replacement-cost-value.hired-builder

## .what

when a hired contractor's work causes total loss of your home, builder's risk policies often pay only **depreciated value (ACV)** for the prior structure — not full replacement cost (RCV). however, you have additional recovery avenues through contractor liability.

## .why this matters

- $3k electrical job by contractor causes fire → $400k house destroyed
- builder's risk pays **depreciated value** of prior structure (~$320k)
- you're $80k+ short of rebuild cost
- **but**: contractor's GL policy may cover the gap
- **but**: subrogation may recover your losses

---

## the gap

| component | typical valuation |
|-----------|-------------------|
| new work (the $3k electrical) | RCV (full cost) |
| prior structure (the $400k house) | **ACV (depreciated)** |

> "For renovation policies, the existing structure is typically insured at a depreciated value (actual cash value) while all renovations are insured as full replacement cost."
> — [Builder's Shield](https://www.buildersshield.com/28255/replacementcostvsactualcashvalue/)

---

## hired-builder advantage: multiple recovery paths

| path | what it covers | speed |
|------|----------------|-------|
| your builder's risk | property damage (ACV) | weeks |
| contractor's GL | their liability for damage | months |
| subrogation | your insurer recovers from contractor | background |

> "First-party claims generally offer a quicker resolution process, given the direct relationship between the claimant and their insurer."
> — [Insurance Claims Authority](https://insuranceclaimsauthority.com/first-party-vs-third-party-claims.html)

## case studies: contractor-caused fires

### case study: pittsfield, MA — mr. rooter torch fire (2022)

- **date**: january 2022
- **location**: 38 Beacon Avenue, Pittsfield, MA
- homeowner called Mr. Rooter to thaw frozen pipes
- employee used flame/torch/heat gun near sawed wood in basement
- fire smoldered for hours; discovered at 6:30 PM
- **damage**: just under $58,000
- **lawsuit**: Preferred Mutual (homeowner's insurer) sued Mr. Rooter for reimbursement
- **status**: jury trial demanded
- source: [Berkshire Eagle](https://www.berkshireeagle.com/news/central_berkshires/suit-claims-plumbing-company-used-flame-torch-to-thaw-pipe-sparking-fire/article_d73c4778-bdde-11ed-b6b4-63889011d428.html)

### case study: beach park, IL — torpedo heater fire (2025)

- **date**: january 22, 2025
- **residents**: Breon Pitts, Patricia Lear, Willie Pitts
- plumber placed torpedo heater (kerosene) beneath mobile home to thaw frozen pipes
- black smoke emerged from closet; structure engulfed
- fire burned for ~4 hours
- **damage**: 2 mobile homes total loss, multiple units damaged
- **action**: family filed lawsuit against contractor
- source: [CBS Chicago](https://www.cbsnews.com/chicago/news/beach-park-illinois-house-fire-plumber-heater/)

### case study: aiea, HI — propane torch fire (2025)

- **date**: december 4, 2025
- **location**: Pearl Regency high-rise, 12th floor
- plumber used propane torch on frozen shower valve nut
- ignited insulation material in wall space
- **damage**: $10,000 estimated
- **response**: 5 fire units, 22 personnel
- source: [Honolulu Star-Advertiser](https://www.staradvertiser.com/2025/12/04/breaking-news/aiea-apartment-fire-caused-by-plumber-with-propane-torch/)

### case study: tucson, AZ — apartment fire

- plumbers used propane torch to sweat copper pipe
- caught inside of wall on fire without notice
- two-alarm fire
- **damage**: $1 million, 8 apartments destroyed
- **displaced**: 5 families
- source: [Firehouse](https://www.firehouse.com/home/news/10509091/plumbers-torch-blamed-for-starting-fire-that-destroyed-8-arizona-apartments)

### case study: notre-dame cathedral (2019)

- fire occurred while renovation in progress
- **suspected cause**: cigarette or short circuit
- **contractor insurance**: AXA insured two contractors for ~€10 million each
- **cathedral status**: self-insured by French state
- **estimated repair**: €2+ billion
- **gap**: contractor liability "a drop in the ocean"
- source: [Insurance Journal](https://www.insurancejournal.com/news/international/2019/04/16/523998.htm)

## claim disputes with hired work

### case study: broken arrow, OK — state farm reversal (2025)

- **date of fire**: may 13, 2025
- **cause**: squirrel chewed through wire in garage
- **initial determination**: two State Farm inspectors said "total loss"
- **reversal**: weeks later, State Farm said NOT total loss, should be repaired
- **independent assessment**: two structural engineers confirmed total loss
- **protest**: homeowner placed yard sign: "All we have gotten is lies, denies and delays"
- **regulatory action**: Oklahoma AG launched State Farm investigation
- sources: [FOX23](https://www.fox23.com/news/fox23-investigates-ba-couple-puts-sign-in-yard-accusing-state-farm-of-delays-underpayment-after/article_1e7c981e-6067-461b-acab-51bcb714a6d7.html)

### case study: indiana GRQ factory — $112M bad faith verdict (2023)

- **location**: South Bend, Indiana
- **cause**: 100-year flood damaged electrical system
- **claim filed**: over $24 million
- **initial payment**: $2.68 million

## the subrogation process

when your builder's risk pays, your insurer may recover from the contractor:

> "Subrogation is a particularly important issue in Builders Risk insurance because the parties to a construction contract clearly intend for the Builders Risk insurance to be their sole remedy for covered losses."
> — [IRMI](https://www.irmi.com/articles/expert-commentary/builders-risk-naming-of-insureds-reloaded)

| step | what happens |
|------|--------------|
| 1 | your builder's risk pays you (ACV) |
| 2 | insurer "steps into your shoes" |
| 3 | insurer pursues contractor's GL policy |
| 4 | if successful, may recover your deductible too |

---

## contractor liability limits matter

| contractor GL limit | your $400k total loss | gap |
|---------------------|----------------------|-----|
| $1M | covered | none |
| $500k | covered | none |
| $100k | $100k max | $300k+ |
| uninsured | $0 | $400k |

**verify contractor's GL limits before work begins.**

## hired-builder protection checklist

| action | why |
|--------|-----|
| verify contractor's GL limits | ensures recovery path |
| get named as additional insured | direct claim rights |
| ask: "is prior structure at RCV or ACV?" | determines builder's risk payout |
| verify contractor pulls permits | licensed work = covered work |
| document all work | supports any claim |

---

## bottom line

hired-builder total loss has **better recovery prospects** than owner-builder:
- contractor's GL provides additional coverage layer
- subrogation recovers losses in background
- permits typically handled by licensed contractor

**but still verify: ask for RCV on prior structure, check contractor's GL limits.**
