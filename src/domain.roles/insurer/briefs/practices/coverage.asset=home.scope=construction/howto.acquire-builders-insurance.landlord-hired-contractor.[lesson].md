# howto.acquire-builders-risk.landlord-hired-contractor

## .what

how to acquire builder's risk insurance as a landlord on a rental property when hiring a licensed, permitted contractor for significant renovation work (e.g., $20k electrical upgrade).

## .why

- standard landlord insurance excludes losses when construction is active
- vacancy clauses (30-60 days) can void coverage while renovation proceeds
- licensed contractor's GL covers their negligence — not property damage to your building
- builder's risk bridges the gap between landlord policy and construction completion
- standalone policy isolates claim history from landlord policy (see [analysis.endorsement-vs-standalone-builders-insurance](./analysis.endorsement-vs-standalone-builders-insurance.[analysis].md))

## why licensed + permitted matters (but isn't enough)

### what licensed contractor provides

| coverage type | what it covers | protects you? |
|---------------|----------------|---------------|
| contractor's GL | their negligence causing injury/damage to others | YES (via additional insured) |
| contractor's workers comp | their employees' injuries | YES (you're not liable) |
| contractor's auto | vehicle accidents | YES |

### what licensed contractor does NOT provide

| gap | example |
|-----|---------|
| property damage to YOUR building | fire during renovation destroys structure |
| theft of materials | copper wire stolen from site |
| vandalism | windows broken while property vacant |
| weather damage | storm damages exposed electrical |

**builder's risk fills these gaps.**

---

## benefits of licensed + permitted contractor

| benefit | impact |
|---------|--------|
| lower premiums | insurers view licensed work as lower risk |
| no coverage denial | unpermitted work can void claims |
| inspection documentation | city inspection proves work was done correctly |
| subrogation path | if defect causes loss, clear liability chain |
| no vacancy exclusion issues | active permitted work = legitimate construction activity |

## step-by-step acquisition process

### 1. notify your landlord insurer first

before starting work:

| action | why |
|--------|-----|
| call your landlord insurance agent | they may offer renovation endorsement |
| ask about vacancy clause | confirm coverage during construction |
| document notification | email confirmation for records |

some landlord policies offer "dwelling under construction" endorsements — cheaper than standalone builder's risk.

### 2. gather required documents

| document | where to get it |
|----------|-----------------|
| property deed / proof of ownership | your records |
| electrical permit | city permit office (contractor pulls this) |
| contractor's license number | request from contractor |
| contractor's COI (certificate of insurance) | request from contractor |
| project scope / contract | your contractor agreement |
| project timeline | start date + expected completion |
| total project value | $20,000 for electrical work |

### 3. get quotes from builder's risk specialists

| provider | notes |
|----------|-------|
| Steadily | specializes in landlord insurance; online quotes |
| US Assure (Zurich) | established builder's risk administrator |
| BuildersRisk.net | online instant quotes for smaller projects |
| your current landlord insurer | may offer endorsement or standalone |
| local insurance agent | can shop multiple carriers |

### 4. verify coverage includes

| coverage element | why it matters |
|------------------|----------------|
| extant structure | renovations must cover whole building, not just new work |
| testing endorsement | covers damage from electrical testing/commissioning |
| theft and vandalism | higher risk during renovation |
| materials in transit | covers materials before installation |
| vacancy provision | explicitly permits vacancy during construction |

### 5. list contractor as additional insured

| action | benefit |
|--------|---------|
| add contractor to your builder's risk | they're protected if your policy pays a claim |
| get added to contractor's GL | their policy defends you in liability claims |

this creates mutual protection.

### 6. coordinate timing

| timing | action |
|--------|--------|
| before permit pulled | have builder's risk bound |
| during construction | policy active |
| at completion | transition back to landlord insurance |
| after final inspection | notify landlord insurer of completed upgrades |

## expected costs for $20k electrical project

| factor | estimate |
|--------|----------|
| standard rate | 1-5% of project value = $200-1,000 |
| minimum premium | most carriers have ~$700 minimum |
| **likely cost** | **$700-1,000** (minimum premium applies) |
| policy duration | 3-12 months (match to project timeline) |

### cost factors

| factor | impact on premium |
|--------|-------------------|
| licensed contractor | lower |
| permitted work | lower |
| rental property (vs owner-occupied) | slightly higher |
| electrical work specifically | neutral to slightly higher |
| property location | varies by region |
| construction type (frame vs masonry) | varies |

### standalone vs landlord policy endorsement

some landlord insurers offer "renovation endorsements" as an alternative to standalone builder's insurance. **standalone is recommended.**

| factor | endorsement | standalone |
|--------|-------------|------------|
| upfront cost | lower (~$200-500) | higher (~$700-1,000) |
| claim impact on landlord premium | YES — 10-40% surcharge for 3-7 years | NO — isolated CLUE report |
| 5-year cost if claim occurs | $2,000-4,500+ | $700-1,000 |
| non-renewal risk | YES | NO |

**why standalone wins:** the $400-700 upfront savings from endorsements is eliminated by a single claim. claims under endorsements appear on your landlord policy's CLUE report, which leads to premium surcharges and potential non-renewal. standalone policies create separate claim histories that don't affect landlord insurance economics.

> see [analysis.endorsement-vs-standalone-builders-insurance](./analysis.endorsement-vs-standalone-builders-insurance.[analysis].md) for detailed economics.

## what to require from your licensed contractor

### before work begins

| document | verify |
|----------|--------|
| license | active, appropriate trade (electrical) |
| COI - general liability | minimum $1M per occurrence |
| COI - workers compensation | state minimum (required in most states) |
| additional insured endorsement | YOU listed on their GL |
| permit | pulled and posted on site |

### why this matters even with builder's risk

| scenario | who responds |
|----------|--------------|
| fire damages your building | your builder's risk pays you |
| your builder's risk subrogates | contractor's GL reimburses your insurer |
| visitor injured on site | contractor's GL defends you (if additional insured) |
| contractor's worker injured | contractor's workers comp responds |

**licensed contractor + builder's risk = complete protection.**

## common mistakes to avoid

| mistake | consequence |
|---------|-------------|
| assuming landlord policy covers renovation | claim denied — exclusion for construction |
| relying only on contractor's insurance | their GL doesn't cover your property damage |
| not notifying landlord insurer | vacancy clause may void coverage |
| waiting until after work starts | may not be able to get coverage mid-project |
| not getting testing endorsement | electrical testing damage excluded |
| not verifying contractor's COI | no subrogation path if defect causes loss |

## post-renovation steps

| step | action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | obtain final inspection approval |
| 2 | get certificate of completion from contractor |
| 3 | notify landlord insurer of completed upgrades |
| 4 | request premium discount for updated electrical |
| 5 | cancel builder's risk (or let it expire) |
| 6 | retain all permits and inspection records |

### premium impact of electrical upgrade

| factor | typical impact |
|--------|----------------|
| updated electrical panel | 5-10% discount on landlord policy |
| updated wiring | additional discount possible |
| documentation required | permit + inspection certificate |

## rental property specific considerations

### vacancy during renovation

| concern | solution |
|---------|----------|
| tenant moved out for work | builder's risk covers vacant property |
| vacancy clause in landlord policy | notify insurer; get vacancy permit or builder's risk |
| extended timeline | ensure policy duration covers contingencies |

### tenant notification

| if | then |
|----|------|
| tenant in place during work | coordinate access, notify of work schedule |
| tenant temporarily relocated | document relocation, may need tenant's renter's insurance updated |
| property vacant | builder's risk is primary coverage |
