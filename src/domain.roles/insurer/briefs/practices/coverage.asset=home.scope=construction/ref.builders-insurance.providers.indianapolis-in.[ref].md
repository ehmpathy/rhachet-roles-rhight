## coverage considerations for electrical work

### what's covered

| peril | covered? |
|-------|----------|
| fire from electrical work | YES |
| theft of materials (copper wire) | YES |
| vandalism | YES |
| electrical storm damage | YES |
| explosion | YES |
| weather damage to exposed electrical | YES |

### what's NOT covered

| exclusion | notes |
|-----------|-------|
| faulty workmanship | contractor GL covers this |
| mechanical/electrical breakdown of tools | excluded |
| worker injuries | contractor workers comp covers this |
| general liability | separate policy |
| flood/earthquake | requires endorsement |

### electrical-specific risks

| risk | mitigation |
|------|------------|
| fire from arc flash | builder's risk covers property damage |
| theft of copper | builder's risk covers materials |
| exposed wire damage from weather | builder's risk covers if weather is covered peril |

## deep dive: brokers vs carriers

### what's the difference?

| entity | represents | compensation | duty |
|--------|------------|--------------|------|
| **carrier** | itself | premiums | pay valid claims |
| **agent** (captive) | one carrier | commission from carrier | sell that carrier's products |
| **agent** (independent) | multiple carriers | commission from carriers | shop among carriers |
| **broker** | YOU (the client) | fee or commission | find best coverage for you |

### broker fiduciary duty

| jurisdiction | broker duty | source |
|--------------|-------------|--------|
| **Indiana** | professional duty of care | common law |
| **California** | fiduciary duty to client | statutory |
| **New York** | fiduciary duty to client | Insurance Law § 2103 |
| **most states** | varies — check local law | state insurance code |

a broker who represents you owes YOU a duty of care — not the carrier.

### if a broker-placed carrier denies your claim

#### step 1: internal appeal (carrier)

| action | deadline | notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| request written denial | immediate | carrier must explain in writing |
| file internal appeal | 180 days from denial | submit additional documentation |
| escalate to supervisor | after first denial affirmed | ask for senior adjuster review |

most states require carriers to respond to appeals within 30-60 days.

#### step 2: external review

| action | when | notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| state insurance commissioner complaint | after internal appeal | free, but limited enforcement |
| external review (independent) | if state offers | third party reviews denial |
| mediation | if policy includes | faster than litigation |

Indiana Department of Insurance: [in.gov/idoi](https://www.in.gov/idoi/)

#### step 3: professional help

| option | cost | when to use |
|--------|------|-------------|
| **public adjuster** | 5-15% of settlement | complex claims, documentation help |
| **insurance attorney** | contingency (25-40%) or hourly | claim denial, bad faith, litigation |
| **appraisal clause** | policy-defined | dispute over amount, not coverage |

#### step 4: sue the broker (if negligent)

broker negligence grounds:

| claim | example |
|-------|---------|
| failure to procure coverage | broker didn't secure policy you requested |
| failure to advise | broker didn't explain exclusions |
| misrepresentation | broker said coverage included X when it didn't |
| failure to renew | broker let policy lapse without notice |

**Indiana statute of limitations:** 2 years for negligence claims.

to prevail against broker:
1. prove broker owed duty
2. prove broker breached duty
3. prove breach caused your loss
4. prove damages
