# ref.builders-insurance.carriers.rcv-on-extant

## .what

verified inventory of builder's risk carriers and their valuation methods for extant structures.

## .why

- most carriers default to ACV on extant structures
- RCV on extant is specialty coverage — must ask
- knowing which carriers to ask saves quote time

---

## tl;dr

**industry standard:** ACV on extant structures, RCV on new construction/renovation only.

**verified exception:** PURE Programs offers RCV on both extant and new construction.

From [Texas Independent Insurance](https://www.knowyouragent.com/builders-risk.html):
> "Most Builders Risk/Renovation Insurance policies provide actual cash value coverage on the [extant] structures and replacement cost coverage on the new construction or renovations."

From [IA Magazine](https://www.iamagazine.com/2025/02/12/builders-risk-vs-homeowners-endorsements-for-luxury-home-renovation-2/):
> "Some reduce coverage for the [extant] structure to actual cash value and named perils, while some offer broad perils with replacement cost coverage for [extant] and new course of construction."

---

## verified carriers

### PURE Programs — RCV confirmed

**valuation:** replacement cost on extant AND new construction

From [PURE Programs](https://www.pureprograms.com/solutions/es-insurance-homes-under-construction-or-renovation):
> "We will pay the reconstruction cost up to the coverage limit for both [extant] property and property under construction, with no reduction for depreciation"

| attribute | value |
|-----------|-------|
| market | E&S (surplus lines) |
| minimum | luxury projects, completed value up to $30M |
| eligibility | projects that don't qualify with admitted insurers |
| perils | broad form |

**note:** PURE Programs is the surplus lines arm of PURE Insurance. requires surplus lines broker.

---

### Zurich/US Assure — ACV confirmed

**valuation:** ACV on extant structures

From [US Assure](https://usassure.com/products/builders-risk-insurance/remodeling):
> "If [extant] structure coverage is needed, renovation limit must be at least 20% of the [extant] structure value"

| attribute | value |
|-----------|-------|
| market | admitted |
| threshold | reno ≥20% of extant structure value |
| extant valuation | ACV (depreciated) |
| reno valuation | RCV |

## carriers to ask (not verified)

these carriers may offer RCV on extant structures but require direct verification:

### USLI

From [BuildersRisk.net](https://buildersrisk.net/best-builders-risk-insurance-companies/):
> "Replacement cost valuation as opposed to actual cash value in the event of a loss"

**note:** statement does not clarify if RCV applies to extant structures specifically. must ask.

| attribute | value |
|-----------|-------|
| rating | A++ (AM Best) |
| parent | Berkshire Hathaway |
| market | admitted and non-admitted |

**question to ask:** "does your replacement cost valuation apply to the extant structure, or only to new construction/renovation materials?"

---

### Chubb

Chubb offers extended replacement cost on homeowners policies but builder's risk terms differ.

From [Chubb](https://www.chubb.com/us-en/agents-brokers/resources/home-valuation.html):
> "Extended replacement cost coverage pays for the full cost to rebuild your home, even if it exceeds your dwelling coverage limit."

**note:** this is homeowners coverage. Chubb's builder's risk valuation for extant structures not verified.

**questions to ask:**
1. "does your builder's risk policy cover extant structures at RCV?"
2. "can I maintain my Masterpiece homeowners coverage in construction with RCV intact?"

---

### Markel

From [Markel](https://www.markel.com/us/industries/construction/large-builders-risk):
> "Entertain renovation projects including the [extant] structure"

**note:** valuation method not specified. must ask.

---

### AIG / Lexington

AIG offers builder's risk through both admitted (AIG) and surplus (Lexington) markets.

**note:** valuation method for extant structures not verified. must ask.

## why RCV on extant is rare

| reason | explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **moral hazard** | RCV on depreciated structure could exceed its economic value |
| **adverse selection** | only owners of old structures would buy RCV |
| **price difficulty** | depreciation calculation is subjective |
| **market practice** | ACV became standard, RCV is exception |

---

## questions to ask each carrier

1. "does your builder's risk policy cover extant structures?"
2. "what valuation method — ACV or RCV — applies to extant structures?"
3. "can I endorse for RCV on the extant structure?"
4. "what is the premium difference for RCV vs ACV?"
5. "what is the minimum renovation threshold to include extant structure?"

## action plan for $20k electrical project

### if property qualifies for luxury market ($750k+ rebuild)

1. **call PURE Programs** (via surplus lines broker) — verified RCV on extant
2. **call Chubb** — ask about Masterpiece + builder's risk coordination
3. **call AIG Private Client** — ask about extant structure valuation

### if property is standard market

1. **call USLI** — ask if RCV applies to extant structures
2. **fallback: Option B** — DP policy + builder's risk (reno only)

### 20% threshold issue

$20k reno on ~$200k property = 10%, below US Assure threshold. options:
- bundle additional scope to reach 20%
- use Option B (DP endorsement + builder's risk reno-only)
- find carrier without 20% threshold
