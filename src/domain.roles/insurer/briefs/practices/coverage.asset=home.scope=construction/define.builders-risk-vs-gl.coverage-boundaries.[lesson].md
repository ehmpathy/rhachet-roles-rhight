# define.builders-risk-vs-gl.coverage-boundaries

## .what

defines the coverage boundaries between builder's risk insurance and general liability (GL) for residential construction/renovation. explains which policy steps in for which loss type.

## .why

homeowners and landlords often assume one policy covers everything. understanding boundaries prevents gaps that leave replacement cost unprotected.

---

## tl;dr

| loss type | builder's risk | general liability |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| fire destroys structure during construction | **YES** | no |
| theft of building materials | **YES** | no |
| vandalism to project | **YES** | no |
| weather damage (wind, hail, lightning) | **YES** | no |
| contractor damages neighbor's property | no | **YES** |
| visitor injured at job site | no | **YES** |
| contractor damages client's furniture | no | **YES** |
| defective workmanship causes later damage | no | **YES** (completed operations) |

**key insight**: builder's risk protects YOUR property during construction. GL protects against claims from OTHERS for injury or damage your project caused.

---

## builder's risk: what it covers

builder's risk is first-party property insurance — it protects the structure you're building or renovating.

### covered perils

| peril | covered? |
|-------|----------|
| fire | yes |
| lightning | yes |
| theft (external) | yes |
| vandalism | yes |
| wind/hail | yes |
| explosion | yes |
| collapse | yes |
| materials in transit | yes |
| materials in storage | yes |
| debris removal | yes |

source: [US Assure](https://usassure.com/resources/articles/what-does-builders-risk-insurance-cover), [BuildersRisk.net](https://buildersrisk.net/what-does-builders-risk-cover/)

### covered property

- building structure under construction
- materials and fixtures (installed or awaiting installation)
- temporary structures (scaffolding)
- property in transit to job site
- property at temporary storage

source: [Procore](https://www.procore.com/library/builders-risk-insurance), [Adjusters International](https://www.adjustersinternational.com/pubs/adjusting-today/builders-risk-insurance/index.html)

### what builder's risk does NOT cover

| exclusion | why |
|-----------|-----|
| bodily injury to anyone | that's GL territory |
| damage to third-party property | that's GL territory |
| employee theft | internal theft excluded |
| faulty workmanship | defects excluded |
| earthquake | requires separate endorsement |
| flood | requires separate endorsement |
| mechanical breakdown | not a covered peril |
| wear and tear | not sudden/accidental |
| contractor's tools/equipment | not part of structure |

source: [Bethany Insurance](https://bethanyins.com/what-is-not-covered-with-builders-risk-insurance/), [ContractorHub](https://blog.contractorhub.com/blog/1-what-is-not-covered-by-builders-risk-insurance)

## general liability: what it covers

GL is third-party liability insurance — it protects against claims from others for injury or damage your operations caused.

### covered claims

| claim type | covered? |
|------------|----------|
| visitor injured at job site | yes |
| contractor damages client's furniture | yes |
| contractor damages neighbor's fence | yes |
| advertising injury (libel, slander) | yes |
| completed operations (post-completion defects) | yes |
| legal defense costs | yes |

source: [Insureon](https://www.insureon.com/construction-contracting-business-insurance/general-liability), [Procore](https://www.procore.com/library/general-liability-insurance)

### what GL does NOT cover

| exclusion | why |
|-----------|-----|
| damage to the construction project itself | that's builder's risk territory |
| employee injuries | requires workers' comp |
| contractor's own tools/property | requires inland marine |
| professional errors (design defects) | requires professional liability |
| intentional damage | not accidental |
| pollution | requires pollution liability |

source: [Procore](https://www.procore.com/library/general-liability-insurance), [HUB International](https://www.hubinternational.com/blog/2021/09/builders-risk-insurance-vs-contractors-insurance/)

## the boundary: which steps in when

### scenario 1: fire destroys house during renovation

| question | answer |
|----------|--------|
| what's lost? | the structure and materials |
| whose property? | yours (the owner) |
| which policy? | **builder's risk** |
| GL involved? | no — no third-party claim |

source: [Engage Insurance](https://engage-ins.com/newsroom-blog/2026/4/15/the-latest-perspective-builders-risk-insurance-the-coverage-that-stands-between-your-project-and-a-total-loss)

### scenario 2: contractor's ladder falls on neighbor's car

| question | answer |
|----------|--------|
| what's lost? | neighbor's property |
| whose property? | third party |
| which policy? | **contractor's GL** |
| builder's risk involved? | no — not construction property |

source: [BBDG Law](https://www.bbdglaw.com/builders-risk-insurance-vs-general-liability-which-does-your-construction-business-need/)

### scenario 3: visitor trips on wiring, breaks leg

| question | answer |
|----------|--------|
| what's lost? | third party suffered bodily injury |
| whose fault? | construction operations |
| which policy? | **GL** |
| builder's risk involved? | no — not property damage to project |

source: [Insureon](https://www.insureon.com/construction-contracting-business-insurance/general-liability)

### scenario 4: theft of copper wiring from job site

| question | answer |
|----------|--------|
| what's lost? | building materials |
| whose property? | yours (the owner) |
| which policy? | **builder's risk** |
| GL involved? | no — no third-party claim |

source: [US Assure](https://usassure.com/resources/articles/why-a-homeowners-policy-is-not-enough-for-home-building-and-remodeling)

### scenario 5: faulty wiring causes fire 2 years after completion

| question | answer |
|----------|--------|
| what's lost? | structure (yours) + potentially neighbor's property |
| whose fault? | contractor's workmanship |
| which policy? | **contractor's GL** (completed operations) for third-party damage |
| your coverage? | your homeowner's/landlord policy (construction phase ended) |

source: [Meslee](https://www.meslee.com/blog/business-insurance/what-happens-after-the-build-why-completed-operations-coverage-is-a-contractors-most-overlooked-risk/)

## why homeowner's policy is NOT enough during construction

standard homeowner's policies have critical gaps during renovation:

| gap | homeowner's policy | builder's risk |
|-----|-------------------|----------------|
| theft of materials | excluded | covered |
| materials in transit | excluded | covered |
| vacant property (>30 days) | excluded or limited | covered |
| scaffolding/temporary structures | excluded | covered |
| debris removal limits | 5% of dwelling (~$500-1000) | $50k-100k+ |

source: [US Assure](https://usassure.com/resources/articles/why-a-homeowners-policy-is-not-enough-for-home-building-and-remodeling), [BuildersRisk.net](https://buildersrisk.net/homeowners-coverage-vs-builders-risk-insurance/), [Rough Notes](https://roughnotes.com/homeowners-and-builder-risk-2/)

**critical**: if you vacate during major renovation, homeowner's vacancy clause may void coverage entirely.

source: [US Assure](https://usassure.com/resources/articles/builders-risk-insurance-for-homeowners-when-is-it-needed)

## coverage timeline

| phase | active coverage |
|-------|-----------------|
| before construction | homeowner's/landlord policy |
| during construction | **builder's risk** (primary) + contractor's GL |
| construction complete | homeowner's/landlord policy resumes |
| defect discovered later | contractor's GL (completed operations) |

source: [DBI Agency](https://dbiagency.com/builders-risk-coverage-what-it-means-for-construction-projects/), [Program Business](https://programbusiness.com/news/managing-gap-builders-risk-insurance/)

---

## the gap: completed operations

builder's risk ends at project completion. GL's "completed operations" coverage handles defects discovered later.

| coverage | duration |
|----------|----------|
| builder's risk | construction phase only |
| GL completed operations | typically 10 years (varies by state statute of repose) |

**risk**: if contractor lacks completed operations coverage or goes out of business, you have no recourse.

source: [Meslee](https://www.meslee.com/blog/business-insurance/what-happens-after-the-build-why-completed-operations-coverage-is-a-contractors-most-overlooked-risk/), [Unica](https://www.unicainsurance.com/news/builders-risk-and-wrap-up-coverage-a-comprehensive-solution-for-construction-projects/)

---

## summary: who protects what

| insurance type | protects | against |
|----------------|----------|---------|
| **builder's risk** | your property during construction | fire, theft, weather, vandalism |
| **contractor's GL** | you and contractor | third-party injury/damage claims |
| **homeowner's/landlord** | your property after construction | fire, theft, liability (post-completion) |

for replacement cost of your home during construction: **builder's risk is the policy that matters**.
