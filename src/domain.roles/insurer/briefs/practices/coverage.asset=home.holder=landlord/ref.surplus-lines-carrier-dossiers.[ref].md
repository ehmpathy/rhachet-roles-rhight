# ref.surplus-lines-carrier-dossiers

## .scope

**this reference applies to:**
- landlords researching specific surplus lines carriers
- anyone comparing denial history across carriers
- property owners needing to evaluate carrier litigation patterns

**this reference does NOT apply to:**
- admitted carrier research (see standard carrier comparisons)
- FAIR plan evaluation (see separate FAIR plan briefs)

---

## .what

carrier-by-carrier dossiers on surplus lines insurers: denial rates, lawsuit history, bad faith verdicts, and specific case studies.

## .why

> **not all surplus lines carriers are equal. some have 67% denial rates; others have established bad faith verdicts against them. this reference provides the data to make informed choices.**

## Sutton Specialty Insurance Company

### overview

| attribute | detail |
|-----------|--------|
| type | surplus lines / non-admitted |
| market | residential property, high-risk homeowners |
| primary broker | Bamboo Ide8 Insurance Services |
| california denial rate (2024) | **67%+** |
| national average | 37% |

### denial rate data

per NAIC data compiled by Weiss Ratings:
- sutton specialty rejected more than two-thirds of residential claims in california in 2024
- denial rate nearly double the national average
- among the highest of 11 surplus lines carriers reviewed

> "sutton specialty rejected more than two-thirds of claims on residential homes in california in 2024."
>
> — [SF Standard](https://sfstandard.com/2025/04/30/california-unregulated-insurance-boom-claims-denied/)

### case study: michelle fuller

| fact | detail |
|------|--------|

| purchase | 2005 for $985,000 |
| claim | flood damage from tenant |
| denial reason | "wear and tear" |
| outcome | foreclosure |
| current status | on food stamps and welfare |

> "a property insurer — sutton specialty insurance co., which issued a policy through middleman bamboo ide8 — refused to pay for damage, and described it as uncovered wear and tear."
>
> — [SF Standard](https://sfstandard.com/2025/04/30/california-unregulated-insurance-boom-claims-denied/)

## Lloyd's of London (Certain Underwriters)

### overview

| attribute | detail |
|-----------|--------|
| type | surplus lines market / non-admitted syndicates |
| structure | syndicates of underwriters, not single company |
| market | complex risks, high-value property, exotic exposures |
| headquarters | London, UK |
| regulatory status | not licensed in any US state; operates via surplus lines |

### denial pattern

lloyd's position: "only a small number have been declined and only for legitimate reasons"

reality:
- federal class action alleged systematic denial of volcano claims
- lawsuit alleged fraudulent scheme in hawaii
- multiple breach of contract suits

### case study: hawaii kilauea volcano (2018)

| fact | detail |
|------|--------|
| event | kilauea eruption destroyed homes |
| carrier | lloyd's of london |
| claim status | lloyd's declared homes "total loss" |
| outcome | claims denied anyway |
| lawsuit | federal class action filed |

> "lloyd's made the highly unusual and illegal decision to deny payment to homeowners even though lloyd's themselves declared the home a 'total loss'."
>
> — [Insurance Business](https://www.insurancebusinessmag.com/us/news/breaking-news/lloyds-of-london-hit-with-federal-class-action-pointing-at-virtually-worthless-insurance-122020.aspx)

### case study: hawaii scheme

| allegation | detail |
|------------|--------|
| scheme | lloyd's and brokers directed homeowners away from state-established coverage |
| method | artificially inflated coverage limits to exceed state program thresholds |
| example | one policyholder's coverage set at $351,000 — exactly $1,000 over the $350,000 HPIA limit |
| effect | homeowners paid more for surplus lines with fewer protections |
| policy problems | lava exclusion rendered policies "essentially worthless" |

> "lloyd's of london and its affiliated insurance brokers 'steered' hawaiian homeowners throughout the state away from comprehensive home insurance coverage established by the state of hawaii."
>
> — [Program Business](https://programbusiness.com/news/lloyds-london-steered-hawaii-homeowners-artificially-inflating-coverage-kickback-scheme-federal/)

### investigation quality concerns

| concern | detail |
|---------|--------|
| remote adjusters | "the only investigation that took place was from behind a computer from the insurance adjuster's desk in little rock, arkansas" |
| no site visits | claims denied without physical inspection |

### recent litigation (2024-2025)

| case | court | year | issue |
|------|-------|------|-------|
| VT Halter Marine v. Certain Underwriters of Lloyd's | Mississippi Supreme Court | 2024 | breach of contract, $3.3M disputed |
| 3131 Veterans Blvd LLC v. Certain Underwriters | 2nd Circuit | 2025 | coverage dispute |

## common denial tactics

### the "wear and tear" playbook

| step | what happens |
|------|-------------|
| 1 | you file legitimate claim (water damage, fire, etc.) |
| 2 | adjuster reviews (often remotely, no site visit) |
| 3 | carrier cites "wear and tear" exclusion |
| 4 | claim denied regardless of actual cause |
| 5 | you appeal |
| 6 | appeal denied |
| 7 | your options: accept loss or sue (costs $10K-$50K+) |

### policy exclusion layers

carriers use multiple exclusions that overlap:

| exclusion type | example language |
|----------------|------------------|
| wear and tear | "damage from deterioration over time" |
| maintenance | "failure to maintain" |
| pre-condition | "condition extant at policy inception" |
| vacancy | "property vacant more than X days" |
| water damage sublimit | "$10,000 maximum for water events" |

### delay tactics

from james river case:
- 16 months to issue denial
- no settlement offer until 6 days after lawsuit filed
