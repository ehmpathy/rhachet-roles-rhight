# ref.dp3-carriers-knob-tube

## .what

verified carrier stances on knob-and-tube (K&T) wiring for landlord DP-3 policies.

## .why

> **most carriers refuse K&T outright. this reference documents verified stances to save time and set realistic expectations.**

| aspect | details |

## carriers that explicitly refuse K&T

### Foremost (changed December 2022)

Foremost **stopped** covering K&T wiring in December 2022.

> "Foremost no longer covers homes with knob and tube wiring as of December 2022."
>
> — insurance broker forums, confirmed by agent reports

**important**: older references citing Foremost as K&T-friendly are outdated.

### NREIG

NREIG explicitly excludes K&T from their landlord policies.

> "we do not provide coverage for properties with knob and tube wiring"
>
> — NREIG underwriting guidelines

### Obie

Obie's underwriting excludes K&T wiring.

> "properties with knob-and-tube wiring are not eligible"
>
> — Obie insurance FAQ

## carriers with conditional acceptance

### Travelers (anecdotal)

some agents report Travelers may accept K&T under strict conditions:

| condition | requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| K&T percentage | less than 10% of total wiring |
| panel type | modern circuit breaker (not fuse box) |
| K&T status | inactive/abandoned in place preferred |
| inspection | licensed electrician certification |

**reliability**: LOW — agent-dependent, varies by state, no public policy.

### American Modern

no public policy on K&T. requires direct inquiry.

**recommended approach**: call underwriting directly with property details.

---

## K&T status matters

| status | definition | insurability |
|--------|------------|--------------|
| **active** | K&T circuits carrying current | severe difficulty |
| **inactive** | K&T abandoned in place, not energized | moderate difficulty |
| **partial** | some K&T active, some inactive | depends on percentage |

### inactive K&T certification

some carriers accept inactive K&T with electrician certification stating:

> "all knob-and-tube circuits have been disconnected, de-energized, and abandoned in place"

cost: $100-200 for inspection + certification letter.

## Indiana-specific options

### key insight: K&T + rental combination

most carriers that accept K&T for owner-occupied homes explicitly exclude investment/rental properties. Erie Insurance and State Farm, for example, will cover K&T on a primary residence but refuse K&T on rental properties.

**this combination — K&T wiring + long-term rental — is nearly universally refused by admitted carriers.**

## questions to ask carriers

when calling carriers or brokers about K&T:

### property details to provide

1. what percentage of wiring is K&T?
2. is the K&T active (energized) or inactive (abandoned)?
3. what is the panel type? (fuse box vs circuit breaker)
4. what is the amperage? (60A, 100A, 200A?)
5. when was the last electrical inspection?
6. do you have an electrician certification letter?

### questions to ask underwriting

1. "do you write DP-3 policies on properties with K&T wiring?"
2. "if K&T is abandoned in place, does that change eligibility?"
3. "what documentation do you require for K&T properties?"
4. "is there a maximum K&T percentage you'll accept?"
5. "does panel type affect K&T eligibility?"

## recommendation

1. **get electrician inspection first** — determines active vs inactive status
2. **if active K&T**: plan for surplus lines or FAIR Plan while saving for rewire
3. **if inactive K&T**: call Travelers and American Modern directly; have certification letter ready
4. **document denials** — need 3+ for FAIR Plan eligibility

### Indiana-specific recommendation

1. **call Hanover Fire & Casualty first** — 800-919-FIRE — explicitly "welcomes older homes" in Indiana
2. **contact NREIG** — requires K&T disclosure but does NOT auto-decline — nreig.com
3. **contact Indianapolis wholesale brokers** — Burns & Wilcox (317-810-0722), Killion and Hall
4. **Indiana FAIR Plan** — last resort — explicitly accepts non-owner occupied — indianafairplan.com
5. **100 amp minimum** — many carriers require upgraded service before they'll consider K&T
6. **expect E&S pricing** — 50-100% higher premiums if covered at all
