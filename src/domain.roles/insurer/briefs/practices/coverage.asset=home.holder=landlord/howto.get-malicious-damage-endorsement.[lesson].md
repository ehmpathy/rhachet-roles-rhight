# howto.get-malicious-damage-endorsement

## .scope

**this guide applies to:**
- landlords with DP-3 policies who want coverage for intentional tenant damage
- landlords who discovered their standard policy excludes tenant vandalism

**this guide does NOT apply to:**
- commercial property owners (different policy types)
- landlords with specialty policies that already include this coverage

---

## .what

a malicious damage endorsement extends your DP-3 policy to cover intentional destruction by tenants — damage that standard vandalism coverage excludes.

## .why

> **standard DP-3 vandalism coverage only protects against third-party vandals. tenants have legal access, so their destruction is excluded.**

without this endorsement, you absorb the full cost of tenant destruction beyond the security deposit.

---

## endorsement names by insurer

insurers use different names for this coverage:

| name | what it covers |
|------|----------------|
| **tenant damage endorsement** | intentional damage by tenants |
| **malicious mischief endorsement** | broader intentional property damage |
| **tenant vandalism endorsement** | vandalism caused by tenants specifically |
| **vandalism coverage rider** | extended vandalism to include occupants |
| **intentional damage coverage** | deliberate destruction by any occupant |

when you call your insurer, ask about all these terms — they may have it under a name you don't expect.

## typical costs

| factor | typical range |
|--------|---------------|
| annual premium | $50-400/year additional |
| deductible | $500-1,000 per incident |
| coverage sublimit | $1,000-2,500 unless higher limit purchased |
| full coverage option | available at higher premium |

costs vary significantly by:
- property location
- property value
- tenant history
- claim history

## how to request from your current insurer

### step 1: gather your information

before you call, have ready:
- current policy declarations page
- property address and value
- current tenant status (occupied, vacant, between tenants)
- any recent claims history

### step 2: contact your agent

call your insurance agent directly. do not use the general customer service line — agents have more flexibility and knowledge.

### step 3: ask specific questions

**coverage questions:**
1. "does my current DP-3 policy cover intentional damage by tenants?"
2. "do you offer a tenant damage or malicious damage endorsement?"
3. "what's the difference between your standard vandalism coverage and tenant-caused damage coverage?"

**limit questions:**
4. "what coverage limits are available? are there sublimits?"
5. "what's the deductible for malicious damage claims?"

**requirement questions:**
6. "what tenant verification documentation do you require?"
7. "do I need regular property inspections?"
8. "what documentation do you require for a claim?"

### step 4: get a quote

request a formal quote with:
- annual premium increase
- coverage limits
- deductible
- any conditions or requirements

### step 5: review terms carefully

before you sign, understand:
- what triggers coverage (intentional vs accidental)
- what's excluded (wear and tear, prior damage)
- documentation requirements
- claim process

### option 1: shop competitors

contact these carriers for quotes with tenant damage coverage:

| insurer | notes |

| State Farm | traditional carrier, wide availability |
| Allstate | offers vandalism endorsements |
| Travelers | landlord policy options |
| Cincinnati Insurance | commercial and landlord policies |

### option 2: specialty landlord insurers

some carriers specialize in high-risk or landlord-specific coverage:
- NREIG (National Real Estate Insurance Group)
- Foremost
- American Modern

### option 3: independent insurance broker

an independent broker can:
- shop multiple carriers for you
- find coverage your current insurer doesn't offer
- compare terms and prices across carriers

### option 4: self-insure (reserve fund)

if endorsement is unavailable or too expensive:
- maintain a reserve fund equal to 3-6 months rent per property
- use security deposits as first line of defense
- pursue civil action against destructive tenants

## insurer requirements

to qualify for malicious damage coverage, insurers typically require:

### tenant verification

| requirement | what it means |
|-------------|---------------|
| background checks | credit, criminal, rental history |
| income verification | typically 3x monthly rent |
| employment verification | stable employment |
| reference checks | prior landlord references |
| documentation | keep records of all verification |

### property documentation

| requirement | what it means |
|-------------|---------------|
| move-in inspection | timestamped photos of every room |
| video walkthrough | proves baseline condition |
| written inventory | documents all fixtures, appliances |
| tenant signature | acknowledges condition |
| annual inspections | documented property checks |

### claim requirements

| requirement | what it means |
|-------------|---------------|
| police report | proves criminal intent |
| move-out documentation | timestamped photos of damage |
| repair estimates | establishes cost |
| proof of loss | formal claim documentation |

## questions to ask when you shop for coverage

### coverage scope

1. is malicious damage by tenants covered, or only third-party vandalism?
2. does coverage apply during tenancy AND post-eviction?
3. what about damage discovered after tenant moves out?

### limits and deductibles

4. what's the coverage limit for malicious damage?
5. are there sublimits regardless of my dwelling coverage?
6. what's the deductible — flat or percentage-based?

### requirements

7. what tenant verification documentation do you require?
8. do I need regular property inspections? how often?
9. what happens to my coverage if I skip tenant verification?

### claims

10. what documentation do you require for a claim?
11. how long does the typical claim take to process?
12. will tenant verification records help my claim succeed?

### price

13. what's the annual premium for this endorsement?
14. are there discounts for good records or inspections?
15. does my premium increase after a claim?

## vacancy considerations

if your property sits vacant between tenants:

| scenario | coverage status |
|----------|-----------------|
| property occupied by tenant | endorsement applies |
| property vacant < 30 days | typically still covered |
| property vacant 30-60 days | coverage may lapse |
| property vacant > 60 days | likely excluded; need vacancy endorsement |

ask your insurer about vacancy thresholds and whether you need a separate vacancy endorsement.

---

## red flags when you shop

avoid policies with:

| red flag | why it matters |
|----------|----------------|
| no tenant damage coverage available | leaves you exposed to most common risk |
| sublimits under $2,500 | won't cover significant damage |
| deductibles over $2,500 | effectively self-insure for moderate claims |
| excessive documentation requirements | impractical to maintain |
| no clear claim process | may face denials |

---

## after you get the endorsement

### maintain documentation

| when | what to document |
|------|------------------|
| before tenant moves in | full photo/video inspection |
| at lease signature | tenant acknowledges condition |
| during tenancy | periodic inspections (quarterly recommended) |
| at move-out | full photo/video inspection |
| after damage discovered | police report, repair estimates |

### report claims promptly

most policies require:
- immediate notification of damage
- police report filed within 24-72 hours
- formal claim within 30-60 days
