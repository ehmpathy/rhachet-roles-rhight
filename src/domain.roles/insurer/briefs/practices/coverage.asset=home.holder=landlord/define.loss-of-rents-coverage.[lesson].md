# define.loss-of-rents-coverage

## .scope

**this coverage applies to:**
- landlords with DP-3 (or similar dwelling) policies
- rental properties that become uninhabitable due to covered perils

**this coverage does NOT apply to:**
- vacancy due to tenant disputes or non-renewal
- properties with homeowners (HO-3) policies on rental use (coverage void)
- losses from non-covered perils (flood, earthquake, neglect)

---

## .what

loss-of-rents coverage (also called "fair rental value" or "rental income coverage") pays landlords monthly rent equivalents when a covered peril makes the property uninhabitable.

## .why

> **covered loss + uninhabitable property = insurer pays lost rent until repairs complete**

landlords depend on rental income. when fire, storm, or other covered events force tenants out, this coverage bridges the income gap during repairs.

---

## how it works

| aspect | details |
|--------|---------|
| **trigger** | covered peril makes property uninhabitable |
| **payout basis** | fair market rental value |
| **duration** | until repairs complete OR policy limit reached |
| **typical limit** | 20% of dwelling coverage |
| **max duration** | 12 months (standard); extended coverage available |
| **waiting period** | 48-72 hours typical |

### payout calculation

insurers pay based on **fair market rental value**, not necessarily actual rent:

- surveyed against comparable properties in area
- considers square footage, amenities, location
- may be higher or lower than actual rent charged

**example:**
- property rents for $2,000/month
- fire damages kitchen — uninhabitable
- repairs take 4 months
- insurer pays ~$8,000 (4 × $2,000)

### coverage limits

| dwelling coverage | typical loss-of-rents limit (20%) |
|-------------------|-----------------------------------|
| $200,000 | $40,000 |
| $300,000 | $60,000 |
| $400,000 | $80,000 |
| $500,000 | $100,000 |

at $2,000/month rent, a $60,000 limit covers ~30 months — but the 12-month cap usually applies first.

---

## covered perils

loss-of-rents coverage pays when these events make the property uninhabitable:

| peril | example scenario |
|-------|------------------|
| **fire** | kitchen fire renders unit unsafe |
| **windstorm** | hurricane damages roof, water intrusion |
| **hail** | roof damage causes leaks throughout |
| **vandalism** | break-in causes extensive damage |
| **burst pipes** | frozen pipe floods multiple rooms |
| **falling objects** | tree falls through roof |
| **smoke damage** | neighboring fire smoke permeates unit |
| **vehicle collision** | car crashes into structure |

### the key requirement

the damage must **force tenant evacuation** or **prevent new tenants from occupying**. minor damage that allows continued occupancy does not trigger coverage.

---

## exclusions

loss-of-rents does NOT cover income lost due to:

| exclusion | why excluded |
|-----------|--------------|
| **flood** | requires separate flood insurance (NFIP or private) |
| **earthquake** | requires separate endorsement |
| **earth movement** | landslide, sinkhole, etc. |
| **neglect** | deferred maintenance, failure to repair |
| **wear and tear** | gradual deterioration |
| **mold** | unless caused by covered peril |
| **tenant disputes** | eviction, non-payment, lease termination |
| **vacancy by choice** | property not actively rented |
| **code violations** | government-ordered closure for violations |
| **intentional acts** | arson by owner, fraud |

### the flood gap

flood is the most common surprise exclusion. a burst pipe (covered) is different from external flooding (not covered). landlords in flood zones need separate flood insurance to protect rental income.

## claim scenarios

### scenario 1: fire damage (covered)

| factor | value |
|--------|-------|
| monthly rent | $1,800 |
| cause | electrical fire in kitchen |
| repair duration | 3 months |
| waiting period | 72 hours |
| payout | ~$5,400 (3 × $1,800) |

### scenario 2: burst pipe in winter (covered)

| factor | value |
|--------|-------|
| monthly rent | $2,200 |
| cause | frozen pipe bursts, floods unit |
| repair duration | 6 weeks |
| waiting period | 48 hours |
| payout | ~$3,300 (1.5 × $2,200) |

### scenario 3: hurricane roof damage (covered)

| factor | value |
|--------|-------|
| monthly rent | $1,500 |
| cause | hurricane removes portion of roof |
| repair duration | 8 months (contractor delays) |
| waiting period | 72 hours |
| payout | ~$12,000 (8 × $1,500) |

### scenario 4: flooding (NOT covered without flood policy)

| factor | value |
|--------|-------|
| monthly rent | $2,000 |
| cause | river overflows, floods ground floor |
| repair duration | 4 months |
| payout | $0 (flood excluded from standard DP-3) |

**fix:** purchase separate flood insurance with loss-of-rents endorsement.

### scenario 5: tenant-caused damage (depends)

| factor | value |
|--------|-------|
| monthly rent | $1,600 |
| cause | tenant causes fire while cooking |
| repair duration | 2 months |
| payout | ~$3,200 (tenant negligence is covered peril for landlord) |

tenant negligence causing fire is typically covered — the landlord's policy responds to the peril (fire), not the cause (tenant behavior).

## documentation requirements

### to file a claim, gather:

| document | purpose |
|----------|---------|
| **lease agreement** | proves rental terms and monthly rate |
| **rent payment history** | bank statements showing consistent income |
| **damage photos/videos** | documents extent of damage |
| **contractor estimates** | establishes repair timeline |
| **tenant communication** | shows evacuation was required |
| **property management records** | if applicable, shows active management |

### if property was vacant at time of loss:

| document | purpose |
|----------|---------|
| **marketing records** | proves property was actively listed |
| **showing history** | demonstrates intent to rent |
| **prior tenant lease** | shows recent rental history |

insurers scrutinize vacant property claims more heavily. document that the vacancy was temporary and you were actively seeking tenants.

---

## comparison: loss-of-rents vs business interruption

| aspect | loss-of-rents | business interruption |
|--------|---------------|----------------------|
| **who it protects** | landlord (property owner) | business operator (tenant) |
| **what it pays** | lost rental income | lost business profits + operating expenses |
| **trigger** | property damage makes space unrentable | business operations disrupted |
| **typical policy** | DP-3 landlord policy | commercial property policy |

if you lease to a business tenant, both coverages may apply — your loss-of-rents for your rental income, their business interruption for their operating losses.

---

## common mistakes

| mistake | consequence |
|---------|-------------|
| **assuming flood is covered** | $0 payout when flooding occurs |
| **underestimating repair time** | 12-month limit exceeded before repairs complete |
| **poor documentation** | claim denied or reduced |
| **not reporting promptly** | late reporting can void coverage |
| **assuming any vacancy triggers coverage** | only covered perils qualify |

---

## how to verify your coverage

1. **read your declarations page** — find the loss-of-rents limit
2. **check the percentage** — is it 20% of dwelling or a fixed amount?
3. **review duration cap** — 12 months standard, can you extend?
4. **confirm covered perils** — named perils or open perils?
5. **check flood status** — do you need separate flood insurance?
