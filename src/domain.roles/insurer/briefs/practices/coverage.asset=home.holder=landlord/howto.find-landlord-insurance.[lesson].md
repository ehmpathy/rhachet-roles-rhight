# howto.find-landlord-insurance

## .what

how to shop for and select a DP-3 landlord insurance policy for your rental property.

## .why

- landlord insurance is not legally required but practically essential
- lenders typically require it as a mortgage condition
- wrong coverage leaves you exposed to claim denial
- prices and coverage vary significantly between carriers

---

## what landlord insurance covers

### core coverages

| coverage | what it protects | typical limits |
|----------|------------------|----------------|
| **dwelling** | structure damage (fire, wind, theft) | replacement cost |
| **liability** | injuries on property, legal defense | $100k-$1M+ |
| **loss of rent** | income lost if property is uninhabitable | 6-12 months rent |
| **other structures** | detached garage, shed, fence | 10% of dwelling |

### what is NOT covered

| not covered | who covers it |
|-------------|---------------|
| tenant's belongings | tenant's renters insurance (HO-4) |
| your personal items unrelated to property | your homeowner's policy |
| flood damage | separate flood policy |
| earthquake damage | separate earthquake policy |
| tenant-caused intentional damage | civil suit against tenant |

---

## DP-1 vs DP-2 vs DP-3

| policy | coverage type | what's covered |
|--------|---------------|----------------|
| DP-1 | named perils | only listed perils (fire, lightning, etc.) |
| DP-2 | broad perils | more perils than DP-1 |
| DP-3 | open perils | all perils EXCEPT listed exclusions |

**recommendation:** DP-3 provides the most comprehensive protection. pay the premium difference.

ask your homeowner's insurance company if they offer landlord policies. bundle discounts may apply.

### independent agents

local insurance agents can shop multiple carriers for you. useful if you have complex situations (multiple properties, mixed use, etc.).

### investor networks

real estate investor groups often share carrier recommendations. check local meetups or online forums (BiggerPockets, etc.).

## what to compare

### coverage limits

| coverage | minimum recommended |
|----------|---------------------|
| dwelling | full replacement cost |
| liability | $300,000+ (consider $1M) |
| loss of rent | 12 months |
| medical payments | $5,000+ |

### deductibles

| deductible | tradeoff |
|------------|----------|
| higher ($2,500+) | lower premium, more out-of-pocket per claim |
| lower ($500-$1,000) | higher premium, less out-of-pocket per claim |

### exclusions

read the exclusions section carefully. common exclusions:

- flood and earthquake (always excluded)
- mold (often excluded or limited)
- bed bugs and pests
- wear and tear
- intentional tenant damage

### endorsements to consider

| endorsement | what it adds |
|-------------|--------------|
| water backup | sewer/drain backup damage |
| umbrella | additional liability beyond policy limits |
| equipment breakdown | HVAC, appliances |
| ordinance or law | code upgrade costs after loss |

## quote process

### information you'll need

- property address
- year built
- square footage
- construction type (frame, brick, etc.)
- roof age and material
- number of units
- monthly rent amount
- current occupancy status
- claims history (past 5 years)
- LLC or personal ownership

### questions to ask

1. is this a DP-3 (open peril) policy?
2. what is the dwelling coverage limit?
3. is loss of rent included? how many months?
4. what is the liability limit?
5. what are the exclusions?
6. can I add water backup coverage?
7. what discounts are available?
8. how do I file a claim?

---

## red flags

| red flag | risk |
|----------|------|
| DP-1 instead of DP-3 | limited coverage |
| no loss of rent | no income protection |
| actual cash value (ACV) | depreciated payout |
| liability under $100k | inadequate protection |
| unknown carrier | may not pay claims |

---

## cost expectations

| factor | effect on premium |
|--------|-------------------|
| property location | higher in disaster-prone areas |
| property age | older = higher |
| roof age | older = higher |
| claims history | prior claims = higher |
| deductible | higher deductible = lower premium |
| coverage limits | higher limits = higher premium |

typical range: $800-$2,500/year for single-family rental (varies widely by location).

---

## require tenant renters insurance

your landlord policy does NOT cover tenant belongings. require tenants to carry renters insurance (HO-4):

| coverage | recommended minimum |
|----------|---------------------|
| personal property | $20,000+ |
| liability | $100,000+ |

add this requirement to your lease. it protects tenants AND reduces your liability exposure.

---

## verification checklist

before you bind coverage:

- [ ] policy is DP-3 (open peril)
- [ ] dwelling coverage = replacement cost (not ACV)
- [ ] liability limit is adequate ($300k+)
- [ ] loss of rent is included
- [ ] read and understand exclusions
- [ ] carrier is financially stable (check AM Best grade)
- [ ] premium fits your budget
- [ ] you know how to file a claim
