# howto.survive-insurance-nonrenewal-obsolete-electrical

## .scope

**this guide applies to:**
- landlords who receive non-renewal notices that cite electrical issues
- landlords who cannot afford immediate full rewire ($12,000-42,000)
- landlords who cannot find standard carriers to insure obsolete electrical

**this guide does NOT apply to:**
- landlords with budget for immediate electrical upgrade
- properties with modern 100+ amp service and circuit breaker panels

---

## .what

when your insurance carrier drops you for knob-and-tube wire systems, fuse boxes, or recalled panels — and you cannot afford immediate replacement — you still have options to maintain coverage and protect your investment.

## .why

> **gaps in coverage expose you to total loss. mortgage lenders will force-place expensive policies. you need a bridge strategy until you can upgrade.**

---

## immediate actions (first 30 days)

### step 1: understand your timeline

| notice type | typical deadline | extension possible? |
|-------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| non-renewal | 30-90 days | rarely |
| cancellation | 10-30 days | sometimes |
| conditional | varies | if you show progress |

### step 2: get electrician certification

even if you cannot afford full rewire, get a licensed electrician to:

| action | purpose | cost |
|--------|---------|------|
| inspect entire system | document current condition | $150-400 |
| provide written assessment | letter for insurers | included |
| identify immediate hazards | prioritize repairs | included |
| certify inactive K&T | some insurers accept abandoned-in-place | $100-200 |

> "some insurance companies will write a policy if the knob and tube wiring has been disconnected and abandoned in place."
>
> — Vargas Insurance

### step 3: address critical hazards first

| hazard | cost | impact |
|--------|------|--------|
| replace recalled panel | $1,500-4,000 | removes absolute disqualifier |
| COPALUM crimp on aluminum wire | $50-100/connection | may satisfy insurers |
| remove active K&T from occupied spaces | $2,000-8,000 | partial remediation |
| fix double-tapped breakers | $150-500 | removes code violation |

## coverage options when standard carriers refuse

### option 1: FAIR plan (state-mandated)

FAIR (Fair Access to Insurance Requirements) plans exist in most states as insurers of last resort.

| aspect | details |
|--------|---------|
| availability | most states |
| coverage type | basic fire and vandalism |
| what's excluded | liability, theft, water damage often excluded |
| cost | 2-5x standard premiums |
| acceptance | cannot refuse based on electrical |

**state FAIR plan contacts:**

| state | plan name | notes |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| California | California FAIR Plan | largest in US |
| Florida | Citizens Property | state-run |
| Massachusetts | MPIUA | oldest FAIR plan |
| New York | NY Property Insurance Underwriting Association | |
| Texas | TWIA (coastal), FAIR Plan (other) | |

> "FAIR plans are designed to provide coverage for properties that are unable to obtain insurance in the private market."
>
> — Insurance Information Institute

### option 2: surplus lines / non-admitted carriers

surplus lines insurers are not bound by state rate regulations and can write policies that standard carriers refuse.

| aspect | details |
|--------|---------|
| cost | 2-4x standard premiums |
| coverage | often full coverage available |
| financial backing | not backed by state guaranty funds |
| process | must go through licensed surplus lines broker |

**how to access:**

1. contact independent insurance broker
2. request surplus lines quotes
3. verify carrier financial ratings (AM Best A- or better)
4. compare coverage terms carefully

> "Lloyd's of London and other surplus lines carriers specialize in risks that standard insurers won't touch."
>
> — Risk & Insurance

### option 3: specialty landlord insurers

some carriers specialize in older properties:

| carrier | notes |
|---------|-------|
| Foremost | non-standard property risks |
| American Modern | older homes, vacant properties |
| NREIG | investor-focused |
| Steadily | landlord-specific, higher risk tolerance |
| Obie | landlord policies, some older home acceptance |

### option 4: independent insurance broker

independent brokers access multiple carriers and surplus lines markets:

| benefit | explanation |
|---------|-------------|
| market access | shops 20+ carriers |
| surplus lines | licensed to access non-admitted markets |
| advocacy | negotiates on your behalf |
| expertise | knows which carriers accept what |

**questions to ask broker:**

1. "which carriers will write policies on homes with [your electrical issue]?"
2. "can you access surplus lines markets?"
3. "what documentation will improve my chances?"
4. "what partial upgrades would expand my options?"

## partial upgrade strategies

### prioritized upgrade order

if you can only afford incremental upgrades, prioritize:

| priority | upgrade | cost | impact |
|----------|---------|------|--------|
| 1 | replace recalled panel | $1,500-4,000 | removes absolute disqualifier |
| 2 | upgrade to 100-amp service | $2,000-5,000 | removes capacity concern |
| 3 | remove K&T from kitchens/baths | $2,000-5,000 | addresses highest-risk areas |
| 4 | COPALUM on aluminum wire | $1,500-3,000 | accepted remediation |
| 5 | remove remaining K&T | $8,000-30,000 | full resolution |

### electrician certification letter

with partial upgrades, get electrician to certify:

| certification | what it says |
|---------------|--------------|
| inactive K&T | "all knob-and-tube circuits have been disconnected and abandoned in place" |
| safe condition | "the electrical system has been inspected and poses no immediate fire hazard" |
| load capacity | "the service capacity is adequate for current use" |
| code compliance | "no code violations were observed" |

> "an electrical certification from a licensed electrician can sometimes satisfy underwriting requirements even without full replacement."
>
> — Progressive

## fund the upgrade

### option 1: PACE programs

Property Assessed Clean Energy (PACE) programs finance energy and safety upgrades:

| aspect | details |
|--------|---------|
| repayment | via property tax bill |
| term | 5-25 years |
| transfers | stays with property on sale |
| approval | based on equity, not credit |
| interest | typically 6-9% |

**limitations:**
- not available in all states
- some lenders object
- adds to property tax burden

### option 2: home equity

| product | typical rate | term |
|---------|--------------|------|
| HELOC | prime + 1-2% | 10-20 years |
| home equity loan | 7-12% | 5-30 years |
| cash-out refinance | varies | 15-30 years |

### option 3: unsecured loans

| product | typical rate | term |
|---------|--------------|------|
| personal loan | 8-20% | 2-7 years |
| credit card | 15-25% | variable |
| contractor payments | varies | negotiated |

### option 4: negotiate with contractor

| approach | how it works |
|----------|--------------|
| staged payments | pay as phases complete |
| payment plan | monthly payments over 6-12 months |
| partial now | kitchen/bath now, remainder later |
| trade | offer rent discount to electrician tenant |

## if you cannot find any carrier

### understand force-placed insurance

if you have a mortgage and cannot obtain coverage, your lender will force-place insurance:

| aspect | details |
|--------|---------|
| cost | 5-10x standard premiums |
| coverage | protects lender only |
| your liability | you pay the premium |
| cancellation | lender can call loan if unpaid |

> "lender-placed insurance typically costs five to ten times as much as a policy you could buy yourself."
>
> — Consumer Financial Protection Bureau

### self-insurance considerations

**never recommended, but if no other option:**

| factor | consideration |
|--------|---------------|
| legal requirement | some states require coverage |
| mortgage requirement | most mortgages mandate coverage |
| liability exposure | one lawsuit could exceed property value |
| total loss risk | fire destroys investment entirely |

**if you own free and clear and choose to self-insure:**

| requirement | purpose |
|-------------|---------|
| reserve fund | 6-12 months of property value |
| liability umbrella | separate policy for liability only |
| LLC ownership | asset protection |
| tenant insurance requirement | shift some risk to tenants |

> "operating without property insurance is a catastrophic risk that most landlords cannot afford to take."
>
> — National Association of Residential Property Managers

## LLC and trust strategies

### separate property into LLC

| benefit | explanation |
|---------|-------------|
| asset protection | limits exposure to single property |
| separate insurance | LLC can obtain coverage separately |
| different underwriting | commercial policies have different rules |

**caution:** some carriers won't insure LLC-owned residential properties.

### land trust ownership

| benefit | explanation |
|---------|-------------|
| privacy | owner not listed on public records |
| insurance flexibility | some carriers more flexible with trusts |
| estate planning | easier transfer |

|----------|-------------------|
| sell as-is, disclose electrical | 15-30% below market |
| sell to investor | 20-40% below market |
| sell to flipper | 25-50% below market |

### target buyers

| buyer type | appeal |
|------------|--------|
| cash investors | no mortgage insurance requirement |
| flippers | expect to renovate anyway |
| contractors | can do work at cost |
| owner-occupants with cash | no lender requirements |

### 30-day action plan

| day | action |
|-----|--------|
| 1-3 | contact independent broker |
| 4-7 | get electrician inspection and letter |
| 8-14 | apply to FAIR plan and surplus lines |
| 15-21 | get quotes on partial upgrades |
| 22-30 | secure any coverage before deadline |

### 6-month upgrade plan

| month | action |
|-------|--------|
| 1 | secure bridge coverage (FAIR/surplus) |
| 2 | replace recalled panel if applicable |
| 3 | upgrade to 100-amp service |
| 4 | remove K&T from kitchens/baths |
| 5 | remediate aluminum wire connections |
| 6 | shop for standard coverage with upgrades |

## documentation checklist

### to show insurers

| document | purpose |
|----------|---------|
| electrician inspection report | proves current condition |
| certification of inactive K&T | proves abandonment |
| panel replacement receipt | proves remediation |
| photos of panel interior | proves no violations |
| upgrade timeline | shows commitment to resolution |

### to protect yourself

| document | purpose |
|----------|---------|
| all carrier denial letters | record of attempts |
| broker communications | proof you tried |
| FAIR plan application | proof of last resort |
| upgrade quotes | proof of intent |
