# ref.admitted-carriers-best-performers

## .what

comparison of top-performing admitted carriers for landlord insurance — denial rates, claims satisfaction, and guaranty fund protection.

## .why

> **admitted carriers offer two critical advantages: lower denial rates and guaranty fund protection. this reference documents the best performers.**

---

## the admitted advantage

| factor | admitted carrier | surplus lines |
|--------|------------------|---------------|
| state licensing | yes | no |
| guaranty fund protection | yes | **no** |
| rate approval | regulated | unregulated |
| typical denial rate | 5.8%-16.6% | 40%-67% |
| bad faith recourse | strong | limited |

## top admitted carriers

### Chubb — lowest denial rate

| metric | value | source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| denial rate | **5.8%** | KFF analysis 2023 |
| J.D. Power claims satisfaction | **773/1000** | J.D. Power 2024 |
| NAIC complaint index | below average | NAIC 2023 |
| financial strength | A++ (Superior) | AM Best |

**why Chubb leads:**
- lowest denial rate among major insurers (5.8% vs 37% national average)
- highest J.D. Power claims satisfaction score (773)
- targets high-value properties with comprehensive coverage
- known for paying claims without excessive pushback

**caveats:**
- premium pricing (20-40% above standard carriers)
- underwriting requirements can be strict
- best for properties valued $1M+

> "Chubb's 5.8% denial rate makes it the least likely major insurer to deny a claim."
>
> — KFF Analysis, 2023

## guaranty fund protection

### what is a guaranty fund?

state-run backup that pays claims if your admitted insurer becomes insolvent.

| aspect | details |
|--------|---------|
| coverage | pays outstanding claims if insurer fails |
| funding | assessments on other insurers |
| eligibility | **admitted carriers only** |
| surplus lines | **explicitly excluded** |

### state coverage limits

| state | property limit | notes |
|-------|----------------|-------|
| **most states** | $300,000 | standard limit |
| New York | $500,000 | higher limit |
| New Jersey | $500,000 | higher limit |
| Utah | $500,000 | higher limit |
| Washington | $500,000 | higher limit |
| California | $500,000 | for certain claims |

### why it matters for landlords

| scenario | admitted carrier | surplus lines |
|----------|------------------|---------------|
| $50,000 fire claim awaited | guaranty fund pays | **you lose $50,000** |
| $200,000 liability judgment | guaranty fund pays | **you pay out of pocket** |
| insurer fails mid-policy | new coverage arranged | **scramble for coverage** |

> "When a surplus lines carrier fails, policyholders have no guaranty fund protection — they become unsecured creditors."
>
> — NAIC, Insurance Insolvency Guide

## admitted vs surplus: the math

### scenario: $200,000 claim denied

| path | admitted carrier | surplus lines |
|------|------------------|---------------|
| initial denial | appeal process | appeal process |
| internal appeal | often reversed | rarely reversed |
| external appeal | state regulator | limited recourse |
| bad faith lawsuit | strong precedent | harder to pursue |
| insurer fails | guaranty fund | **total loss** |

### scenario: carrier becomes insolvent

| event | admitted carrier | surplus lines |
|-------|------------------|---------------|
| $50,000 claim awaited | guaranty fund pays | **$0** |
| unexpired premium | guaranty fund refunds | **$0** |
| new coverage search | regulator assists | **on your own** |

## red flags for any carrier

even admitted carriers can have issues. watch for:

| red flag | what it means |
|----------|---------------|
| NAIC complaint index > 1.5 | more complaints than expected |
| AM Best below A- | financial concerns |
| recent downgrades | financial decline |
| pattern of litigation | claims-process issues |
| significant rate increases | possible exit from market |

## how to verify carrier status

### check admitted status

1. visit your state's department of insurance website
2. search "company search" or "license lookup"
3. enter carrier name
4. verify "admitted" or "licensed" status

### check financial strength

1. visit [AM Best](https://web.ambest.com/ratings-insurance)
2. search carrier name
3. look for A- or better rating
4. check for recent outlook changes

### check complaint history

1. visit [NAIC Consumer Information Source](https://content.naic.org/cis_consumer_information_source.htm)
2. search carrier name
3. compare complaint index to 1.0 baseline
4. review complaint categories
