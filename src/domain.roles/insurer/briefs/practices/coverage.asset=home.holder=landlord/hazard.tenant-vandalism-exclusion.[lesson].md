# hazard.tenant-vandalism-exclusion

## .scope

**this hazard applies to:**
- landlords with standard DP-3 policies
- any rental property where tenants have legal access via lease

**this hazard does NOT apply to:**
- owner-occupied properties (different risk profile)
- commercial properties (different policy types)
- damage by third parties without permission (covered as vandalism)

---

## .what

standard DP-3 policies exclude intentional damage caused by tenants. "vandalism" coverage only applies to destruction by third parties — not by people with legal access to the property.

## .why

> **insurers define vandalism as destruction by someone WITHOUT permission to be on the property. tenants have permission via the lease.**

this definition gap leaves landlords exposed to the most common source of property destruction: angry or malicious tenants.

---

## the coverage gap

| vandal type | has permission? | repairs covered? | loss-of-rents covered? |
|-------------|-----------------|------------------|------------------------|
| burglar | no | ✅ yes | ✅ yes |
| stranger | no | ✅ yes | ✅ yes |
| trespasser | no | ✅ yes | ✅ yes |
| current tenant | yes (lease) | ❌ no | ❌ no |
| former tenant (within 10 days) | yes (recent) | ❌ no | ❌ no |

## what IS covered (tenant-caused)

accidental damage from tenant negligence is typically covered:

| scenario | covered? | why |
|----------|----------|-----|
| tenant accidentally starts kitchen fire | ✅ yes | fire is covered peril; negligence ≠ intent |
| tenant's bathtub overflows, floods unit | ✅ yes | water damage from negligence |
| tenant's candle causes fire | ✅ yes | fire peril, not intentional |
| tenant forgets stove on, causes damage | ✅ yes | negligence, not malice |

**the distinction:** negligent accidents are covered; deliberate destruction is not.

---

## the post-eviction trap

damage discovered after tenant moves out is still excluded:

| timeline | covered? |
|----------|----------|
| damage during tenancy | ❌ no |
| damage within ~10 days of vacancy | ❌ no |
| damage discovered at move-out inspection | ❌ no |

insurers presume the tenant caused damage before departure. the vacancy clause doesn't reset the tenant exclusion.

### common scenario

1. tenant receives eviction notice
2. tenant destroys property before departure
3. landlord discovers damage at move-out inspection
4. landlord files vandalism claim
5. **claim denied** — tenant had legal access when damage occurred

---

## why landlords assume they're covered

| assumption | reality |
|------------|---------|
| "vandalism is a covered peril" | only for third-party vandals |
| "my policy covers property damage" | not intentional damage by occupants |
| "tenant broke the law, so it's vandalism" | legal definition ≠ insurance definition |
| "I have comprehensive coverage" | comprehensive still has exclusions |

the word "vandalism" in everyday language includes any malicious destruction. in insurance language, it specifically excludes people with legal access.

## how to close the gap

### option 1: malicious damage endorsement

| aspect | details |
|--------|---------|
| what it covers | intentional tenant damage to structure |
| typical cost | $50-200/year additional premium |
| requirements | may require tenant background checks |
| availability | not all insurers offer it |

### option 2: expanded vandalism endorsement

| aspect | details |
|--------|---------|
| what it covers | extends vandalism definition to include tenants |
| typical cost | varies by insurer |
| requirements | varies |
| availability | limited |

### option 3: tenant legal liability (TLL)

| aspect | details |
|--------|---------|
| what it covers | accidental tenant damage only |
| does NOT cover | intentional destruction |
| who pays | tenant or landlord |
| use case | supplements, does not replace malicious damage coverage |

**TLL does NOT solve this problem** — it explicitly excludes "expected or intended" damage.

### option 4: security deposit (first line of defense)

| aspect | details |
|--------|---------|
| typical amount | 1-2 months rent |
| coverage | first dollars of damage |
| limitation | rarely covers major destruction |
| best practice | exhaust deposit before insurance claim |

## documentation requirements (if you have endorsement)

to successfully claim under a malicious damage endorsement:

### before tenancy

| document | purpose |
|----------|---------|
| move-in inspection | timestamped photos of every room |
| video walkthrough | proves baseline condition |
| written inventory | documents all fixtures, appliances |
| tenant signature | acknowledges condition |

### after damage discovered

| document | purpose |
|----------|---------|
| move-out inspection | timestamped photos of damage |
| police report | proves criminal intent; often required |
| repair estimates | establishes cost |
| contractor assessments | professional documentation |

### the burden of proof

**you must prove:**
1. damage was intentional (not wear and tear)
2. tenant caused it (not prior condition)
3. damage exceeds normal use

without move-in documentation, claims are routinely denied as "unproven" or classified as wear and tear.

---

## wear and tear vs malicious damage

| wear and tear (not claimable) | malicious damage (claimable with endorsement) |
|-------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| faded paint | paint splashed on walls |
| worn carpet in traffic areas | carpet torn up or burned |
| small nail holes | fist-sized holes in walls |
| minor scuffs on floors | flooring deliberately destroyed |
| loose door handles | doors kicked off hinges |
| normal appliance wear | appliances deliberately broken |

## financial exposure

### example: moderate tenant destruction

| damage | repair cost |
|--------|-------------|
| holes in walls (multiple rooms) | $2,000 |
| flooring replacement | $4,000 |
| appliance replacement | $3,000 |
| deep clean | $1,000 |
| **total** | **$10,000** |

with $3,000 security deposit: **$7,000 unrecovered loss**

### example: severe tenant destruction

| damage | repair cost |
|--------|-------------|
| plumbing sabotage (cement in pipes) | $8,000 |
| HVAC destruction | $6,000 |
| structural damage | $10,000 |
| full renovation required | $15,000 |
| lost rent (3 months repair) | $6,000 |
| **total** | **$45,000** |

with $3,000 security deposit: **$42,000 unrecovered loss**
