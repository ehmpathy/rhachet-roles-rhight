# ref.builders-insurance-providers.owner-builder

## .what

which insurers offer builders insurance to owner-builders, what restrictions apply, and what work types are covered or excluded.

## .why this matters

- not all insurers accept owner-builders
- restrictions on work types can leave gaps in coverage
- knowing providers helps owner-builders shop effectively

---

## providers that accept owner-builders

| provider | owner-builder eligible | project limit | notes |
|----------|------------------------|---------------|-------|
| US Assure (Zurich) | yes, with restrictions | up to $75M | requires 2 years experience for some policies |
| Nationwide | yes | varies | customizable endorsements |
| State Farm | yes | varies | owner or contractor can purchase |
| Chubb | residential focus | varies | designed for homebuilders |
| AmRisk | yes | varies | specializes in residential |
| Liberty Mutual | yes | varies | commercial solutions |

> "Either the property owner or the contractor can purchase a builder's risk policy. The contract between them typically specifies who is responsible for obtaining the policy."
> — [State Farm](https://www.statefarm.com/insurance/small-business/business-owners-policy/builder-risk)

## what IS covered

| category | examples |
|----------|----------|
| structure | framing, foundation, roof |
| materials | lumber, bricks, drywall |
| fixtures | plumbing materials, electrical materials |
| perils | fire, wind, theft, vandalism |

> "Builder's risk insurance covers the property on construction sites, like lumber, bricks, plumbing and electrical materials."
> — [NerdWallet](https://www.nerdwallet.com/business/insurance/learn/builders-risk)

---

## what is NOT covered (standard exclusions)

| exclusion | why |
|-----------|-----|
| **faulty workmanship** | not a covered peril |
| **testing damage** | plumbing/electrical tests that cause damage |
| **mechanical/electrical breakdown** | equipment failure |
| **floods/earthquakes** | require separate riders |
| **employee theft** | separate coverage needed |
| **design defects** | professional liability territory |

> "In almost every builders risk policy, damage arising from 'testing' is an excluded cause of loss—for example, if plumbing tests result in water damage to the unfinished structure."
> — [Landesblosch](https://www.landesblosch.com/blog/builders-risk-insurance-the-ultimate-guide)

---

## testing endorsement (important for electrical/plumbing)

standard policies exclude damage from testing new systems. if you install electrical or plumbing, ask for the **testing endorsement**.

| without endorsement | with endorsement |
|---------------------|------------------|
| testing damage excluded | testing damage covered |
| startup failures excluded | commissioning covered |
| trial runs excluded | examination covered |

> "If you're installing any machinery, equipment, electric, or plumbing, ask your broker whether the testing endorsement is included."
> — [US Assure](https://usassure.com/resources/articles/what-does-builders-risk-insurance-cover)

---

## owner-builder specific restrictions

| restriction | impact |
|-------------|--------|
| experience requirement | some insurers require 2+ years |
| project type limits | some exclude ground-up construction |
| duration limits | typically 12 months max, extensions available |
| vacancy clauses | coverage may lapse if site unattended |

---

## cheaper alternative: dwelling under construction endorsement

some agents suggest adding a "Dwelling Under Construction" endorsement to your homeowners policy instead of standalone builders insurance.

| pros | cons |
|------|------|
| cheaper | vacancy exclusions may still apply |
| simpler | may not cover new additions fully |
| | materials/partial work may be underinsured |

> "These endorsements commonly do not fully remove vacancy-related exclusions for vandalism or theft."
> — [Construction Coverage](https://constructioncoverage.com/builders-risk-insurance)

---

## lender and permit requirements

builders insurance is not legally required, but often practically required:

| entity | requirement |
|--------|-------------|
| construction lenders | almost all require active policy for draws |
| some cities | permit issuance requires proof of insurance |
| some counties | same |

> "Almost all construction lenders won't give loan draws unless an active policy is in place."
> — [Construction Coverage](https://constructioncoverage.com/builders-risk-insurance)

---

## how to shop as owner-builder

1. **get quotes from multiple insurers** — some specialize in owner-builders
2. **ask about testing endorsement** — critical for electrical/plumbing work
3. **verify vacancy clauses** — ensure coverage during gaps in work
4. **check experience requirements** — some insurers require 2 years
5. **compare standalone vs endorsement** — know the tradeoffs
