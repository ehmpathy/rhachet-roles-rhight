# define.defect-equals-damage

## .what

when the defect IS the damage — no fire, no flood, no collapse — neither homeowners nor builder's risk insurance covers it. permit status is irrelevant.

## .why

- clarifies the coverage gap for owner-builders
- distinguishes "defect = damage" from "defect → ensuing loss"
- explains why permit does not help in these scenarios
- identifies the only remedy: out-of-pocket repair

---

## tl;dr

| scenario | ensuing peril? | covered? |
|----------|----------------|----------|
| floor wrong → uneven, squeaky | NO | NOT COVERED |
| floor wrong → collapse → injury | YES (collapse) | COVERED |
| bathtub wrong → crooked, gaps | NO | NOT COVERED |
| bathtub wrong → leak → water damage | YES (water) | COVERED |
| foundation wrong → cracks visible | NO | NOT COVERED |
| foundation wrong → collapse → structural damage | YES (collapse) | COVERED |
| electrical wrong → code violation found | NO | NOT COVERED |
| electrical wrong → fire → house burns | YES (fire) | COVERED |

**the line**: did a COVERED PERIL (fire, water, collapse, explosion) occur between defect and damage?

## the principle

### what insurance covers

homeowners policies cover damage from **named perils**:

| covered peril | examples |
|---------------|----------|
| fire | electrical fire, kitchen fire |
| water | burst pipe, appliance leak |
| collapse | structural failure |
| wind/hail | storm damage |
| theft | break-in |
| vandalism | intentional damage by others |

### what insurance excludes

| exclusion | policy language |
|-----------|-----------------|
| faulty workmanship | "faulty, inadequate or defective workmanship, repair, construction, renovation" |
| defective materials | "materials used in repair, construction, renovation" |
| maintenance | "wear and tear, deterioration" |
| settling | "settling, shrinkage, bulging or expansion" |

**source**: [ISO HO-3 policy form via IRMI](https://www.irmi.com/articles/expert-commentary/ensuing-loss-getting-around-a-property-policys-defective-construction-exclusion)

### the ensuing loss exception

> "But if loss by a Covered Peril ensues, we will pay for that ensuing loss."

this exception ONLY applies when:
1. excluded cause (defect) occurs
2. covered peril (fire/water/collapse) follows
3. damage results from the covered peril

if step 2 never happens → no coverage.

## scenario analysis: owner-builder with permit

### scenario 1: floor installed wrong

**defect = damage (NOT COVERED)**

| situation | analysis |
|-----------|----------|
| floor is uneven | defect IS the damage |
| floor squeaks | defect IS the damage |
| gaps between boards | defect IS the damage |
| subfloor not level | defect IS the damage |

no fire. no water. no collapse. the defect itself is what needs repair.

| insurance type | covers? | why |
|----------------|---------|-----|
| homeowners | NO | faulty workmanship exclusion |
| builder's risk | NO | excludes workmanship defects |
| permit helps? | NO | permit proves compliance, not coverage |

**ensuing loss version (COVERED)**

| situation | analysis |
|-----------|----------|
| floor collapses → you fall through | collapse is covered peril |
| floor catches fire → burns | fire is covered peril |

**cost to owner-builder**: full repair out of pocket ($2,000-15,000 for flooring)

**source**: [Lawyers.com - Faulty Workmanship](https://legal-info.lawyers.com/insurance/property-insurance/insurance-coverage-and-exclusions-for-faulty-workmanship.html)

## case law: defect = damage denials

### Eckstein v. Cincinnati Insurance

**facts**: construction defects caused water intrusion → mold damage.

**holding**: 
- water and mold damage = COVERED (ensuing loss)
- cost to fix the roof defect = NOT COVERED

**principle**: insurance pays for damage FROM the defect, not repair OF the defect.

**source**: [IRMI - Ensuing Loss](https://www.irmi.com/articles/expert-commentary/ensuing-loss-getting-around-a-property-policys-defective-construction-exclusion)

### Bloom v. Western National Mutual (MN)

**facts**: faulty installation → water entry → mold and rot.

**holding**: NOT COVERED. court found mold/rot was not "separable and distinct" from the defective work — it was the direct result of the defect, not an ensuing peril.

**principle**: some courts construe ensuing loss narrowly — if the damage is too closely tied to the defect, no coverage.

**source**: [IRMI - Ensuing Loss](https://www.irmi.com/articles/expert-commentary/ensuing-loss-getting-around-a-property-policys-defective-construction-exclusion)

### Foundation crack cases (general pattern)

**typical denial reasons**:

| reason | frequency |
|--------|-----------|
| faulty construction exclusion | common |
| settling/expansion exclusion | common |
| no sudden/accidental event | common |
| maintenance/wear exclusion | sometimes |

**source**: [ValuePenguin - Foundation Repair](https://www.valuepenguin.com/does-homeowners-insurance-cover-house-foundation-repair)

## who pays for what: the complete picture

### the core principle

> if your product is the issue → you fix it (or your contractor does)
> if cascade damage occurs → insurance covers the cascade, not the defect

### scenario matrix

| situation | who fixes defect | cascade covered? | who pays cascade |
|-----------|------------------|------------------|------------------|
| defect found, no cascade (owner-builder) | YOU | N/A | N/A |
| defect found, no cascade (hired-builder) | CONTRACTOR (warranty/GL) | N/A | N/A |
| defect → cascade (owner-builder) | YOU | YES | your homeowners |
| defect → cascade (hired-builder) | CONTRACTOR | YES | your homeowners → subrogates to contractor |

### example: bathtub leak → water damages ceiling

**what's covered vs not covered**

| item | covered by homeowners? |
|------|------------------------|
| ceiling drywall repair | YES (water damage) |
| mold remediation | YES (water damage) |
| subfloor replacement | YES (water damage) |
| reinstall bathtub correctly | NO (defect repair) |

**who pays for the bathtub reinstall?**

| scenario | who pays |
|----------|----------|
| owner-builder | YOU ($500-2,000) |
| hired-builder | contractor's warranty or GL |

### the hired-builder advantage

| step | what happens |
|------|--------------|
| 1 | defect causes cascade (fire/flood/collapse) |
| 2 | your homeowners pays cascade damage |
| 3 | contractor fixes the defect (warranty/GL) |
| 4 | your insurer subrogates against contractor for cascade costs |
| 5 | contractor's insurance reimburses your insurer |
| 6 | your premiums unaffected (recovery offsets loss) |

**net cost to you**: $0 (or just deductible)

### the owner-builder reality

| step | what happens |
|------|--------------|
| 1 | defect causes cascade (fire/flood/collapse) |
| 2 | your homeowners pays cascade damage |
| 3 | YOU fix the defect (out of pocket) |
| 4 | no subrogation target exists |
| 5 | your insurer absorbs full loss |
| 6 | your premiums increase 10-40% |

**net cost to you**: defect repair + deductible + premium increase over 3-5 years

### quantified comparison

| cost item | owner-builder | hired-builder |
|-----------|---------------|---------------|
| cascade damage | covered | covered |
| defect repair | $500-10,000+ (you) | $0 (contractor) |
| deductible | $1,000-2,500 | $1,000-2,500 |
| premium impact | +$2,000-4,000 over 5 years | ~$0 (subrogation) |
| **total out of pocket** | **$3,500-16,500+** | **$1,000-2,500** |

### which policy responds: construction vs post-construction

| phase | permit status | cascade policy | defect repair |
|-------|---------------|----------------|---------------|
| construction | open permit | builder's risk | NOT covered |
| post-construction | permit closed | homeowners | NOT covered |

**builder's risk has the same ensuing loss logic**:

| scenario | builder's risk covers? |
|----------|----------------------|
| defect found, no cascade | NO (workmanship exclusion) |
| defect → fire → structure burns | YES (fire is covered peril) |
| defect → collapse → damage | YES (collapse is covered peril) |
| cost to repair the defect itself | NO (never covered) |

**during open permit (construction phase)**:

| step | owner-builder | hired-builder |
|------|---------------|---------------|
| 1 | defect causes fire | defect causes fire |
| 2 | builder's risk pays fire damage | builder's risk pays fire damage |
| 3 | YOU fix defect | contractor fixes defect (warranty/GL) |
| 4 | no subrogation target | subrogation against contractor |

**source**: [MEREDA - Faulty Workmanship Exclusion](https://mereda.org/2021/09/14/the-faulty-workmanship-exclusion-in-builders-risk-insurance-measure-thrice-cut-once/)

**key point**: the policy name changes (builder's risk → homeowners), but the coverage logic is identical. cascade = covered. defect = never covered.

## what permit does (and doesn't) provide

### permit provides

| benefit | how it helps |
|---------|--------------|
| evidence of inspection | work was examined by authority |
| code compliance record | met standards at time of inspection |
| burden shift | insurer must prove defect exists |

### permit does NOT provide

| limitation | why |
|------------|-----|
| coverage for defect repair | exclusion applies regardless |
| guarantee defect-free | inspections are limited |
| immunity from exclusions | policy language controls |

**bottom line**: permit helps if insurer claims work was defective. permit does NOT help if defect = damage and no ensuing loss.

## the coverage gap visualized

```
COVERED (ensuing loss):

  defect ──────→ [FIRE/WATER/COLLAPSE] ──────→ damage
                 ↑
                 covered peril intervenes
                 
  insurance pays for: damage from the peril
  insurance does NOT pay for: repair of the defect


NOT COVERED (defect = damage):

  defect ══════════════════════════════════════→ repair needed
                 ↑
                 no covered peril
                 
  insurance pays for: $0
  you pay for: full repair cost
```

## owner-builder remedy options

### when defect = damage (no ensuing loss)

| option | applies when |
|--------|--------------|
| pay out of pocket | always available |
| warranty from subcontractor | if you hired licensed sub for that work |
| contractor bond claim | if contractor was bonded |
| small claims court | if you can prove contractor negligence |
| home warranty | rarely covers pre-purchase defects |

### the harsh reality

owner-builders who do their own work have NO third-party to pursue:
- no contractor warranty
- no subcontractor bond
- no insurance subrogation
- no one else's policy

**you are the contractor. you bear the repair cost.**

| question | answer |
|----------|--------|
| does permit help when defect = damage? | NO — exclusion applies |
| does builder's risk help? | NO — excludes workmanship |
| does homeowners help? | NO — excludes workmanship |
| only covered scenario? | ensuing loss (fire/water/collapse) |
