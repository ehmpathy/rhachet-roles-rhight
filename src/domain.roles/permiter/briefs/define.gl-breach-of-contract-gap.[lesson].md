# define.gl-breach-of-contract-gap

## .what

if GC signs contract promising "no secret subs" and "all subs have comp/GL" then breaches it, their GL insurance will NOT cover your losses from that breach. GL covers bodily injury and property damage — not contract disputes.

## .why

- GL covers "bodily injury" and "property damage" from an "occurrence"
- breach of contract is a business risk, not an accident
- pure economic loss from contract breach = NOT covered
- your recourse is a breach of contract lawsuit, not an insurance claim

---

## tl;dr

| scenario | GL covers? | why |
|----------|-----------|-----|
| GC breaches contract (used secret subs) | **NO** | not "bodily injury" or "property damage" |
| secret sub causes bodily injury to third party | **MAYBE** | depends on action over exclusion |
| you sue GC for breach | **NO** | GL doesn't cover contract disputes |
| injury occurs + you're additional insured | **MAYBE** | coverage for the injury, not the breach |

**the lie itself isn't covered. only resulting injury/damage might be.**

---

## what GL actually covers

### the coverage grant (ISO CGL)

> "We will pay those sums that the insured becomes legally obligated to pay as damages because of 'bodily injury' or 'property damage' to which this insurance applies."

**source**: [IRMI - Contractual Liability and the CGL Policy](https://www.irmi.com/articles/expert-commentary/contractual-liability-and-the-cgl-policy)

### what this means

| term | definition |
|------|------------|
| bodily injury | physical injury, sickness, disease, death |
| property damage | physical injury to tangible property; loss of use |
| occurrence | an accident, including continuous or repeated exposure |

### what is NOT covered

| loss type | covered? |
|-----------|----------|
| physical injury to person | YES |
| physical damage to property | YES |
| economic loss from contract breach | **NO** |
| defense costs for breach of contract suit | **NO** |
| your increased costs from GC's lie | **NO** |

---

## why breach of contract is excluded

### the logic

> "Insurance policies typically do not cover liabilities from a breach of contract because it is viewed as a business risk within the control of the insured."

**source**: [Harper Grey - CGL and Breach of Contract](https://www.harpergrey.com/uncategorized/claims-for-breach-of-contract-are-not-generally-covered-under-a-cgl-policy/)

### the explicit exclusion

many GL policies contain a "breach of contract exclusion":

> "This insurance does not apply to any claim or suit for breach of contract or breach of warranty."

**source**: [IRMI - Breach of Contract Endorsements](https://www.irmi.com/articles/expert-commentary/beware-the-stunted-cgl-breach-of-contract-endorsements)

## the scenario: GC lies about subs

### what GC promised

> "Contractor represents that all subcontractors will maintain workers' compensation and general liability insurance with minimum limits of $1,000,000. Contractor will not use any subcontractors without prior written approval."

### what GC did

- used unauthorized subcontractor
- sub had no workers' comp
- sub had no GL

### what happens when sub gets injured

```
SUB EMPLOYEE INJURED
        │
        ▼
no workers' comp → no exclusive remedy
        │
        ▼
sub employee sues YOU + GC
        │
        ▼
you tender to GC's GL (as additional insured)
        │
        ├─────────────────────────────────┐
        ▼                                 ▼
GC's GL has action over exclusion?    no exclusion?
        │                                 │
        ▼                                 ▼
   DENIED                            MAY COVER
        │                           the injury claim
        ▼                                 │
your homeowners defends                   ▼
        │                           GC's GL defends you
        ▼
you sue GC for breach of contract
        │
        ▼
GC's GL covers your breach claim?
        │
        ▼
       NO — breach of contract excluded
```

## what about fraudulent misrepresentation?

### the argument

> "GC didn't just breach a contract — GC made a fraudulent misrepresentation that induced me to hire them. That's a tort, not just a contract claim."

### the reality

| claim type | GL covers? | why |
|------------|-----------|-----|
| breach of contract | NO | business risk exclusion |
| negligent misrepresentation | MAYBE | if it caused "property damage" |
| fraudulent misrepresentation | UNLIKELY | intentional acts often excluded |

### the "bodily injury or property damage" requirement

even if you frame the claim as tort (misrepresentation), GL still requires:
- **bodily injury**, or
- **property damage**

your loss = "I had to pay $50K in defense costs because GC lied about sub insurance"

that's **economic loss**, not bodily injury or property damage.

**source**: [Haynes Boone - GL Can Cover Breach-of-Contract](https://www.haynesboone.com/news/blogs/policyholder-playbook-episode-44-general-liability-policies-can-cover-breach-of-contract-claims)

## when GL might apply

### the narrow exception

> "In some instances, breach-of-contract claims may be covered by a general liability policy if plaintiffs establish that there was a basis in fact for imposing tort liability on the insured."

**source**: [Haynes Boone](https://www.haynesboone.com/news/blogs/policyholder-playbook-episode-44-general-liability-policies-can-cover-breach-of-contract-claims)

### what this means

| scenario | GL covers? |
|----------|-----------|
| GC's lie → you paid higher defense costs | NO — economic loss |
| GC's lie → sub injured third party → property damage | MAYBE — if additional insured covers |
| GC's lie → fire from sub's work → your house burns | YES — property damage from occurrence |

**key**: the lie itself isn't covered. only if the lie led to physical injury/damage that would otherwise trigger GL.

## your actual recourse

### if GC breaches contract

| action | how it works |
|--------|--------------|
| sue GC for breach of contract | prove breach, prove damages |
| pursue GC's bond (if bonded) | claim against performance bond |
| withhold payment | leverage unpaid balance |
| report to license board | disciplinary action |

### if injury occurs

| action | how it works |
|--------|--------------|
| tender to GC's GL | claim defense as additional insured |
| if GL denies (action over) | your homeowners defends |
| then sue GC for breach | recover your defense costs |

### the problem

| factor | risk |
|--------|------|
| GC may be judgment-proof | LLC with no assets |
| lawsuit takes years | legal fees compound |
| GC may go out of business | no recovery |

**this is why certificate verification matters more than contract language.**

---

## protection strategy

### don't rely on contract alone

| protection | reliability |
|------------|-------------|
| contract language | LOW — recourse after harm |
| verified certificates | HIGH — protection while harm occurs |
| withhold payment until certificates | HIGH — leverage |
| additional insured status | HIGH — their GL defends you |

### the verification protocol

| step | action |
|------|--------|
| 1 | require certificates before work starts |
| 2 | verify YOU are additional insured |
| 3 | require certificate for each sub |
| 4 | withhold 10% until certificates provided |
| 5 | if new sub appears, stop work until certificate |

---

## key takeaways

| question | answer |
|----------|--------|
| if GC lies about subs, does their GL cover my loss? | **NO** — breach of contract excluded |
| what if I call it fraud/misrepresentation? | still NO — requires bodily injury or property damage |
| what if the secret sub causes injury? | **MAYBE** — depends on action over exclusion |
| what's my recourse for the breach? | sue GC for breach of contract (separate from GL) |
| why does this matter? | contract language gives recourse AFTER; certificates give protection WHILE harm occurs |
