# define.owner-builder-exemption.rental-exclusion

## .what

the owner-builder exemption requires **owner-resident** status — not just ownership. you must occupy the property as your primary residence.

## .why this matters

- landlords cannot DIY electrical or plumber work on rental units
- property investors must hire licensed contractors
- tenants are protected as "the public" that license laws serve

---

## the rule

| property type | owner-builder allowed? | why |
|---------------|------------------------|-----|
| primary residence (owner-resident) | YES | you bear your own risk |
| rental property (tenant-occupied) | NO | tenants are the protected public |
| vacation home (owner uses occasionally) | VARIES | some jurisdictions allow, others don't |
| investment property (never occupied by owner) | NO | you act as a contractor |

---

## why residency matters

the owner-builder exemption exists because:

| rationale | applies to rentals? |
|-----------|---------------------|
| you bear the risk of your own work | NO — tenant bears risk |
| you live with the consequences | NO — tenant lives with consequences |
| you don't deceive a customer | NO — tenant is effectively a customer |
| inspection still catches safety issues | YES — but not sufficient alone |

**tenants didn't choose DIY electrical.** they're the "public" that contractor license requirements protect.

---

## indianapolis specifics

per Indianapolis DBNS:

> "In most cases, homeowners in Indianapolis can pull permits and perform work on their own **primary residence** as an owner-builder."

| requirement | indianapolis rule |
|-------------|-------------------|
| ownership required | yes |
| residency required | yes — primary residence |
| rental property allowed | no |

---

## what landlords must do

| scenario | required approach |
|----------|-------------------|
| electrical work on rental | hire Marion County licensed contractor |
| pipe work on rental | hire licensed plumber |
| HVAC work on rental | hire licensed HVAC contractor |
| general repairs (no permit needed) | can DIY |

---

## edge cases

### "I move out and rent my former primary residence"

once you no longer reside there, owner-builder exemption ends. future work requires licensed contractors.

### "I live in one unit of a duplex and rent the other"

| unit | owner-builder allowed? |
|------|------------------------|
| your unit (owner-resident) | yes |
| rental unit | no — requires licensed contractor |

### "I'm between tenants — property is vacant"

still a rental property. vacancy doesn't restore owner-builder status.

### "I plan to move in after renovations"

some jurisdictions allow this with documentation of intent. verify with local permit office. Indianapolis DBNS should be consulted directly.

---

## consequences of violation

| violation | potential consequence |
|-----------|----------------------|
| DIY work on rental without license | permit application rejected |
| unpermitted work discovered | retroactive permit required + penalties |
| work fails inspection | must hire licensed contractor to remediate |
| tenant injury from faulty work | personal liability exposure |
| insurance claim denied | see `ref.insurance-unpermitted-work-discovery` |
