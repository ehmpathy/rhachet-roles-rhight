# hazard.subcontractor-injury-gl-gap

## .what

GC's GL may NOT cover injuries to subcontractor employees — depends on "action over" exclusion language in the policy.

## .why

- standard GL excludes injuries to "employees of the insured"
- subcontractors are NOT employees of the GC (independent contractors)
- BUT many GL policies add "action over" or "worker injury" exclusions
- these broader exclusions CAN exclude subcontractor employee injuries
- result: coverage depends on policy wording, not standard assumptions

## the standard exclusion vs action over

### standard employer's liability exclusion (ISO CGL)

> "This insurance does not apply to bodily injury to an employee of the insured arising out of and in the course of employment by the insured."

**key**: excludes injuries to employees OF THE INSURED (the GC).

subcontractors are NOT employees of the GC — they're independent contractors.

**under standard language**: GC's GL should cover claims by injured sub employees.

### action over exclusion (policy endorsement)

many insurers add broader exclusions:

> "This insurance does not apply to bodily injury to any employee of the insured, any contractor, or any employee of any contractor."

**key**: excludes injuries to employees of ANY contractor — including subs.

**under action over language**: GC's GL will NOT cover claims by injured sub employees.

**source**: [IRMI - Employers Liability Exclusion in the CGL Policy](https://www.irmi.com/articles/expert-commentary/employers-liability-exclusion-in-the-cgl-policy)

## why this matters for property owners

### the lawsuit chain

| step | what happens |
|------|--------------|
| 1 | sub's employee injured |
| 2 | sub has no workers' comp |
| 3 | injured worker sues GC AND property owner |
| 4 | property owner tenders to GC's GL (as additional insured) |
| 5 | GC's GL checks for action over exclusion |
| 6a | if NO exclusion → GC's GL defends you |
| 6b | if exclusion exists → GC's GL denies |
| 7 | your homeowners defends you |

### why sub employees sue property owners

> "The immunity shield provided by the exclusive remedy rule typically only protects the direct employer and does not extend to other negligent parties on a complex construction site, opening the door for third-party lawsuits."

**source**: [Morrow & Sheppard - Third-Party Liability](https://www.morrowsheppard.com/blog/third-party-liability-can-a-subcontractor-sue-a-general-contractor-for-injuries-on-the-job/)

### when property owners are liable

| factor | increases liability |
|--------|---------------------|
| retained control over site | YES |
| knew of hazard, didn't warn | YES |
| created the dangerous condition | YES |
| negligent hire of GC | YES |

**source**: [World Construction Today - When Injured Workers Can Sue](https://www.worldconstructiontoday.com/news/when-an-injured-worker-can-sue-a-general-contractor-or-owner/)

## the insured contract exception

### what can restore coverage

even if action over exclusion exists, an exception may apply:

> "The employers liability exclusion does not apply to injuries to employees of the insured if and to the extent that the employer has assumed liability in an 'insured contract.'"

**source**: [Amwins - Contractor's GL Coverage Limitations](https://www.amwins.com/resources-insights/article/contractor-s-general-liability-11-common-coverage-limitation-endorsements)

### what this means

| has indemnity agreement? | action over exclusion applies? |
|--------------------------|-------------------------------|
| YES (in contract) | MAY NOT apply — "insured contract" exception |
| NO | applies — GL denies |

**critical**: require indemnification language in GC contract AND verify GC's GL has the insured contract exception intact.

## case law: courts have denied coverage

### the risk is real

> "In certain cases, courts have interpreted a CGL policy's employers liability exclusion in a way that would defeat coverage for a general contractor and other upstream parties sued by a downstream subcontractor's employees, which contradicts the parties' fundamental expectations and frustrates the intended risk transfer."

**source**: [IRMI - Misapplication of the Employers Liability Exclusion](https://www.irmi.com/articles/expert-commentary/misapplication-of-the-employers-liability-exclusion-in-cgl-policies-precluding-coverage-for-non-employer-insureds)

### New York example

> "In one appellate-level case out of New York, the court denied coverage to an insured for injuries sustained by an employee of a stage subcontractor the insured had hired."

**source**: [Saxe Doernberger & Vita - NY Labor Laws and Action Over Exclusions](https://www.sdvlaw.com/publications/new-york-labor-laws-and-action-over-exclusions/)

### California statutory employee doctrine

> "An unlicensed subcontractor may not be an independent contractor but is instead deemed a statutory employee of the general contractor. Labor Code section 2750.5 operates to conclusively determine that a general contractor is the employer of not only its unlicensed subcontractors but also those employed by the unlicensed subcontractors."

**source**: [Wolff Law - When Unlicensed Workers Can Sue](https://www.wolfflaw.com/when-may-unlicensed-construction-workers-sue-a-contractor-or-a-p.html)

**implication**: if sub is unlicensed, sub's employees may be legally YOUR employees — you become liable.

## the protection stack

### what to require from GC

| document | verifies |
|----------|----------|
| GC's workers' comp certificate | GC's employees covered |
| GC's GL certificate | GL coverage exists |
| additional insured endorsement | you're on GC's GL |
| review GL for action over exclusion | know if gap exists |
| indemnification clause in contract | triggers insured contract exception |
| sub insurance requirements in GC contract | GC must verify sub coverage |

### what GC should require from each sub

| document | verifies |
|----------|----------|
| sub's workers' comp certificate | sub's employees covered — blocks lawsuits |
| sub's GL certificate | sub has GL coverage |
| additional insured (GC + owner) | both covered under sub's GL |

### the chain of protection

```
SUB'S EMPLOYEE INJURED
        │
        ▼
sub's workers' comp pays
        │
        ▼
exclusive remedy blocks lawsuit
        │
        ▼
NO ONE GETS SUED
        │
        ▼
YOU: protected
```

**if any link breaks** (sub lacks comp, GC didn't verify, action over exclusion applies), **you're exposed**.

## verification checklist

### before work starts

| item | verified? |
|------|-----------|
| GC has workers' comp | ☐ |
| GC has GL | ☐ |
| you're additional insured on GC's GL | ☐ |
| contract requires GC to verify sub insurance | ☐ |
| contract includes indemnification clause | ☐ |
| GC's GL does NOT have action over exclusion | ☐ |
| (or) GC's GL has insured contract exception | ☐ |

### the hard question to ask

> "Does your GL policy have an action over exclusion, worker injury exclusion, or labor law exclusion that would deny coverage for claims by injured subcontractor employees?"

most GCs won't know. ask for the policy — or ask their broker to confirm in text.

## key takeaways

| question | answer |
|----------|--------|
| does GC's GL cover sub employee injuries? | **DEPENDS** on action over exclusion |
| standard ISO CGL? | should cover (subs aren't GC's employees) |
| with action over exclusion? | **DENIED** (excludes all contractor employees) |
| how to restore coverage? | indemnification clause + insured contract exception |
| best protection? | require subs to carry their own comp |
| if sub lacks comp? | sub can sue you; GC's GL may deny |
