# define.property-vs-liability-coverage

## .what

property insurance covers YOUR stuff. liability insurance covers when YOU hurt OTHERS (or their stuff). additional insured status only matters for liability — not property.

## .why

- clarifies when additional insured status is valuable
- distinguishes property coverage from liability coverage
- explains why subrogation handles property damage without additional insured
- identifies when homeowners need protection from contractor's GL

---

## tl;dr

| coverage type | protects | example policies |
|---------------|----------|------------------|
| property | YOUR stuff | builder's risk, homeowners (dwelling) |
| liability | YOU from claims by OTHERS | homeowners (liability), contractor GL |

| scenario | need additional insured? | why |
|----------|-------------------------|-----|
| defect → fire → YOUR house burns | NO | your property insurance covers you |
| visitor injured on YOUR site | YES | you could be sued |
| contractor damages NEIGHBOR's property | YES | neighbor could sue you |

## the two coverage types

### property coverage

**what it is**: pays to repair/replace YOUR property when damaged by covered perils.

| policy | covers |
|--------|--------|
| builder's risk | structure under construction |
| homeowners (dwelling) | your house |
| homeowners (contents) | your stuff inside |

**who gets paid**: YOU (the property owner)

**how claims work**:
1. your property damaged
2. you file claim with YOUR insurer
3. your insurer pays you
4. your insurer subrogates against responsible party

### liability coverage

**what it is**: pays when YOU are legally responsible for injury/damage to OTHERS.

| policy | covers |
|--------|--------|
| homeowners (liability) | injuries on your property, damage you cause |
| contractor GL | injuries/damage contractor causes |
| umbrella | excess liability above other policies |

**who gets paid**: the injured third party (or their insurer)

**how claims work**:
1. third party injured/damaged
2. third party sues YOU
3. your liability insurer defends you
4. your liability insurer pays settlement/judgment

## why additional insured matters for liability (not property)

### property damage to YOUR house

**scenario**: contractor's defect → fire → your house burns

| step | what happens | additional insured needed? |
|------|--------------|---------------------------|
| 1 | fire damages your house | — |
| 2 | you file claim with YOUR builder's risk/homeowners | NO |
| 3 | your insurer pays you | NO |
| 4 | your insurer subrogates against contractor's GL | NO |
| 5 | contractor's GL reimburses your insurer | NO |

**you are made whole through your own policy + subrogation. no additional insured needed.**

### liability claim against YOU

**scenario**: visitor injured on your construction site

| step | what happens | additional insured helps? |
|------|--------------|--------------------------|
| 1 | visitor trips on contractor's equipment, breaks leg | — |
| 2 | visitor sues YOU (property owner) and contractor | — |
| 3 | WITHOUT additional insured: your homeowners defends you | costs your policy |
| 4 | WITH additional insured: contractor's GL defends you | costs their policy |

**additional insured = their policy defends you instead of yours.**

## why they sue YOU (not just the contractor)

### the plaintiff's strategy

injured parties sue EVERYONE involved — standard practice to maximize recovery.

| reason | explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **joint and several liability** | sue everyone, collect from whoever has money |
| **deep pockets** | your home equity often exceeds contractor's assets |
| **contractor undercapitalized** | small LLC with minimal insurance or assets |
| **property owner duty** | you hired them, your property, your responsibility |
| **maximize recovery odds** | lawyer names everyone, sorts liability later |

### who gets named in the lawsuit

| party | why named |
|-------|-----------|
| YOU (property owner) | deep pockets, property owner duty |
| general contractor | direct control of work |
| subcontractor who caused damage | actual cause |
| sub's employer (if different) | respondeat superior |
| material supplier (sometimes) | product liability |

### the cost of defense

| item | typical cost |
|------|--------------|
| attorney fees | $200-500/hour |
| total defense cost | $20,000-100,000+ |
| even if you "win" | you still paid to defend |

**the real value of additional insured**: contractor's GL pays your $50K+ defense costs, not your homeowners policy.

### example: excavator damages neighbor's foundation

| step | what happens |
|------|--------------|
| 1 | contractor's excavator cracks neighbor's foundation |
| 2 | neighbor sues YOU and contractor |
| 3 | your homeowners assigns defense attorney (costs your policy) |
| 4 | contractor's GL assigns their defense attorney |
| 5 | 18 months of litigation |
| 6 | settlement: contractor pays damages |
| 7 | WITHOUT additional insured: your defense cost $40K, your premiums rise |
| 8 | WITH additional insured: contractor's GL paid your defense too |

## owner-builder vs hired-builder: when additional insured matters

### owner-builder with hired subs

| scenario | additional insured value |
|----------|-------------------------|
| sub's defect → fire → your house | NO — your builder's risk covers, subrogates against sub |
| sub injures visitor | YES — sub's GL defends you |
| sub damages neighbor | YES — sub's GL defends you |
| sub's worker injured | YES — sub's workers comp + GL respond first |

**recommendation**: require additional insured status from every sub you hire.

### hired general contractor (GC)

| scenario | additional insured value |
|----------|-------------------------|
| GC's defect → fire → your house | NO — your builder's risk covers, subrogates against GC |
| GC's worker injures visitor | YES — GC's GL defends you |
| GC's sub damages neighbor | YES — GC's GL defends you |
| GC's sub injures themselves | YES — GC's coverage responds first |

**recommendation**: require additional insured status from GC. GC should require it from all subs.

### the chain of protection (hired-builder)

```
                  INJURY/DAMAGE OCCURS
                          │
                          ▼
              who caused it? ─────────────────┐
                    │                         │
                    ▼                         ▼
               GC or sub                 random accident
                    │                         │
                    ▼                         ▼
          GC's GL defends you          your homeowners
          (if additional insured)       defends you
                    │
                    ▼
          GC subrogates against
          sub (if sub caused it)
```

**source**: [Progressive - Contractor Damage](https://www.progressive.com/answers/property-damage-caused-by-contractors/)

## when additional insured status is valuable

### scenarios where it helps

| scenario | why additional insured helps |
|----------|------------------------------|
| worker injured on site | contractor's workers comp + GL respond first |
| visitor injured | contractor's GL defends you |
| contractor damages neighbor | contractor's GL covers neighbor's claim against you |
| subcontractor causes injury | contractor's GL covers claims that name you |
| completed operations claim | someone injured by finished work, you're named |

### scenarios where it doesn't matter

| scenario | why additional insured irrelevant |
|----------|-----------------------------------|
| defect → fire → your house | your property insurance covers you |
| defect → flood → your house | your property insurance covers you |
| defect → collapse → your house | your property insurance covers you |
| contractor doesn't finish work | contract dispute, not insurance |

## the subrogation path (property) vs direct defense (liability)

### property damage: subrogation

```
YOUR PROPERTY DAMAGED
        │
        ▼
your builder's risk / homeowners
        │
        ▼
    pays you
        │
        ▼
subrogates against contractor's GL
        │
        ▼
contractor's GL reimburses your insurer
        │
        ▼
    YOU: made whole
```

no additional insured needed — subrogation handles recovery.

### liability claim: direct defense

```
THIRD PARTY INJURED
        │
        ▼
    sues YOU
        │
        ├──────────────────────────────┐
        ▼                              ▼
WITHOUT additional insured      WITH additional insured
        │                              │
        ▼                              ▼
your homeowners defends         contractor's GL defends
        │                              │
        ▼                              ▼
your policy pays                contractor's policy pays
        │                              │
        ▼                              ▼
your premiums may rise          your premiums unaffected
```

additional insured = defense costs contractor, not you.

## how to get additional insured status

### what to request

| document | purpose |
|----------|---------|
| certificate of insurance (COI) | proves contractor has coverage |
| additional insured endorsement | adds you to their policy |

### what to verify

| item | check for |
|------|-----------|
| GL coverage | minimum $1M per occurrence |
| workers comp | required in most states |
| your name | listed as additional insured |
| property address | listed on certificate |
| policy dates | cover your project timeline |

**source**: [NH Insurance Dept - Verify Contractor Insurance](https://www.insurance.nh.gov/news-and-media/blog/how-verify-if-your-contractor-has-right-insurance)

### typical cost

| item | cost |
|------|------|
| additional insured endorsement | $0-50 (often free) |
| certificate of insurance | free |

most contractors can add you at no charge — standard practice in construction.

## what if contractor is uninsured?

### your exposure

| scenario | who pays |
|----------|----------|
| contractor injures visitor | YOU (via homeowners liability) |
| contractor damages neighbor | YOU (via homeowners liability) |
| contractor's worker injured | YOU (possibly — depends on state) |
| contractor damages your house | YOU (homeowners covers, no subrogation target) |

**source**: [AllLaw - Contractor Injury Liability](https://www.alllaw.com/articles/nolo/personal-injury/homeowner-liable-injuries-contractor.html)

### why this matters for owner-builders

| if you hire unlicensed/uninsured help | risk |
|---------------------------------------|------|
| "helper" injures themselves | you may be liable |
| "helper" injures visitor | you may be liable |
| "helper's" work causes damage to neighbor | you're liable |
| "helper's" work defective | no warranty, no GL to subrogate against |

## coverage requirements by role

### owner-builder with hired subs

**require from each sub:**

| coverage | minimum | why |
|----------|---------|-----|
| GL | $1M per occurrence | covers their liability |
| workers comp | state minimum | covers their employee injuries |
| auto | $500K | if they drive to your site |
| additional insured | YOU named | their GL defends you |

**you provide:**

| coverage | purpose |
|----------|---------|
| builder's risk | covers structure under construction |
| homeowners liability | covers gaps, visitor injuries |
| umbrella (optional) | excess liability protection |

### hired general contractor

**require from GC:**

| coverage | minimum | why |
|----------|---------|-----|
| GL | $1M+ per occurrence | covers their liability |
| workers comp | state minimum | covers their employees |
| additional insured | YOU named | their GL defends you |
| certificate showing subs covered | verify chain | GC responsible for subs |

**you provide:**

| coverage | purpose |
|----------|---------|
| builder's risk | may be GC's responsibility — verify contract |
| homeowners liability | backup coverage |

## summary: when each matters

| I'm worried about... | coverage needed | additional insured? |
|---------------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| fire damages my house | builder's risk / homeowners | NO |
| visitor sues me | homeowners liability OR contractor GL | YES (helpful) |
| contractor damages neighbor | contractor GL | YES (helpful) |
| worker injured on my site | contractor workers comp + GL | YES (helpful) |
| defect needs repair | contractor warranty / GL | NO (contract issue) |

---

## key takeaways

| question | answer |
|----------|--------|
| do I need additional insured for property claims? | NO — subrogation handles it |
| do I need additional insured for liability claims? | YES — their policy defends you |
| owner-builder: require from subs? | YES — standard practice |
| hired GC: require from GC? | YES — GC handles sub coverage |
| what does additional insured cost? | usually free ($0-50) |
| what if contractor is uninsured? | you absorb all risk |
