# ref.insurance-home-total-loss-replacement-cost-value

## .what

renovation builder's risk policies often cover the prior structure at **depreciated value (ACV)**, not full replacement cost (RCV). this creates a gap when a small job burns down the whole house.

## .why this matters

- $3k electrical job causes fire → $400k house destroyed
- you expect $400k payout to rebuild
- policy pays **depreciated value** of prior structure
- 20-year-old house might pay out $320k or less
- you're $80k+ short of rebuild cost

---

## the gap

| component | typical valuation |
|-----------|-------------------|
| new work (the $3k electrical) | RCV (full cost) |
| prior structure (the $400k house) | **ACV (depreciated)** |

> "Many builders risk policies provide only actual cash value coverage for existing structures... which could be a 30-50 percent shortfall."
> — [IRMI](https://www.irmi.com/articles/expert-commentary/key-considerations-when-buying-builders-risk-coverage)

---

## RCV is NOT standard — but easy to get if you ask

**default**: ACV for prior structure

> "For renovation policies, the existing structure is typically insured at a depreciated value (actual cash value) while all renovations are insured as full replacement cost."
> — [Builder's Shield](https://www.buildersshield.com/28255/replacementcostvsactualcashvalue/)

**to get RCV**: request ISO endorsement IH 70 04 or IH 70 05 with RCV selected on declarations

### availability

| aspect | reality |
|--------|---------|
| commonly available | yes — most insurers offer it |
| default | no — ACV unless you ask |
| cost | higher premium (varies by situation) |
| homeowner awareness | **low** — most don't know to ask |

> "Coverage can be provided by declaration to the carrier for an additional premium."
> — [J.S. Held](https://www.jsheld.com/insights/articles/builders-risk-complexities-in-insuring-existing-property)

### when insurers may refuse RCV

| scenario | why |
|----------|-----|
| structure worth less than rebuild cost | moral hazard concern |
| very old or deteriorated structure | underwrite risk |
| renovation < 20% of structure value | some carriers require minimum ratio |

> "Underwriters may be unwilling to take on the risk of the uncertainties presented."
> — [J.S. Held](https://www.jsheld.com/insights/articles/builders-risk-complexities-in-insuring-existing-property)

> "The renovation limit must be at least 20% of the structure value when including structure coverage."
> — [US Assure](https://usassure.com/resources/articles/7-tips-to-avoid-common-residential-builders-risk-claim-problems)

### what to say

> "I want replacement cost value on the prior structure, not just the renovation work. Please add the endorsement for RCV on the full property."

if refused, ask why and get it in written form.

## owner-builder vs hired-builder

| factor | owner-builder | hired-builder |
|--------|---------------|---------------|
| permit risk | **high** — unpermitted work = denial | lower — contractor pulls permits |
| recovery paths | builder's risk only | builder's risk + contractor GL + subrogation |
| scrutiny | higher — DIY quality questioned | lower — licensed work assumed |
| total loss outcome | **higher risk** | better prospects |

## statistics

- **4,440 fires per year** in structures under construction
- **$370 million** annual property damage
- **2/3 of U.S. homes** underinsured for total loss
- **74%** of Marshall Fire claimants underinsured
- sources: [NFPA](https://www.nfpa.org/education-and-research/research/nfpa-research/fire-statistical-reports/fires-in-structures-under-construction), [United Policyholders](https://uphelp.org/claim-guidance-publications/underinsurance-101/)

## protection checklist

| action | why |
|--------|-----|
| ask: "is prior structure at RCV or ACV?" | determines payout on total loss |
| verify policy limit = full rebuild cost | avoids coinsurance penalty |
| pull permits (owner-builder) | avoids denial for unpermitted work |
| verify contractor GL limits (hired-builder) | ensures recovery path |
| check if homeowner's policy suspended | may have no coverage while construction active |

---

## bottom line

a $3k job can burn down a $400k house. if your builder's risk only covers the prior structure at ACV, you could be $80k+ short of rebuild cost.

**ask for RCV on the prior structure, not just the new work.**
