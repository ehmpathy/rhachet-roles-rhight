# ref.builders-insurance-premium.owner-builder

## .what

owner-builders often face **higher premiums** and **underwriting restrictions** on builders insurance compared to licensed contractors.

## .why this matters

- owner-builders may be denied coverage or pay 20-50% more
- some insurers (Zurich) require 2+ years construction experience
- understanding underwriting criteria helps owner-builders shop effectively

---

## the myth

| builder | qualifications | experience | premium |
|---------|---------------|------------|---------|
| licensed contractor | state license, exam, bond | 10+ years | base rate |
| owner-builder | none | potentially zero | **same?** |

**common assumption**: builders insurance is property insurance covering perils, so skill should not affect price.

**reality**: insurers DO price for experience, and some refuse owner-builders entirely.

---

## what the research shows

### owner-builders pay more

> "A first-time owner-builder may face higher premiums due to a lack of experience in managing subcontractors and job site safety."
> — [Stanton Insurance](https://www.stantoninsurance.net/blog/builders-risk-insurance-for-owner-builders)

> "Inexperienced contractors may be required to pay higher premiums or may find it more difficult to obtain coverage."
> — [Liberty Insurance](https://libertyinsurance.com/builders-risk-insurance-rates-guide/)

### some insurers refuse owner-builders

> "Zurich requires contractors to have two years of project-related experience... inexperience brings longer completion times, project management challenges."
> — [US Assure](https://usassure.com/resources/articles/7-tips-to-avoid-common-residential-builders-risk-claim-problems)

### GL is even harder

> "Most general liability insurance policies are written for businesses, not individuals... Owner-builders may need to seek out specialty insurers."
> — [Stanton Insurance](https://www.stantoninsurance.net/blog/builders-risk-insurance-for-owner-builders)

---

## why insurers care about experience

builders insurance IS property insurance covering perils, not performance. but insurers still price for experience because:

### reason 1: project completion risk

inexperienced builders take longer. longer projects = more exposure time.

### reason 2: subcontractor management

owner-builders often fail to verify subcontractor insurance, leaving gaps.

### reason 3: claims frequency

while individual claim outcomes are no-fault, inexperienced builders file more claims.

## what IS covered (regardless of skill)

| peril | example |
|-------|---------|
| fire | electrical fire, arson |
| wind | tornado, hurricane |
| theft | stolen materials, tools |
| vandalism | break-in damage |
| collapse | structural failure |

### what is NOT covered

| exclusion | example |
|-----------|---------|
| faulty workmanship | bad wire connection |
| design defects | architect error |
| wear and tear | gradual deterioration |

---

## the fire risk objection

### the question

does a novice doing electrical work increase fire risk through faulty workmanship?

**yes.** and insurers DO account for this — through experience requirements and higher premiums.

### but the base rate is still low

| metric | value |
|--------|-------|
| homes with ANY fire annually | ~0.3% |
| electrical fires as % of all fires | 6-7% |
| homes with electrical fire annually | **~0.02%** (1 in 5,000) |

even if DIY work doubled fire risk: 2 in 5,000. this explains why some insurers accept owner-builders at all.

source: [USFA](https://www.usfa.fema.gov/statistics/residential-fires/electrical.html), [NFPA](https://www.nfpa.org/education-and-research/research/nfpa-research/fire-statistical-reports/home-fires-caused-by-electrical-failure-or-malfunction)

## what DOES affect premiums

| factor | impact | source |
|--------|--------|--------|
| **location** | greatest influence | [Procore](https://www.procore.com/library/builders-risk-insurance) |
| **experience** | 20-50% higher for inexperienced | [Liberty Insurance](https://libertyinsurance.com/builders-risk-insurance-rates-guide/) |
| construction type | wood-frame most expensive | IRMI |
| project value | base for 1-4% rate | industry standard |
| project duration | longer = more exposure | underwriting |

---

## implications for owner-builders

### what this means

- your lack of experience WILL raise your premium (often 20-50% more)
- some insurers will refuse coverage entirely (Zurich requires 2 years experience)
- coverage for fire/theft/weather still applies once you have a policy

### how to improve your position

1. **get quotes from multiple insurers** — some specialize in owner-builders
2. **document prior construction experience** — even informal
3. **pull permits early** — shows commitment to code compliance
4. **verify subcontractor insurance** — reduces your risk profile

---

## bottom line

builders insurance IS priced on **who** builds, not just **what** is built.

| prices by | impact on owner-builders |
|-----------|-------------------------|
| location | same as contractors |
| materials | same as contractors |
| value | same as contractors |
| duration | same as contractors |
| **experience** | **higher premiums or denial** |

**reality**: owner-builders face higher premiums, underwriting restrictions, and potential denial — contrary to the myth that builders insurance is indifferent to builder qualifications.
