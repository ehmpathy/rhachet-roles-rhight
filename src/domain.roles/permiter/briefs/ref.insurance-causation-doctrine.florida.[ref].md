# ref.insurance-causation-doctrine.florida

## .what

Florida applies the **concurrent causation doctrine** for property damage claims with mixed causes.

## .the rule

when damage results from multiple events and at least one is a covered peril, coverage may exist — UNLESS the policy contains anti-concurrent causation (ACC) language.

## .key case

**Sebo v. American Home Assurance Co., 208 So. 3d 694 (Fla. 2016)**

- **facts**: water damage from design/construction defects (May-Aug 2005) plus Hurricane Wilma (Oct 2005). insurer offered $50k citing defect exclusion. policy was all-risk with >$8M coverage.
- **holding**: Florida Supreme Court applied concurrent cause doctrine. coverage exists when damage results from multiple events if at least one is covered.
- **critical factor**: the defect exclusion lacked ACC language (while other exclusions had it). court stated: "Because [insurer] did not explicitly avoid the [concurrent cause doctrine], we find that the plain language of the policy does not preclude recovery."

## .anti-concurrent causation clauses

Florida courts **enforce** ACC clauses as written. if your policy contains ACC language in the relevant exclusion, coverage will be denied even if a covered peril contributed.

**Florida's rule**: coverage exists if:
1. no efficient proximate cause can be determined
2. covered and excluded causes jointly caused loss
3. policy does NOT contain applicable ACC provision

if ACC language exists → denial likely.

## .construction defect claims

- most policies exclude faulty workmanship, design flaws, defective materials
- BUT **subsequent damage** may be covered (water damage to ceilings from bad roof, even if roof repair is excluded)

## .practical implication for unpermitted work

| scenario | likely outcome |
|----------|----------------|
| policy has ACC clause in defect exclusion | denial if unpermitted work contributed |
| policy lacks ACC clause + covered peril contributed | coverage under Sebo |
| unpermitted work directly caused damage (no other peril) | denial |
| damage unrelated to unpermitted work | coverage |

**action items:**
- read your policy for ACC language in exclusions
- if mixed causation (storm + defect), Sebo may help
- expect litigation for mixed causation claims
