# ref.permit-required.electrical-panel-upgrade.indianapolis-in

## .what

a permit **is required** to upgrade a fuse box to a breaker panel in Indianapolis, Indiana (Marion County).

## .proof chain

```
IRC R105.1 (base code)     →  permits required for electrical work
        ↓
Indiana Residential Code 2020  →  adopts IRC 2018
        ↓
Indianapolis Ch. 536-201   →  local enforcement
        ↓
IRC R105.2 (exemptions)    →  lists what is NOT required
        ↓
panel upgrade NOT listed   →  permit required
```

## .applicable code

Indiana adopts the **Indiana Residential Code 2020**, which is based on the **International Residential Code 2018 (IRC 2018)**.

Indianapolis/Marion County enforces electrical permits under **Chapter 536 of the Indianapolis-Marion County Code of Ordinances** (Buildings and Construction), specifically **Section 536-201**.

## .code text: IRC R105.1 — permit required (baseline)

> "Any owner or authorized agent who intends to construct, enlarge, alter, repair, move demolish, or change the occupancy of a building or structure, or to erect, install, enlarge, alter, repair, remove, convert or replace any electrical, gas, mechanical or plumbing system, or to cause any such work to be done, shall first make application to the building official and obtain the required permit."

— IRC R105.1

**key phrase**: "install, enlarge, alter, repair, remove, convert or replace any electrical... system"

this establishes the baseline: **all electrical system work requires a permit by default**.

## .code text: IRC R105.2 — work exempt from permit

the following electrical work is **exempt** from permits per IRC R105.2:

> 1. "Listed cord-and-plug connected temporary decorative lighting."
>
> 2. "Reinstallation of attachment plug receptacles but not the outlets therefor."
>
> 3. "Replacement of branch circuit overcurrent devices of the required capacity in the same location."
>
> 4. "Electrical wiring, devices, appliances, apparatus or equipment operating at less than 25 volts and not capable of supplying more than 50 watts of energy."
>
> 5. "Minor repair work, including the replacement of lamps or the connection of approved portable electrical equipment to approved permanently installed receptacles."

— IRC R105.2, as adopted by Indiana Residential Code 2020

## .analysis

| work type | exempt? | reason |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| replace individual breaker (same capacity, same location) | yes | item 3: "replacement of branch circuit overcurrent devices of the required capacity in the same location" |
| replace lamp/bulb | yes | item 5: "replacement of lamps" |
| plug in portable equipment | yes | item 5: "connection of approved portable electrical equipment to approved permanently installed receptacles" |
| **upgrade fuse box to breaker panel** | **no** | not listed; constitutes service equipment replacement, not minor repair |
| service upgrade (e.g., 100A to 200A) | no | not listed; requires load calculation and inspection |
