# ref.permit-requires-local-license

## .what

to pull a permit, contractors must be licensed or registered in that jurisdiction. out-of-jurisdiction licenses do not automatically authorize permit pulls.

## .why this matters

- users may assume a state license works everywhere
- contractors may be surprised by local registration requirements
- permit applications will be rejected without proper local authorization

## .the general rule

permits require one of:
1. **homeowner status** (owner-occupied residential property), OR
2. **local contractor registration** (licensed in that jurisdiction)

a license from another city, county, or state does NOT automatically transfer.

## .exceptions to the rule

### florida - state preemption (HB 735)

**strongest counterexample.** Florida law explicitly prohibits local contractor licenses for many trades.

> as of July 1, 2025, local specialty contractor licenses were "sunset" statewide

- certified contractors can work anywhere in Florida without local licensing
- local jurisdictions prohibited from requiring local contractor licenses for: painting, flooring, cabinetry, interior remodel, tile/stone, plastering, stucco
- business tax receipts (sales tax registration) may still be required

sources: flhouse.gov, contractorcampus.com, miamidade.gov, licensesetc.com

### louisiana - state license preempts local exams

> "Upon issuance of a state license, these contractors are excluded from local, municipal, or parish regulatory authority examination procedures and may bid and perform work within any local jurisdiction upon paying all appropriate fees."

- state license allows work in any parish without re-licensing
- local occupational licenses (for tax) are separate from contractor licenses

sources: lslbc.gov, louisianacontractorauthority.com

### interstate reciprocity (limited)

| agreement | scope | caveat |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| California ↔ AZ, LA, MS, NV, NC | waives trade exam | must still apply, pass business exam |
| Florida (2020 law) | accepts contractors from any state | requires 10+ years experience or equivalent certification |
| Mississippi | reciprocity with various states | waives trade exam only |

sources: cslb.ca.gov, contractorlicensinginc.com, msboc.us

### states with true statewide licensing

| state | notes |
|-------|-------|
| Arizona | ROC licenses statewide, no separate city contractor licenses found |
| Nevada | state board issues statewide licenses |
| North Carolina | state license valid in any county |

### states with no state license (local only)

these 17 states have no statewide contractor license requirement:

Colorado, Delaware, Idaho (private work), Illinois, Indiana, Iowa, Kansas, Kentucky, Maine, Missouri, Nebraska, New Hampshire, New York, Ohio, Oklahoma, South Dakota, Wyoming

in these states, requirements are entirely local - may need one local license or none.

source: procore.com/library/contractors-license-guide-all-states

## .key nuance

**permits vs. licenses are separate concepts:**

| concept | authority | what it grants |
|---------|-----------|----------------|
| contractor license | state or local board | authorization to perform construction work |
| permit | local permit office | authorization for specific construction project |

even in states with statewide licensing (Florida, Louisiana), **permits are still local**. you apply to the local permit office. the state license just means you don't need a SECOND local contractor license.

## .summary

| pattern | prevalence |
|---------|------------|
| local contractor registration required for permits | **most jurisdictions** |
| state license preempts local licensing | Florida, Louisiana |
| statewide license with no local registration | Arizona, Nevada, North Carolina |
| no state license (local rules vary) | 17 states |
| interstate reciprocity | limited (CA, FL, MS + partners) |

**bottom line:** assume local registration is required unless you verify otherwise for that specific jurisdiction.
