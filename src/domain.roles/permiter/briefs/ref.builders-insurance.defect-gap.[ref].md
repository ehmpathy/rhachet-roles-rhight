# ref.builders-risk-defect-gap

## .what

builder's risk insurance does NOT cover direct defect damage. this is a gap for owner-builders: when defect = damage (no fire), there is no coverage.

## .why

- clarifies what builder's risk actually covers
- identifies the "no ensuing loss" gap for owner-builders
- explains why homeowners insurance doesn't fill this gap
- shows what endorsements exist to address it

---

## tl;dr

| scenario | homeowners | builder's risk | covered? |
|----------|------------|----------------|----------|
| defect → fire → damage | YES (ensuing loss) | N/A (post-construction) | YES |
| defect = damage (direct) | NO (workmanship exclusion) | NO (excludes workmanship) | NO |
| defect → collapse → damage | YES (ensuing loss) | NO (excludes workmanship) | partial |

**the gap**: when a defect causes damage WITHOUT an intervening covered peril (fire, water, collapse), neither policy covers it.

## builder's risk basics

### what it covers

| coverage | description |
|----------|-------------|
| structure under construction | materials installed on site |
| materials in transit | components en route to site |
| materials stored off-site | components at supplier |
| fire, wind, theft, vandalism | named perils |
| debris removal | cleanup after covered loss |

### what it excludes

| exclusion | why |
|-----------|-----|
| faulty workmanship | the work itself, not damage that results |
| design defects | same as workmanship |
| normal wear and tear | not a sudden loss |
| employee injuries | requires GL or workers comp |
| professional errors | requires E&O coverage |
| **defective materials** | requires endorsement |

**source**: [The Hartford - Builder's Risk Insurance](https://www.thehartford.com/insights/construction/builders-risk-insurance)

## the defect = damage gap

### when defect IS the damage

| example | covered? | why not |
|---------|----------|---------|
| improper foundation → cracks | NO | defect manifest, no ensuing peril |
| faulty wire → arc marks | NO | defect manifest, no fire |
| undersized beam → sag | NO | defect manifest, no collapse |

in these scenarios:
- homeowners excludes faulty workmanship
- builder's risk excludes faulty workmanship
- no policy responds

### when defect CAUSES damage (ensuing loss)

| example | covered? | why |
|---------|----------|-----|
| improper wire → fire → house damage | YES | fire is covered peril |
| faulty plumbing → burst → water damage | YES | water is covered peril |
| undersized beam → collapse → structural damage | YES | collapse is covered peril |

**the test**: did something ELSE (fire, water, collapse) happen between defect and damage?

---

## LEG 3 endorsement (partial fix)

### what it is

LEG 3 (London Engineering Group) is an endorsement that expands builder's risk coverage for defect-related damage.

### what it covers

| coverage | example |
|----------|---------|
| damage CAUSED BY defect | faulty concrete → structural failure → damage to other components |
| does NOT cover | cost to repair the defective concrete itself |

### limitations

| what's covered | what's excluded |
|----------------|-----------------|
| consequential damage to OTHER property | cost to fix the defect |
| repair of non-defective work damaged | replacement of defective work |

**source**: [Government Construction Law](https://www.governmentconstructionlaw.com/2024/01/does-builders-risk-insurance-cover-costs-to-repair-or-replace-defective-work/)

## owner-builder specific gaps

### what builder's risk provides owner-builders

| coverage | status |
|----------|--------|
| structure during construction | YES |
| materials and supplies | YES |
| fire, theft, vandalism | YES |
| your own faulty work | NO |
| liability for injuries | NO (separate GL needed) |

### the liability gap

builder's risk is **property** coverage, not **liability** coverage.

| scenario | covered by builder's risk? |
|----------|---------------------------|
| visitor injured on site | NO — need GL |
| subcontractor damages neighbor's property | NO — need GL |
| your work causes someone else's injury | NO — need GL |

**source**: [UBuildIt - Owner-Builder Insurance](https://www.ubuildit.com/insurance-for-owner-builders/)

## comparison: contractor vs owner-builder

### if contractor's work is defective

| step | what happens |
|------|--------------|
| 1 | defect discovered (with or without fire) |
| 2 | contractor's GL or E&O responds |
| 3 | contractor's insurance pays repair |
| 4 | homeowner made whole |

### if owner-builder's work is defective

| step | what happens |
|------|--------------|
| 1 | defect discovered (no fire) |
| 2 | builder's risk excludes workmanship |
| 3 | homeowners excludes workmanship |
| 4 | owner-builder pays out of pocket |

**the gap**: owner-builders have no third-party insurance to cover their own defective work.

---

## options to fill the gap

### during construction

| option | covers | cost |
|--------|--------|------|
| LEG 3 endorsement | damage FROM defect (not defect itself) | varies |
| extended builder's risk | debris, scaffolds, some defects | +10-20% |
| separate GL policy | liability to third parties | $500-2000/yr |

### post-construction

| option | covers | limitation |
|--------|--------|------------|
| homeowners (ensuing loss) | fire/water from defect | not defect itself |
| warranty from contractors | subcontractor's work | owner-builder = no warranty |
| home warranty plan | systems/appliances | excludes prior defects |

## practical implications

### for owner-builders with electrical work

| scenario | insurance response |
|----------|-------------------|
| loose connection → fire → house burns | COVERED (ensuing loss) |
| loose connection → arc marks, no fire | NOT COVERED |
| improper wire gauge → overheat → fire | COVERED (ensuing loss) |
| improper wire gauge → code violation found | NOT COVERED |

**the pattern**: fire = coverage. no fire = no coverage.

### risk assessment

| risk | likelihood | severity | mitigation |
|------|------------|----------|------------|
| defect causes fire | LOW | HIGH | ensuing loss covers |
| defect found, no fire | MEDIUM | MEDIUM | out of pocket repair |
| defect causes injury | LOW | HIGH | GL policy needed |
