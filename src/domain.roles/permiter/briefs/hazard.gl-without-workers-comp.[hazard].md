# hazard.gl-without-workers-comp

## .what

contractor has GL but no workers' comp. you think you're protected. you're not.

## .why

- GL explicitly excludes employee injuries
- without comp, no exclusive remedy blocks lawsuits
- worker sues YOU, GL denies, your homeowners defends
- most common construction injury scenario = unprotected

## why GL doesn't cover worker injuries

### the policy language

every GL policy contains an "employer's liability exclusion":

> "This insurance does not apply to bodily injury to an employee of the insured arising out of and in the course of employment by the insured."

**source**: [ISO CGL Form CG 00 01](https://www.irmi.com/term/insurance-definitions/employers-liability-exclusion)

### the logic

| coverage | purpose | employee injuries? |
|----------|---------|-------------------|
| workers' comp | pay employees for job injuries | YES — primary |
| GL | pay third parties for injuries | NO — excluded |

GL assumes workers' comp exists. when it doesn't, the gap is YOUR problem.

---

## why workers' comp is irreplaceable

### what comp does that GL cannot

| function | workers' comp | GL |
|----------|---------------|-----|
| pays worker medical bills | YES | NO |
| pays worker lost wages | YES | NO |
| blocks worker lawsuits | YES (exclusive remedy) | NO |
| covers employee injuries | YES | NO (excluded) |

### the exclusive remedy doctrine

> "Workers' compensation is the exclusive remedy for employees injured on the job. In exchange for guaranteed benefits, employees give up the right to sue their employer — or in many cases, the property owner."

**source**: [NOLO - Workers' Comp Exclusive Remedy](https://www.nolo.com/legal-encyclopedia/can-i-sue-my-employer-workplace-injury.html)

without comp:
- worker gets no guaranteed benefits
- worker retains full right to sue
- worker sues everyone with money (including you)

## the false sense of security

### what contractor says

> "I have a million dollars in GL coverage. You're protected."

### what that actually means

| scenario | GL covers? |
|----------|-----------|
| contractor damages neighbor's fence | YES |
| visitor trips on contractor's equipment | YES |
| contractor's employee falls off ladder | **NO** |
| contractor's employee electrocuted | **NO** |
| contractor's employee cuts hand | **NO** |

**the most common construction injuries (worker falls, cuts, strains) are ALL excluded from GL.**

## who is at risk

### high-risk contractor profiles

| profile | comp likely? | your risk |
|---------|--------------|-----------|
| licensed GC with employees | YES | LOW |
| small contractor, 1-2 assistants | MAYBE | HIGH |
| handyman with "assistant" | NO | VERY HIGH |
| day laborer / cash worker | NO | EXTREME |

### the "assistant" problem

contractor says: "he's not my employee, he's just assisting"

reality:
- if contractor controls the work, "assistant" is an employee
- if assistant is injured, assistant sues YOU
- your homeowners treats this as uninsured contractor
- you're fully exposed

**source**: [AllLaw - Contractor Injury Liability](https://www.alllaw.com/articles/nolo/personal-injury/homeowner-liable-injuries-contractor.html)

## what to require

### before work starts

| document | verifies |
|----------|----------|
| workers' comp certificate | comp coverage exists |
| GL certificate | GL coverage exists |
| both active for project dates | no gaps |

### if contractor claims exemption

| contractor says | your response |
|-----------------|---------------|
| "I'm a sole proprietor, exempt" | "show me the state exemption certificate" |
| "I don't have employees" | "who is the person with you?" |
| "he's my brother, not employee" | "if he's paid or regular, he's an employee" |
| "I'll get comp if needed" | "get it before you start, or find another contractor" |

### verify exemption legitimacy

| state | sole proprietor exempt? | source |
|-------|------------------------|--------|
| Indiana | YES (can opt out) | [IN Workers' Comp Board](https://www.in.gov/wcb/) |
| California | NO (must carry or waive) | [CA DIR](https://www.dir.ca.gov/dwc/) |
| Texas | YES (not required) | [TX DWC](https://www.tdi.texas.gov/wc/) |

**check your state** — exemptions vary.
