# rule.require-sole-proprietor-waiver

## .what

when contractor claims sole proprietor exemption from workers' comp, **REQUIRE** a signed waiver/hold harmless agreement. without it, they can sue you if injured.

## .why

- sole proprietors are often exempt from comp requirements
- without comp, no exclusive remedy blocks their lawsuit
- a signed waiver shifts liability back to them
- BUT waivers have limits — not a perfect substitute for comp

## the rule

```
CONTRACTOR CLAIMS SOLE PROPRIETOR EXEMPTION
                    │
                    ▼
         do they have employees?
                    │
         ┌──────────┴──────────┐
         ▼                     ▼
        YES                   NO
         │                     │
         ▼                     ▼
    NOT EXEMPT           may be exempt
    REQUIRE COMP              │
                              ▼
                    REQUIRE EITHER:
                    ├─ comp anyway (best)
                    └─ signed waiver + hold harmless
```

## why sole proprietors can sue you

### the gap

| with workers' comp | without workers' comp |
|-------------------|----------------------|
| comp pays medical/wages | no guaranteed benefits |
| exclusive remedy blocks suit | can sue anyone |
| you're protected | you're exposed |

### what happens if uninsured sole proprietor is injured

| step | outcome |
|------|---------|
| 1 | sole proprietor injured on your property |
| 2 | no comp = no exclusive remedy |
| 3 | sole proprietor sues YOU |
| 4 | your homeowners defends |
| 5 | $20K-100K+ defense costs |

## state exemption rules

### who is typically exempt

| state | sole proprietor exempt? | source |
|-------|------------------------|--------|
| Indiana | YES — can opt out | [IN Workers' Comp Board](https://www.in.gov/wcb/) |
| California | YES — must file exemption | [CSLB](https://www.cslb.ca.gov/OnlineServices/WebApplication/InteractivePDFs/WorkersCompensationExemption.aspx) |
| Texas | YES — comp not required | [TX DWC](https://www.tdi.texas.gov/wc/) |
| New York | YES — but must file CE-200 | [NY WCB](https://www.wcb.ny.gov/icexempt/wavhelp.jsp) |
| Georgia | YES — file Form WC-10 | [GA SBWC](https://sbwc.georgia.gov/) |

**source**: [The Hartford - Workers' Comp Exemption](https://www.thehartford.com/workers-compensation/workers-comp-exemption)

### verify the exemption is legitimate

| contractor says | your response |
|-----------------|---------------|
| "I'm exempt as sole proprietor" | "show me state exemption certificate" |
| "I filed the form" | "provide copy of approved exemption" |
| "I don't need one in this state" | verify with state — requirements vary |

## the waiver solution

### what to require

a signed **waiver of liability and hold harmless agreement** that:

1. acknowledges contractor is NOT your employee
2. acknowledges contractor is responsible for own insurance
3. releases you from liability for contractor's injuries
4. indemnifies you if contractor sues anyway

### sample language

> "Contractor acknowledges that Contractor is an independent contractor, not an employee of Owner. Contractor represents that Contractor maintains adequate insurance coverage for any injuries sustained while performing work. Contractor releases Owner from any liability for bodily injury sustained by Contractor while performing work on the Property. Contractor agrees to indemnify and hold harmless Owner from any claims, suits, or damages related to Contractor's injury on the Property."

**source**: [LegalZoom - Hold Harmless Agreement](https://www.legalzoom.com/articles/using-a-hold-harmless-agreement)

## limitations of waivers

### waivers are NOT perfect protection

| limitation | explanation |
|------------|-------------|
| **state law varies** | some states limit enforceability |
| **void if too broad** | courts may strike down overreaching clauses |
| **negligence exception** | may not protect you if YOU were negligent |
| **fraud/duress voids** | invalid if contractor was tricked/forced |
| **public policy limits** | some states won't enforce against workers |

**source**: [Insureon - Hold Harmless Agreement](https://www.insureon.com/insurance-glossary/hold-harmless-agreement)

### states that limit waivers

| state | limitation |
|-------|------------|
| New York | Labor Law makes property owners liable regardless |
| California | strict limits on indemnification |
| Louisiana | anti-indemnity statute |
| many others | partial enforcement only |

**bottom line**: waiver helps but is NOT a guarantee.

## protection hierarchy

### ranked by reliability

| rank | protection | why |
|------|------------|-----|
| 1 | **contractor carries comp** | exclusive remedy blocks all suits |
| 2 | **signed waiver + your umbrella** | waiver shifts liability; umbrella covers gaps |
| 3 | **signed waiver only** | some protection; enforceability uncertain |
| 4 | **verbal assurance** | NO protection |
| 5 | **no documentation** | fully exposed |

## what to actually do

### if contractor has comp

| action | outcome |
|--------|---------|
| request certificate | verify coverage |
| verify active dates | confirm covers project |
| done | you're protected |

### if contractor claims exemption

| action | outcome |
|--------|---------|
| request state exemption certificate | verify legitimate exemption |
| request signed waiver/hold harmless | shift liability |
| document everything in text | evidence if needed |
| consider requiring comp anyway | strongest protection |

### if contractor refuses both

| contractor says | your response |
|-----------------|---------------|
| "I don't need comp or waiver" | "then I can't hire you" |
| "no one asks for this" | "I do — it protects us both" |
| "it's insulting" | "it's standard risk management" |

## permit implications

### some jurisdictions require proof

> "The law requires homeowners to provide proof of workers' compensation compliance when applying for a building permit. If the homeowner qualifies for an exemption, the homeowner must complete Form CE-200 and file it with the local building department."

**source**: [NY WCB - Attestation of Exemption](https://www.wcb.ny.gov/icexempt/wavhelp.jsp)

### Indiana example

> "Upon approval of both the Department of Revenue and the Worker's Compensation Board, you will receive your validated Certificate of Exemption."

**source**: [Indiana WCE-1 Form](https://forms.in.gov/download.aspx?id=7134)
