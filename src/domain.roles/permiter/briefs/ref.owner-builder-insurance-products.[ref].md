# ref.owner-builder-insurance-products

## .what

insurance products that cover owner-builders while work is in progress under an open permit.

## .why this matters

- standard homeowner's insurance doesn't cover active construction
- owner-builders face property and liability gaps
- specific products exist to fill these gaps
- cost is typically 1-5% of project value

---

## the coverage gap

### why homeowner's insurance doesn't cover construction

| homeowner's policy | builder's risk policy |
|--------------------|----------------------|
| designed for occupancy | designed for construction |
| covers finished home | covers work in progress |
| excludes "course of construction" | specifically covers it |
| liability for residents/guests | liability for workers/subs |

> "A standard homeowners insurance policy does not offer adequate coverage for many risks that personal lines clients face during construction."
> — [US Assure](https://usassure.com/resources/articles/builders-risk-insurance-for-homeowners-when-is-it-needed)

---

## primary product: builder's risk insurance

also called: **course of construction insurance**

### what it covers

| coverage | description |
|----------|-------------|
| property damage | fire, storm, vandalism to structure |
| theft | stolen materials, equipment, fixtures |
| materials in transit | supplies damaged before arrival |
| materials on-site | supplies stored at job site |
| soft costs | delays, permit re-fees, architect time |
| natural disasters | hurricane, tornado, severe weather |

### what it does NOT cover

| exclusion | notes |
|-----------|-------|
| faulty workmanship | the defect itself, not consequential damage |
| design errors | architect/engineer malpractice |
| employee injuries | need workers' comp separately |
| contractor tools | contractor's own policy |
| earth movement | earthquake, landslide (rider available) |
| flood | separate flood policy needed |

### who can purchase

> "Almost every entity or person with a financial interest in the construction project may be allowed to purchase builder's risk insurance."
> — [Distinguished](https://distinguished.com/blog/builders-risk-insurance-for-homeowners/)

owner-builders qualify. you don't need to be a licensed contractor.

### cost

| project value | typical annual cost | rate |
|---------------|---------------------|------|
| $50,000 | $500-2,500 | 1-5% |
| $100,000 | $1,000-5,000 | 1-5% |
| $200,000 | $2,000-8,000 | 1-4% |

> "Builder's risk insurance costs between 1% and 4% of the budget."
> — [Insurance.com](https://www.insurance.com/home-and-renters-insurance/home-insurance-basics/building-a-house.html)

**renovation vs new construction**: renovation policies often cost more because they must cover both new work AND the value of the structure that could be damaged.

### policy duration

| term | use case |
|------|----------|
| 3 months | small project |
| 6 months | medium renovation |
| 12 months | major renovation, new build |
| extensions | available with underwriter approval |

## secondary product: general liability insurance

### what it covers

| coverage | description |
|----------|-------------|
| bodily injury | visitor/worker injured on site |
| property damage | damage to neighbor's property |
| personal injury | defamation, ad injury |
| legal defense | attorney fees for covered claims |

### why owner-builders need it

> "If your workers are injured, or your subcontractors are not licensed or do not carry liability insurance, you could face lawsuits."
> — [CSLB](https://www.cslb.ca.gov/consumers/know_risks_of_owner_-_builder/The_Downside_Of_Being_An_Owner_-_Builder.aspx)

### cost

| coverage limit | typical annual cost |
|----------------|---------------------|
| $500k/$1M | $400-800 |
| $1M/$2M | $600-1,500 |

## workers' compensation considerations

### the rule

| situation | workers' comp required? |
|-----------|------------------------|
| you work alone | NO |
| you hire employees | YES (most states) |
| you hire licensed subs | they carry their own |
| you hire unlicensed workers | YOU may be liable |

### owner-builder exposure

> "Workers' compensation insurance typically excludes owners, volunteers and independent contractors."
> — [The Hartford](https://www.thehartford.com/workers-compensation/self-employed-independent-contractors)

**risk**: if you hire unlicensed workers who get injured, your homeowner's policy may not cover it, and you could face lawsuits.

**mitigation**: only hire licensed, insured subcontractors — verify their coverage.

## combined products

some insurers offer bundled owner-builder packages:

| package | includes |
|---------|----------|
| owner-builder policy | builder's risk + general liability |
| construction package | builder's risk + liability + umbrella |

## cost summary for owner-builder project

| product | typical cost | covers |
|---------|--------------|--------|
| builder's risk | 1-5% of project | property, materials, theft |
| general liability | $400-1,500/year | injuries, lawsuits |
| workers' comp | varies | employee injuries (if applicable) |

**example**: $100,000 renovation
- builder's risk: $1,000-5,000
- general liability: $600-1,000
- **total**: ~$1,600-6,000 for full coverage

---

## when coverage applies

| phase | homeowner's policy | builder's risk |
|-------|-------------------|----------------|
| before permit pulled | YES | NO |
| permit open, work active | LIMITED | YES |
| work complete, final inspection | TRANSITION | ENDS |
| certificate of occupancy issued | YES | NO |

**important**: coordinate with your homeowner's insurer. some policies automatically exclude active construction; others provide limited coverage.

---

## how to purchase

### steps

1. estimate project value (materials + labor value)
2. determine project duration
3. get quotes from:
   - your homeowner's insurer (ask about endorsements)
   - specialized construction insurers
   - independent agents
4. verify coverage for owner-builder (not all policies allow)
5. name all interested parties (you, lender if applicable)

### questions to ask

| question | why it matters |
|----------|----------------|
| does this cover owner-builder? | some policies require licensed GC |
| what perils are covered? | fire, theft, weather, etc. |
| is renovation covered? | some only cover new construction |
| what's excluded? | flood, earthquake, faulty work |
| can I extend if needed? | projects often run over |
