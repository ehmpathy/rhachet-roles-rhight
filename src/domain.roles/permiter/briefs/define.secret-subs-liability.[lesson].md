# define.secret-subs-liability

## .what

when a GC uses unauthorized or uninsured subcontractors ("secret subs"), contract language does NOT protect you. the certificate of insurance does.

## .why

- contract language = recourse AFTER lawsuit
- additional insured status = defense DURING lawsuit
- you cannot control who GC actually brings to site
- your only reliable protection is GC's GL policy covering you

---

## tl;dr

| protection type | when it helps | reliability |
|-----------------|---------------|-------------|
| certificate (additional insured) | DURING lawsuit — their GL defends you | **HIGH** |
| contract language | AFTER lawsuit — sue GC for breach | **LOW** |

**get the certificate, not the promise.**

## why contract language fails

### what the contract says

> "Contractor represents that all work shall be performed by Contractor's employees or by subcontractors who maintain workers' compensation and general liability insurance with minimum limits of $1,000,000."

### what happens when GC violates it

| step | what happens |
|------|--------------|
| 1 | GC brings uninsured helper anyway |
| 2 | helper gets injured |
| 3 | helper has no workers' comp (no exclusive remedy) |
| 4 | helper sues YOU (property owner) |
| 5 | you defend yourself ($20K-100K+) |
| 6 | THEN you sue GC for breach of contract |
| 7 | GC may be judgment-proof (no assets) |
| 8 | you absorb the loss |

**the contract gave you a claim, not protection.**

---

## why additional insured works

### what the certificate shows

```
CERTIFICATE OF LIABILITY INSURANCE

INSURED: ABC Construction LLC
ADDITIONAL INSURED: [YOUR NAME], [YOUR ADDRESS]

COVERAGE: Commercial General Liability
LIMITS: $1,000,000 per occurrence / $2,000,000 aggregate

THIS CERTIFICATE CONFIRMS COVERAGE FOR CLAIMS ARISING FROM
THE NAMED INSURED'S OPERATIONS AT THE ABOVE LOCATION.
```

### what happens when secret sub causes injury

| step | what happens |
|------|--------------|
| 1 | GC brings uninsured helper |
| 2 | helper gets injured |
| 3 | helper sues YOU |
| 4 | you tender claim to GC's GL insurer |
| 5 | GC's GL defends you (you're additional insured) |
| 6 | claim "arises from GC's operations" — covered |
| 7 | their policy pays defense, their premiums affected |
| 8 | you're protected |

**the certificate gave you actual protection.**

---

## why GC's GL covers secret subs

### the policy language

GL policies cover claims "arising out of the named insured's operations."

| who caused injury | arises from GC's operations? | covered? |
|-------------------|------------------------------|----------|
| GC's direct employee | YES | YES |
| GC's authorized sub | YES | YES |
| GC's unauthorized sub | YES — GC brought them | YES |
| GC's secret helper | YES — GC brought them | YES |
| random trespasser | NO | NO |

**key insight**: the GL policy doesn't care if the sub was authorized. it covers claims arising from GC's operations — and GC brought that person to your site.

## the critical difference

### contract = promise

| characteristic | contract language |
|----------------|-------------------|
| what it is | GC's promise about future behavior |
| enforcement | sue for breach AFTER harm occurs |
| depends on | GC having assets to collect |
| timing | helps you recover AFTER lawsuit |

### certificate = coverage

| characteristic | additional insured certificate |
|----------------|-------------------------------|
| what it is | proof you're covered under their policy |
| enforcement | insurance company defends you |
| depends on | policy being active (insurer has assets) |
| timing | helps you DURING lawsuit |

## what you can actually control

| action | you control it? | value |
|--------|-----------------|-------|
| require certificate before work starts | YES | **HIGH** |
| verify you're named as additional insured | YES | **HIGH** |
| confirm policy dates cover project | YES | **HIGH** |
| withhold payment until certificates received | YES | **HIGH** |
| monitor who GC brings to site daily | NO | LOW |
| force GC to obey contract | NO | LOW |
| prevent GC from lying | NO | LOW |

---

## the only contract clause that matters

### payment tied to verification

> "Owner shall withhold 10% of each progress payment until Contractor provides certificates of insurance for all subcontractors currently performing work on the project. Final payment shall not be due until all certificates have been provided."

### why this works

| mechanism | how it helps |
|-----------|--------------|
| money is leverage | GC wants to get paid |
| ongoing verification | not just upfront |
| you control release | not relying on GC's honesty |

## the protection stack

### ranked by reliability

| rank | protection | why |
|------|------------|-----|
| 1 | GC's GL + you as additional insured | insurer defends you regardless of who GC used |
| 2 | payment tied to sub certificates | you control the leverage |
| 3 | your umbrella policy | backup layer above homeowners |
| 4 | GC's workers' comp | covers GC's direct employees |
| 5 | contract indemnification | recourse after, if GC has assets |
| 6 | contract representations | evidence for breach claim |

## what to actually do

### before work starts

| action | outcome |
|--------|---------|
| require GC's GL certificate | verify coverage exists |
| verify YOU are named as additional insured | confirm your protection |
| verify policy dates | confirm coverage spans project |
| add payment-withhold clause to contract | create ongoing leverage |

### during work

| action | outcome |
|--------|---------|
| request sub certificates before each draw | ongoing verification |
| withhold payment if missing | enforce compliance |
| document who's on site | evidence if needed |

### if GC refuses

| GC says | your response |
|---------|---------------|
| "I don't use subs" | "great, then no certificates needed — but if I see anyone else, work stops" |
| "my GL covers all of it" | "then add me as additional insured — it's free" |
| "that's not standard" | "it's standard in commercial — I require it" |
| refuses entirely | find another GC |
