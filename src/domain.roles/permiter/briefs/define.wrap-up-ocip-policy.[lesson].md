# define.wrap-up-ocip-policy

## .what

a wrap-up or OCIP (Owner Controlled Insurance Program) is a single insurance policy purchased by the property owner that covers ALL workers on a project — GC, subs, and secret subs alike.

## .why

- eliminates certificate chasing
- covers workers you don't know exist (secret subs)
- single policy = no coverage gaps
- owner controls the program, not GC
- typically used on large projects but available for residential

---

## tl;dr

| traditional model | wrap-up / OCIP |
|-------------------|----------------|
| GC buys own GL + comp | owner buys single policy |
| each sub buys own | all subs covered under owner's policy |
| you chase certificates | no certificates needed |
| secret subs = gap | secret subs = covered |
| action over exclusion = your problem | no exclusion issue — your policy |

**wrap-up = you control the coverage, not GC.**

---

## how it works

### traditional insurance model

```
PROPERTY OWNER
      │
      ├── homeowners liability (yours)
      │
      ▼
GENERAL CONTRACTOR
      │
      ├── GC's workers' comp (GC's employees)
      ├── GC's GL (third-party claims)
      │
      ▼
SUBCONTRACTOR A          SUBCONTRACTOR B          SECRET SUB
      │                        │                       │
      ├── sub A comp           ├── sub B comp          ├── no comp
      ├── sub A GL             ├── sub B GL            ├── no GL
      │                        │                       │
      ▼                        ▼                       ▼
   COVERED                  COVERED                  GAP
```

### wrap-up / OCIP model

```
PROPERTY OWNER
      │
      ├── OCIP policy (covers entire project)
      │     │
      │     ├── workers' comp for all
      │     ├── GL for all
      │     ├── excess liability
      │     └── builders risk (optional)
      │
      ▼
ALL WORKERS ON SITE — COVERED
      │
      ├── GC employees — covered
      ├── Sub A employees — covered
      ├── Sub B employees — covered
      └── Secret sub employees — COVERED
```

## what OCIP covers

### typical coverage lines

| coverage | what it covers |
|----------|----------------|
| workers' compensation | all worker injuries on project |
| general liability | third-party injuries, property damage |
| excess / umbrella | limits above primary policies |
| builders risk | structure under construction |
| professional liability (optional) | design errors |
| pollution liability (optional) | environmental claims |

**source**: [Construction Coverage - OCIP Insurance](https://constructioncoverage.com/ocip-insurance)

### who is covered

| party | covered under OCIP? |
|-------|---------------------|
| property owner | YES |
| general contractor | YES |
| named subcontractors | YES |
| sub-subcontractors | YES |
| day laborers on site | YES |
| secret subs | **YES** — anyone on site |

**source**: [Embroker - Wrap-Up Insurance](https://www.embroker.com/coverage/ocips-and-ccips/)

## why OCIP protects against secret subs

### the coverage trigger

OCIP covers all "bodily injury arising out of operations at the project site."

| factor | how OCIP handles it |
|--------|---------------------|
| worker is GC's employee? | irrelevant — covered |
| worker is authorized sub? | irrelevant — covered |
| worker is secret sub? | irrelevant — covered |
| worker has own comp? | irrelevant — OCIP is primary |

**the question is not "who employed them" but "were they injured at the project site?"**

### vs. traditional model

| scenario | traditional | OCIP |
|----------|-------------|------|
| secret sub injured | depends on GC's policy language | **COVERED** |
| action over exclusion | blocks coverage | **no exclusion — your policy** |
| sub has no comp | can sue you | **OCIP comp pays, exclusive remedy** |

## OCIP vs. CCIP

### two types of wrap-up

| type | who purchases | who controls |
|------|---------------|--------------|
| OCIP | property owner | property owner |
| CCIP | general contractor | general contractor |

### for homeowners

| choice | recommendation |
|--------|----------------|
| OCIP | **preferred** — you control coverage |
| CCIP | acceptable if GC is reputable and you verify terms |

## cost structure

### how price is set

| factor | impact on premium |
|--------|-------------------|
| project value | primary driver — larger = higher |
| project duration | longer = higher |
| trade types | high-risk trades = higher |
| location | state workers' comp rates vary |
| claims history | prior claims = higher |

### typical cost range

| project size | OCIP cost (approximate) |
|--------------|------------------------|
| $500K renovation | $15K-30K |
| $1M+ new build | $30K-60K |
| $5M+ commercial | $100K-200K |

**cost is typically 1-3% of project value.**

### cost savings

| savings source | how much |
|----------------|----------|
| volume discount | 10-20% vs. individual policies |
| no duplicate coverage | eliminates overlap |
| reduced admin | one broker, one policy |
| claims control | you manage, not GC |

**source**: [Davis Wright Tremaine - Pros and Cons of OCIP](https://www.dwt.com/blogs/government-contracts-insider/2024/02/pros-and-cons-of-ocip-project-insurance-coverage)

## when to consider OCIP

### good candidates

| scenario | OCIP makes sense? |
|----------|-------------------|
| major renovation ($250K+) | YES |
| new construction | YES |
| multiple subs (5+) | YES |
| high-risk trades (roof, electrical) | YES |
| GC has questionable insurance | YES |
| you want control over coverage | YES |

### less suitable

| scenario | why OCIP may not fit |
|----------|---------------------|
| small project (<$100K) | cost may exceed benefit |
| single trade (e.g., paint only) | simple certificate works |
| licensed GC with strong coverage | traditional model adequate |

### providers

| provider type | examples |

| specialty wrap-up brokers | Alliant, Willis Towers Watson, Aon |

|------|---------|

## OCIP administration

### your responsibilities

| task | what's involved |
|------|-----------------|
| enroll all contractors | GC and subs must be enrolled in program |
| collect payroll data | comp premiums based on payroll |
| file monthly reports | track who's on site |
| manage claims | you're the point of contact |

### admin options

| option | cost |
|--------|------|
| self-administer | $0 but time-intensive |
| broker administers | included in commission |
| third-party administrator | $5K-20K based on project |

---

## contract language for OCIP

### require enrollment

> "All contractors and subcontractors performing work on the Project shall be enrolled in the Owner's Owner Controlled Insurance Program (OCIP). Contractors shall not include insurance costs in their bids for coverages provided by the OCIP. Contractors shall provide payroll and other information required for OCIP administration."

### coverage confirmation

> "The OCIP provides workers' compensation and general liability coverage for all enrolled contractors and their employees while at work at the Project site. This coverage applies regardless of tier of subcontractor."

---

## limitations

| limitation | explanation |
|------------|-------------|
| doesn't cover off-site work | only project site |
| doesn't cover contractor's other jobs | project-specific |
| admin burden | you must track enrollment |
| upfront cost | premium due at start |
| minimum project size | some insurers require $1M+ |
