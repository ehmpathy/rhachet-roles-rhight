# hazard.sued-from-hired-builder-negligence

## .what

when your hired contractor causes injury or damage to a third party, YOU get sued too — even though you didn't do the work.

## .why

- plaintiffs sue everyone with money (joint and several liability)
- your home equity makes you a target
- defense costs $20K-100K+ even if you "win"
- without additional insured status, your policy pays your defense

---

```
CONTRACTOR NEGLIGENCE
```

## additional insured vs not: the real difference

### defense cost comparison

| scenario | without additional insured | with additional insured |
|----------|---------------------------|------------------------|
| who defends you | your homeowners policy | contractor's GL policy |
| defense costs paid by | your policy limits | their policy limits |
| claim on whose record | YOURS | THEIRS |
| premium impact | YOUR premiums rise | their premiums rise |
| non-renewal risk | YOU face it | they face it |

### cost of defense (litigation benchmarks)

| dispute value | defense cost through trial | source |
|---------------|---------------------------|--------|
| $250K at stake | $91K-145K | Norton Rose Fulbright 2024 |
| $500K at stake | $150K-250K | industry estimate |
| $1M+ at stake | $200K-500K+ | industry estimate |

**source**: [Norton Rose Fulbright 2024 Litigation Trends Survey](https://www.nortonrosefulbright.com/-/media/files/nrf/nrfweb/knowledge-pdfs/norton-rose-fulbright---2024-annual-litigation-trends-survey.pdf)

### the leverage problem

builders use additional insured claims as settlement leverage:

> "The builder uses the disputed additional-insured claim as settlement leverage... demanding an amount that reflects not only the subcontractor's supposed liability for the alleged construction defect, but also the amount that represents the builder's defense costs, which can exceed the damages allegedly resulting from the construction defect."

**source**: [CLM Magazine - Outsized Influence](https://www.theclm.org/Magazine/articles/outsized-influence/3308)

### outcome comparison

| outcome | without additional insured | with additional insured |
|---------|---------------------------|------------------------|
| case dismissed early | you paid $10-30K defense | they paid your defense |
| summary judgment (win) | you paid $30-60K | they paid |
| trial (win) | you paid $50-150K | they paid |
| settlement | you contribute + defense costs | they pay most/all |
| your claims record | claim filed | no claim on your record |
| your premiums | +10-40% for 3-5 years | unaffected |

## mitigation: additional insured status

### how it protects you

| with additional insured | effect |
|------------------------|--------|
| contractor's GL defends you | their policy pays your attorney |
| their limits apply | not your homeowners limits |
| their claims record | not your claims record |
| their premium impact | not your premium impact |

### what to require

| document | purpose |
|----------|---------|
| certificate of insurance | proves coverage exists |
| additional insured endorsement | names YOU on their policy |
| primary and non-contributory | their policy pays first |
| waiver of subrogation | they can't sue you later |

### verify these minimums

| coverage | minimum |
|----------|---------|
| general liability | $1M per occurrence |
| aggregate | $2M |
| completed operations | included |
| workers compensation | state minimum |

---

## the contract clause

### require this language

> "Contractor shall name Owner as additional insured on Contractor's commercial general liability policy. Contractor's insurance shall be primary and non-contributory. Contractor shall provide certificate of insurance that proves such coverage prior to start of work."

### what each term means

| term | protection |
|------|------------|
| additional insured | you're covered under their policy |
| primary | their policy responds first |
| non-contributory | your policy doesn't share costs |
| certificate | proof before work starts |

## if contractor is uninsured

### your exposure

| scenario | who pays |
|----------|----------|

| contractor damages neighbor | YOU (homeowners liability) |
| contractor's worker injured | YOU (possibly) |
| judgment exceeds homeowners limits | YOU (personal assets) |

### the worst case

| event | consequence |
|-------|-------------|
| uninsured contractor causes $500K injury | — |
| your homeowners liability limit | $300K |
| judgment against you | $500K |
| your homeowners pays | $300K |
| you pay personally | $200K |
| result | lien on your home, wage garnishment |

## red flags: skip this contractor

| red flag | risk |
|----------|------|
| no certificate of insurance | may be uninsured |
| won't add you as additional insured | problem with coverage |
| "my insurance covers all of it" | doesn't understand coverage |
| no workers comp | you're liable for worker injuries |
| certificate shows expired dates | lapsed coverage |
| low limits ($100K GL) | insufficient for serious injury |
