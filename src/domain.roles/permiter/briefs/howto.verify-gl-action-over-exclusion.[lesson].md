# howto.verify-gl-action-over-exclusion

## .what

a certificate of insurance proves coverage exists — it does NOT prove that coverage applies to subcontractor injuries. you must verify the policy itself for action over exclusions.

## .why

- certificate = proof of coverage
- action over exclusion = coverage won't apply to sub injuries
- additional insured status is worthless if the claim is excluded
- you need to see the policy or get broker confirmation

---

## tl;dr

| document | what it tells you |
|----------|-------------------|
| certificate of insurance | coverage exists, limits, you're additional insured |
| policy declarations page | coverage exists, endorsements attached |
| policy endorsements | whether action over exclusion exists |
| broker letter | written confirmation of coverage scope |

**the certificate is not enough. request the endorsements.**

---

## what to request

### minimum: certificate + endorsements list

| document | purpose |
|----------|---------|
| certificate of insurance (COI) | proves GL exists, you're additional insured |
| list of policy endorsements | shows what exclusions are attached |
| additional insured endorsement copy | proves you're actually on it |

### better: specific endorsement review

| document | purpose |
|----------|---------|
| CG 21 47 (Employment-Related Practices Exclusion) | check if attached |
| CG 22 95 (Total Pollution Exclusion with Building Heating Equipment Exception) | not relevant here |
| any endorsement titled "Action Over" or "Labor Law" or "Worker Injury" | **the danger zone** |

### best: broker confirmation letter

request a letter from GC's insurance broker that states:

> "We confirm that [GC Name]'s Commercial General Liability policy does not contain an action over exclusion, labor law exclusion, or worker injury exclusion that would preclude coverage for bodily injury claims by employees of subcontractors. The employers liability exclusion applies only to employees of the named insured, not to employees of subcontractors."

---

## what to look for

### exclusion language that creates the gap

**standard employer's liability (OK):**
> "This insurance does not apply to bodily injury to an employee of the insured..."

this only excludes GC's own employees. sub employees are NOT "employees of the insured."

**action over exclusion (PROBLEM):**
> "This insurance does not apply to bodily injury to any employee of the insured, any contractor, or any employee of any contractor..."

this excludes ALL contractor employees — including subs.

**labor law exclusion (PROBLEM):**
> "This insurance does not apply to any claim for bodily injury to an employee of any contractor or subcontractor..."

same effect — excludes sub employee claims.

---

## endorsement names to watch for

| endorsement name | risk |
|------------------|------|
| "Action Over Exclusion" | HIGH — excludes sub employee claims |
| "Labor Law Exclusion" | HIGH — excludes sub employee claims |
| "Worker Injury Exclusion" | HIGH — excludes sub employee claims |
| "Employee Injury Exclusion" | MEDIUM — check scope |
| "Employers Liability Exclusion (Amended)" | MEDIUM — check if scope expanded |
| "Contractor's Limitation" | MEDIUM — check what it limits |

**source**: [Amwins - Contractor's GL Coverage Limitation Endorsements](https://www.amwins.com/resources-insights/article/contractor-s-general-liability-11-common-coverage-limitation-endorsements)

## the insured contract exception

### what it is

even if action over exclusion exists, an exception may preserve coverage:

> "This exclusion does not apply to liability assumed by the insured under an 'insured contract.'"

### what "insured contract" means

an "insured contract" includes contracts where the insured assumes tort liability of another. your construction contract with indemnification clause qualifies.

### verify it's intact

some insurers REMOVE this exception via endorsement. ask:

> "Does the policy contain the standard insured contract exception to the employers liability exclusion, and has that exception been modified or removed by endorsement?"

**source**: [IRMI - Employers Liability Exclusion](https://www.irmi.com/articles/expert-commentary/employers-liability-exclusion-in-the-cgl-policy)

## questions to ask GC

### direct questions

| question | good answer | bad answer |
|----------|-------------|------------|
| "Does your GL have an action over exclusion?" | "No" or "I'll check with my broker" | "What's that?" |
| "Does your GL cover claims by sub employees?" | "Yes, standard coverage" | "My policy covers all of it" |
| "Can you provide the endorsement list?" | "Yes, I'll request from broker" | "The certificate is enough" |
| "Will your broker confirm in text?" | "Yes" | refuses |

### if GC doesn't know

most GCs don't know their policy details. escalate to their broker:

> "Please have your insurance broker provide written confirmation that your GL policy does not contain an action over exclusion, labor law exclusion, or similar endorsement that would exclude coverage for bodily injury claims by employees of your subcontractors."

---

## red flags

| red flag | risk |
|----------|------|
| GC refuses to provide endorsement list | may be concealed |
| GC says "the certificate is all you need" | doesn't understand coverage |
| broker won't confirm in text | knows there's an exclusion |
| policy is from non-standard insurer | more likely to have broad exclusions |
| premium is unusually low | coverage may be limited |
| GC recently switched insurers | may have lost coverage for claims |

## if action over exclusion exists

### your options

| option | protection level |
|--------|-----------------|
| require GC to remove endorsement | HIGH — if insurer allows |
| require GC to add "insured contract" exception | HIGH — restores coverage via contract |
| require certificates from each sub directly | HIGH — sub's comp blocks lawsuit |
| require wrap-up/OCIP policy | HIGH — single policy covers all |
| accept the gap + increase your umbrella | MEDIUM — you're still first to defend |
| find different GC | depends on new GC's coverage |

### endorsement removal

GC can ask their insurer to remove action over exclusion. cost varies:

| factor | impact on premium |
|--------|-------------------|
| GC's loss history | higher if prior claims |
| project size | larger = higher |
| sub count | more subs = higher |
| trade type | high-risk trades = higher |

typical cost: $500-5,000 per year for endorsement removal.

## contract language that helps

### if insured contract exception exists

include indemnification clause in your contract:

> "Contractor agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Owner from and against any and all claims, suits, damages, losses, and expenses arising out of or related to bodily injury to any person, including but not limited to employees of Contractor or any subcontractor, to the fullest extent permitted by law."

this triggers the "insured contract" exception and restores coverage even if action over exclusion exists.

### verify with broker

> "Please confirm that the indemnification clause in our construction contract qualifies as an 'insured contract' under [GC]'s GL policy and that the insured contract exception to the employers liability exclusion has not been removed or modified."

## verification checklist

### before work starts

| item | verified? |
|------|-----------|
| COI received with you as additional insured | ☐ |
| endorsement list requested | ☐ |
| no action over / labor law / worker injury exclusion | ☐ |
| OR insured contract exception intact | ☐ |
| broker confirmation letter received | ☐ |
| indemnification clause in contract | ☐ |
| sub certificate protocol established | ☐ |

---

## the fallback: sub certificates

if you can't verify GC's GL covers sub injuries, require certificates from each sub:

| sub document | verifies |
|--------------|----------|
| sub's workers' comp certificate | exclusive remedy blocks lawsuit |
| sub's GL certificate | sub has coverage |
| you as additional insured on sub's GL | sub's GL defends you |

**this is the most reliable protection** — sub's comp blocks the lawsuit before it starts.

---

## key takeaways

| question | answer |
|----------|--------|
| does certificate prove coverage for sub injuries? | NO — only proves coverage exists |
| what proves coverage applies? | policy endorsements + broker confirmation |
| what exclusions create the gap? | action over, labor law, worker injury |
| how to restore coverage if exclusion exists? | insured contract exception + indemnification clause |
| best protection regardless? | sub certificates (sub's comp blocks lawsuit) |
