# ref.insurance-causation-doctrine.indiana

## .what

Indiana is a **"yellow light" jurisdiction** — no clear established rule on causation doctrine. courts apply general contract interpretation principles.

## .the rule

no definitive efficient proximate cause or concurrent causation rule. courts analyze policy language and apply contract principles.

## .key case

**Sheehan Construction Company, Inc. v. Continental Casualty Company (Ind. 2010)**

- **landmark Indiana Supreme Court decision**
- **held**: faulty workmanship MAY constitute an "accident" and "occurrence" under CGL policy terms
- **key factor**: if defective work by subcontractors was done **unintentionally and without design**, it can trigger coverage
- the 1986 ISO form was modified to nullify the "your work" exclusion if damage was caused by a subcontractor

## .the subcontractor exception

significant protection in Indiana:

| work done by | "your work" exclusion applies? |
|--------------|-------------------------------|
| insured contractor directly | YES — excluded |
| subcontractor | NO — subcontractor exception preserves coverage |

**implication**: if unpermitted work was done by a subcontractor, coverage for property damage may exist under the subcontractor exception.

## .occurrence analysis

Indiana courts focus on whether damage was "accidental" from insured's perspective:

| scenario | "occurrence"? |
|----------|---------------|
| unintentional defective work | YES |
| intentional cost-cut or known code violation | NO — not accidental |
| negligent installation | YES |

## .coverage scope

| damage type | covered? |
|-------------|----------|
| damage TO defective work itself | NO |
| damage CAUSED BY defective work to other property | YES |

example: defective roof leaks and damages computer below → computer damage covered, roof repair not covered.

## .relevant statutes

| statute | rule |
|---------|------|
| IC 32-27-3-1 et seq. | before a construction defect lawsuit, claimant must serve written notice at least 60 days in advance |
| 10-year statute of repose | outside limit for contractor/design professional liability, runs from substantial completion |

## .anti-concurrent causation clauses

no definitive Indiana case law. courts would likely follow "freedom of contract" principles and enforce ACC clauses unless ambiguous or contrary to public policy.

## .practical implication for unpermitted work

| scenario | likely outcome |
|----------|----------------|
| unpermitted work by subcontractor caused damage | coverage likely (subcontractor exception) |
| unpermitted work by homeowner/GC caused damage | denial likely ("your work" exclusion) |
| unpermitted work was unintentional/negligent | may qualify as "occurrence" |
| unpermitted work was intentional code violation | NOT an "occurrence" — denial |
| damage to other property from defect | coverage likely |
| damage to defective work itself | denial |

**action items:**
- check if unpermitted work was done by subcontractor (coverage more likely)
- check if work was unintentional/negligent vs. intentional violation
- provide 60 days written notice before any construction defect lawsuit
