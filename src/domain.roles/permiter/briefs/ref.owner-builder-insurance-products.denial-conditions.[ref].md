# ref.owner-builder-insurance-products.denial-conditions

## .what

conditions under which builder's risk / owner-builder insurance claims are denied, with case law and documented scenarios.

## .why this matters

- builder's risk is not a guaranteed payout
- specific exclusions and conditions apply
- understanding denial triggers helps avoid them
- policy language matters enormously

---

## category 1: standard exclusions

### faulty workmanship exclusion

**what's excluded**: the cost to repair or replace defective work itself

**what's covered**: resulting damage from the defect (depends on policy)

| scenario | covered? |
|----------|----------|
| poorly installed roof | NO — the roof repair |
| water damage from that roof | MAYBE — if LEG 3 provision exists |
| defective concrete pour | NO — the concrete |
| structural damage from settling | MAYBE — resulting damage |

> "Most builder's risk policies include exclusions for faulty workmanship and typically do not cover the costs of repairing the defective work itself."
> — [MEREDA](https://mereda.org/2021/09/14/the-faulty-workmanship-exclusion-in-builders-risk-insurance-measure-thrice-cut-once/)

**case law**: *Archer Western – De Moya Joint Venture v. Ace American Insurance Co.* (Florida, 2024) — contractor claimed $840M project damage from concrete mixed with excessive fly ash. Insurer initially denied; court found factual dispute requiring trial due to LEG 3 provision language.

### the "ensuing loss" exception (fire from faulty work)

**critical distinction**: faulty workmanship exclusion does NOT exclude fires caused by faulty work.

| what | covered? |
|------|----------|
| faulty work itself | NO |
| fire caused by faulty work | YES — fire is a covered peril |
| fire damage to structure | YES |
| fire damage to materials | YES |
| cost to redo the faulty work | NO |

> "If certain defective work (excluded) caused a collapse or a fire (covered event), then coverage would be afforded."
> — [Smith Currie](https://www.smithcurrie.com/publications/common-sense-contract-law/the-interplay-of-builders-risk-and-commercial-general-liability-coverage/)

> "If there's faulty electrical equipment at a construction site that causes a fire—this would exclude the cost of replacing and installing that faulty component, but the rest of the loss is covered."
> — [MEREDA](https://mereda.org/2021/09/14/the-faulty-workmanship-exclusion-in-builders-risk-insurance-measure-thrice-cut-once/)

**example: electrician accidentally starts fire while on panel work**

| damage | covered? |
|--------|----------|
| $50k fire damage to house | YES |
| $3k to redo electrical work | NO |
| **net coverage** | ~$47k |

**why this works**: most builder's risk policies contain an "ensuing loss" exception — if excluded cause (faulty work) leads to covered peril (fire, collapse), the result IS covered.

> "Almost all builders risk policies contain exclusions for faulty design, materials and workmanship. However, most also include an ensuing loss exception, which basically says if the loss is caused by an otherwise insured peril, the resulting loss will be covered."
> — [IA Magazine](https://www.iamagazine.com/markets/builders-risk-5-common-coverage-exclusions-and-clauses)

**what this means for contractor negligence**:

| scenario | outcome |
|----------|---------|
| contractor accidentally starts fire | fire damage COVERED |
| contractor installs defective wires | wire repair NOT covered |
| defective wires later cause fire | fire damage COVERED |
| contractor drops tool, damages floor | floor repair NOT covered |
| contractor error causes collapse | collapse damage COVERED |

**the exclusion protects insurers from payment for bad work — not from payment for fires.**

### water damage exclusions

| type | typically covered? |
|------|-------------------|
| storm/weather water | YES |
| burst pipe (covered peril) | YES |
| water from faulty plumbing install | NO — faulty workmanship |
| flood | NO — requires separate flood policy |
| groundwater intrusion | NO — earth movement exclusion |

> "Builder's risk policies often exclude losses from water intrusion... the insurer may deny the claim if the water damage is due to improper plumbing installation."
> — [Hartford](https://www.thehartford.com/insights/construction/builders-risk-insurance-coverages-exclusions)

### earth movement exclusion

| type | covered? |
|------|----------|
| earthquake | NO (rider available) |
| landslide | NO |
| soil settlement | NO |
| minor soil movement | NO — exclusion is broad |
| cracking from settling | NO |

### design error exclusion

| scenario | covered? |
|----------|----------|
| architect's design flaw | NO |
| engineer's calculation error | NO |
| resulting structural failure | MAYBE — depends on policy |

**case law**: *Mattress King LLC v. Hanover Insurance Company* (Colorado) — crane damaged warehouse concrete slab. Claim DENIED because engineering report showed slab wasn't designed to support construction loads. Court ruled insurer did not breach good faith.

## category 2: policy condition failures

### coverage already ended

| trigger | consequence |
|---------|-------------|
| 60 days after occupancy | coverage terminates |
| property put to intended use | coverage terminates |
| policy term expired | no coverage |

> "Coverage ends 60 days after the property is occupied in whole or in part, or put to its intended use."
> — [US Assure](https://usassure.com/resources/articles/7-tips-to-avoid-common-residential-builders-risk-claim-problems)

**documented denial**: fire occurred after partial occupancy began; claim denied because coverage had terminated.

### percentage complete misrepresented

| scenario | consequence |
|----------|-------------|
| reported 30% complete | coverage limited to that percentage |
| actually 80% complete | claim denied or reduced |
| honest mistake | still denied |

> "Claims have been denied when the percentage complete was incorrectly reported on the policy due to honest mistakes."
> — [US Assure](https://usassure.com/resources/articles/builders-risk-insurance-lessons-learned-from-claim-setbacks)

**documented denial**: remodeling project 80% complete had fire; coverage for prior $400k structure denied because agent excluded it on application.

### failed subjectivities / special conditions

| subjectivity | example |
|--------------|---------|
| security requirements | alarm, fencing, locks |
| fire protection | extinguishers, sprinklers |
| reporting requirements | inventory reports, progress reports |
| inspection requirements | periodic site inspections |

> "Insurance professionals have seen an increase in builders risk denials based on failure to comply with policy subjectivities or special conditions."
> — [Foley Hoag](https://foleyhoag.com/news-and-insights/blogs/energy-and-climate-counsel/2023/may/navigating-builders-risk-subjectivities-how-to-avoid-unnecessary-denials-of-a-builders-risk-claim/)

**documented denial**: vandalism claim denied because "reasonable security measures weren't in place."

### late reporting

| requirement | consequence of violation |
|-------------|--------------------------|
| prompt notice of loss | claim denied |
| timely claim submission | claim denied |
| documentation requirements | reduced payout or denial |

## category 3: documentation failures

### missing documentation

| missing item | consequence |
|--------------|-------------|
| photos of damage | validity questioned |
| receipts for materials | value disputed |
| estimates for repair | payout reduced |
| inventory reports | payment limited to % of reports submitted |

> "Claims lack photos, receipts, estimates, consistent reports, or other necessary documentation — insurers may question validity or reduce payout."
> — [Burton & Company](https://burtonandcompany.com/why-home-building-companies-insurance-claims-get-denied/)

### inconsistent reporting

| issue | consequence |
|-------|-------------|
| contradictory statements | claim questioned |
| changing story | fraud investigation |
| incomplete information | denial possible |

## category 4: misrepresentation

### material misrepresentation

| type | consequence |
|------|-------------|
| false information on application | policy rescinded |
| concealed relevant facts | claim denied |
| underreported project value | coinsurance penalty |
| wrong property description | coverage gap |

> "Insurers frequently deny claims if there's evidence of false or incomplete information on file."
> — [Wexford Insurance](https://www.wexfordins.com/post/why-apartment-building-insurance-claims-get-denied)

### intent requirement (varies by state)

| state rule | requirement |
|------------|-------------|
| strict states | any misrepresentation = denial |
| intent states (e.g., Texas) | misrepresentation must be material AND intentional |

> "In Texas law, a misrepresentation by itself is not sufficient to deny a claim — the misrepresentation must be material and based on intent to deceive."
> — [Wyly Law Firm](https://wylylawfirm.com/understanding-misrepresentation-in-insurance/)

## category 5: underinsurance

### coinsurance penalty

| scenario | consequence |
|----------|-------------|
| insured for $100k | policy limit |
| actual value $200k | coinsurance penalty applies |
| claim for $50k loss | payout reduced proportionally |

> "Underinsuring a project could lead to a coinsurance penalty, and claims may be denied with damages capped at policy limits."
> — [US Assure](https://usassure.com/resources/articles/7-tips-to-avoid-common-residential-builders-risk-claim-problems)

## category 6: coverage gaps

### work before policy inception

| scenario | covered? |
|----------|----------|
| work done before policy start | NO |
| materials purchased before | depends on policy |
| pre-extant conditions | NO |

### structures over 30% complete

| scenario | consequence |
|----------|-------------|
| policy excludes >30% complete | coverage denied |
| unless underwriting approval | must be obtained in advance |

> "Many policies exclude structures that are over 30% complete unless underwriting approval is granted."
> — [Hartford](https://www.thehartford.com/insights/construction/builders-risk-insurance-coverages-exclusions)

## how to avoid denial

### before purchase

| action | why it matters |
|--------|----------------|
| accurately report project value | avoid coinsurance penalty |
| accurately report % complete | avoid coverage gap |
| disclose all relevant facts | avoid misrepresentation |
| read exclusions carefully | know what's not covered |
| understand subjectivities | comply from day one |

### during project

| action | why it matters |
|--------|----------------|
| maintain security | avoid security-related denials |
| document everything | support future claims |
| submit required reports | avoid reporting-based denials |
| report changes to insurer | maintain accurate coverage |
| report losses promptly | avoid late-notice denials |

### at claim time

| action | why it matters |
|--------|----------------|
| document damage thoroughly | support claim validity |
| gather receipts and records | prove values |
| report immediately | meet notice requirements |
| don't exaggerate | avoid fraud allegations |
| cooperate with adjuster | facilitate process |
