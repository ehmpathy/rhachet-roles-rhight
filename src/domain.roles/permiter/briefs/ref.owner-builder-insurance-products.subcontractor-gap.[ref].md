# ref.owner-builder-insurance-products.subcontractor-gap

## .what

coverage gap when a contractor subs out work without disclosure — and why builder's risk protects the homeowner when contractor insurance fails.

## .the scenario

you hire a licensed, insured electrician for $3k breaker panel work. they:
1. say they'll do the work themselves
2. actually sub it out to someone else
3. that someone causes damage (fire, injury, property damage)

**question**: who pays?

---

## why contractor's insurance may NOT cover you

### the "hammer clause" / subcontractor warranty limitation

most contractor general liability policies include subcontractor requirements:

| requirement | what it means |
|-------------|---------------|
| subs must be insured | contractor must verify sub's coverage |
| subs must name contractor as additional insured | sub's policy must protect contractor |
| subs must meet minimum limits | $1M or whatever policy specifies |
| contractor must have contracts with subs | written agreements required |

**if any of these fail**: contractor's policy may deny coverage for that sub's work.

> "Insurers may deny coverage based on the fact that faulty work was done by a subcontractor who did not have insurance that complied with the subcontractor's warranty limitation form... failure to comply with subcontractor endorsement terms results in coverage nullified relative to any loss from the work of the subcontractor."
> — [Amwins](https://www.amwins.com/resources-insights/article/contractor-s-general-liability-11-common-coverage-limitation-endorsements)

### the undisclosed sub scenario

| what happened | coverage status |
|---------------|-----------------|
| contractor said they'd do work | you relied on their insurance |
| contractor secretly subbed it out | sub may not meet policy requirements |
| sub caused damage | contractor's policy may deny |
| you file claim against contractor | denied due to subcontractor exclusion |

**you're left with**:
- lawsuit against contractor (may be uncollectible)
- lawsuit against sub (may be uninsured/judgment-proof)
- your own coverage (if you have builder's risk)

## the $3k electrical panel example

### scenario A: no builder's risk

| event | outcome |
|-------|---------|
| hired "ABC Electric" for panel upgrade | $3k job |
| ABC subs to unlicensed worker without notice | violation of contract |
| sub causes electrical fire | $50k damage |
| you claim against ABC's insurance | DENIED — sub didn't meet policy requirements |
| you sue ABC | ABC is small LLC, no assets |
| you sue sub | sub is judgment-proof |
| **result** | you pay $50k out of pocket |

### scenario B: with builder's risk

| event | outcome |
|-------|---------|
| same scenario | same fire, same $50k damage |
| you claim on YOUR builder's risk policy | COVERED — protects your property |
| builder's risk pays $50k | minus deductible |
| builder's risk subrogates against ABC/sub | their problem now |
| **result** | you're made whole |

---

## what builder's risk covers in this scenario

| coverage | applies? |
|----------|----------|
| fire damage to structure | YES |
| damage to materials | YES |
| damage to other property | YES |
| your soft costs (delays, re-permits) | usually YES |
| contractor's liability | NO (that's their policy) |
| injury to sub | NO (that's workers' comp) |

**key point**: builder's risk covers YOUR property interest. it doesn't matter whose negligence caused it or whether their insurance pays.

## why this matters even with "good" contractors

### contractors lie

| reason | frequency |
|--------|-----------|
| capacity overflow — sub to meet deadlines | common |
| cost savings — cheaper labor | common |
| specialty work — sub has specific skill | common |
| undisclosed to avoid permit issues | common |

you may never know work was subbed until a problem occurs.

### contractors make mistakes

| mistake | consequence |
|---------|-------------|
| didn't verify sub's insurance | policy exclusion triggered |
| sub's policy lapsed | no coverage |
| sub's limits too low | insufficient coverage |
| no written contract with sub | policy requirement violated |

even honest contractors can fail to maintain compliance.

### contractors go out of business

| scenario | your recourse |
|----------|---------------|
| contractor dissolves LLC | no assets to collect |
| contractor declares bankruptcy | claim discharged |
| contractor unresponsive | expensive litigation |

your builder's risk policy doesn't depend on a payout from contractor.

## how to protect yourself

### option 1: builder's risk (recommended)

| cost | coverage |
|------|----------|
| 1-5% of project | your property, materials, structure |

for $3k job: ~$30-150 for short-term policy

### option 2: verify contractor practices

| verification | what to ask |
|--------------|-------------|
| "will YOU do the work?" | get it in written form |
| "do you use subs?" | if yes, verify their insurance |
| certificate of insurance | verify current, adequate limits |
| additional insured status | ask to be named |

### option 3: contract terms

| clause | protection |
|--------|------------|
| "no subs without written approval" | gives you control |
| "all subs must provide COI" | documentation |
| "contractor indemnifies for sub's work" | contractual protection |

**reality**: options 2 and 3 give you lawsuit rights, not guaranteed payment. builder's risk gives you coverage.

## cost-benefit for small jobs

| job size | builder's risk cost | coverage |
|----------|---------------------|----------|
| $3,000 | $30-150 | $3k+ in materials/damage |
| $10,000 | $100-500 | $10k+ protection |
| $25,000 | $250-1,250 | $25k+ protection |

**the math**: ~1-5% premium to eliminate subcontractor gap risk.

---

## when builder's risk is overkill

| scenario | builder's risk needed? |
|----------|------------------------|
| small cosmetic work (paint, fixtures) | probably not |
| no fire/water/structural risk | probably not |
| contractor is large, established, bonded | lower risk (but not zero) |
| you can absorb the loss | personal risk tolerance |

| scenario | builder's risk recommended |
|----------|---------------------------|
| electrical work | YES — fire risk |
| plumbing work | YES — water damage risk |
| structural work | YES — collapse risk |
| HVAC/gas work | YES — fire/CO risk |
| any work >$5k | YES — material loss |
