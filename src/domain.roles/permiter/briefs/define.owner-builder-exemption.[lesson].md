# define.owner-builder-exemption

## .what

homeowners can legally do their own electrical, plumbing, HVAC, and structural work on their primary residence WITHOUT a trade license — they just need permits and inspections.

## .why this matters

- DIY dads are legal
- license requirements apply to work for OTHERS, not yourself
- permits ensure safety via inspection, not via installer credentials
- most jurisdictions allow owner-builders for primary residence

---

## the common misconception

| misconception | reality |
|---------------|---------|
| "I need an electrician license to do electrical" | NO — you need a license to do electrical FOR OTHERS |
| "only plumbers can do plumbing" | NO — you can do your own plumbing on your home |
| "unpermitted = unlicensed" | NO — permits and licenses are separate requirements |
| "DIY is illegal" | NO — DIY is legal with proper permits |

---

## license vs permit

| requirement | who it applies to | purpose |
|-------------|-------------------|---------|
| license | contractors who work for others | proves competency to public |
| permit | anyone who does permitted work | ensures inspection happens |

**the permit system doesn't care WHO does the work — it cares that the work gets inspected.**

## what owner-builders can do

| work type | owner-builder allowed? | notes |
|-----------|------------------------|-------|
| electrical panel upgrade | YES | permit + inspection required |
| circuit additions | YES | permit + inspection required |
| whole-house rewire | YES | permit + inspection required |
| plumbing repipe | YES | permit + inspection required |
| water heater install | YES | permit + inspection required |
| HVAC replacement | YES | some jurisdictions restrict gas work |
| bathroom addition | YES | multiple permits may be needed |
| room addition | YES | structural inspection required |
| deck/patio | YES | permit + inspection required |
| roofing | YES | permit may be required |

---

## owner-builder requirements

even without a license, owner-builders must:

| requirement | detail |
|-------------|--------|
| own the property | you or spouse on title |
| occupy the property | primary residence, not rental |
| pull permits | before work begins (or retroactive) |
| meet code | work must comply with adopted codes |
| pass inspections | city inspector verifies compliance |
| not sell immediately | some jurisdictions restrict sale for 1-2 years |

---

## what owner-builders CANNOT do

| restriction | reason |
|-------------|--------|
| work on rental property | owner-builder requires occupancy |
| work for others | that's contracting — requires license |
| skip permits | permits required regardless of who does work |
| skip inspections | inspections required regardless of installer |
| certain gas work | some jurisdictions restrict to licensed only |

---

## why this exemption exists

the license requirement exists to protect the PUBLIC from incompetent contractors. when you work on your own home:

- you bear the risk
- you live with the results
- you're not deceiving a customer
- the permit/inspection system still catches safety issues

---

## indianapolis specifics

per `ref.homeowner-permit-exemption.indianapolis-in`:

> "Any construction that requires a permit requires the applicant to be either a Marion County licensed contractor or the property owner."

> "In most cases, homeowners in Indianapolis can pull permits and perform work on their own primary residence as an owner-builder."

| requirement | indianapolis rule |
|-------------|-------------------|
| who can pull permits | licensed contractor OR property owner |
| primary residence required | yes |
| inspection required | yes |
| license required for owner | no |

## jurisdiction variations

| jurisdiction type | typical rule |
|-------------------|--------------|
| most US cities | owner-builder allowed for primary residence |
| some strict cities | may require licensed contractor for certain trades |
| rural areas | often more permissive |
| HOA communities | may have additional restrictions (not legal, contractual) |

**always verify**: call your local permit office to confirm owner-builder eligibility.

## practical implications

### for new work

| scenario | what to do |
|----------|------------|
| DIY electrical upgrade | pull permit → do work → call for inspection |
| DIY bathroom remodel | pull permits (electrical, plumbing, structural) → do work → inspections |
| DIY deck | pull permit → do work → call for inspection |

### for retroactive permits

| scenario | what to do |
|----------|------------|
| you did work without permit | apply for retroactive permit → inspection |
| previous owner did work | see `howto.retroactive-permit.inherited-work` |

## common questions

### "can I hire assistants?"

yes — you can hire unlicensed workers to assist you. YOU are the responsible party as owner-builder. some jurisdictions require you to be "actively involved" not just supervising.

### "what if I sell the house?"

disclosure requirements vary. some jurisdictions require disclosure of owner-builder work. some restrict sale for 1-2 years after owner-builder permit.

### "what if inspection fails?"

you fix the issues and call for re-inspection. same as any contractor would do.

### "is there liability?"

yes — as owner-builder, you're responsible for the work quality. if it causes damage, that's on you. this is why inspection matters.
