# hazard.prepurchase-unpermitted-work.summary.tldr

## .the hazard

you bought a house. previous owner made changes (electrical, plumbing, HVAC, bathroom, kitchen) without permits. you don't know. years later, something fails. insurer searches permits. finds none. may deny claim.

## .can this happen?

**yes.**

## .does ignorance protect you?

**no.**

| factor | protects you? |
|--------|--------------|
| previous owner did the work | NO |
| you didn't know | NO |
| 10+ years passed | NO |
| seller didn't disclose | NO |
| home inspection passed | NO |
| work was done to code | HELPS but doesn't guarantee |

## .how often does it happen?

**rarely — but it does happen.**

| source | quote |
|--------|-------|
| Tim_Kline (16 yrs insurance repair) | "I have never seen a claim denied for any of your reasons" |
| marksr (forum moderator) | "they rarely do so" |

**documented denials:**
- Bridgewater, NJ: fire in unpermitted basement → denied
- Michael Thomas: unpermitted additions with defects → denied

## .when do denials happen?

| scenario | outcome |
|----------|---------|
| unpermitted work **directly caused** damage | denial likely |
| damage **unrelated** to unpermitted work | coverage likely |
| both covered peril AND unpermitted work contributed | depends on state |

**key factor: causation.** not mere existence of unpermitted work.

## .types of subtle unpermitted work

| work | visibility | risk if it fails |
|------|------------|------------------|
| rewired circuits | NONE | fire claim denied |
| replaced pipes | NONE | leak/flood claim denied |
| water heater swap | LOW | flood/fire claim denied |
| HVAC replacement | LOW | fire/CO claim denied |
| bathroom remodel | LOW | water damage denied |
| kitchen remodel | LOW | fire claim denied |

## .why you don't know

- seller may not know (prior owner did it)
- seller may not disclose
- home inspection doesn't check permits
- title insurance doesn't cover it
- no one tells you to check

## .how insurers find out

at claim time, adjuster:
1. inspects damaged system
2. notes equipment age
3. searches permit database
4. finds no permit
5. denies under code violation or defect exclusion

## .your options

**before purchase:**
- pull permit history from local permit office
- compare permit dates to equipment ages
- hire trade-specific inspectors if gaps found

**after purchase (if you discover unpermitted work):**
- disclose to insurer
- pursue retroactive permits
- consult attorney

**after denial:**
- appeal — denials are sometimes negotiable
- litigate — depends on state doctrine (see state briefs)

## .state doctrine matters

| state | doctrine | mixed causation outcome |
|-------|----------|------------------------|
| Florida | concurrent causation | coverage possible if no ACC clause |
| Oregon | efficient proximate cause | what "set in motion" determines |
| Indiana | unclear | subcontractor exception may help |

## .bottom line

| question | answer |
|----------|--------|
| can they deny? | YES |
| will they always deny? | NO — rare |
| when do they deny? | unpermitted work directly caused damage |
| does ignorance protect you? | NO |
| can you detect this before purchase? | YES — permit search |
| do most buyers do this? | NO |

## .the math

- ~40-50% of homes have unpermitted work (HomeLight)
- ~35% of homeowners would skip permits (Clever Real Estate)
- denial frequency: unknown (insurers don't publish)
- cost of permit search: $100-300
- cost of denied claim: $50k-500k+
