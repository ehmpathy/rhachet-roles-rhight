# rule.require-additional-insured

## .rule

**REQUIRE** from contractors:

| coverage | protects you from | when to require |
|----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **workers' comp** | worker sues you for injury | ALWAYS — any contractor with employees |
| **GL + additional insured** | third party sues you (visitor, neighbor) | when work could injure non-workers or damage adjacent property |

**PREFER** both even for small jobs — the cost is minimal and the protection is significant.

---

## critical distinction: two different protections

### workers' comp vs GL — they protect against DIFFERENT risks

```
WORKER INJURED ON YOUR PROPERTY
        │
        ├─── contractor HAS workers' comp
        │         │
        │         ▼
        │    worker files workers' comp claim
        │         │
        │         ▼
        │    worker CANNOT sue you (comp is exclusive remedy)
        │         │
        │         ▼
        │    YOU: protected
        │
        └─── contractor LACKS workers' comp
                  │
                  ▼
             worker sues YOU directly
                  │
                  ▼
             your homeowners defends
                  │
                  ▼
             YOU: $20K-100K+ defense costs


THIRD PARTY (visitor/neighbor) INJURED
        │
        ├─── you ARE additional insured on contractor's GL
        │         │
        │         ▼
        │    contractor's GL defends you
        │         │
        │         ▼
        │    YOU: protected, their premiums affected
        │
        └─── you are NOT additional insured
                  │
                  ▼
             your homeowners defends
                  │
                  ▼
             YOU: $20K-100K+ defense costs, your premiums rise
```

### what each coverage does

| coverage | covers | does NOT cover |
|----------|--------|----------------|
| **workers' comp** | worker injuries on the job | third-party injuries |
| **contractor's GL** | third-party injuries, property damage | worker injuries (that's comp) |
| **additional insured** | YOU under contractor's GL | worker injuries |

### the common confusion

| scenario | correct coverage |
|----------|-----------------|
| roofer falls off ladder | **workers' comp** (not GL) |
| roofer drops shingles on pedestrian | **GL** (third party) |
| electrician electrocuted | **workers' comp** |
| electrical fire injures your visitor | **GL** (third party) |
| plumber floods your basement | **GL** (property damage) |

## when to require each

### workers' comp: ALWAYS require

| contractor type | require workers' comp? |
|-----------------|----------------------|
| any contractor with employees | **YES — ALWAYS** |
| sole proprietor (no employees) | check state law — may be exempt |
| "helper" you pay cash | risky — may be deemed your employee |

**why**: without workers' comp, injured worker sues YOU. workers' comp is the exclusive remedy — it blocks lawsuits against you.

### GL + additional insured: based on risk

| risk factor | require GL + additional insured? |
|-------------|--------------------------------|
| work could injure visitor | **YES** |
| work could damage neighbor's property | **YES** |
| fire/flood could spread beyond your home | **YES** |
| multi-unit structure (condo/townhouse) | **YES** |
| interior work, single-family, no visitors | **PREFER** (low risk but cheap protection) |

## the math: why require it

### cost-benefit analysis

| factor | without additional insured | with additional insured |
|--------|---------------------------|------------------------|
| defense cost if sued | $20K-100K+ (your policy) | $0 (their policy) |
| premium impact | +10-40% for 3-5 years | none |
| 5-year excess cost | $2K-10K+ in premiums | $0 |
| cost to obtain | — | $0-50 (usually free) |

**ROI**: $0-50 prevents $20K-100K+ exposure.

|---------------|---------------------------|--------|

## the risk transfer

```
WITHOUT additional insured:
  contractor negligence → lawsuit → YOUR policy defends → YOUR premiums rise

WITH additional insured:
  contractor negligence → lawsuit → THEIR policy defends → YOUR premiums unaffected
```

## the standard (commercial vs residential)

| context | additional insured status |
|---------|--------------------------|
| commercial construction | **ALWAYS required** — industry standard |
| residential (GC-managed) | **often required** — professional GCs expect it |
| residential (owner-builder) | **rarely requested** — homeowners don't know to ask |

**the gap**: residential homeowners face the same liability exposure as commercial owners but rarely obtain the same protection.

**this rule closes that gap.**

## what to require

### for worker injury protection (workers' comp)

| document | purpose |
|----------|---------|
| workers' comp certificate | proves comp coverage exists |
| verify active dates | confirm coverage spans your project |

| coverage | minimum | why |
|----------|---------|-----|
| workers' compensation | state minimum | blocks workers from suing you |

**if contractor claims exemption**: verify with state — sole proprietors may be exempt, but get it in writing.

### for third-party protection (GL + additional insured)

| document | purpose |
|----------|---------|
| certificate of insurance (COI) | proves GL coverage exists |
| additional insured endorsement | adds YOU to their policy |

| coverage | minimum | why |
|----------|---------|-----|
| general liability | $1M per occurrence | covers serious third-party injuries |
| aggregate | $2M | covers multiple claims |
| completed operations | included | covers injuries after work complete |

### contract language

> "Contractor shall name Owner as additional insured on Contractor's commercial general liability policy. Contractor's insurance shall be primary and non-contributory. Contractor waives all rights of subrogation against Owner. Contractor shall provide certificate of insurance prior to commencement of work."

---

## when to enforce

| stage | action |
|-------|--------|
| before contract signed | include requirement in contract |
| before work starts | verify COI received with your name |
| before final payment | confirm coverage was active throughout |

### if contractor refuses

| contractor response | your action |
|--------------------|-------------|
| "I don't have insurance" | do not hire — you absorb all risk |
| "I can't add you" | ask why — may indicate coverage issues |
| "It costs extra" | offer to pay the $25-50 — worth it |
| "I've never been asked" | explain the requirement — educate them |
| "My insurance covers you anyway" | incorrect — require the endorsement |

---

## exceptions (when to accept without)

| situation | acceptable? | condition |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| very small job (<$500) | maybe | if contained to your property, no injury risk |
| friend/family as helper | maybe | but your homeowners may not cover their injuries |
| licensed professional with high limits | maybe | if their $2M+ limits clearly sufficient |

**default**: require it. exceptions should be conscious decisions, not oversights.

---

## the decision framework

```
STEP 1: Does contractor have employees?
  │
  ├─ YES → REQUIRE workers' comp (blocks worker lawsuits against you)
  │
  └─ NO (solo) → check state exemption rules, get in writing


STEP 2: Could work injure a third party (visitor, neighbor, pedestrian)?
  │
  ├─ YES → REQUIRE GL + additional insured
  │
  └─ NO → continue to step 3


STEP 3: Could work damage adjacent property (fire spread, flood, excavation)?
  │
  ├─ YES → REQUIRE GL + additional insured
  │
  └─ NO → continue to step 4


STEP 4: Interior only, single-family, no visitors during work?
  │
  └─ PREFER GL + additional insured (low risk, but cheap protection)
```
