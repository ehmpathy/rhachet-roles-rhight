# ref.indianapolis-electrical-retroactive-permit

## .what

retroactive electrical permit process in Indianapolis-Marion County for unpermitted panel upgrades (e.g., fuse box to breaker panel).

## .why

- panel upgrades require electrical permits per IRC R105.1
- retroactive permits can legalize prior unpermitted work
- insurance coverage may be affected by permit status
- sale or refinance may require permit documentation

## permit requirements for panel upgrade

Indianapolis requires electrical permits for:

- panel changes (fuse box to breaker)
- service upgrades (e.g., 100A to 200A)
- new circuits
- whole-house rewire

| document | requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| electrical plan | panel schedule, circuit assignments |
| licensed electrician | Indiana-licensed contractor must certify or inspect |
| permit application | mark as "as-built" or "retroactive" |

## permit fees (Indianapolis)

| work type | fee range |
|-----------|-----------|
| panel upgrade (200A) | $100–$350 |
| whole-house rewire | $250–$600+ |
| re-inspection (if fail) | $50–$100 |
| **retroactive penalty** | typically 2x standard fee |

**estimate for retroactive panel permit**: $200–$700 (standard fee + penalty)

## process for retroactive permit

### step 1: gather documentation

| document | purpose |
|----------|---------|
| photos of panel | show current installation |
| photos of breakers | show labels, amperage ratings |
| purchase records | prove when you acquired property |
| home inspection report | may document prior work |
| any receipts | if previous owner left records |

### step 2: hire licensed electrician

for retroactive permits, you need a licensed electrician to:

- inspect the completed work
- certify it meets code (or identify deficiencies)
- provide affidavit of compliance
- pull the retroactive permit on your behalf

**Indiana electrician license levels**:

| level | authority |
|-------|-----------|
| apprentice | cannot pull permits |
| journeyman | can do work, limited permit authority |
| master electrician | can pull permits, supervise, certify |

### step 3: apply for retroactive permit

submit application through [Accela portal](https://aca-prod.accela.com/indy/Default.aspx) or in person:

- mark application as "as-built" or "retroactive"
- include electrician's assessment
- disclose that work was done by prior owner or unknown party (if applicable)

### step 4: inspection

inspector will examine:

| item | what they check |
|------|-----------------|
| panel | proper installation, labels, ground connections |
| breakers | correct amperage, proper seating |
| grounding | grounding electrode, bonding |
| clearances | working space around panel |

### step 5: corrections (if needed)

if work fails inspection:

- licensed electrician fixes deficiencies
- re-inspection scheduled ($50–$100 fee)
- repeat until passed

### step 6: final approval

once passed:

- permit is finalized
- property records updated
- you have documentation for insurance and sale

## penalties and fees summary

| item | amount |
|------|--------|
| standard panel permit | $100–$350 |
| retroactive penalty | 2x standard (typical) |
| re-inspection | $50–$100 each |
| electrician inspection/certification | $150–$400 (varies) |

**total estimate**: $400–$1,200 based on scope and corrections

---

## what if you don't know who did the work

| situation | approach |
|-----------|----------|
| previous owner did it | you inherit responsibility |
| unknown contractor | hire licensed electrician to certify |
| no records at all | electrician inspection substitutes |
| work predates your ownership | DBNS may offer flexibility on penalties |

**key point**: Indianapolis DBNS typically works with innocent purchasers. explain your situation when you apply — they may waive or reduce penalties.

---

## insurance implications

| situation | risk |
|-----------|------|
| unpermitted panel upgrade | claim denial if electrical fire |
| permitted panel upgrade | standard coverage |
| fuse box still present | some insurers charge higher premiums |

**recommendation**: obtain retroactive permit before you disclose to insurer, then notify them of the update.
